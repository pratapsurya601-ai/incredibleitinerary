Budget Option
🏡 Local Islands (Maafushi)
Maafushi is the original budget Maldives. Guesthouses with beachfront access, licensed restaurants, and local excursion operators at a fraction of resort prices. You share the island with locals — which is a feature, not a bug. ₹5,000–₹8,000/night for a solid guesthouse room.
Mid-Range Option
🌊 Mid-Range Resorts
Private island resorts with beach bungalows, house reefs, restaurants and activities included or packaged. No locals visible, perfectly manicured, water bungalows may be available. ₹18,000–₹30,000/night all-inclusive or room-only. Seaplane transfer sometimes required.
Luxury Option
🛖 Overwater Bungalows
The iconic experience — a villa on stilts over the Indian Ocean with a glass floor panel, private deck, ladder directly into the lagoon. Brands like Six Senses, Soneva, One&Only, Coco Bodu Hithi. ₹50,000–₹1,50,000+/night. Worth saving up for at least 2 nights of the trip.
All Budgets
🤿 Marine Life
Maldives has some of the best marine biodiversity on the planet. Manta rays, whale sharks (seasonal — best May–November at South Ari Atoll), hawksbill turtles, black-tip reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, vibrant coral gardens. Almost every island has excellent snorkelling directly off the beach.

"The Maldives is not one place — it's 1,200 coral islands, 26 atolls, and a decision: which version of paradise do you want to afford?"

Visa: Maldives grants a free 30-day visa on arrival to all nationalities, including Indians. No prior application needed. Just a valid passport, onward ticket, and proof of accommodation. Extensions available. Tourist visa: completely free.
Flights from India: Mumbai (BOM) or Delhi (DEL) to Malé (MLE) — ₹25,000–₹45,000 return. IndiGo, Air India, and Maldivian all operate this route. Book 2–3 months ahead for best prices. Flight time: approximately 3.5 hours from Mumbai, 4.5 hours from Delhi.

Maldives 5-Day Itinerary Days 1–3 — Arrival, Snorkelling & Diving
01
Day One · Arrival & First Dip
Landing in Paradise
Fly into Malé · Speedboat or seaplane to island · Check in · First snorkel off the house reef
💰 Speedboat transfer: ₹2,000–₹5,000 return · Seaplane: ₹15,000–₹25,000 return
Arrival
Malé International Airport (MLE) — Transfer Immediately
Malé airport sits on its own island — Hulhulé. You cannot stay here; you need to transfer to your accommodation island. Budget travellers heading to Maafushi take a public ferry from the nearby jetty (₹150, 1.5hrs) or a speedboat (₹2,000–₹3,000 one way, 45 minutes). Resort guests are met by resort staff and whisked to a speedboat or a seaplane. Seaplanes are only available from 6am–4pm — if your flight arrives late, you may overnight in Malé first.
Public ferry to Maafushi: ₹150 · Speedboat: ₹2,000–₹3,000 · Seaplane: ₹15,000–₹25,000 one way
Afternoon
Check In — and Walk Into the Ocean
Drop your bags and walk straight to the beach. The Maldives house reef — the coral reef immediately surrounding your island — is usually snorkellable without a boat. Rent fins and a mask from the dive centre (₹500–₹800/day) or bring your own. The water is 28–30°C year-round, crystal clear, with visibility often exceeding 20 metres. Your first snorkel will likely be the best you've ever had. Turtles are commonly spotted within 50 metres of shore.
Snorkel equipment rental: ₹500–₹800/day · Water shoes recommended for reef entry
Sunset
Sunset on the Sandbank — Your First Maldives Moment
Every island has a sandbank nearby — a pure white strip of sand rising just above the water, ringed by turquoise. Many guesthouses offer a sunset sandbank trip (₹1,500–₹2,500). You wade out, stand on the sandbank, watch the sun drop into the Indian Ocean. No boats, no buildings, no people — just you and the ocean. This is the quintessential image of the Maldives and it's as extraordinary in reality as it looks in photographs.
Sandbank sunset trip: ₹1,500–₹2,500/person · Usually 1 hour · Book with guesthouse
02
Day Two · Guided Snorkelling
Manta Rays, Turtles & Sunset Cruise
Guided snorkel trip · Manta rays · Whale sharks (seasonal) · Sunset dhoni cruise
💰 Guided snorkel trip: ₹1,500–₹2,500 · Sunset cruise: ₹1,500
8:00 AM
Guided Snorkelling Trip — Manta Rays & Whale Sharks
Book a morning guided snorkelling excursion (₹1,500–₹2,500/person) through your guesthouse or a local operator. A traditional dhoni wooden boat takes you to 3–4 snorkel spots including channels known for manta ray cleaning stations and areas where whale sharks congregate. Whale shark sightings depend on season — South Ari Atoll is the most reliable year-round spot. Hawksbill sea turtles, black-tip reef sharks, and Napoleon wrasse are commonly seen on most trips. The guides know the best spots.
Guided snorkel trip: ₹1,500–₹2,500/person · 3–4hrs · Life jackets provided · Non-swimmer friendly
Afternoon
Beach Time — Do Absolutely Nothing
This is the Maldives. Permission granted to lie on a beach and stare at turquoise water for three hours. Read a book. Float in the lagoon. Watch the colourful parrotfish nibbling coral directly below your feet. This is the correct way to spend a Maldives afternoon. Resist the urge to optimise.
6:00 PM
Sunset Dhoni Cruise
A traditional Maldivian dhoni boat cruise at sunset — ₹1,500/person, usually 1.5 hours. You drift across the lagoon as the sky turns pink and orange, watching for dolphins (spinner dolphins are common in the Maldives and often approach the bow). The crew serve fresh watermelon or fresh coconut. This is one of the most beautiful evenings you will have in your life.
Sunset cruise: ₹1,500/person · Dolphin sighting likely but not guaranteed · 1.5 hours
03
Day Three · Go Deeper
Diving & Dolphin Cruise at Dusk
PADI intro dive or certified dive · Coral gardens · Dolphin cruise at sunset
💰 Intro dive: ₹5,000 · PADI Open Water course: ₹25,000 · Dolphin cruise: ₹1,500
Morning
Scuba Diving — The Underwater World in Full
Every guesthouse island and resort has a dive centre. For non-certified divers: a Discover Scuba Diving experience (₹5,000–₹7,000) takes you 5–10 metres deep with a divemaster by your side — no experience needed, 2 hours total. Certified divers: a single fun dive costs ₹5,000–₹8,000. Dive packages of 5 dives: ₹18,000–₹25,000. The PADI Open Water Diver certification course takes 3–4 days and costs ₹25,000–₹35,000 — the Maldives is one of the best places in the world to get certified due to visibility and marine life density.
Intro Scuba: ₹5,000–₹7,000 · Single fun dive: ₹5,000–₹8,000 · PADI OWD course: ₹25,000–₹35,000
Evening
Dolphin Cruise — Spinner Dolphins at Golden Hour
A second evening boat trip, specifically to find spinner dolphin pods. Spinner dolphins are abundant in the Maldives and frequently come within metres of boats, leaping and spinning in the bow wave. The sight of 50+ dolphins in formation against a setting sun is extraordinary. ₹1,500/person, 1.5 hours. Some operators combine this with a night snorkel (with underwater torch — octopus, lobster and moray eels are nocturnal).
Dolphin cruise: ₹1,500/person · Night snorkel add-on: ₹800 · Best season: Nov–Apr

04
Day Four · Sandbank & Bioluminescence
Deserted Islands & the Glowing Ocean
Day trip to private sandbank · Picnic lunch · Deserted island · Bioluminescent beach at night (seasonal)
💰 Sandbank picnic trip: ₹3,000–₹5,000 · Bioluminescence: free (Jun–Oct, select islands)
9:00 AM
Sandbank Picnic Day Trip — The Perfect Maldives Day
A full-day excursion (₹3,000–₹5,000/person, usually with lunch) takes you to a remote sandbank for 3–4 hours. You are completely alone on a strip of white sand in the middle of the Indian Ocean. The operator brings a barbecue lunch — fresh grilled fish, salads, fresh coconuts. Swimming all around. This is one of the most memorable experiences the Maldives offers and is available to both budget and luxury travellers.
Full-day sandbank picnic: ₹3,000–₹5,000/person · Includes lunch · BBQ fish · Book ahead
Afternoon
Deserted Island Exploration
Many day trips also include a visit to an uninhabited island covered in vegetation — you can explore the interior, walk along beaches with no footprints, find hermit crabs, and watch nesting sea birds. These islands feel like they're from another time. The contrast between the resort-polished Maldives experience and this raw, wild version of the same place is striking.
Night
Bioluminescent Beach — The Ocean Glows Blue
Certain Maldives islands experience bioluminescence — caused by phytoplankton (Noctiluca scintillans) that emit blue light when disturbed. The beach and shallows appear to glow electric blue as waves break. The phenomenon is most reliable June to October (monsoon season) on specific islands including Vaadhoo Island and some Raa Atoll beaches. Walk into the water and watch your hands glow. It is genuinely one of the most surreal natural phenomena on Earth. Check current conditions with your operator.
Free — occurs naturally · Best: Jun–Oct · Some resorts organise dedicated viewing excursions
05
Day Five · Final Morning & Departure
One Last Snorkel, Then Goodbye
Early morning house reef snorkel · Pack up · Transfer to Malé · Departure flight
💰 Transfer back: ₹2,000–₹5,000 · Allow 3hrs before international flight
6:30 AM
Dawn Snorkel — The Reef Waking Up
The first light of dawn over the Indian Ocean, alone in the water with fish. The Maldivian house reef at dawn is peaceful and less visited than midday. Parrotfish, clownfish in their anemones, sleeping reef sharks. Float and watch. This is your last morning in the Maldives — make it count. Then pack, check out, and take your transfer back to Malé.
Transfer
Return to Malé — Allow Plenty of Time
International flights from Malé typically depart in the late afternoon or evening. Allow at least 3 hours before your flight from when you arrive at Malé airport. If you have time in Malé city, cross to the island by local ferry (₹10) and explore the colourful painted streets, the Friday Mosque (old coral stone mosque, one of the finest in the Indian Ocean), and the fish market at the harbour — one of the world's busiest tuna trading spots.
Malé local ferry: ₹10 · Fish market: free · Friday Mosque: free · Respectful dress required

Maldives 5-Day Itinerary Insider Tips & Packing List
Insider Tips — Read Before You Go

Know Before You Go

💰
Budget Maldives IS Real — Maafushi Is Your Base
Maafushi island (45 minutes by speedboat from Malé) has over 50 guesthouses, licensed restaurants serving alcohol in designated areas, a beautiful beach, and every excursion a resort offers at 30–50% lower prices. You do not need to spend ₹2L+ per night to experience the Maldives. The marine life is identical regardless of your accommodation category.
🦈
Whale Shark Season — Plan Around It
Whale sharks are present year-round in South Ari Atoll but peak May–November. For guaranteed sightings, choose a guesthouse in South Ari Atoll (Dhigurah island is excellent). Manta rays peak November–April at North Malé Atoll and Baa Atoll (UNESCO Biosphere Reserve — magnificent manta feeding aggregations). Ask operators for current conditions.
🍺
Alcohol Rules on Local Islands
The Maldives is a Muslim country. Local inhabited islands are completely dry — no alcohol permitted anywhere except in the resort's designated "tourist beach" area. Resorts are private islands where alcohol flows freely. If alcohol is important to you, choose a resort over a guesthouse. Guesthouses designate a "bikini beach" area where swimwear is acceptable — follow local dress guidelines elsewhere on the island.
🌊
Reef-Safe Sunscreen Only
Chemical sunscreens (oxybenzone, octinoxate) are devastating to coral reefs. Use only mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide or titanium dioxide) in the water. Some resorts now prohibit chemical sunscreen entirely. Reef damage in the Maldives is a real and urgent environmental issue — your choices matter. Alternatively, wear a UV rashguard in the water instead of sunscreen.
🛥️
Book Transfers Before You Arrive
Speedboat transfers must be booked in advance — especially during peak season (Dec–Jan). Your guesthouse or hotel will arrange this, but confirm it. Seaplanes only fly in daylight hours (6am–4pm) — if you arrive after 4pm at Malé, you'll need to overnight at a Malé airport hotel before continuing. Factor this into your planning.
💳
Cash vs Card on Local Islands
Resorts accept cards for everything. Local island guesthouses increasingly accept cards but many excursion operators and restaurants are cash-only. Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR) is the local currency but USD is universally accepted. Bring USD cash. Carry ₹500 USD equivalent — more if diving or buying multiple excursions. ATMs exist in Malé and Maafushi but can be out of service.
⛵
Best Value: Combine Local Island + 2 Resort Nights
For the best of both worlds: spend 3 nights on a local island like Maafushi (snorkelling, excursions, local food) and 2 nights in an overwater bungalow resort. This way you get the iconic Instagram overwater bungalow experience without paying resort prices for all 5 nights. Many budget travellers do this split successfully.
🌡️
Seasons: When to Go and What to Expect
Dry season (Nov–Apr): calm seas, clear skies, best visibility, best snorkelling. Peak Dec–Jan — most expensive. Wet season (May–Oct): afternoon rain showers, rougher seas some days, cheaper rates, bioluminescence possible, whale shark season peaks. The "wet" season is not bad — most days are still sunny. Avoid April (transition season — hot and humid).
Packing for Maldives
🤿 Snorkel & mask — own mask fits better than rental. Essential if snorkelling daily.
🩱 Multiple swimsuits — you'll wear them constantly. 3+ recommended.
☀️ Mineral sunscreen only — reef-safe zinc oxide SPF 50. Protect coral.
👙 Rashguard / UV top — best sun protection while snorkelling. No sunscreen needed.
💊 Seasickness tablets — speedboat rides can be choppy in wet season.
🔋 Waterproof phone case — or underwater camera for reef photos.
👗 Modest cover-up — required on local islands away from the tourist beach.
💵 USD cash — universal. Carry $100–200 for excursions and tips.
💧 Reusable water bottle — single-use plastic is an island ecosystem problem.
Honest take: The Maldives is real at every price point. A guesthouse in Maafushi with a house reef snorkel, a whale shark excursion, and a sunset dhoni cruise is genuinely extraordinary — and costs less than one night at a luxury resort. The fish don't care what your room costs.
