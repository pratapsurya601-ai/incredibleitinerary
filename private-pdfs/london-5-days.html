Day 1
🏰 Tower of London & South Bank
The 1,000-year-old Tower of London holds the Crown Jewels — the most valuable jewellery collection in the world. Tower Bridge opens for tall ships. Borough Market is London's finest food market. The Tate Modern is one of the world's great contemporary art galleries — free entry.
Day 2
🏛️ British Museum & West End
Completely free, the British Museum holds 8 million objects — the Rosetta Stone, Elgin Marbles, Egyptian mummies, Lewis chessmen. Allow 4 hours. Then Covent Garden's street performers, the National Gallery, and Trafalgar Square. Dinner in Soho.
Day 3
👑 Westminster & Royal London
Big Ben, Parliament, Westminster Abbey, Buckingham Palace (Changing of Guard at 11am on alternating days). Hyde Park's Serpentine Gallery, the Diana Memorial. Evening in Notting Hill — pastel townhouses, Portobello Road market, excellent neighbourhood restaurants.
Day 4
🌍 Greenwich & East London
Sail from Westminster pier to Greenwich — Royal Observatory, the Prime Meridian line (stand in two hemispheres at once!), Cutty Sark clipper ship. Evening: Brick Lane for the best Indian food outside India, London's largest Bengali community and legendary curry houses.

"When a man is tired of London, he is tired of life; for there is in London all that life can afford." — Samuel Johnson, 1777. Still entirely true.

UK Visa for Indian Travellers: UK Standard Visitor Visa — apply online at gov.uk. Fee: approximately ₹11,000. Processing: 3 weeks standard (6-week service available). Required: 3-month bank statement, employment letter, hotel bookings, return flight tickets, ITR last 2 years. The UK visa is separate from Schengen — you need both if combining UK + Europe. Apply 6–8 weeks ahead in peak season.
Flights from India: Delhi/Mumbai to London Heathrow (LHR) — ₹50,000–₹90,000 return. Air India flies direct from Delhi (~9 hrs). British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and Vistara operate the route. Indirect flights via Middle East (Emirates/Etihad/Qatar) and Doha are often cheaper. Check Skyscanner 2–3 months ahead.

Day One · Historic East London
Tower of London, Tower Bridge & Tate Modern
Tower of London (Crown Jewels) · Borough Market lunch · Shakespeare's Globe · Tate Modern · South Bank evening walk
💰 Tower of London: ₹3,500 · Tate Modern: FREE · Borough Market: ₹600–₹1,200
Arrival
Heathrow to Central London — Tube is the Best Option
Take the Piccadilly Line from Heathrow to central London (50–60 minutes, ₹700). The Elizabeth Line (Crossrail) is faster and more comfortable — runs to Paddington and Bond Street (30 minutes, ₹800). The Heathrow Express train (15 minutes to Paddington) costs ₹2,000 — only worth it if you're in a rush. Buy an Oyster card or contactless pay with your debit/credit card directly on the Tube (better rates than cash). London hotels are expensive — Travelodge and Premier Inn offer budget options from ₹6,000/night.
Tube from Heathrow: ₹700 · Budget hotel: ₹6,000–₹10,000/night · Mid: ₹12,000–₹22,000
10:00 AM
Tower of London — Crown Jewels & 1,000 Years of History
The Tower of London has been a royal palace, prison, treasury and zoo over its 1,000-year history. Anne Boleyn was beheaded here. The Crown Jewels are kept in the Jewel House — the Imperial State Crown (2,868 diamonds), the Sceptre with the Cross, and the Sovereign's Orb. The Yeoman Warder (Beefeater) tours are hilarious and historically rich — join one at the gate. Book online to save ₹400 and time at the ticket desk. Allow 2.5–3 hours.
Entry: ₹3,500 adults (book online) · Beefeater tours: included · Open 9am–5:30pm (Sun/Mon from 10am)
1:00 PM
Borough Market — London's Greatest Food Market
Walk across Tower Bridge (free, extraordinary views) and south to Borough Market — London's oldest and most celebrated food market, operating since the 12th century. Open Monday–Saturday. Vendors include: Raclette and cheese stalls, Spanish jamón, Ethiopian injera, Indian street food, freshly shucked oysters, wild mushrooms. Perfect for lunch — budget ₹600–₹1,200 for a full lunch from multiple vendors. Never buy food directly across from any vendor without tasting first — samples are freely offered.
Borough Market entry: free · Lunch: ₹600–₹1,200 · Closed Sundays
3:00 PM
Shakespeare's Globe Theatre & Tate Modern
The reconstructed Globe Theatre stands 200 metres from the original site where Shakespeare's plays premiered in 1599. Guided tours run daily (₹1,800) or see an evening performance (₹1,200 standing yard tickets — authentic, atmospheric, brilliant). Adjacent is the Tate Modern — housed in a converted Bankside Power Station, it's one of the world's finest collections of modern and contemporary art, completely FREE. The Turbine Hall permanent installation is a spectacular space. The Switch House building has a free rooftop terrace with panoramic Thames views.
Globe tour: ₹1,800 · Tate Modern: FREE · Rooftop terrace: free
02
Day Two · Free Museums Day
British Museum, National Gallery & Covent Garden
British Museum (Rosetta Stone) · Covent Garden street performers · National Gallery (Van Eyck, Turner) · Trafalgar Square · Piccadilly Circus
💰 British Museum: FREE · National Gallery: FREE · Lunch: ₹800–₹1,500
9:00 AM
British Museum — 8 Million Objects, Zero Entry Fee
One of the greatest museums in the world and completely free. The Rosetta Stone (196 BC — the key to deciphering Egyptian hieroglyphics) is in Room 4. The Elgin Marbles (Greek Parthenon sculptures) in Room 18 are extraordinary. The Egyptian mummies in Rooms 62–63 include artefacts 3,000 years old. The Lewis Chessmen (Room 40) are the most famous medieval chess pieces in existence. Arrive at 10am when it opens — the Great Court fills with visitors by noon. Buy the museum map for £2. Allow 3–4 hours minimum.
Entry: FREE · Great Russell Street, Bloomsbury · Café inside · Map: ₹200
1:30 PM
Covent Garden — Street Performance Capital of London
The covered market piazza of Covent Garden is London's premier street performance venue — circus acts, opera singers, comedians and living statues perform in the central space. The Apple Market inside sells handmade jewellery, crafts and antiques. Lunch options: Flat Iron steak (₹2,000), Dishoom restaurant (excellent, queue outside — Bombay café food — expect 30 min wait), or grab a falafel wrap from one of the market vendors. The area connects to the Seven Dials neighbourhood — independent shops and excellent coffee.
Covent Garden: free to wander · Dishoom: ₹2,000/person · Seven Dials: great coffee spots
3:30 PM
National Gallery — Free European Masterpieces from 1200–1900
On the north side of Trafalgar Square, the National Gallery is completely free and holds 2,300 paintings. Highlights: Van Eyck's Arnolfini Portrait (Room 56), Vermeer's Young Woman Standing at a Virginal, Monet's Water Lilies at Twilight, Turner's The Fighting Temeraire (voted the greatest painting in Britain in 2005), Velázquez's The Rokeby Venus, da Vinci's The Virgin of the Rocks. The café on the lower level does excellent afternoon tea (₹1,800/person).
Entry: FREE · Trafalgar Square, WC2N · Audio guide: ₹400 · Café: ₹1,800 afternoon tea
Evening
Trafalgar Square, Piccadilly Circus & Soho Dinner
Trafalgar Square with Nelson's Column and the four lions is free and photogenic at any hour. Walk down to Piccadilly Circus — London's Times Square equivalent, neon signs and crowds. Continue into Soho for dinner — one of London's most diverse dining areas. Dishoom (Carnaby Street) has a 1940s Bombay café feel and is enormously popular with Londoners for its black dal, bacon naan roll and Irani chai. Alternatively, Chinatown Gerrard Street is 2 minutes away for excellent dim sum.
Piccadilly Circus: free · Soho dinner: ₹1,500–₹3,000/person · Dishoom: book ahead online

03
Day Three · Royal London
Westminster, Buckingham Palace & Notting Hill
Big Ben · Westminster Abbey · Buckingham Palace (Changing of Guard 11am) · Hyde Park · Notting Hill evening
💰 Westminster Abbey: ₹3,000 · Hyde Park: free · Notting Hill dinner: ₹2,500
9:00 AM
Big Ben & Houses of Parliament — Britain's Most Iconic Image
The Elizabeth Tower (universally called Big Ben, which is actually the bell inside) underwent a major restoration completed in 2022. The Gothic Revival Houses of Parliament are among the finest Victorian buildings in the world. Free external viewing from Westminster Bridge — best views are from the South Bank (Lambeth Bridge side). Interior tours of Parliament are available when Parliament is in recess (check parliament.uk — guided tours ₹2,500). The Victoria Tower Gardens has a replica of Rodin's Burghers of Calais statue.
Exterior: free · Parliament tours (recess only): ₹2,500 · Westminster Bridge: best photo spot
10:00 AM
Westminster Abbey — 1,000 Years of English History
The gothic abbey where every English and British monarch since 1066 has been crowned, and where 17 monarchs are buried. The Poets' Corner has memorials to Chaucer, Shakespeare, Dickens, Darwin. The recently added grave of the Unknown Warrior. The 13th century Cosmati pavement. The Henry VII Lady Chapel — one of the finest examples of English Gothic architecture. Book online (₹3,000, slightly cheaper than at door). The multimedia guide included in the ticket is excellent. Allow 2 hours.
Entry: ₹3,000 · Book at westminster-abbey.org · Audio guide included · Closed Sundays to tourists
11:00 AM
Buckingham Palace — Changing of the Guard
The Changing of the Guard ceremony (when the New Guard takes over from the Old Guard) happens at Buckingham Palace at 11am. It runs daily April–July and on alternate days August–March — check the Royal website before going. Arrive 30–45 minutes early for a good viewing spot by the palace gates. Free to watch. The palace interior opens for summer tours August–September (₹3,500) when the King is in Scotland. The Queen's Gallery, St James's Park, and the Royal Mews are all adjacent.
Changing of Guard: FREE · Palace summer tour: ₹3,500 · St James's Park: free (flamingos!)
04
Day Four · Maritime & East London
Greenwich, the Meridian & Brick Lane Curry
River ferry from Westminster Pier · Royal Observatory · Prime Meridian · Cutty Sark · Brick Lane evening for Indian food
💰 Ferry: ₹1,200 return · Observatory: ₹1,500 · Brick Lane curry: ₹1,500–₹2,500
10:00 AM
River Thames Ferry to Greenwich — Best Way to Arrive
Take the Thames Clipper river ferry from Westminster Pier (or Embankment) to Greenwich — 45 minutes on the Thames, passing the South Bank, Canary Wharf financial district, and the O2. Far more atmospheric than the Tube. Use your Oyster card for discounted fares. Greenwich itself is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — the Royal Naval College (Christopher Wren's masterpiece), the Painted Hall (Britain's Sistine Chapel), the National Maritime Museum (free), and the Royal Observatory on the hill above.
Thames Clipper: ₹600 single · Observatory: ₹1,500 · Painted Hall: ₹600 · Maritime Museum: FREE
Noon
Royal Observatory & Prime Meridian — Stand in Two Hemispheres
The Royal Observatory Greenwich is where Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) originates and where the Prime Meridian of the world — 0° longitude — is marked by a brass line in the courtyard. Stand with one foot in the Eastern Hemisphere and one in the Western. The Observatory houses John Harrison's revolutionary maritime chronometers (H1–H4) that solved the longitude problem and saved thousands of ships from wreck. The camera obscura provides a live view over the Thames. The view from the hill over Canary Wharf and the City of London is spectacular.
Observatory entry: ₹1,500 · Prime Meridian courtyard: included · Hill: free access
Evening
Brick Lane — Indian Food Capital of London
Brick Lane in East London is home to London's largest Bengali community and the highest concentration of Indian and Bangladeshi restaurants in Britain. Dozens of curry houses compete for business — restaurant hosts will tout from the doorstep. Ignore them and look for restaurants that are full of families and locals, not tourist groups. The best restaurants: Tayyabs (Punjabi — famous lamb chops, queue expected), Café Spice Namaste (Parsee food, more upscale), or simply choose a packed-looking curry house on Brick Lane itself. Budget: ₹1,500–₹2,500/person including rice, naan and drinks.
Brick Lane curry dinner: ₹1,500–₹2,500 · Bagel shops open 24hrs for late night snacks (₹150)

London tip: British Museum, National Gallery, Tate Modern, Natural History Museum, Science Museum, V&A Museum are ALL completely FREE — London's cultural offering is extraordinary and largely at no cost.

🃏
Oyster Card — Essential from Day One
Buy an Oyster card at Heathrow station (₹500 deposit, refundable). Load credit and tap in/out on Tube, bus and river ferry. Daily fare capping means you never overpay — after 3–4 journeys per day, you pay nothing more. Contactless bank cards work equally well. Never buy paper tickets — they cost 3x more.
🌧️
Always Carry an Umbrella
London weather is famously unpredictable — a sunny morning can turn to heavy rain within an hour. A compact fold-flat umbrella is essential. Don't rely on weather forecasts beyond 2 hours. The saying is true: there is no bad weather in London, only inappropriate clothing.
🏛️
London's Free Museums Are World Class
The British Museum, National Gallery, Natural History Museum, Science Museum, V&A, Tate Modern — all completely free. This is genuinely unusual globally. Plan your paid attractions (Tower of London, Westminster Abbey, Royal Observatory) and fill the rest with free museums — you could spend 10 days in London's free museums alone.
🎭
West End Theatre — London at Its Best
A West End show is one of London's great experiences. Check tkts.co.uk (official discounted tickets) for same-day half-price seats. The shows: Hamilton, Les Misérables, The Phantom of the Opera, Mamma Mia. Day seats for many shows are sold from 10am on the day at the box office — cheaper than online.
