Your Route — The Classic Portugal Circuit

Destination by Destination
Lisbon
Days 1–3
Europe's sunniest capital — yellow trams on steep cobblestone hills, pastéis de nata from 1837, Alfama neighbourhood's white-washed houses, Fado music in candlelit tasca restaurants, and the 25th of April Bridge glowing in the evening light.
Sintra
Day 2 (from Lisbon)
UNESCO World Heritage town 30km from Lisbon. Pena Palace — a fairy-tale yellow-and-red palace on a forested mountain, built 1854. Moorish Castle, Quinta da Regaleira's mysterious initiatic well. Europe's most dramatic royal landscape.
Porto
Days 4–5
UNESCO old town, Port wine caves in Vila Nova de Gaia, Ribeira waterfront, azulejo tile churches, Livraria Lello (the "Harry Potter bookshop"), Francesinha sandwiches, and a city that wears its soul on its sleeve.
Algarve
Days 6–7
Southern Portugal's golden coast. Ponta da Piedade's limestone sea stacks, Benagil Cave (kayak in), Lagos old town, and Sagres — Europe's southwestern tip where Henry the Navigator launched the Age of Discovery. Stunning Atlantic cliffs.

"Portugal is what happens when a country stops trying to impress the world and just becomes quietly, impossibly beautiful. The pastéis, the trams, the fado — you'll understand when you get there."

Schengen Visa (Indians): Apply at the Portuguese consulate or VFS Global 6–8 weeks before travel. Fee €80 (~₹7,200). Required: bank statements, confirmed hotel bookings, return flight, travel insurance. A valid Schengen visa also allows entry to 26 other European countries.

Pre-Trip Essentials
Visa · Flights · Money · Transport · SIM
Visa: Schengen visa — apply at Portuguese consulate/VFS 6–8 weeks ahead. Fee €80 (~₹7,200). Documents: bank statement, hotel booking, return flight, travel insurance.
Flights: Lisbon (LIS) from Mumbai/Delhi via Doha, Dubai, or Frankfurt. Total 14–18hrs. Return tickets: ₹55,000–90,000. Book 2–3 months ahead for best prices.
Currency: Euro (€). €1 = ~₹90. Cards accepted everywhere in Portugal. Use Multibanco ATMs for best exchange rates. Withdraw €200–300 on arrival.
Intercity trains: CP (comboios.pt) Alfa Pendular train Lisbon→Porto: 3hrs, €25 (book 2 weeks ahead online). Porto→Algarve: fly 1hr (~₹2,000) or rent a car (4hrs drive).
SIM card: NOS or MEO from Lisbon airport. 20GB data for €15 (~₹1,350). Excellent 4G coverage across Portugal including Algarve.
City transport: Lisbon metro €1.50/trip. Trams €3. Porto: metro €1.20. Algarve: rental car essential (€40–80/day from Faro airport).
01
Day One · Arrival
Lisbon — Belém, Tram 28 & Fado
Humberto Delgado Airport → Metro → Belém Tower → Jerónimos → Tram 28 → Alfama → Fado dinner
Full day approx. €30–50pp (₹2,700–4,500) excl. accommodation
Arrival
Lisbon Airport → City Centre by Metro
Humberto Delgado Airport (LIS) is 7km from Lisbon city centre. The Red Line metro runs directly from the airport to Alameda in 15 minutes (€1.50). Buy a reloadable Viva Viagem card (€0.50) at the metro machines for all Lisbon transport. Taxis to Baixa cost €12–15 (metered). Rideshares (Bolt, Uber) available for €8–12. Best area to stay: Baixa/Chiado for central location. Budget €50–80/night, mid-range €100–180/night.
Metro to city centre: €1.50 (₹135) · Hotel: €50–80 budget, €100–180 mid
Morning
Belém Tower + Jerónimos Monastery + Original Pastéis de Belém
Belém Tower (€6, ₹540) is a 16th-century fortress on the Tagus river — UNESCO, Manueline Gothic architecture, built to celebrate Vasco da Gama's discoveries. Next door: Jerónimos Monastery (€10, ₹900) — UNESCO, the most spectacular Manueline building in Portugal with its impossibly elaborate stone carvings. Vasco da Gama is buried here. Then: Pastéis de Belém bakery (since 1837, the original custard tart recipe kept secret for 189 years). Two pastéis de nata for €2.60 with cinnamon and powdered sugar — the best €2.60 you will spend in Europe.
Belém Tower: €6 (₹540) · Jerónimos: €10 (₹900) · Pastéis de Belém: €1.30/tart
Afternoon
Tram 28 — Lisbon's Most Iconic Experience
The yellow Tram 28 climbs through streets so narrow the tram's sides nearly brush the building walls — through Alfama, past the Sé Cathedral, up the steep Graça hill and down through Estrela. €3 (₹270) for a journey that no bus or metro can replicate. Beware pickpockets on board — keep bags in front. Ride it one way to Sé Cathedral and walk back through Alfama's medieval alleys: white-washed houses, laundry strung between windows, cats sleeping in doorways.
Tram 28: €3 (₹270) · Sé Cathedral: free exterior
Evening
Fado Dinner — Book Ahead
Fado is Portugal's soul — UNESCO-listed intangible heritage, a mournful, achingly beautiful music of longing (saudade). Authentic fado at Mesa de Frades (a tiny converted chapel) or Tasca do Chico (intimate, 20 seats) in Alfama. Both require advance booking — call 3–4 days ahead. Cover charge ~€25 including dinner or minimum spend. Performers enter unannounced mid-dinner. You will be the only table not silently crying by the end. One of the great cultural experiences in Europe.
Fado dinner: €25–40pp cover + food · Book 3–4 days ahead

Portugal 7-Day Itinerary Days 2–3 · Lisbon & Sintra
02
Day Two · Fairy-Tale Day Trip
Sintra — Palaces on a Mountain
Train from Rossio (1hr) · Pena Palace · Moorish Castle · Quinta da Regaleira · Lisbon sunset
Full day approx. €35–45pp (₹3,150–4,050) all entries
9:00 AM
Train: Lisbon Rossio → Sintra (40 Minutes)
Trains run every 15–20 minutes from Lisbon Rossio station. €5 return (₹450) — one of Europe's great bargain train rides through wooded suburbs. Sintra station is walkable to the centre. Arrive by 9:30am to beat the queues at Pena Palace. This is genuinely important — after 10:30am the ticket queue can be 45–60 minutes. Buy your Pena Palace ticket online at parquesdesintra.pt in advance to skip entirely.
Train return: €5 (₹450) · Buy Pena Palace tickets online to skip queues
Morning
Pena Palace — Portugal's Most Visited Sight
Built in 1854 for Queen Maria II, Pena Palace sits at 529m on a forested mountain above Sintra. Half yellow, half blood-red, with turrets, terraces, Moorish arches, and Gothic spires — a Romantic fantasy made stone. Inside: original 19th-century royal apartments intact, staterooms with period furniture, the king's study, the queen's quarters. The palace terrace overlooks the Atlantic, Lisbon, and on clear days — the Algarve. One of the most photogenic buildings in all of Europe. €14 (₹1,260) — worth every cent.
Pena Palace: €14 (₹1,260) · Buy online to skip queues
Midday
Moorish Castle + Quinta da Regaleira
Moorish Castle (€8, ₹720): 10th-century Moorish battlements walkable from Pena Palace through the forest — panoramic views over Sintra town and the Atlantic coast. Then descend to town for lunch (local pastel de Sintra pastry, €1.50) before Quinta da Regaleira (€10, ₹900): a neo-Manueline palace with initiatic wells — spiral stone staircases descending into the earth, connected by underground tunnels used for Masonic initiation rituals. Simultaneously beautiful and genuinely strange. Allow 90 minutes.
Moorish Castle: €8 (₹720) · Quinta da Regaleira: €10 (₹900)
Evening
Return Lisbon — Sunset Viewpoint + Ginjinha
Return train to Lisbon by 5pm. Head directly to Miradouro da Graça — Lisbon's best viewpoint, free, with the castle and Alfama below and the Tagus river and 25th April Bridge ahead in the golden evening light. Then: A Ginjinha bar on Largo de São Domingos (since 1840) — Ginjinha is Lisbon's sour cherry liqueur, served in a tiny shot glass for €1.50. It has been made by the same family for 185 years. Possibly the best €1.50 in Europe.
Miradouro da Graça: Free · Ginjinha shot: €1.50 (₹135)
03
Day Three · The Real Lisbon
LX Factory, Time Out Market & Azulejo Museum
LX Factory · Time Out Market · Museu do Azulejo · Bairro Alto bar-hopping
Full day approx. €25–40pp (₹2,250–3,600) food + entries
Morning
LX Factory — Creative Hub in a Former Textile Factory
LX Factory is a large 19th-century textile factory complex transformed into a creative village of independent boutiques, design studios, bookshops, restaurants, and art galleries. Free to enter and wander. On Sundays, the Sunday Market (10am–8pm) fills the courtyard with vintage clothing, artisan food, and handmade goods. Livraria Ler Devagar inside is one of Lisbon's most beautiful bookshops (a bicycle dangling from the industrial ceiling, floor-to-ceiling books). Always buzzing, always interesting.
LX Factory: Free · Sunday Market: Free entry · Shopping optional
Midday
Time Out Market — Best Food Market in Europe
Time Out Market Lisbon is a converted 1882 market hall housing 40 of Lisbon's best restaurant stalls under one roof — all curated, all quality. €6–15 per dish. Recommended: bifanas (pork sandwiches, the Portuguese street food), arroz de marisco (seafood rice), grilled octopus, pastel de bacalhau (codfish cakes), and pastéis de nata from the Manteigaria counter. Sit at the long communal tables. It is packed by 1pm — arrive by 12:15.
Lunch at Time Out Market: €12–20pp (₹1,080–1,800)
Afternoon
Museu Nacional do Azulejo — Unmissable
The National Tile Museum tells the 500-year story of azulejo — the blue-and-white painted tin-glazed tiles that cover every church, palace, and train station in Portugal. Housed in a beautiful 16th-century convent with its own tiled cloisters. The highlights: a 23-metre panoramic tile panel of Lisbon before the 1755 earthquake, Moorish geometric tiles, 18th-century narrative hunting scenes, and contemporary azulejo art. €5 (₹450). Genuinely one of the best museums in Portugal. Allow 1.5 hours.
Entry: €5 (₹450) · Duration: 1.5 hours
Evening
Bairro Alto Bar-Hopping or Cais do Sodré Pink Street
Bairro Alto (Upper District) is Lisbon's bohemian bar neighbourhood — dozens of tiny bars lining steep narrow streets, drinks from €4–8, a different crowd at every door, conversation spilling onto the cobblestones. Or head to Rua Nova do Carvalho — the "Pink Street" in Cais do Sodré, Lisbon's old sailors' district now painted neon pink, with bars, live music, and a younger international crowd. Both are safe, both are fun, both are quintessentially Lisbon.
Drinks: €4–8 each · Dinner in Bairro Alto: €15–25pp

Portugal 7-Day Itinerary Days 4–5 · Porto
04
Day Four · Portugal's Soul City
Porto — Ribeira, Lello & Sunset
Alfa Pendular train (3hrs) · Ribeira waterfront · Livraria Lello · Porto Cathedral · Serra do Pilar sunset
Train: €25 (₹2,250) booked ahead · Day approx. €20–35pp
7:00 AM
Alfa Pendular: Lisbon → Porto (3 Hours)
The Alfa Pendular is Portugal's premium intercity train — comfortable, punctual, and scenic. Book online at cp.pt at least 2 weeks ahead for €25 (₹2,250). Walk-in price can be €45–55. The train follows the Atlantic coast through vineyards, pine forests, and fishing villages. Arrive Porto Campanhã or São Bento station. Note: São Bento station itself is a destination — its entrance hall is lined with 20,000 azulejo tiles depicting Portuguese history. Arrive and just stand there for five minutes.
Alfa Pendular: €25 (₹2,250) booked 2 weeks ahead · Book at cp.pt
Morning
Ribeira Waterfront — UNESCO World Heritage
The Ribeira (riverside district) is Porto at its most photogenic — colourful crumbling medieval houses stacked above the Douro river, old fishing boats (barcos rabelos) moored below, the Dom Luís I double-decker iron bridge (1886) arching across to Vila Nova de Gaia. Walk the riverside promenade from Praça da Ribeira west and east. Free. Every street climbing up from the water contains a café, a tiled church front, or a viewpoint that makes you stop and stare. Porto's old town is UNESCO-listed for good reason.
Ribeira waterfront: Free · Riverside café coffee: €1.20
Afternoon
Livraria Lello — The Most Beautiful Bookshop in the World
Livraria Lello (1906) is a neo-Gothic bookshop with a sinuous red Art Nouveau wooden staircase that spirals to the upper floor, stained glass ceiling, and carved wooden balconies. J.K. Rowling taught English in Porto and visited regularly — it partially inspired Hogwarts. €5 voucher required (₹450, redeemable on any book purchase). Queue forms from 11am — go at opening (9am) or late afternoon. Genuinely one of the most beautiful interiors in Portugal. Even if you are not a book lover, go.
Entry voucher: €5 (₹450, redeemable on books) · Go before 10am or after 5pm
Sunset
Porto Cathedral + Miradouro da Serra do Pilar
Porto Cathedral (Sé do Porto) is a 12th-century Romanesque fortress-church on a hill above the Ribeira — free exterior. Then cross the Dom Luís I bridge (upper level) on foot to Vila Nova de Gaia and climb to Miradouro da Serra do Pilar — a terrace before the 17th-century rotunda with the most panoramic view in Porto: the entire Ribeira, the six bridges, the Douro glittering in evening light. The best sunset spot in the city. Free. Get there 30 minutes before sunset.
Porto Cathedral: Free exterior · Miradouro: Free · Sunset: worth every minute
05
Day Five · Wine & Tiles
Porto — Port Wine Caves + Azulejo Stations
Vila Nova de Gaia wine caves · São Bento azulejo tiles · Clérigos Tower · Francesinha dinner
Wine cave tour: €15–20 (₹1,350–1,800) · Full day approx. €30–50pp
Morning
Port Wine Caves — Vila Nova de Gaia
Vila Nova de Gaia (across the Dom Luís bridge) is lined with the lodges (armazéns) of Portugal's great Port wine houses: Taylor's, Graham's, Sandeman, Calem, Ramos Pinto. Each offers 45–60 minute cellar tours (dark stone caves lined with massive wooden barrels) followed by a tasting of 2–3 Ports: Tawny, Ruby, Vintage. Book morning slots at Taylor's or Graham's (views over the Douro from the terrace). €15–20 (₹1,350–1,800) including tasting. Essential Porto experience — you will leave knowing exactly what good Port tastes like.
Cave tour + tasting: €15–20 (₹1,350–1,800) · Book morning slot online
Midday
São Bento Station — 20,000 Azulejo Tiles
São Bento railway station's entrance hall is not a tourist attraction — it is an active train station that happens to contain one of the great decorative art installations in Europe. 20,000 blue-and-white azulejo tiles completed in 1930 by artist Jorge Colaço depict scenes from Portuguese history: the conquest of Ceuta, the Battle of Valdevez, the arrival of the Court in Porto, rural scenes of the Minho region. It is free. You do not need a train ticket to enter. Stand in the centre of the hall and look up.
São Bento Station: Free to enter · No ticket needed
Afternoon
Clérigos Tower + Majestic Café
Clérigos Tower (€5, ₹450) is Porto's most recognisable landmark — a 76m Baroque granite tower with 225 steps to a narrow platform with 360-degree views of Porto's roofscape to the Atlantic. Then: Café Majestic (Rua de Santa Catarina) — a 1921 Belle Époque café listed among the most beautiful in the world. Coffee and pastry €4–8. J.K. Rowling wrote parts of Harry Potter here (as a regular in the 1990s). Slightly touristy now, but genuine and beautiful.
Clérigos Tower: €5 (₹450) · Café Majestic coffee + pastry: €4–8
Dinner
Francesinha — Porto's Signature Dish
The Francesinha is Porto's gift to world cuisine — a layered sandwich of bread, ham, linguiça sausage, fresh sausage, and steak, encased in melted cheese, then drowned in a fiery, slightly sweet tomato-beer sauce with a fried egg on top. Some people's idea of heaven; others are confused. €10–14 at Café Santiago (Rua de Passos Manuel) — the most famous Francesinha address in Porto, queues from 7pm. Budget option: any old tasca in the Bonfim neighbourhood. Eat it. You will not regret it.
Francesinha at Café Santiago: €10–14 (₹900–1,260) · Queue from 7pm

06
Day Six · Golden Cliffs
Algarve — Lagos, Ponta da Piedade & Sea Caves
Fly Porto → Faro (1hr) · Rent car · Ponta da Piedade · Lagos old town · Benagil Cave boat tour
Flight: €30–60 (₹2,700–5,400) · Rental car: €40–80/day · Boat tour: €20–25pp
Morning
Porto → Faro Flight + Rental Car
Fly Porto (OPO) to Faro (FAO): 1 hour, Ryanair/TAP, €30–60 depending on timing. Book 2–4 weeks ahead for best prices. At Faro Airport: collect your rental car immediately — Algarve's best sights are spread along 150km of coastline and public transport is poor. Most international car hire brands (Hertz, Europcar, local agents) at Faro airport. Full-sized car €40–80/day. Roads are excellent, driving is straightforward on the N125 coastal road. Book rental before you fly — airport walk-ins are significantly more expensive.
Flight: €30–60 (₹2,700–5,400) · Rental car: €40–80/day · Book ahead
Morning
Ponta da Piedade — Lagos's Golden Sea Stacks
Ponta da Piedade is a headland of golden-orange limestone sea stacks, grottos, and arches 2km south of Lagos — the most dramatic coastal scenery in mainland Portugal. Park at the top and walk down the 149 steps to the water level where the turquoise Atlantic pushes between the rocks. Free entry. Bring water (no facilities, full sun). Late morning light turns the cliffs a deep amber. The stairs are manageable but steep. Allow 45 minutes down and back.
Ponta da Piedade: Free · Parking: Free · Bring water and sunscreen
Midday
Lagos Old Town — Whitewashed & Wonderful
Lagos is the Algarve's most charming town — Roman walls enclosing whitewashed streets, azulejo-fronted churches (Igreja de Santo António, free), and the best fresh grilled fish lunch you will eat. Cervejaria Os Arcos on the riverfront does whole grilled sea bream and espada (swordfish) for €12–16. Lagos was Henry the Navigator's launch point for Portuguese exploration in the 15th century. The slave market building (now a museum, €4) is a sobering and important visit. Walk the town walls: free, views to the sea.
Lagos grilled fish lunch: €12–16pp · Slave market museum: €4 (₹360)
Afternoon
Benagil Cave — Algarve's Most Famous Sight
Benagil Cave is a sea cave accessible only by water — a cathedral-like arch of golden limestone with a hole in the dome ceiling and a sandy beach inside, the sea glittering through the entrance. You cannot swim there safely (the current is strong). Options: guided kayak tour from Benagil beach (€25–35, 1.5hrs), motorboat tour from Lagos marina (€20, includes multiple caves), or SUP tour. Book tours from Lagos marina in the morning for afternoon slots. The cave is best lit 10am–2pm when sunlight streams through the dome hole. Do not miss this.
Benagil Cave boat tour: €20–25 (₹1,800–2,250) · Book from Lagos marina
07
Day Seven · Edge of the World
Sagres — The End of Europe
Fortaleza de Sagres · Cape St. Vincent · Atlantic cliffs · Fly home from Faro
Sagres fortress: €3 (₹270) · Cape St. Vincent: Free
Morning
Fortaleza de Sagres — Henry the Navigator's Base
Sagres sits at Europe's southwestern extremity — the windswept promontory from which Prince Henry the Navigator organised the 15th-century Portuguese maritime expeditions that changed the world. The Fortaleza de Sagres (€3, ₹270) is a stark 16th-century fortress on a wind-battered clifftop. Inside: the mysterious Rosa dos Ventos (compass rose in stone, 43m diameter, visible from above), Henry's original chapel, and a small museum. The winds here are Atlantic raw even in summer. Dress in layers.
Fortaleza de Sagres: €3 (₹270) · Allow 45 minutes
Morning
Cape St. Vincent — Europe's Most Southwestern Point
Three kilometres west of Sagres, Cape St. Vincent is the extreme southwestern tip of Continental Europe — 75m sheer basalt cliffs plunging straight into the Atlantic. A working lighthouse (not always open), a small café, and absolute nothing to the west until Brazil. The feeling here is genuinely elemental: standing at the edge of the known world, the wind trying to push you off, the ocean stretching without interruption to the Americas. Free. Go at golden hour if your flight allows. Unforgettable.
Cape St. Vincent: Free · Small café for last coffee · Drive 3km from Sagres
Afternoon
Drive to Faro + Departure (FAO)
Faro Airport is 110km from Sagres — approximately 1.5hrs on the A22 toll motorway (€4–6 in tolls). Return rental car at Faro Airport 2 hours before departure — allow extra time if returning with a full tank (petrol stations near the airport). Faro Airport (FAO) is small and manageable. Emirates/Iberia/Ryanair fly back to Lisbon for connecting flights, or direct TAP Air Portugal flights to Lisbon, Heathrow, Dubai (connecting to India). Get one last pastel de nata at the airport café.
Drive Sagres → Faro: 1.5hrs · Toll: €4–6 · Return car 2hrs before flight
Algarve Essential: Rental car is non-negotiable for Days 6–7. The best beaches, cliffs, and caves are 5–30km apart with no useful public transport connecting them. Sagres, Cape St. Vincent, Ponta da Piedade, and Benagil are each separate stops requiring a car.

Insider Tips
📋
Schengen Visa — Apply Early
Apply 6–8 weeks before travel. Never leave it to the last 3 weeks. Peak summer slots at VFS Global fill up weeks in advance. Documents required: confirmed hotel, return flights, €50/day bank balance, travel insurance with €30,000 coverage.
🏰
Sintra: Arrive by 9:30am
After 10:30am, the Pena Palace ticket queue is 45–60 minutes. Buy tickets online at parquesdesintra.pt before you go. Visit mid-week if possible — weekends are significantly more crowded from Lisbon day-trippers.
🥐
Pastéis de Nata: Two Addresses
Pastéis de Belém (Lisbon, since 1837) uses the original secret recipe. Manteigaria (Chiado) is the modern rival. Both are exceptional. Eat them warm, dusted with cinnamon and powdered sugar. They are best straight from the oven — never take away.
🚂
Trains: Book 2 Weeks Ahead
Alfa Pendular (Lisbon → Porto, 3hrs) costs €25 booked 2 weeks ahead at cp.pt but €45–55 at the station. The train fills quickly in summer. Book the moment your dates are confirmed. Window seats on the right side going north for Atlantic views.
🎭
Fado Dinner: Book 3–4 Days Ahead
Authentic fado venues in Alfama (Mesa de Frades, Tasca do Chico, Clube de Fado) have small capacity and fill up. Call or book online 3–4 days ahead. Avoid tourist fado shows advertised on the street — book where Portuguese locals eat.
🌞
Best Months: Apr–June & Sept–Oct
July–August: very crowded everywhere, Sintra queues double, Algarve beaches packed, prices +30%. April, May, September, October: pleasant 18–24°C, manageable crowds, best hotel prices, wildflowers on the Algarve cliffs.
🚗
Algarve: Rental Car Essential
The best cliff walks, secret beaches, Benagil Cave, Sagres, and Cape St. Vincent are only reachable by car. Book your Faro rental car online before flying. Local companies (Auto Jardim, Goldcar) are cheaper than international brands.
💳
Tip Culture & Cash
Tipping not expected but 5–10% appreciated at sit-down restaurants. Cards accepted almost everywhere. Carry €50–100 cash for markets, small cafés, trams, and tips. Avoid airport currency exchange — Multibanco ATMs give the best rates.
Packing List
Comfortable walking shoes — Lisbon's cobblestones are brutal on flat-soled feet
Light jacket — Lisbon and Porto evenings cool even in summer; Sagres always windy
Swimwear — Algarve beaches and sea cave kayaking (Days 6–7)
All Schengen visa documents (printed) + passport with 6 months validity
Cards (Visa/Mastercard) + €200 cash for markets and small venues
Compact travel umbrella — Atlantic rain arrives suddenly in spring and autumn
Daypack for Sintra mountain hike and Ponta da Piedade cliff walk
Sunscreen SPF 50 — UV near the Atlantic is intense even in April
Camera — every street in Lisbon, Porto, and the Algarve is worth photographing
Travel insurance documents (mandatory for Schengen visa, minimum €30,000)
European power adapter (Type F plug, 230V)
CP train bookings printed / downloaded (Lisbon → Porto Alfa Pendular)
