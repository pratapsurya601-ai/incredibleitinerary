Total driving distance: ~750 km · Best done by private car or train

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

October – March is ideal. Temperatures 10–28°C. Avoid April–June (45°C+). Monsoon (July–Sept) brings greenery but limited desert access.

✈️ How to Reach

Fly into Delhi (DEL), then take the train or fly to Jaipur (JAI). Return from Jaisalmer via Jodhpur (JDH) to Delhi.

🚆 Getting Around

Private cab (₹6,000–12,000 total for the route) is most flexible. Trains connect all cities but book 30+ days ahead on IRCTC.

💳 Money Matters

Carry cash — smaller towns and markets are cash-only. ATMs available in all cities. Budget ₹3,000–5,000 cash per day.

Day 1 · Jaipur

The Pink City Awaits

Arrival · City Orientation · First Sunset at Nahargarh

🌅 Morning

Arrive & Check In

  • Arrive at Jaipur Airport or Railway Station. Pre-book an Ola/Uber cab — auto drivers quote 3–4× the meter price to new arrivals.
  • Check into your hotel and freshen up. Most hotels allow early check-in if you call ahead.
  • Breakfast at Rawat Misthan Bhandar — legendary pyaaz kachori (₹25/piece) that locals queue for every morning on Station Road.
☀️ Afternoon

Old City Walk & Hawa Mahal

  • Walk through Johari Bazaar — Jaipur's famous jewellery market. Don't buy on Day 1; prices drop significantly if you return on Day 2 morning.
  • Photograph Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds) from the street — the iconic 953-windowed pink facade. Entry ₹200 for the interior museum.
  • Walk to City Palace complex (₹700 entry). The Mubarak Mahal textile museum alone is worth the entry fee.
  • Lunch at Peacock Rooftop Restaurant inside Hotel Pearl Palace — ₹400–600 per person, views of the old city, excellent Dal Baati Churma.
🌙 Evening

Nahargarh Fort Sunset

  • Hire an auto or cab to Nahargarh Fort (~15 min from City Palace). Arrive 45 minutes before sunset for the best light on the city below.
  • Sundowner at Padao Restaurant inside Nahargarh — cold Kingfisher with the entire pink city spread before you. ₹500–700 per person.
  • Return to hotel, rest. Tomorrow is a packed day — Amber Fort opens at 8 AM.
💡 Local Tip

Book Amber Fort tickets online at rajasthantickets.rajasthan.gov.in the night before. Walk-up queues on weekends stretch to 90 minutes. Online entry is ₹550 for foreigners, ₹100 for Indians.

Day 2 · Jaipur

Forts, Elephants & Bazaars

Amber Fort · Jal Mahal · Shopping · Chokhi Dhani

🌅Morning (7:30 AM)

Amber Fort — Start Early

  • Reach Amber Fort by 7:30 AM to beat the crowds and the heat. The morning light on the golden-amber battlements is extraordinary.
  • Book a jeep ride up (₹800 round-trip per jeep, share with 3–4 others) rather than the elephant ride — the elephant experience raises ethical concerns.
  • Inside: Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors), Ganesh Pol gate, and the rooftop views of Maota Lake. Allow 2–2.5 hours minimum.
  • En route back, stop at Jal Mahal (Water Palace) — photograph from the road, no interior access but strikingly beautiful at 9 AM light.
☀️Afternoon

Jantar Mantar & Shopping

  • Jantar Mantar (UNESCO World Heritage) — the world's largest stone sundial still accurate to 2 seconds. ₹200 entry. Allow 45 minutes with the audio guide.
  • Spend 2 hours in Bapu Bazaar & Johari Bazaar — buy block-print textiles (₹200–800/metre), blue pottery (₹150–600/piece), and lac bangles.
  • Tip: Bargain to 40–60% of the first quoted price. Walk away — they'll call you back.
  • Lunch at Laxmi Misthan Bhandar (LMB), Johari Bazaar — a Jaipur institution since 1954. Their dal baati churma thali is ₹450.
🌙Evening

Chokhi Dhani — Rajasthani Cultural Village

  • Chokhi Dhani (20 km from city, ₹1,200–1,800/person all-inclusive) — Rajasthani folk performances, camel rides, puppet shows, and a traditional thali dinner. Kitsch but genuinely fun and the food is outstanding.
  • Alternatively, book a table at 1135 AD at Amber Fort — fine dining in a 17th-century setting (₹3,500–5,000/person).
⚠️ Watch Out For

Gem touts near Amber Fort. They'll befriend you, take you to a "government emporium" (it's not), and sell overpriced stones. If anyone mentions exporting gems or asks where you're from before you're inside the fort — walk away.

Day 3 · Jaipur → Jodhpur

Road to the Blue City

Scenic Drive · Stepwells · Mehrangarh Fort First Look

🌅Morning (Early Start)

Depart Jaipur → Jodhpur (5.5 hrs)

  • Leave Jaipur by 7 AM. The drive via NH48 is mostly highway — flat, dotted with tiny dhabas and occasional peacocks on the road.
  • Stop at Abhaneri Stepwell (Chand Baori), 90 km from Jaipur — one of the world's largest stepwells, 13 stories deep, 3,500 steps. Featured in The Dark Knight Rises. Allow 45 minutes.
  • Driver dhaba lunch at Hotel Ratan Vilas, Nagaur or any clean-looking roadside stop. Dal-roti-sabzi, ₹120–180 per person.
☀️Afternoon (Arrive ~2 PM)

Check In & First Blue City Walk

  • Check into hotel. Drop bags and head immediately to Clock Tower (Ghanta Ghar) and Sardar Market — the chaotic, beautiful heart of old Jodhpur.
  • Walk the lanes of the old city — the indigo-blue houses are not painted blue for Instagram. It's a centuries-old Brahmin tradition using copper sulphate to keep insects away.
  • Find a rooftop cafe in the old city — Indique or On the Rocks — and get your first view of Mehrangarh Fort rising above the blue city. A glass of lassi: ₹80.
🌙Evening

Sunset at Mehrangarh (Outside)

  • Walk up to Mehrangarh Fort outer walls for sunset — the fort glows gold as the city turns blue below. Save the interior for tomorrow morning when it's cooler.
  • Dinner at Jhankar Choti Haveli — rooftop with fort views, live folk music Thu–Sat, excellent Rajasthani thali (₹600–900/person).

Day 4 · Jodhpur

Inside Mehrangarh

The Greatest Fort in India · Jaswant Thada · Bishnoi Village

🌅Morning (8 AM — Beat the Heat)

Mehrangarh Fort — Full Exploration

  • Mehrangarh Fort (₹600 Indians / ₹1,000 foreigners — includes audio guide) is widely considered the finest fort in Rajasthan. Allow 3 hours minimum.
  • Must-see inside: Phool Mahal (Flower Palace) with its 24-carat gold ceiling; Moti Mahal (Pearl Palace); the armoury with Mughal cannonballs still lodged in the walls.
  • The audio guide (free with entry) narrated by William Dalrymple is exceptional — genuinely one of the best museum audio guides in India.
  • From the fort ramparts, spot the Chamunda Devi Temple and the sweeping blue city below — the best photograph opportunity of the entire trip.
☀️Afternoon

Jaswant Thada & Bishnoi Village

  • Jaswant Thada (₹30 entry) — a stunning white marble cenotaph, called the "Taj Mahal of Marwar." Quiet, beautiful, 15-minute walk from Mehrangarh.
  • Afternoon: Bishnoi Village Safari (₹1,500–2,500/person) — a half-day excursion to traditional Bishnoi villages where blackbuck, chinkaras, and peacocks roam freely. The Bishnoi community has protected wildlife for 500+ years before it was fashionable.
  • Lunch back in the city: Gypsy Restaurant near Circuit House, famous for its laal maas (fiery Rajasthani mutton, ₹400). Order well done unless you want to cry.
🌙Evening & Departure Prep

Old City Shopping & Farewell Dinner

  • Jodhpur is famous for antique furniture and iron craft (Umaid Bhawan Road). Jodhpuri jutis (leather shoes, ₹400–1,200) and mirrorwork fabrics are excellent buys.
  • Farewell dinner at Stepwell Cafe near Toorji Ka Jhalra — ₹300–500 per person, rooftop seating overlooking the ancient stepwell lit at night.
  • Pack and prepare for tomorrow's early departure to Jaisalmer.

Days 5–6 · Jodhpur → Jaisalmer

The Golden City & Sam Dunes

Desert Fort · Patwon Ki Haveli · Camel Safari · Overnight Desert Camp

🚗Day 5 Morning — Drive (4.5 hrs)

Jodhpur → Jaisalmer Road Trip

  • Leave Jodhpur by 7 AM. The NH125 drive through the Thar Desert is strikingly beautiful — flat, endless, dotted with tiny villages, wind turbines, and migrating birds in winter.
  • Stop at Pokhran (midway) — where India conducted its nuclear tests in 1998. The old fort here is worth a 30-minute visit.
  • Arrive Jaisalmer by noon. Check in, freshen up, and head straight to the fort.
☀️Day 5 Afternoon

Jaisalmer Fort & Patwon Ki Haveli

  • Jaisalmer Fort is one of the world's few living forts — 5,000 people still live inside. No entry fee to walk in; museum is ₹150. Explore the Jain Temples inside (stunning sandstone carvings) and the many rooftop cafes with desert views.
  • Patwon Ki Haveli (₹150 entry) — five interconnected mansions built by a wealthy merchant, each with more elaborate jharokha (latticed window) carvings than the last.
  • Nathmal Ki Haveli — built by two brothers who competed to outdo each other; the left and right sides are slightly different. Free to view from outside.
🌙Day 5 Evening → Day 6

Sam Sand Dunes — Desert Camp Overnight

  • Head to Sam Sand Dunes (42 km from Jaisalmer, 1 hour). Arrive at 4 PM for the golden hour — the Thar Desert dunes turn deep amber as the sun sets. Truly one of India's most magical landscapes.
  • Camel ride (₹300–500/30 min) to your desert camp. Book a mid-range desert camp (₹3,500–6,000/person all-inclusive) with attached bathroom, cultural shows, dinner, and bonfire.
  • Night in the desert under one of India's clearest skies. Zero light pollution — the Milky Way is visible with the naked eye Oct–Feb.
  • Day 6 morning: sunrise over the dunes (set alarm for 5:30 AM). Breakfast at camp, then return to Jaisalmer by 10 AM.
💡 Desert Camp Booking Tips

Avoid the cheapest camps (₹800–1,200) — shared bathrooms, thin mattresses, and poor food. The ₹3,500–5,000 range gets you a comfortable experience. Recommended: Desert Haveli Camp, Mirage Desert Camp, or Royal Camp Jaisalmer. Book directly by phone to avoid OTA markups.

Day 7 · Jaisalmer → Delhi

Gadisar Lake & Goodbye Rajasthan

Last Morning · Gadisar Lake · Departure

🌅Morning

Gadisar Lake — The Perfect Last Morning

  • Return from Sam Dunes camp to Jaisalmer by 9:30 AM. Don't check out yet — leave bags at hotel.
  • Gadisar Lake (10-minute auto from city) is a 14th-century reservoir surrounded by temples and shrines. Rent a wooden boat (₹200/30 min) and row among migratory birds. In winter, thousands of ducks, pelicans, and painted storks gather here.
  • The ornate Tilon Ki Pol archway leading to the lake was built by a famous courtesan — the king tried to demolish it, she added a Vishnu temple on top so it couldn't be removed. True story.
  • Final shopping: Khadi fabric scarves (₹150–300), camel-leather bags (₹500–1,500), desert spices.
☀️Midday Onwards

Departure — Jaisalmer to Delhi

  • By Train: Jaisalmer has one daily direct train to Delhi (Jaisalmer–Delhi SF Express), departing ~5 PM, arriving next morning. Book 30 days ahead; 3AC is ₹1,100–1,400. Book at irctc.co.in or via Cleartrip/MakeMyTrip.
  • By Flight: Fly Jaisalmer (JSA) to Delhi (DEL) via IndiGo or Air India — 1.5 hrs, ₹2,500–6,000. One morning flight at ~10 AM. Taxi to airport: ₹400 (15 min).
  • By Road + Flight: Drive Jaisalmer → Jodhpur (4.5 hrs), fly Jodhpur → Delhi. Gives 4 extra hours in Jaisalmer AND cheaper Delhi flight options.

Avoid These Mistakes

❌ Don't Underestimate Distances

Jaipur → Jodhpur is 340 km, Jodhpur → Jaisalmer is 290 km. Each drive is 4–5 hours. Plan transit days accordingly — don't cram full sightseeing on drive days.

❌ Don't Visit in May–June

Temperatures regularly hit 47–48°C in Jaisalmer in summer. Fort stone surfaces become too hot to touch. Even locals migrate. Oct–March only.

✅ Do Book Trains Early

Rajasthan trains fill up 30–60 days ahead, especially in peak season (Dec–Jan). If you can't get a confirmed berth, use Tatkal quota (opens 1 day before at premium).

✅ Do Carry Small Notes

Temple offerings, auto fares, street food — everything under ₹200. Vendors rarely have change for ₹500 notes. Break big notes at restaurants or petrol stations.
