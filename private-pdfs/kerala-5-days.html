Kerala 5-Day Itinerary Day 1 — Kochi
01
Day One · Fort Cochin
Kochi — Heritage, Spice & the Chinese Fishing Nets
Arrive · Fort Cochin walk · Kathakali optional
💰 Budget: ₹800–₹1,500 · Couple: ₹3,000–₹6,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Arrive & Check In to Fort Cochin
Always stay in Fort Cochin — not the main city (Ernakulam). Fort Cochin is a 20-minute ferry ride away (₹5) and where all the heritage, cafes and atmosphere are. Ernakulam is just a transit hub.
Afternoon
Jew Town & Mattancherry Walk
Walk from your hotel to Mattancherry (20 min). Mattancherry Palace / Dutch Palace (₹10 entry) — ceiling murals depicting the Ramayana, 400 years old. Jew Town: spice warehouses, antique dealers, the Paradesi Synagogue (₹5). The whole area smells of cardamom, pepper and nutmeg.
Entry: ₹10–₹20 · Free to walk
5:30 PM
Chinese Fishing Nets at Sunset
The most photographed image in Kerala. Cantilever fishing nets operated by teams of fishermen — the same technique used for 500 years. Come at 5:30pm when the light turns golden. Walk the full seafront stretch (1km).
Free · Best: 5:00–6:30pm
Evening
Kathakali Performance (Optional but Recommended)
Kerala Kathakali Centre — arrive 1 hour before the show to watch the 2-hour makeup transformation. Classical dance drama telling stories from the Ramayana. The makeup process is as fascinating as the performance. Shows at 6pm and 8pm daily.
₹350–₹500 · Book at hotel or walk in
Dinner
Dal Roti (Budget) · Fort House Restaurant (Couple)
Budget: Dal Roti near Princess Street — best value dinner in Fort Cochin, ₹150–₹250, ask for Kerala fish curry. Couple: Fort House Restaurant on the water — seafood, candlelit, ₹1,500–₹2,500 for two. Order the karimeen (pearl spot fish) if available.
Local tip: Buy your houseboat ticket for Day 3 today — don't wait until you arrive in Alleppey. Book through your hotel, not from touts at the boat jetty. Always book overnight (not day trips).

Kerala 5-Day Itinerary Day 2 — Munnar
02
Day Two · Hill Station
Munnar — Mist, Tea & Mountain Air
Drive up · Tea gardens · Eravikulam Park · Sunset at Top Station
💰 Budget: ₹600–₹1,000 · Couple: ₹2,000–₹4,000 (excl. accommodation)
6:00 AM
Kochi to Munnar (4 hrs)
Budget: KSRTC bus from Ernakulam bus stand (₹150, 4 hrs) — sit on the left side for valley views. Couple/family: private car (₹2,500–₹3,500 one way). The mountain road through rubber plantations, cardamom forests and mist is spectacular. Stop at Cheeyappara Waterfalls halfway (free, roadside).
Afternoon
Eravikulam National Park
Home of the Nilgiri tahr (endangered mountain goat found only here) and the best panoramic views in Munnar. Rolling tea gardens, high-altitude grassland, peaks. Book tickets online — they sell out. Entry ₹145/person.
₹145 entry · Book online at kerala.gov.in
3:00 PM
Tata Tea Museum
India's most famous tea brand, started here in Munnar. The museum walks you through how tea goes from leaf to cup — plucking, withering, rolling, fermentation, drying. 45 minutes, genuinely interesting. Buy fresh Munnar tea to take home (₹150–₹300/250g).
₹100 entry · Includes tea tasting
5:00 PM
Top Station Sunset (25km from Munnar)
The highest point in Munnar at 1,880m. On clear days you can see Tamil Nadu on the other side of the mountains and the Neelakurinji valley below. Shared jeep from town ₹150 per person. Go on a weekday — weekends are crowded.
Jeep ₹150/person · Free entry
Dinner
In-Property Dinner / Rapsy Restaurant
Estate stays usually cook to order — ask for Kerala sadya on banana leaf (authentic Munnar-style). Budget option: Rapsy Restaurant in Munnar town, ₹100–₹200/meal, excellent local food.
Leave Munnar by 8am tomorrow to reach Alleppey in time for houseboat check-in at 1pm. The Munnar–Alleppey road passes through Kottayam — stop for a Kerala sadya lunch on a banana leaf (₹80–₹120, unlimited refills).

Kerala 5-Day Itinerary Day 3 — Alleppey Houseboat
03
Day Three · The Backwaters
Alleppey — The Overnight Houseboat
Drive from Munnar · Board houseboat · Sunset on Vembanad Lake
💰 Budget: ₹3,500–₹5,000/person · Couple: ₹12,000–₹20,000 for two (all meals incl.)
8:00 AM
Munnar to Alleppey (5 hrs)
~165km through rubber plantations and paddy fields. Budget: KSRTC bus ₹200, 5 hrs. Private car ₹3,500–₹4,500. Stop in Kottayam for banana leaf lunch. Arrive Alleppey by 1pm, drop bags at hotel.
1:00 PM
Board Your Houseboat
Check-in is at 12pm–1pm. The first hour — slowly moving through Alleppey's narrow urban canals — is magical. You pass under low bridges, through market areas, past people living their daily lives at water level. The city gradually gives way to open lagoon.
Shared: ₹3,500–₹5,000/person · Private: ₹12,000–₹20,000/boat
Afternoon
Vembanad Lake & Kuttanad Region
The boat moves into Vembanad Lake — Kerala's largest lake — then into the Kuttanad backwaters: rice paddy fields below sea level, tiny canal villages, toddy tappers climbing coconut palms. Watch kingfishers, egrets and cormorants fish alongside you.
6:00 PM
Sunset — Anchored in Open Water
Your captain finds a quiet spot away from other boats as the sun sets over the water. Ask to face west. The lake turns gold, then pink, then dark. This is the most peaceful experience in India.
Dinner
On-Board Kerala Feast (Included)
Your cook prepares a full Kerala meal on the boat: fish curry, rice, appam, stew, sambar, pickle, pappadam, payasam (rice pudding) for dessert. All ingredients bought fresh that morning. One of the best meals you'll have in India.
⚠️ Never book a day-only houseboat. Day boats run tourist circuits with 50 other boats — you spend 4 hours on the main canal and turn around. The overnight experience is completely different: the backwaters clear out after 4pm and the real Kerala emerges.

Kerala 5-Day Itinerary Day 4 — Alleppey Village
04
Day Four · Village Canals
Alleppey — Kayak, Village Life & Marari Beach
Sunrise on water · Village canals by kayak · Afternoon at beach
💰 Budget: ₹500–₹1,000 · Couple: ₹2,000–₹4,000 (excl. accommodation)
5:30 AM
Sunrise on the Backwaters
Set your alarm. The backwaters at dawn — mist sitting on still water, egrets taking flight, fishermen setting nets in silence — is the most peaceful experience in India. Watch from the bow of the houseboat as you slowly move back toward Alleppey.
8:00 AM
Breakfast on Board, Return to Jetty
Houseboats return to their home jetty by 9am. A full Kerala breakfast is served: puttu (steamed rice cake) with kadala curry, banana, and chai. Check out, store your bags at the hotel.
Morning
Village Canals by Kayak or Bicycle
This is what most travellers miss. The small canals that houseboats can't access are where real Kerala lives — children swimming, women washing clothes, coir rope being made by hand, toddy tappers climbing palms. Kayak rental: ₹300–₹500/hr. Bicycle: ₹100/day.
Kayak: ₹300–₹500 · Guided kayak tour: ₹1,500–₹2,500 for two
Afternoon
Marari Beach (20km from Alleppey)
One of Kerala's cleanest and least crowded beaches. A long stretch of white sand with almost no commercial development. Fishing village at one end. Auto from Alleppey ₹200–₹300. Marari Beach Resort (₹8,000–₹15,000) is one of Kerala's most peaceful luxury properties if budget allows.
Auto ₹200–₹300 each way
Dinner
Karimeen (Pearl Spot Fish) — Local Restaurant
Order karimeen — Kerala's signature fish found only in the Alleppey backwaters. Grilled in a banana leaf, mildly spiced. Only available in Alleppey. Any local restaurant near the canals will have it. ₹150–₹300 per plate. Do not leave Alleppey without eating this.
₹80 Ayurvedic massage tip: The Government Ayurvedic Dispensary in Alleppey offers subsidised treatments (₹80–₹200). Not the luxury experience but 100% authentic — the same doctors and formulations used for clinical treatment.

Kerala 5-Day Itinerary Day 5 — Varkala
05
Day Five · The Cliff Coast
Varkala — Kerala's Most Dramatic Beach
Train from Alleppey · Cliff walk · Sunset · Fly home
💰 Budget: ₹800–₹1,500 · Couple: ₹3,000–₹6,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Alleppey to Varkala by Train (2 hrs)
Best option: train from Alleppey (Alappuzha) railway station, ₹80, 2 hrs. The coastal railway gives great views. Book on IRCTC in advance during peak season (Dec–Jan). Taxi from Varkala station to the cliff is ₹150. Road option: 3 hrs, ₹1,500–₹2,000 by car.
Train: ₹80 · Taxi from station to cliff: ₹150
Afternoon
Papanasam Beach — The Cliff Base
Red laterite cliffs, 15–30m high, drop straight into the Arabian Sea. The stretch at the base of the cliffs (not the tourist section at the top) is where locals swim. The waves are stronger than Alleppey — swim between the red flags. The sea here is said to wash away sins (papanasam = destroyer of sin).
4:00 PM
Janardana Swamy Temple
One of Kerala's most important temples, 2,000 years old, sitting at the northern end of the beach. Non-Hindus cannot enter but the exterior, the setting and the surrounding area are beautiful. The approach through the beach is the best angle.
Free · Respectful dress required
5:30 PM
Cliff Walk — The Best Sunset in Kerala
The 1km cliff-top path with the Arabian Sea 25m below. Restaurants, cafes and yoga studios line the cliff edge. Walk the full length. The cliff turns gold, then deep red as the sun drops into the sea — unlike any sunset in India.
Dinner
Cliff House · The Treatment · Abhi's
All three cliff-top restaurants are excellent. The Treatment is most consistent — Kerala prawn curry, ₹350–₹450. Order fresh lime soda (₹40) and watch the last light fade over the sea. Trivandrum airport is 45 min away — pre-book your taxi (₹600–₹800).
⚠️ Don't go to Kovalam. Kovalam is overcrowded, overpriced and has seen better days. Varkala is 10x more dramatic and far less touristy. Unless your hotel is already booked in Kovalam, always choose Varkala.

Pro Tips
🐟
Order karimeen in Alleppey
Kerala's signature pearl spot fish, found only in the backwaters. Grilled in banana leaf. Only available in Alleppey — order it the moment you get off the houseboat.
🎭
Watch the Kathakali makeup
Arrive 1 hour before the show to watch the 2-hour makeup transformation. Buy the full show ticket (₹500–₹800). Without context the dance looks strange — with context it's extraordinary.
🌅
5:30am houseboat sunrise
Mist on still water, egrets flying, fishermen setting nets in silence. The most peaceful experience in India. Set your alarm. You won't regret it.
💆
One Ayurvedic treatment minimum
Even without the wellness plan: one 60-min Abhyanga massage (₹1,200–₹2,000) tells you exactly why Kerala Ayurveda is different from any spa anywhere else.
