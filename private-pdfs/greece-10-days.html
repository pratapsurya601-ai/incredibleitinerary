Destinations & Highlights
Athens
Days 1–2

The cradle of Western civilisation. Acropolis + Parthenon, Plaka neighbourhood, National Archaeological Museum, Monastiraki flea market, rooftop dinner with Acropolis views. A city that rewards every hour.

Mykonos
Days 3–4

Most glamorous Greek island. Little Venice with houses hanging over water, iconic windmills, party beaches (Paradise, Super Paradise), celebrity yachts, and some of the most electric nightlife in the Mediterranean.

Santorini
Days 5–6

The postcard Greece. Oia sunset (world's best), caldera views, black sand beaches (Perissa/Kamari), wine tasting with volcano view. Book your caldera hotel 3–4 months ahead — it will sell out.

Crete
Days 7–8

Greece's largest island. Palace of Knossos (3,500 years old), Samaria Gorge hike (Europe's longest), Elafonissi pink sand beach, Chania old town Venetian harbour. Vastly underrated by most tourists.

Before You Fly

Pre-trip essentials

🛂
Schengen Visa (Required)

Indians require a Schengen Visa. Apply at the Greek consulate or VFS Global. €80 fee (~₹7,200). Documents: 3-month bank statement, hotel bookings, return flight ticket, travel insurance. Straightforward process — Greece is one of the easier Schengen applications.

Apply 6–8 weeks before travel · Allow extra time in peak season

✈️
Flights to Athens (ATH)

Fly from Mumbai or Delhi via Dubai (Emirates), Doha (Qatar Airways), or Abu Dhabi (Etihad). Total journey 12–16 hrs with one stop. Athens International Airport (ATH) — Metro Line 3 to city centre costs €9 and takes 40 min. Affordable and easy.

Return fares: ₹55,000–95,000 · Book 3–4 months ahead for best prices

⛴️
Island Ferries — Book Early

Book at ferryhopper.com or directferries.com. High season (July–August) sells out weeks ahead. Conventional ferries are cheaper and have more deck space. High-speed ferries halve the time but cost more. Check weather in spring — rough seas can cancel sailings.

Book at least 3 weeks ahead in summer · Piraeus Port = Athens ferry hub

💶
Currency: Euro

€1 = ~₹90. ATMs are everywhere on all islands. Many small tavernas and local shops are cash-only — do not rely entirely on cards. Exchange in India before flying for a better rate. Airports have poor rates. Carry at least €150–200 in small notes.

€50/day budget · €120–150/day mid · €300+/day luxury islands

📶
SIM Card

Cosmote or Vodafone Greece SIMs available at Athens airport arrivals and mobile shops across all islands. €10 for 5GB — cheap and reliable. Greece has good 4G coverage across the islands. Download offline maps before visiting smaller coves and beaches.

Cosmote has the best island coverage · Available at airport Day 1

🏨
Accommodation — Book Far Ahead

Santorini and Mykonos are the most in-demand hotel destinations in all of Europe. Caldera-view hotels in Santorini and anything decent in Mykonos during July–August will be fully booked 3–4 months in advance. Book as soon as your visa is confirmed.

Santorini caldera views: 3–4 months ahead · Crete: 2 weeks is fine

Days 1–2 · Athens
Day 1 · Athens · Ancient Capital

Arrive Athens — Acropolis & Plaka

Arrival Land at Athens International (ATH). Metro Line 3 to Syntagma or Monastiraki: €9, 40 min. Stay in Monastiraki or Syntagma areas. Budget €45–70. Mid-range €90–160. Boutique €200+.
8:00 AM Acropolis (€20 combo ticket, ₹1,800 — covers 7 sites) — go first thing, doors open 8am. Parthenon, Erechtheion, Temple of Athena Nike. By 10am it becomes crowded and extremely hot in summer. The view over Athens from the top is the best in the city.
10:30 AM Acropolis Museum (€10) directly below the Acropolis — world-class, houses the original Parthenon sculptures (the Elgin Marbles that Britain has yet to return). The building itself is architectural drama — ancient ruins visible through the glass floor below you.
Afternoon Plaka neighbourhood — Athens' oldest neighbourhood, Ottoman-era winding streets, bougainvillea, tavernas. Walk into Monastiraki flea market — always open, always chaotic, excellent for leather sandals and olive wood.
Evening Dinner at Strofi Taverna near the Acropolis — rooftop terrace with direct Parthenon views, excellent Greek food, ~€40/person. Book ahead in peak season. One of Athens' most memorable dining moments.

Day 2 · Athens · Ancient Agora + Museums

Ancient Agora, Museums & Cape Sounion

Morning Ancient Agora (€10) — where Socrates taught, where democracy was debated. The Stoa of Attalos has been fully reconstructed and now houses a superb museum. Smaller and more intimate than the Acropolis but arguably more atmospheric.
11:30 AM National Archaeological Museum (€12) — the finest collection of Greek antiquities on the planet. Mycenaean gold death masks, ancient marble sculptures, and the Antikythera mechanism — a 2,000-year-old analogue computer that still astonishes scientists. Allow 2 hours minimum.
Afternoon Day trip to Cape Sounion (optional, 2hrs, €30 organised tour) — the Temple of Poseidon sits on a 60m cliff at the tip of Attica, looking directly out over the Aegean. Lord Byron carved his name into a column here. Sunset from the temple is spectacular. Highly recommended.
Evening Dinner in Psirri neighbourhood — Athens' most authentic dining quarter. Grilled octopus (€16), saganaki (€8), tzatziki (€5). €25–35/person for a full feast with wine. More local, less touristy than Plaka.

Days 3–4 · Mykonos
Day 3 · Mykonos · Arrival + Chora

Little Venice, Windmills & Sunset Drinks

Arrival Arrive Mykonos by ferry or fly from Athens (45 min, €80–150). Accommodation warning: cheapest hotel in Mykonos in high season is €120+. Book 2–3 months ahead. Stay in Mykonos Town or near a beach you prefer. Budget €120–200. Mid €250–500. Luxury €800+.
Afternoon Mykonos Town (Chora) — get lost in the whitewashed labyrinth of lanes. Every alley is a photograph. Find Little Venice — a row of houses built directly over the water, cafes extending onto the sea. One of the most scenic spots in the whole Mediterranean.
Sunset Kato Mili Windmills — the 5 iconic white windmills overlooking the town, best at golden hour. Walk to Alefkandra Square for sunset drinks (€15–20/cocktail — yes, it is expensive, but the view over Little Venice with the windmills is the image of Mykonos).
Day 4 · Mykonos · Beaches + Delos

Paradise Beach & Sacred Delos Island

Morning Delos Island day trip (€20 boat return + €12 entry) — a 30-min boat from Mykonos Town. The sacred birthplace of Apollo and Artemis. The entire island is one vast ancient ruins site — UNESCO World Heritage, spectacularly preserved. One of the most important archaeological sites in Europe.
Afternoon Paradise Beach (sun lounger €20/day) — Mykonos' most famous party beach, music from noon. Or Agios Sostis for the opposite experience — remote, free, untouched, no sunbeds, local crowd. Both 20 min by bus or taxi from Mykonos Town.
Evening Mykonos nightlife starts late — dinner at 9pm, bars from 11pm, clubs from 1am. Cavo Paradiso (cliff-top club, world-famous DJs), or Skandinavian Bar in town for something more relaxed. This is the party island of Europe — if you want it, it's all here.

Days 5–6 · Santorini
Day 5 · Santorini · Oia + Caldera Walk

Oia Village, Caldera Walk & World's Best Sunset

Morning Check in, breathe in the caldera view. Fira to Oia cliffside walk — 5km along the crater rim, 2 hours, absolutely spectacular. Caldera falls away on your left, the Aegean stretches to the horizon. This is Santorini's best free experience. Wear good shoes — path is uneven.
Afternoon Oia Village — the blue domes and white walls you've seen in every photograph. Wander freely. Shop for local art, lava jewellery, saffron. Lunch: Greek salad €10, fresh grilled fish €20–35 at a terrace taverna. Santo Wines winery — caldera-view wine tasting €20, the most scenic winery experience in Europe.
Sunset Oia sunset — world famous, deservedly so. Arrive at the Castle of Oia viewpoint (Kastro) 90 minutes early to claim a spot. Summer sunset ~7–8pm. Massive crowds but genuinely unforgettable. The sky turns amber, pink, violet — the caldera below glows. Bring a snack and patience.
Day 6 · Santorini · Beaches + Akrotiri

Black Sand Beaches, Red Beach & Minoan Ruins

Morning Akrotiri excavations (€12) — a Minoan Bronze Age city perfectly preserved under volcanic ash from the 1640 BC eruption. Santorini's own Pompeii, arguably more remarkable. Frescoes, multi-storey buildings, intact streets. Allow 1.5 hours. Fascinating and undervisited.
Midday Red Beach — a short 15-min walk from Akrotiri. Dramatic deep-red volcanic cliffs towering over the Aegean, dark volcanic sand, strikingly beautiful. One of the most unusual beaches in Europe. Can get crowded at peak hours — go before noon.
Afternoon Perissa or Kamari — Santorini's black sand beach towns on the island's eastern coast. Volcanic black sand (gets very hot — bring sandals). Sun loungers €10/day. Clear, calm water. Significantly cheaper food and drinks than anywhere near the caldera — eat lunch here.
Evening Final Santorini evening — dinner at a caldera-view terrace. Try Assyrtiko white wine — grown in volcanic soil unique to Santorini, mineral and dry, world-class. Or Vinsanto dessert wine — one of Greece's great treasures. €25–40/person for dinner with wine and view.
Santorini food tip: Avoid restaurants right in Oia village — markups are 60–80% higher than elsewhere. Walk 10 minutes to Finikia village for the exact same views and food at realistic prices. Local tip that saves €20–30 per meal.

Days 7–8 · Crete
Day 7 · Crete · Heraklion — Knossos + Beaches

Palace of Knossos & Minoan Civilisation

Morning Palace of Knossos (€15, 30 min bus from Heraklion) — the Bronze Age Minoan palace complex, 3,500 years old and partially reconstructed. The Minoans had indoor plumbing, frescoed walls, multi-storey buildings — an advanced civilisation that predates classical Greece. Allow 2 hours. Fascinating.
Midday Heraklion Archaeological Museum (€10, city centre) — houses the finest Minoan collection in the world. Original Knossos frescoes (the restored reconstructions at the palace are copies), gold jewellery, carved sealstones. The gold bee pendant is worth the entry alone. 2 hours.
Afternoon Elafonissi (3 hrs from Heraklion, €30 tour or hire a car) — UNESCO-protected pink sand beach, shallow turquoise lagoon. The pink colour comes from crushed coral and shells. Surreal colour in afternoon light. One of the most beautiful beaches in all of Europe. Worth the journey.
Evening Heraklion waterfront for dinner — or drive west and check into Chania for a far more beautiful base. Recommendation: stay in Chania for Day 8, it is the most beautiful town in Crete and deserves a full evening.
Day 8 · Crete · Chania + Samaria Gorge or Balos

Venetian Harbour, Gorge Hike & Turquoise Lagoon

Dawn Chania Venetian Harbour at dawn — free, and one of the most magical moments in all of Greece. The 14th-century lighthouse, the curved harbour wall, the reflection of the old town in still water before tourists arrive. Be there by 7am. You'll understand immediately why Crete is different.
Morning Chania Old Town — Byzantine and Ottoman streets, the covered Agora market hall (free, excellent for local cheese, olive oil, honey, thyme). The leather street off the harbour for handmade sandals. Smaller and more authentic than Athens, more historically layered than Mykonos or Santorini.
Full Day
Option A Samaria Gorge hike (€5 entry, full day) — Europe's longest gorge at 16km. Starts at 1,250m altitude, descends to sea level at Agia Roumeli. Spectacular limestone walls, wildflowers, goat sightings. Arrange transport from Chania (€25 organised tour or local bus). Finish with a boat back to Chania. Moderate fitness required.
Full Day
Option B Balos Lagoon by boat (€25 day trip from Kissamos Port) — the turquoise shallow lagoon at the northwest tip of Crete, accessible by boat or 4WD. Unreal water colour — blue, green, turquoise, all at once. Flamingos visit in spring. One of the most photographed spots in all of Greece.
Crete is Greece's best-kept secret: Despite being the largest Greek island and one of Europe's great destinations, Crete is far less crowded than Mykonos or Santorini outside of August. Hire a car for Day 7–8 for maximum freedom — roads are excellent and scenery is outstanding.

Day 9 · Athens · Neighbourhoods & Culture

The Athens You Missed the First Time

Morning Kolonaki — Athens' upmarket neighbourhood. Designer boutiques, excellent coffee, the National Gallery of Greece (€10). Lycabettus Hill (funicular or walk) — the highest point in Athens, 360° panorama over the city to the sea, with the Acropolis below. Best view of the Parthenon in context.
Midday Koukaki neighbourhood — the hipster alternative to Plaka, immediately south of the Acropolis. Specialty coffee, independent bookshops, excellent lunch options. Try souvlaki here — €2.50–3.50 each, some of the best in Athens at Kostas or O Thanasis nearby.
Afternoon Stavros Niarchos Cultural Centre (free, 30 min from centre) — a stunning piece of Renzo Piano architecture on the waterfront. Houses the Greek National Opera and National Library. The rooftop garden has sweeping sea views. An unexpected highlight — modern Athens at its most confident.
Evening Exarchia neighbourhood — Athens' alternative culture hub. Street art everywhere, independent bars, live music venues. Or return to Monastiraki for a final rooftop dinner with Acropolis views at golden hour. One last Greek salad, one last glass of Assyrtiko, one last look at the Parthenon lit against the night sky.
Day 10 · Athens · Market Morning + Fly Home

Athens Central Market & Departure

Morning Varvakios Agora (Athens Central Market) — the covered fish and meat market in central Athens, operating since 1886. One of the great markets of Europe. Fish hall, meat hall, surrounding stalls of spices, olives, dried herbs, feta. Bring cash. Open from 6am — incredible atmosphere before noon. Free to enter and browse.
Shopping Last chance shopping: Monastiraki flea market for leather sandals (€25–45 handmade), olive wood items, and worry beads (komboloi). Minoan-replica jewellery from museum shops. Greek saffron, dried oregano, thyme honey from the market — excellent gifts that pack flat.
Departure Metro Line 3 from Monastiraki or Syntagma to Athens International Airport (ATH) — 40 min, €9. Aim to be at the airport 3 hours before international departure. Duty-free at ATH is good for Greek olive oil, wines, and honey for gifts.

Greece in 10 Days Insider Tips · Page 9
8 Things That Make the Difference

Insider tips for Greece

🛂
Schengen Visa Strategy

Apply 6–8 weeks ahead at VFS Global or Greek consulate. Always book hotels before applying — you need confirmed reservations. Greek consulate is known to be one of the smoother Schengen processes. €80 fee, typical turnaround 2–3 weeks. Travel insurance is mandatory in the application.

🏨
Santorini Hotel — The 3-Month Rule

Caldera-view hotels in Oia and Firostefani sell out 3–4 months ahead for May to October. The moment you have your visa, book Santorini. Non-negotiable. Fira is the budget fallback — you'll still have caldera views from the town. Oia is worth saving for if you can manage it.

⛴️
Ferry Booking System

Use ferryhopper.com — the best aggregator for Greek island ferries. Book at least 3 weeks ahead in summer for all routes. Mykonos→Santorini and Santorini→Crete are the most booked-out routes. Conventional ferries have deck space to sit in sun — high-speed catamarans keep you below deck.

🌅
Oia Sunset — The 90-Minute Rule

Arrive at the Castle of Oia viewpoint (Kastro Oia) 90 minutes before sunset. The Instagram shot is from the castle ruins looking left. Bring water and a snack — you're waiting but it's worth it. The crowds are massive in July–August; May and September are more manageable. Never skip it.

🏛️
Acropolis Timing is Critical

Open at 8am — be there at 8am. By 10am it's extremely crowded and by midday in July/August it reaches 40°C+ on the exposed rock. The €20 combo ticket covers 7 sites including Acropolis Museum, Ancient Agora, and more — excellent value. Use it over 5 days as sites are spread across Athens.

🍽️
Eating Well in Greece

Gyros €2.50. Greek salad €8–12. Fresh grilled fish €20–40 depending on weight. Don't eat in Oia village — tourist markup is brutal. Walk 10 min to Finikia village for same views, real prices. Always ask to see the kitchen or the day's catch — Greek tavernas are proud to show you. Never order without checking if it's fresh.

📅
When to Go — Month by Month

May: perfect — warm, not crowded, everything open, best hotel prices. June: still excellent with slightly more people. July–August: expensive, crowded, extremely hot (38–42°C). September: best month overall — locals' favourite. October: cooler, quieter, great prices, some island businesses beginning to close. Avoid December–February on islands.

🛡️
Safety + Practical Reality

Greece is extremely safe. No major safety concerns beyond standard tourist awareness. Athens Metro has some pickpocket activity — keep bags in front. Island crime is minimal. Greeks are enormously hospitable to Indian tourists — you will be welcomed everywhere. Healthcare is EU-standard. Emergency: 112 (European standard emergency number).

What to Pack

Packing for Greece

👟Comfortable walking shoes — Athens cobblestones and Acropolis paths are uneven and tiring on unsupportive footwear
👙Swimwear ×3 — you will be swimming every single day. Three sets to avoid wet swimwear repeats
🕶️Quality polarised sunglasses — Mediterranean sun reflects off white buildings and water intensely
☀️SPF 50+ sunscreen — bring from India; island prices are 3× higher and stock is limited in small shops
🧴After-sun lotion — the sun is unforgiving, especially on boat decks and at black sand beaches where reflection adds burn
💊Seasickness tablets — island ferries can be rough, particularly in spring and early autumn winds (Meltemi)
👗Smart-casual evening wear — Mykonos and Santorini restaurants are genuinely dressy; linen shirts and sundresses are the standard
📄Schengen visa + all documents in physical copies — hotel confirmations, insurance, return ticket. Keep originals and photocopies separate
💳Cards + €200 cash — small tavernas and local shops often cash-only; ATMs on islands but can run low in peak season
🎒Daypack for hikes — essential for Samaria Gorge (water, snacks, layers), Acropolis, and beach-to-village walks in Santorini
🌂Light rain jacket — Athens spring can bring brief showers; folds flat and doubles as a layer on evening ferry decks
🩴Sandals or flip-flops — essential for boat decks, beach to taverna transitions, and Chania old town cobblestones in heat
