Day 1
📖 Anne Frank House & Jordaan
Anne Frank's secret annex where she wrote her diary for 2 years during WWII is one of the most moving historical sites in the world. MUST book online months ahead — often sold out completely. The Jordaan neighbourhood surrounding it is Amsterdam's most charming: narrow canals, independent galleries, traditional brown cafés.
Day 2
🎨 Museum Square & Vondelpark
The Rijksmuseum (Rembrandt's Night Watch, Vermeer's The Milkmaid) and Van Gogh Museum (the largest collection of Van Gogh works in the world) face each other across Museum Square. Both require advance booking. Vondelpark adjacent — Amsterdam's equivalent of Central Park, free and beautiful.
Day 3
🌷 Zaanse Schans or Keukenhof
Zaanse Schans: 20 minutes from Amsterdam — working windmills, traditional wooden houses, cheese farms and clog workshops. April only: Keukenhof Gardens (Lisse, 1hr by bus) — 7 million tulips in 800 varieties across 32 hectares. The most spectacular flower display in the world. Book ahead — both are extremely popular.
Day 4
🚲 De Pijp & NEMO
De Pijp neighbourhood with Albert Cuyp Market (Amsterdam's largest outdoor market, free entry, ₹200–₹500 for Dutch stroopwafels and cheese). NEMO Science Museum with remarkable rooftop terrace views over the IJ harbour. Stedelijk Museum for modern design. Evening canal walk at dusk.

"Amsterdam is a city that has never fully decided whether it's in the Middle Ages or the future — and that is precisely its charm." Every canal reflects 400 years of history.

Visa & Flights: Schengen visa required — apply at Netherlands Consulate/VFS Global (₹7,000 approx). Flights: Delhi/Mumbai to Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) — ₹38,000–₹68,000 return via Middle Eastern hubs or KLM via Istanbul/Dubai. Train from Schiphol Airport to Amsterdam Centraal: 17 minutes, ₹300 — most efficient option. Amsterdam OV-chip card for trams and buses (₹200 card + top-up). Alternatively, rent a bicycle from Centraal station (₹1,200/day) and cycle everywhere — the authentic Amsterdam experience.

Day One · History & Canals
Anne Frank House, Jordaan & Canal Cruise
Morning Anne Frank House · Jordaan neighbourhood walk · Westerkerk · Afternoon canal cruise · Herengracht evening
💰 Anne Frank: ₹2,200 · Canal cruise: ₹1,200 · Rijsttafel dinner: ₹2,500
9:00 AM
Anne Frank House — Book Online Months in Advance
Anne Frank and her family hid in the secret annex of this canal house for 761 days between 1942 and 1944. She was 13 when she went into hiding and began her diary. The diary is among the most widely read books in history. The house is preserved exactly as it was found — empty, as the occupants were arrested and the furniture confiscated. The experience is deeply moving and unlike any other historical site. Tickets at annefrank.org sell out weeks or months ahead — book as soon as your dates are confirmed. Evening tickets (after 6pm) are sometimes available on a rolling 6-week basis.
Entry: ₹2,200 · Book at annefrank.org · Evening tickets slightly easier to get · Allow 90 min
11:30 AM
Jordaan Neighbourhood — Amsterdam's Most Charming Quarter
The Jordaan (from "jardin" — French garden) was built in the 17th century as a working-class district and is now Amsterdam's most desirable and charming neighbourhood. Narrow canals (Prinsengracht, Keizersgracht, Herengracht — the three main canal rings are UNESCO World Heritage), houseboats, independent art galleries, antique shops, and traditional Dutch brown cafés (bruine kroegen) where locals drink jenever (Dutch gin) and beer since the 17th century. The Noordermarkt on Saturdays has excellent organic produce and antiques.
Jordaan: free to wander · Bruine kroeg beer: ₹300 · Jenever (Dutch gin) shot: ₹250
3:00 PM
Canal Cruise — The Best Way to See Amsterdam
Amsterdam's canal ring — 165 canals, 1,281 bridges, 2,500 houseboats — is best understood from the water. Rederij Lovers and Blue Boat Company operate regular 1-hour cruises (₹1,200/person) with commentary on the Golden Age merchant houses, the narrowest house in Amsterdam (2.44 metres wide), and the architecture that has barely changed in 400 years. Alternatively, rent a small electric canal boat (₹4,000/hour, seats 6) and navigate yourself — Mokum Boats and Boaty are popular options. The glass-top boats let you see the gabled rooflines reflected in the water.
Regular cruise: ₹1,200/person · Private electric boat: ₹4,000/hr (up to 6 people) · Book ahead

Day Two · World-Class Art
Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum & Vondelpark
9am Rijksmuseum (Night Watch) · Van Gogh Museum · Vondelpark lunch · Heineken Experience or I Amsterdam sign area
💰 Rijksmuseum: ₹2,500 · Van Gogh: ₹2,800 · Both require advance booking
9:00 AM
Rijksmuseum — Rembrandt's Night Watch & Vermeer's Milkmaid
The national museum of the Netherlands holds the finest collection of Dutch Golden Age painting in the world. The Night Watch (Rembrandt van Rijn, 1642) — the largest and most celebrated painting in the collection — is displayed in a dedicated room designed specifically for it. Vermeer's The Milkmaid, The Love Letter, and Woman Reading a Letter. Jan Steen's The Merry Family. The museum building itself — a neo-Gothic palace with bicycle lane running through it — is extraordinary. Book at rijksmuseum.nl. 2–3 hours minimum. The museum café serves excellent Dutch stamppot (mashed potato with vegetables, ₹800).
Entry: ₹2,500 · Book at rijksmuseum.nl · Closed Mondays · Allow 2–3 hours
1:00 PM
Van Gogh Museum — The Largest Van Gogh Collection in the World
The Van Gogh Museum holds 200 paintings and 500 drawings by Vincent van Gogh — more than any other museum. The Bedroom at Arles, The Potato Eaters, Almond Blossom, Skull with Burning Cigarette, and self-portraits spanning his career. The chronological display traces his development from dark Dutch realism to the explosive colour of his Provence years. Van Gogh painted 900 works in his 10-year career before dying at 37. Book at vangoghmuseum.nl — tickets often sell out 2 weeks ahead in peak season. The museum café has good sandwiches.
Entry: ₹2,800 · Book at vangoghmuseum.nl · Allow 2 hours · Closed Mondays (occasionally)
Evening
Heineken Experience — Optional But Fun
The original Heineken brewery (closed 1988) is now an interactive brand experience — the brewing process, a simulated bottling experience, and 2 free beers at the end (₹2,500/person). It's touristy but well-produced. Alternatively, skip it and instead spend the evening in the Leidseplein — Amsterdam's entertainment square — with its outdoor café terraces, street performers, and the Paradiso music venue (a deconsecrated church turned concert hall). Indonesian food (rijsttafel — rice table feast, 20+ dishes) is Amsterdam's classic dinner: try Sampurna near the Jordaan (₹2,000/person).
Heineken Experience: ₹2,500 · Rijsttafel dinner: ₹2,000–₹3,000/person · Leidseplein: free

Day Three · Dutch Countryside
Zaanse Schans or Keukenhof Gardens
Train to Zaanse Schans (windmills, 20 min) OR Keukenhof tulip gardens (April only, 1hr bus) · Return afternoon · Evening canal
💰 Zaanse Schans: ₹2,000 entry + ₹400 train · Keukenhof (April): ₹2,200 + bus ₹600
9:30 AM
Zaanse Schans — Working Windmills 20 Minutes from Amsterdam
Zaanse Schans is an open-air museum village on the Zaan River — working windmills (saw mills, oil mills, paint mills), traditional Dutch wooden houses painted in green (Zaans groen), cheese farms (free samples of young, aged and truffle Gouda — buy directly), wooden clog workshops, and a traditional herring cart (fresh herring eaten Dutch-style, with onions, ₹400). Train from Centraal to Koog-Zaandijk (17 minutes). Walk 10 minutes to the site. Entry to the village is free; individual windmill interiors: ₹400–₹600 each. Allow 3–4 hours. Go on a weekday morning if possible — weekends are very crowded.
Village entry: free · Windmill interior: ₹400–₹600 · Train return: ₹400 · Gouda samples: free
April Only
Keukenhof Gardens — 7 Million Tulips (April Only)
If visiting in April, Keukenhof is mandatory. The gardens at Lisse open for only 8 weeks per year (late March – mid May) and display 7 million bulbs in over 800 varieties. It's the most spectacular flower display in the world. Book tickets online (keukenhof.nl — ₹2,200) and the combination ticket with the bus from Amsterdam (₹600 extra). Peak bloom for tulips is typically mid-April. Daffodils peak in late March, hyacinths in early April. The park also has greenhouses with tender species and a windmill. Allow 3–4 hours.
Entry: ₹2,200 · Book at keukenhof.nl · Combo with bus: extra ₹600 · April only · Sell out quickly

Rent a Bicycle — This Is How Amsterdam Works
Amsterdam has more bicycles (881,000) than residents (821,000). Renting a bicycle (₹1,200/day from MacBike or Starbikes) is the single most authentic Amsterdam experience. Cycle the canal rings, Vondelpark, and through neighbourhoods. Rules: stay in bike lanes (red paths), don't walk in them, give way to trams. Helmets not standard here — locals don't wear them.
🌷
Best Time to Visit — Tulip Season in April
April is the best month — tulips in bloom, Keukenhof open, relatively mild weather (12–18°C). Summer (June–August) is warm (20–25°C) and very crowded. Shoulder season May, September, October: good weather, fewer crowds, lower hotel prices. Winter (November–March) has beautiful light on the canals but cold (2–8°C). King's Day (April 27) — the city turns entirely orange, street markets everywhere.
🧀
Dutch Food Worth Trying
Stroopwafel (syrup waffle, ₹150) — place it over your hot coffee cup for 30 seconds, then eat. Bitterballen (fried beef ragout balls, pub snack, ₹400). Haring (raw herring with onions, ₹400). Poffertjes (mini fluffy pancakes, ₹500). Dutch fries (patat) with mayonnaise (frikadel) — eaten from paper cones at street stands. Gouda cheese direct from Zaanse Schans farms.
📋
Cannabis Laws — Know Before You Go
Cannabis is tolerated (not technically legal) in licensed coffee shops for adults over 18 with ID. As of 2024, Amsterdam has banned tourist access to coffee shops in some areas. Rules change frequently — check current local laws before visiting. Soft drugs are tolerated; hard drugs are absolutely not. This is a personal choice — be aware of the laws and stay safe.
