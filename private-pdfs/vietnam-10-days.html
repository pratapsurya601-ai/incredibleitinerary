Vietnam 10-Day Itinerary Pre-Trip Essentials · Days 1–2 Hanoi
Before You Fly

Pre-Trip Essentials

🛂 Visa — e-Visa Required
Indians require e-Visa. Apply at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn. Cost: $25. Processing: 3 working days. 90-day single entry. Apply at least 1 week before travel.
✈️ Flight — Direct from India
Direct Hanoi or HCMC from Delhi/Mumbai. IndiGo, Air India, Vietnam Airlines. 4–5 hrs. Fly into Hanoi (HAN), fly out of HCMC (SGN) for best routing.
💱 Currency — Vietnamese Dong
₹1 = ~285 VND. ₹10,000 = ~28,50,000 VND. ATMs everywhere. Carry USD $100 as backup. Grab handles most payments in cities.
📱 SIM + Apps
Viettel or Vietnamobile at airport. 30GB for ~₹250. Best coverage. Install: Grab (rides + food), Google Translate (offline Vietnamese), Maps.me.
Days 1–2 · Hanoi

Hanoi: Old Quarter, Culture & History

01
Day One · Hanoi Arrival
Old Quarter & Hoan Kiem Lake
Noi Bai Airport → Old Quarter · Lake walk · Bun cha dinner · Bia hoi corner
💰 Budget: ~₹1,200 · Mid: ~₹3,000 (excl. accommodation)
Arrive
Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) — To Old Quarter
Grab cab to Old Quarter: ~₹400 (200,000 VND). Journey 45 mins. Hotel: Old Quarter area — budget hostels ₹500–800/night, mid-range ₹2,000–4,000, boutique hotels ₹5,000+. Drop bags and head straight out.
Grab cab: ₹400 · Budget hostel: ₹500–800/night
Afternoon
Hoan Kiem Lake + Ngoc Son Temple + 36 Streets
Walk the beautiful lake — free and peaceful. Cross the red Huc Bridge to Ngoc Son Temple on the island. Then explore the Old Quarter's 36 streets — each historically sold one product: tin street, paper street, silk street. Still partly true today.
Hoan Kiem: Free · Ngoc Son Temple: ₹70
Evening
Bun Cha Huong Lien + Bia Hoi Corner
Obama ate here in 2016 with Anthony Bourdain — the most famous bun cha in Vietnam. Grilled pork patties in broth with vermicelli. ₹150. Then head to the bia hoi corner (intersection of Luong Ngoc Quyen and Ta Hien) — 30 paise beer (~₹18) on tiny plastic stools surrounded by locals. This is Hanoi.
Bun cha: ₹150 · Bia hoi: ₹18/glass
Night
Hoan Kiem Walking Street (Weekends)
On Friday, Saturday and Sunday nights the roads around Hoan Kiem Lake become a pedestrian zone with live music, food stalls, and street performers. Free and wonderful. Weekdays — walk Ta Hien street instead.
Free entry
02
Day Two · Hanoi
Culture, History & Egg Coffee
Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum · Temple of Literature · Hoa Lo Prison · Egg coffee
💰 Budget: ~₹600 activities · Must try: egg coffee at Cafe Giang ₹70
Morning
Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum + Museum + One Pillar Pagoda
The mausoleum complex where Ho Chi Minh's embalmed body is preserved — free entry but closed Monday and Friday. Dress respectfully (no shorts, sleeves). Nearby: Ho Chi Minh Museum (₹50) and the elegant One Pillar Pagoda built in 1049.
Mausoleum: Free (closed Mon & Fri) · Museum: ₹50
Afternoon
Temple of Literature — Vietnam's First University
Built in 1070 during the Ly Dynasty, Vietnam's first national university. Five beautiful courtyards with pavilions, stone steles bearing the names of graduates, and a peaceful garden. Perfectly preserved. Allow 1.5 hours to appreciate it properly.
Entry: ₹90 · Open daily 8am–5pm
3:00 PM
Hoa Lo Prison "Hanoi Hilton"
Sobering and important. Built by French colonizers as a prison for Vietnamese political prisoners — then used by North Vietnam to hold American POWs (including John McCain). Exhibits are fascinating though one-sided. Essential historical context for understanding Vietnam.
Entry: ₹100 · 1–1.5 hrs
Evening
Train Street + Egg Coffee at Cafe Giang
Phung Hung Street — a narrow alley where trains pass twice daily (check times: ~7am and ~7pm), restaurants and cafes lining both sides. Then: egg coffee at Cafe Giang (Est. 1946) — whipped egg yolk and condensed milk piled on strong Vietnamese coffee. Strange, then heavenly.
Train Street: Free · Egg coffee: ₹70

Vietnam 10-Day Itinerary Days 3–5 · Ha Long Bay · Hue · Hội An
03
Day Three · UNESCO Wonder
Ha Long Bay — Limestone Karsts & Emerald Water
Hanoi → Ha Long City · Cruise · Kayaking · Sung Sot Cave · Sunset on deck
💰 Day cruise: ₹2,500–3,500 · Overnight 2D/1N: ₹6,000–12,000 (all-inclusive)
6:00 AM
Hanoi to Ha Long City — 3.5 hrs by Bus/Car
Book your cruise in Hanoi the night before — most hotels can arrange. The bus picks you up from your hotel. Overnight cruise strongly recommended — the bay at dawn before other boats arrive is magnificent. Budget: Indochina Sails. Mid: Heritage Cruises. Luxury: Paradise Cruises.
Transfer included in cruise package · Book 1 day ahead
Highlights
Sung Sot Cave + Kayaking + Sunset
Sung Sot (Surprise Cave) — largest in Ha Long, cathedral-like chambers with illuminated stalactites. Kayak through limestone arches and hidden lagoons accessible only by boat. Swim at designated stops. Sunset from the deck with Vietnamese beer in hand — 2,000+ islands around you.
Overnight cruise (2D/1N): ₹6,000–12,000 all-inclusive
04
Day Four · Imperial Hue
Hue — Imperial Citadel & Royal Tombs
Fly/train Hanoi → Hue · Citadel · Thien Mu Pagoda · Royal Tombs · Dong Ba Market
💰 Flight: ₹2,000–3,500 · Train sleeper: ₹1,500 · Activities: ~₹700
Morning
Imperial Citadel — Nguyen Dynasty Capital
Fly or take overnight train Hanoi → Hue (or Da Nang + taxi). The Imperial Citadel was the 19th-century seat of the Nguyen dynasty — Vietnam's last royal family. UNESCO World Heritage. Moats, gates, throne rooms and ornate pavilions across a vast walled city. Allow 2–3 hours.
Entry: ₹400 · Flight: ₹2,000–3,500 · Train sleeper: ₹1,500
Afternoon
Thien Mu Pagoda + Royal Tombs of Minh Mang
Thien Mu Pagoda — 7-tier octagonal tower on the Perfume River, built 1601. Free, 30 min from city by motorbike taxi (₹80). Lunch: Bun bo Hue, the city's fiery beef noodle soup — spicier than pho, utterly addictive (₹120). Royal Tombs of Minh Mang — most architecturally beautiful of all royal tombs.
Thien Mu: Free · Tombs of Minh Mang: ₹250 · Bun bo: ₹120
Evening
Dong Ba Market + Banh Mi from Street Stalls
Hue's main market for local shopping — lacquerware, conical hats, local spices. Excellent for gifts. Pick up banh mi from the street stalls outside — Hue's version uses more vegetables and less meat than Hội An's famous version. ₹40.
Banh mi: ₹40 · Market: free entry, haggle for goods
05–06
Days Five–Six · UNESCO Ancient Town
Hội An — Lanterns, Tailors & My Son
Ancient Town · Lantern street nights · Custom tailoring · My Son Ruins day trip
💰 Ancient Town tickets: ₹450 · My Son day trip: ₹800 · Tailoring: ₹1,000–2,500
Day 5 AM
Arrive Hội An — 1hr Drive from Da Nang
One hour drive from Da Nang (taxi ₹600, Grab ₹350) or train to Da Nang + taxi. Hội An is the most charming town in Southeast Asia — a preserved trading port from the 15th–19th centuries. Get Ancient Town entry tickets (₹450, covers 5 sites: Japanese Covered Bridge, assembly halls, old houses, museums).
Ancient Town tickets: ₹450 · Night walking: free
Day 5 PM
Custom Tailoring — Visit Tailors, Give Reference Photos
Hội An is famous worldwide for its tailors — skilled artisans who can replicate almost any garment in 24 hours. Visit 2–3 tailors, compare fabrics and prices, bring photos of what you want. Budget: shirt ₹1,000–1,500, dress ₹1,500–2,500, suit ₹4,000–8,000. Collect next day. Top shops: Bebe Tailor, Yaly Couture, Kimmy Fashion.
Shirt: ₹1,000–1,500 · Dress: ₹1,500–2,500 · 24hr turnaround
Day 5 Night
Ancient Town at Night — Lantern Magic
After dark the Ancient Town becomes magical — hundreds of silk lanterns in every colour light the narrow streets. On the 14th of every lunar month, all electric lights are turned off and only lanterns glow. Release a lantern boat on the Thu Bon River (₹50). White Rose dumplings ₹150, Cao lau noodles ₹180, Banh mi Phuong ₹60 (Anthony Bourdain called it the best in the world).
Lantern boat: ₹50 · Banh mi Phuong: ₹60 · White rose: ₹150
Day 6 AM
My Son Ruins — Vietnam's Angkor Wat (Day Trip)
4th–13th century Hindu temples built by the Champa kingdom in a remote jungle valley — UNESCO World Heritage. The complex of brick temples is atmospheric and largely free of tourists in the morning. Book a group tour from Hội An (₹800 includes transport, guide, entry). Leave at 7am, back by noon.
Group tour with transport: ₹800 · Solo taxi + entry: ~₹1,000

Vietnam 10-Day Itinerary Days 7–10 · Da Nang · Ho Chi Minh City
07
Day Seven · Da Nang
Dragon Bridge, Marble Mountains & My Khe Beach
30 min from Hội An · Dragon Bridge fire Sat/Sun 9pm · Marble Mountains · Beach
💰 Marble Mountains: ₹250 · Beach: Free · Dragon Bridge fire show: Free
Morning
Marble Mountains — Five Elemental Hills
Five marble-and-limestone hills named after the elements: water, fire, wood, metal, earth. Climb Thuy Son (Water Mountain) — highest and most rewarding — for panoramic views of Da Nang and the East Sea. Caves contain Buddhist and Hindu shrines. Entry ₹250.
Entry: ₹250 · Elevator up available: ₹60 extra
Afternoon
My Khe Beach + Son Tra Peninsula
My Khe Beach — 30km of white sand, clear water, one of the best urban beaches in Central Vietnam. Free, well-maintained, lifeguards present. Afternoon: drive up Son Tra Peninsula (rent motorbike ₹300/day) — viewpoints over the bay, monkey colony, the giant Lady Buddha visible from the sea.
Beach: Free · Motorbike rental: ₹300/day
Sat/Sun 9pm
Dragon Bridge Fire & Water Show
The iconic Dragon Bridge (2013) spans 666m across the Han River, shaped like a dragon with scales and a head at the east end. Every Saturday and Sunday at 9pm the dragon breathes fire and water — free, spectacular, crowd-draws but worth it. Grab a spot 30 mins early.
Free · Saturday & Sunday only · 9:00 PM sharp
08–10
Days Eight–Ten · Saigon
Ho Chi Minh City — War History, Tunnels & Energy
Fly Da Nang → HCMC · War Museum · Cu Chi Tunnels · Mekong · Bui Vien nightlife
💰 Flight: ₹1,500–2,500 · Cu Chi: ₹1,500 · Mekong: ₹2,000
Day 8 AM
Fly Da Nang → HCMC + War Remnants Museum
Fly Da Nang → Ho Chi Minh City (1.5hrs, ₹1,500–2,500) or take overnight bus (₹600, 15hrs). War Remnants Museum — the most powerful museum in Vietnam, documenting the American War from the Vietnamese perspective. Graphic but essential. Don't skip. Reunification Palace nearby (₹180) — exactly as it was on April 30, 1975.
Flight: ₹1,500–2,500 · War Museum: ₹150 · Palace: ₹180
Day 8 PM
Ben Thanh Market + Pham Ngu Lao Street Food
Ben Thanh Market — the tourist hub for souvenirs, lacquerware, silk scarves and Vietnamese coffee. Bargain hard (start at 40% of asking price). Evening: Pham Ngu Lao (backpacker district) for cheap, excellent street food. Banh mi, pho, com tam (broken rice with pork), fresh coconuts.
Banh mi: ₹60 · Com tam: ₹150 · Pho: ₹120
Day 9
Cu Chi Tunnels Day Trip — Underground Vietnam
250km of underground tunnels dug by Viet Cong guerrillas — living quarters, hospitals, kitchens, meeting rooms all underground. You can crawl through a widened section (not for the claustrophobic). The ingenuity is mind-blowing. Book a group tour from HCMC (₹1,500 includes transport + guide). Mekong Delta day trip alternative (₹2,000) — boat through canals, coconut candy factories, floating markets.
Cu Chi group tour: ₹1,500 · Mekong Delta: ₹2,000
Day 10
Bui Vien Walking Street + Rooftop Bar + Airport
Final night: Bui Vien Walking Street — Saigon's neon-lit backpacker street, wall-to-wall bars, live music, street food chaos. Cocktails from ₹200. For a stunning farewell: Chill Sky Bar at AB Tower (₹1,200/cocktail but the view of the city at night is extraordinary). Fly home from Tan Son Nhat Airport (SGN) — arrive 3hrs early.
Bui Vien: Free entry · Chill Sky Bar cocktail: ₹1,200
💡 Saigon vs Hanoi: Spend more time in HCMC if energy and history excite you more than culture and calm. Hanoi is slower, older, more French — Saigon is faster, younger, more American. Both are essential Vietnam. If you had to choose one extra day, give it to HCMC.

Bus/car included in cruise package. 3.5hrs. No need to book separately.

Vietnam 10-Day Itinerary Essential Tips & Packing List
Know Before You Go

Essential Vietnam Tips

🛂
e-Visa — Apply 1 Week Ahead
Indians require e-Visa ($25). Apply at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn — processing takes 3 working days but apply 7+ days ahead for buffer. 90-day single entry. Do NOT attempt visa on arrival — Vietnam does not offer this for Indians.
🚗
Grab App — Always
Metered taxis in Vietnam are infamous for scamming tourists — rigged meters, long routes. Use Grab for every single ride in every city. Price shown upfront. No surprises. Install and register before you land.
🚢
Ha Long Bay Overnight
The overnight cruise is worth the extra cost. Budget operators: Indochina Sails (₹6,000). Mid: Heritage Cruises (₹9,000). Luxury: Paradise Cruises (₹18,000+). All include meals, kayaking, cave visits. Book in Hanoi the day before.
✂️
Hội An Tailors — Bring Photos
Quality varies enormously. Bring clear reference photos on your phone. Visit 2–3 tailors, compare fabrics. Allow 24hrs minimum (48hrs for complex garments). Check stitching before paying. Top recommendation: Yaly Couture (pricier but reliable quality).
🥖
Banh Mi — Eat It Everywhere
₹40–80 for the best sandwich you'll ever eat. The French left Vietnam their baguettes — Vietnamese made them better. Every city has its own style. Hội An's Banh Mi Phuong is most famous (Bourdain-approved), but the street stall outside your guesthouse will also be outstanding.
🍜
Street Food Safety
Vietnam street food is safer than you think. The rule: busy stall = fresh food, high turnover, locals eating there. Avoid empty stalls. Pho, bun cha, com tam, banh cuon — all safe, all incredible. The stomach medication in your bag is for the humidity, not the food.
💰
Ben Thanh Bargaining
Ben Thanh Market in HCMC: start your counter-offer at 40% of asking price. Seller will go to 50–60%. Settle around 50–55%. Never feel bad — this is expected, normal, and enjoyable. If they say "last price" and walk away, they'll come back.
🚂
Reunification Express Train
The Hanoi–HCMC rail line is one of Asia's great train journeys — the Hai Van Pass section near Da Nang (Top Gear's "best coast road") is spectacular. Take the train for at least the Hue→Da Nang leg. Book at vietnam-railway.com. SE trains are the comfortable express services.

Clothing & Essentials

□Rain jacket (tropical showers can appear without warning)
□Light cover-up for temples (shoulders + knees)
□Comfortable walking shoes for Old Quarter cobblestones
□Flip flops or slip-ons (removing shoes at temples)
□Swimwear for Ha Long Bay and beach stops

Health

□DEET mosquito repellent (essential — malaria-free but dengue present)
□Stomach medication (just in case — Norflox + ORS)
□SPF 50 sunscreen (intense tropical sun)
□Hand sanitiser + wet wipes for street food

Money & Tech

□Niyo or Wise card for VND withdrawals (no forex fees)
□USD $100 as emergency backup cash
□Power bank — long days on the road
□Grab app installed and registered before landing
□Google Translate with offline Vietnamese downloaded
□Maps.me or Google Maps offline for cities

□E-Visa printout + digital copy (always)
□Passport (original required at many hotels)
