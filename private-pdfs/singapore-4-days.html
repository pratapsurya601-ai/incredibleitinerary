Your Route
Changi Airport → Marina Bay → Little India / Chinatown → Sentosa / Gardens → Orchard / Departure 4 days · 1 city-state
Zone by Zone
Marina Bay
Day 1
The postcard Singapore. Marina Bay Sands SkyPark, the iconic Merlion, Helix Bridge, and the spectacular Gardens by the Bay. The ArtScience Museum (S$28) sits on the water's edge. At 7:45pm and 8:45pm, the free Supertree Grove light show transforms the bay into something otherworldly.
Chinatown + Little India
Day 2
Old Singapore, beautifully preserved. Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple, the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, and the world-famous Maxwell Food Centre — home of Tian Tian chicken rice. The heritage quarters are free to explore on foot. Cheap hawker food, colourful temples, and colonial shophouses.
Sentosa Island
Day 3
Singapore's pleasure island. Universal Studios Singapore (S$83), Palawan & Siloso beaches (free), Adventure Cove Waterpark (S$38), and the stunning Wings of Time laser-water-fire show (S$18) on the beach at 7:40pm. Accessible by cable car (S$35) or VivoCity monorail (S$4).
Orchard + Bugis + Haji Lane
Day 4
Singapore's shopping and street culture district. Ion Orchard, Paragon, and Takashimaya for retail. Haji Lane in Kampong Glam for street art and indie boutiques. Sim Lim Square for electronics. End at Jewel Changi — the world's tallest indoor waterfall — before your flight.

"Singapore is proof that a city can be impossibly clean, impossibly safe, and impossibly delicious — all at once. Hawker centres alone are worth the flight."

For Indian Travellers: Indians require an eVisa — apply on the Singapore ICA website (ica.gov.sg). S$30, usually instant approval. Do this before booking flights. No eVisa = no entry.

Singapore 4-Day Itinerary Days 1–2
01
Day One · Arrival
Arrival + Marina Bay Sands
Changi Airport → MRT → Hotel → Merlion Park → Gardens by the Bay → Supertree Light Show
Approx. spend: S$50–80 (excl. accommodation)
Arrival
Changi Airport → City by MRT
Take the MRT from Changi Airport Terminal 1/2/3 directly to the city. Buy an EZ-Link card at the MRT counter (S$12 including S$7 travel credit) — use it for all MRT and bus trips throughout your stay. The airport-to-city ride takes 30 minutes and costs S$2.50. Fastest airport-to-city transit in the world.
MRT: S$2.50 · EZ-Link card: S$12 (one-time, includes S$7 credit)
Hotel
Check In — Where to Stay
Budget (S$80–150/night): Bugis or Lavender area — great MRT access, cheap hawker food nearby. Mid-range (S$200–400): Marina Bay/Clarke Quay — walking distance to all Day 1 sights. Luxury (S$400+): Sentosa island or Marina Bay Sands itself. Book at least 3 weeks ahead — Singapore hotels fill fast.
Afternoon
Merlion Park + Helix Bridge + Marina Bay Sands
Merlion Park is free — the half-lion, half-fish statue spouting water into the bay is Singapore's most photographed landmark. Walk across the Helix Bridge (free, beautiful architecture) to Marina Bay Sands. Take the SkyPark Observation Deck for the best city views in Asia — the 57th floor infinity pool view with the city behind it is unmistakable.
Merlion + Helix: Free · MBS Observation Deck: S$28
5:00 PM
Gardens by the Bay — Supertrees + Domes
The Cloud Forest dome (S$28) is extraordinary — a 35-metre indoor mountain wrapped in living plants with a cool mist. The Flower Dome (S$14) displays Mediterranean gardens. OR save money and enjoy the outdoor Supertree Grove for free — 18 tree-like vertical gardens up to 16 storeys tall. The free OCBC Garden Rhapsody light show at 7:45pm and 8:45pm is not to be missed.
Outdoor Supertrees: Free · Cloud Forest: S$28 · Flower Dome: S$14 · Light show: Free
Dinner
Satay by the Bay or Lau Pa Sat Hawker Centre
Satay by the Bay is the open-air hawker spot right inside Gardens by the Bay — skewers S$3–8 each, cold beer, bay views. Alternative: Lau Pa Sat on Robinson Road, an 1894 Victorian cast-iron market now a hawker centre — historic architecture, local food S$5–12/dish. Both are quintessential Singapore evenings.
Budget: S$8–15/person · Satay skewers: S$3–8 each
02
Day Two · Heritage Singapore
Little India + Chinatown
Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple → Maxwell Food Centre → Chinatown Street Market → Clarke Quay
Approx. spend: S$30–50 (excl. accommodation) — mostly free sights
Morning
Little India — Temple + Mustafa Centre
Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple on Serangoon Road is free — a riot of colour and Dravidian towers. Remove shoes before entering. Then Mustafa Centre nearby — open 24hrs, cheap electronics, Indian groceries, and gold at competitive prices. Perfect place to stock up on anything. Lunch: Komala Vilas on Serangoon for banana leaf rice (S$8, vegetarian, excellent).
Temple: Free · Komala Vilas lunch: S$8
Afternoon
Chinatown — Buddha Tooth Temple + Maxwell
Buddha Tooth Relic Temple on South Bridge Road houses a sacred tooth relic of the Buddha — free entry, stunning interior, no shorts allowed. Then walk to Maxwell Food Centre for S$3–6 dishes. Tian Tian Hainanese Chicken Rice (stall #10) is legendary — arrive by 1pm before it sells out. Wander the Chinatown Street Market for souvenirs, spices, and antiques.
Temple: Free · Maxwell lunch: S$3–6/dish
Evening
Ann Siang Hill or Clarke Quay
Ann Siang Hill is a hidden bar street above Chinatown — relaxed rooftop bars, craft cocktails, colonial shophouse settings, S$15–20/drink. Or take the MRT to Clarke Quay for louder, more vibrant riverside bars along the Singapore River. Sunset at Fort Canning Park (free) above Clarke Quay is a perfect end to the heritage day.
Cocktails: S$15–25 · Fort Canning Park: Free

Singapore 4-Day Itinerary Days 3–4
03
Day Three · Pleasure Island
Sentosa Island
Cable Car → Universal Studios or Beaches → Wings of Time Show → VivoCity Dinner
USS day: S$100–120pp · Beach day: S$30–50pp
Morning
Getting to Sentosa — Cable Car or Monorail
Two options: Cable car from Harbourfront MRT station (S$35 return) for spectacular aerial views of the harbour and Sentosa. OR take the Sentosa Express monorail from VivoCity mall Level 3 (S$4). The cable car is worth it on a clear day — views over the container port and city are superb.
Cable car: S$35 return · Monorail: S$4 return
10 AM
Universal Studios Singapore — OR — Beach Day
USS (S$83/adult): Book online in advance. Highlights: Battlestar Galactica (tallest duelling coasters in the world), Transformers: The Ride (incredible 4D), Puss in Boots' Giant Journey (fun for all ages). Allow minimum 4 hours — 6 hours if you want everything. Arrive at 10am sharp to beat queues on Transformers and Battlestar.
Beach Day (free): Palawan Beach and Siloso Beach are free with sunbeds, swimming, and calm waters. Water sports S$20–40. Adventure Cove Waterpark S$38.
USS: S$83 (online) / S$88 (gate) · Beaches: Free · Adventure Cove: S$38
7:40 PM
Wings of Time — Beach Laser Spectacular
Wings of Time at Siloso Beach is the highlight of Sentosa evenings — a 25-minute outdoor show combining lasers, fire jets, water screens, and pyrotechnics set against the sea. Two shows: 7:40pm and 8:40pm. Watching from the beach with the sea breeze and the spray from the water jets is genuinely spectacular. Book online.
S$18/person (standard) · Book online at sentosa.com.sg
Dinner
VivoCity Food Court — Back on the Mainland
Head back via VivoCity mall (connected to Harbourfront MRT). The food court on Level 3 has great hawker food at reasonable prices — S$8–15 per person. More affordable than Sentosa island eateries and excellent quality. VivoCity itself has a rooftop open-air deck with views back towards Sentosa.
Food court: S$8–15/person
04
Day Four · Departure Day
Orchard, Haji Lane & Jewel Changi
Haji Lane street art → Orchard Road shopping → Sim Lim Square → Jewel Changi waterfall
Approx. spend: S$20–30 (excl. shopping) — mostly free sights
Morning
Haji Lane, Kampong Glam — Street Art + Heritage
Haji Lane is Singapore's hipster heartland — a narrow alley in Kampong Glam lined with street murals, indie boutiques, vintage shops, and cafes. Free to walk. Visit the Sultan Mosque (free, covered dress required) and the Malay Heritage Centre (S$8) to understand the cultural fabric of Malay Singapore. The best coffee in Singapore is around here — try Arabica or Symmetry.
Haji Lane: Free · Malay Heritage Centre: S$8 · Sultan Mosque: Free
10:00 AM
Orchard Road — Shopping Boulevard
Orchard Road is one of Asia's great shopping streets — Ion Orchard, Paragon, Takashimaya, 313@Somerset all line the 2km strip. Luxury brands, mid-range international retail, and food halls in every basement. For electronics, head to Sim Lim Square (take MRT to Little India, then walk) — bargain hard, compare multiple stalls before buying. Duty-free allowance applies at the airport.
Ion Orchard basement food hall: S$8–15 for lunch
Departure
Changi Airport — Jewel + Check-in
Arrive at Changi at least 2.5 hours before your flight. But first — Jewel Changi. The RAIN VORTEX inside Jewel is the world's tallest indoor waterfall (40 metres), surrounded by the HSBC Rain Vortex forest walkways. It's free to enter and genuinely breathtaking. Terminal 3 also has a rooftop swimming pool (transit hotel guests only) if you have a long layover.
Jewel Changi: Free to enter · Rain Vortex: Free
Departure tip: Singapore has strict baggage rules. Liquids over 100ml in carry-on will be confiscated. Durian is banned on the MRT and in most hotels. No chewing gum to bring home either — banned in Singapore since 1992.

Singapore 4-Day Itinerary Essential Tips + Packing
Know Before You Go

Essential Tips for Indian Travellers

🛂
eVisa — Required for Indians
Apply on Singapore ICA website (ica.gov.sg/apply-evisa). Fee: S$30 (approx ₹1,860). Most applications are approved instantly or within hours. Print the approval letter. You cannot board your flight without it. Apply at least 3 days before travel to be safe. Validity: 30 days from arrival.
🚇
MRT is Your Best Friend
Singapore's MRT covers everything you need. Buy an EZ-Link card at Changi Airport (S$12, includes S$7 credit). Top up at any MRT station or 7-Eleven. Grab rides cost 4–5x more than MRT. MRT runs 5:30am–midnight daily. Google Maps has perfect MRT directions — trust it completely.
🍜
Hawker Centres Are the Experience
Maxwell Food Centre, Lau Pa Sat, Newton Food Centre, and Old Airport Road Food Centre are the best. Each has 50–100 stalls selling S$3–8 dishes. This is where Singaporeans eat every day — the food is extraordinary. Chicken rice, char kway teow, laksa, roti prata, BBQ sambal stingray, chilli crab. Try everything.
⚖️
Singapore Laws — Very Real
No chewing gum (banned since 1992 — don't bring it). Jaywalking fine: S$50. Littering fines from S$300. Smoking only in designated areas. Drug offences carry the death penalty. Vandalism (including graffiti) is caned. These are not rumours — Singapore enforces its laws. Just be a respectful visitor and you'll have no issues whatsoever.
📱
Essential Apps
Grab — ride-hailing and food delivery. Far cheaper than metered taxis. SingaRide — MRT journey planning. Google Maps — best for walking and MRT directions. Singtel/StarHub apps — top up your SIM data. Get a Singtel or StarHub SIM at the airport arrivals — S$15 for 100GB / 10 days. Strong 4G everywhere including the MRT.
🔌
Practical Essentials
Power sockets: Type G (same as UK — 3 rectangular pins). Voltage: 230V. Bring a Type G adapter or buy one at Changi. Tap water is fully drinkable everywhere — no need to buy bottled water. Weather: 32°C year-round with high humidity. Afternoon rain showers common — quick and heavy, then clear. Carry a compact umbrella.
💳
Money Tips
Cards accepted at virtually every shop, restaurant, and hawker stall. Use a zero-forex card (Niyo Global, Wise, or Fi Money) to avoid the 3.5% international transaction fee charged by Indian banks. ATMs are everywhere and dispense SGD. Avoid currency exchange counters at the airport — rates are poor. Hawker centres accept PayNow/NETS.
🌡️
Weather + Clothing
Singapore is tropical — 30–33°C year-round with 80% humidity. Wear light, breathable cotton or linen. But: malls, MRT, and restaurants are aggressively air-conditioned — bring a light layer. Smart-casual dress required for rooftop bars (no slippers/flip-flops in nicer venues). Comfortable walking shoes are essential — you will walk 12–15km daily.
