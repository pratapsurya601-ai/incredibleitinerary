Day 1 — Sunrise
🌅 Tiger Hill & Ghoom
Tiger Hill (3 am jeep from hotel, ₹80 entry) is the most famous sunrise viewpoint in the Himalayas — on clear days Kanchenjunga (8,586m, the world's third-highest peak) and even Everest are visible above a sea of clouds. Batasia Loop (where the toy train spirals down through a war memorial garden). Ghoom Monastery — one of the highest Buddhist monasteries in India.
Day 2 — Heritage
🚂 Toy Train & Tea Gardens
The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (DHR), a UNESCO World Heritage railway operating since 1881. The 2-hour tourist joy ride from Darjeeling to Ghoom and back on a steam locomotive is one of India's finest travel experiences. Happy Valley Tea Estate: the second-oldest tea estate in Darjeeling, offering public tours (₹100) with guided explanation of orthodox tea processing.
Day 3 — Nature
🦊 Zoo & Mirik Lake
Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park — the world's only zoo dedicated to high-altitude animals, with the largest captive population of red pandas (the most endangered major mammal in Asia), snow leopards, Tibetan wolves and Himalayan tahr. Then Mirik Lake (45km) with boating, tea garden walks and the Singalila ridge views.
Throughout
🍜 Food & Mall Road
Momos (steamed dumplings): everywhere, ₹60–₹100 for 8. Thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup): ₹80–₹120, the perfect cold-weather meal. Butter tea (suja): an acquired taste of salted, buttered tea — try once. Glenary's Bakery & Cafe on Mall Road: legendary institution since 1935 — croissants, cakes, espresso. Chowrasta (main square) for evening strolling.

"Darjeeling was carved out of the clouds. The Gorkha people, the Tibetan refugees, the British tea planters — all chose this ridge for good reason. The air, even now, tastes different."

Best Time to Visit: March–May (spring): the most famous season — rhododendrons in bloom, clear skies, Kanchenjunga usually visible, temperatures 8–20°C. September–November (autumn): post-monsoon clarity, excellent mountain views, harvest season for second-flush tea. December–February: very cold (0–10°C), possible snow, some roads close — dramatic but challenging. Avoid June–August: monsoon, poor visibility, landslides common.

Day One · Sunrise & Monasteries
Tiger Hill Sunrise, Batasia Loop & Ghoom Monastery
3:30am jeep · Tiger Hill sunrise · Batasia Loop toy train spiral · Ghoom Monastery · Observatory Hill · Mall Road afternoon
💰 Tiger Hill jeep + entry: ₹350 · Ghoom/Batasia: ₹50 · Total morning: ₹500–₹700
3:30 AM
Tiger Hill — The Himalayan Sunrise Viewpoint
Your hotel arranges shared jeeps to Tiger Hill (11km, 30 minutes drive on winding roads, departing 3:30–4am depending on season). Tiger Hill stands at 2,590 metres and on clear days offers views of Kanchenjunga (8,586m — world's third highest peak) and, on exceptionally clear days, Makalu and even Everest (87km distant). The sunrise here — the sky turning from indigo to pink to gold while the frozen peaks glow above a sea of clouds — is one of the great travel experiences in Asia. Clear views are most likely in October–November and March–May. Winter mornings can reach -5°C — dress for extreme cold.
Entry: ₹80 · Shared jeep from hotel: ₹150–₹200 return · Arrive 45 mins before sunrise · Warmest clothes essential
7:00 AM
Batasia Loop — Where the Toy Train Spirals Down
On the return from Tiger Hill, the jeep stops at Batasia Loop — a remarkable piece of railway engineering where the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway track spirals around a War Memorial garden (built for Gorkha soldiers) to lose altitude. On clear post-sunrise mornings, with Kanchenjunga behind and the steam train making its loop below, it is extraordinarily photogenic. The garden is beautifully maintained, with rose beds and the names of Gorkha soldiers who fell in various wars inscribed on the memorial. One of India's most unique railway views.
Entry: ₹30 · Garden is part of most Tiger Hill packages · 30-minute stop
8:30 AM
Ghoom Monastery — One of India's Highest Buddhist Monasteries
Ghoom Monastery (Yiga Choeling Gompa, established 1850) is at 2,438 metres and houses a 4.5-metre statue of Maitreya (the future Buddha) and a library of rare Tibetan manuscripts. The monastery belongs to the Yellow Hat sect (Gelugpa) — the same sect as the Dalai Lama. Morning prayers begin at 6am. The interior is richly painted with traditional thangka paintings. Monks study here from as young as 8 years old. Respectful visitors are welcomed. Remove shoes at the entrance. Photography: ask permission before photographing monks.
Entry: free (donations appreciated) · Ghoom is 8km from Darjeeling on the toy train route · Open all day
Afternoon
Observatory Hill & Mall Road — The Town's Soul
Observatory Hill is the ridge at the heart of old Darjeeling, covered with prayer flags and containing the Mahakal Temple (sacred to both Hindus and Buddhists). The views from here over the town and towards Kanchenjunga are excellent. Chowrasta (the main Mall Road square) is the social centre — horse rides for children, musicians, food stalls. Glenary's Bakery at the top of Mall Road has been serving the best coffee and pastries in Darjeeling since 1935. Sit on the terrace with a coffee and the mountain views.
Observatory Hill: free · Glenary's coffee: ₹120 · Pastries: ₹80–₹150 · Horse ride: ₹200 for 15 minutes
02
Day Two · Heritage & Tea
Toy Train Joyride, Happy Valley Tea Estate & Red Panda Zoo
Morning toy train steam joyride · Happy Valley Tea Estate tour · Padmaja Naidu Zoo afternoon · Mall Road evening
💰 Toy train: ₹1,500 · Tea estate: ₹100 · Zoo: ₹100 · Total day: ₹1,800–₹2,500
9:00 AM
Darjeeling Himalayan Railway — UNESCO Steam Toy Train
The DHR (nicknamed "toy train" for its 2-foot narrow gauge) has been running since 1881 and was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999. The tourist joy ride (Darjeeling station to Ghoom and back, 2 hours) uses a vintage steam locomotive built between 1889–1927 — still in daily operation. The ride winds through tea estates, passes through the town's streets at walking pace (passengers can walk alongside), and crosses remarkable viaducts. Book at Darjeeling station on arrival day — the 9am and 11am departures are the most popular. Book early; tourist seats sell out in peak season.
Tourist joyride: ₹1,500 · Book at station counter or irctc.co.in · 2 hours return · Bring a warm layer
12:00 PM
Happy Valley Tea Estate — The Second-Oldest in Darjeeling
Established in 1854, Happy Valley is one of Darjeeling's most accessible tea estates for visitors (2km from town centre, walkable). The estate produces Darjeeling first flush (the most prized, harvested March–April), second flush (May–June, muscatel character) and autumn flush teas. The 1-hour guided tour (₹100) takes you through the plucking fields, then the factory: withering, rolling, oxidising, drying and grading. The guide explains why Darjeeling first flush commands prices of ₹5,000–₹20,000/kg in Japan. End with a tasting — the difference between grades is astonishing. Buy directly from the estate; it's cheaper than the shops in town.
Tour: ₹100 + tea tasting · Estate shop: 100g first flush ₹200–₹800 · Walkable from town (2km) · Open 8am–4pm
2:30 PM
Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoo — Home of the Red Panda
At 2,134 metres, this is one of the world's highest zoos and the only one specifically dedicated to high-altitude Himalayan species. The star attraction is the red panda (Ailurus fulgens) — a rust-orange, raccoon-sized mammal that is more closely related to weasels than bears or giant pandas, and is endangered with under 10,000 remaining in the wild. The Darjeeling zoo has the world's largest captive red panda population and the best captive breeding programme. Also here: snow leopard, Tibetan wolf, Himalayan black bear, red fox, yak and the extraordinary Himalayan tahr. Allow 2 hours.
Entry: ₹100 adults · Camera: ₹100 · Open 8:30am–4:30pm (closed Thursday) · 2km from town · Good walking shoes needed

Day Three · Lakes & Tea Walks
Mirik Lake, Tea Garden Walk & Rock Garden
Drive to Mirik Lake (45km) · Tea garden walk along Mirik ridge · Rock Garden & Ganga Maya Park · Return
💰 Mirik taxi return: ₹1,000–₹1,500 · Boating: ₹150 · Total day: ₹1,500–₹2,500
9:00 AM
Mirik Lake — Himalayan Lake with Tea Estate Views
Mirik (1,495m) is a smaller hill station 45km south of Darjeeling, centred on Sumendu Lake — a peaceful emerald lake surrounded by orange orchards, cardamom farms and Darjeeling tea estates. Pedal boating and rowing boats (₹150 for 30 minutes). The Sino-Tibetan border of colonial-era Mirik is quieter and less developed than Darjeeling. The drive itself is excellent — the road passes through thick tea gardens and Himalayan forests with views of the Singalila Ridge. An excellent place to buy fresh Darjeeling tea directly from small estate stalls along the road (negotiate — prices are lower than in Darjeeling town).
Taxi return from Darjeeling: ₹1,000–₹1,500 · Boating: ₹150 · Lake entry: free · Allow half day
Midday
Tea Garden Walk — Between the Rows
The tea gardens between Darjeeling and Mirik are accessible for walks — most estates along the main road welcome visitors as long as you are not entering during active plucking without permission. The raised paths between the tea bushes offer views across the valley to the Himalayas above. March–April plucking season is most active — you'll see the women workers (predominantly Nepalese and Lepcha communities) with their wicker baskets, plucking only the top two leaves and a bud — the "golden standard" of Darjeeling quality. Photography is fine; ask permission before photographing people working.
Walking: free · Stick to paths between bushes · Plucking season (Mar–Apr, Jun, Oct–Nov) most active
Afternoon
Rock Garden & Ganga Maya Park
On the return from Mirik, stop at Rock Garden (8km from Darjeeling on the Chunnu Summer Falls road) — a terraced garden built around a stream with stepping stones, waterfalls and excellent views. Popular with Bengali tourists. Ganga Maya Park (near Rock Garden) has a small lake. Entry fee ₹40. Best in the late afternoon when the light is gentle. Combined visit takes 1.5 hours. Return to Darjeeling for a final Mall Road walk and the best momos in town at Kunga Restaurant near the bus stand (8 pieces ₹80, considered among the best in the city).
Rock Garden entry: ₹40 · Ganga Maya: ₹40 · Kunga Restaurant momos: ₹80 · Return to hotel by sunset

How to reach: Train from Kolkata to New Jalpaiguri (NJP) — 8 hours by Darjeeling Mail or Teesta Torsa Express, ₹200–₹1,500 depending on class. From NJP: shared jeep to Darjeeling 3 hours (₹200), or the legendary toy train (Siliguri to Darjeeling, 7 hours, ₹500–₹1,500, advance booking essential). Flight to Bagdogra Airport (90km from Darjeeling), then taxi (₹1,500–₹2,000).

🌡️
Tiger Hill — Clear Views Are Not Guaranteed
The sunrise view of Kanchenjunga from Tiger Hill is among India's most spectacular experiences — but clouds obscure it more often than not. October–November and March–April have the highest clear-day probability. Even without mountain views, the sunrise itself over the cloud sea is beautiful. Check with your hotel the night before — local drivers and hotel staff usually know the morning forecast. Don't skip Tiger Hill even if the forecast is uncertain; conditions change by the hour at altitude.
🍵
Buying Darjeeling Tea
Real Darjeeling tea has the Darjeeling GI (Geographical Indication) logo on the pack — a tea cup with the word "Darjeeling." Without it, it's not authentic. Buy directly from Happy Valley or Makaibari estate shops for the best prices. The "muscatel" character (a grape-musky flavour) is unique to second-flush Darjeeling tea (June flush) — ask for it specifically. 100g first flush: ₹200–₹800. Beware of shops near the toy train station selling "Darjeeling tea" in fancy tins that is actually Assam blended tea.
❄️
Cold Weather — It's Serious
Darjeeling can reach -5°C in December–January, even at the main town level (2,042m). Tiger Hill pre-dawn in winter regularly hits -8°C. Even in March–April, mornings are cold (2–8°C). Bring a proper down jacket, gloves, thermal underlayer and a hat. Hotels provide blankets but they are thin — ask for extra. Altitude: at 2,000m, some visitors experience mild headaches on the first day. Drink plenty of water, avoid alcohol, rest on arrival day.
🚂
Toy Train Booking Tips
The tourist joy ride (₹1,500) is the recommended experience. The full Siliguri–Darjeeling 7-hour journey (₹500–₹1,500) is more authentic but logistically complex for a 3-day trip. Book the joyride at Darjeeling station on your first evening (counter opens 8am) or at irctc.co.in. On busy weekends, the morning rides sell out. The 9am departure is recommended — by then the mist has usually lifted for better views. Bring layers; the open-sided steam train is cold even in summer.
Packing for Darjeeling
🧥 Heavy down jacket — essential year-round at Tiger Hill pre-dawn. In winter, it's non-negotiable.
🧤 Gloves & wool hat — Tiger Hill at 4am in October is bitterly cold. Pack both.
👟 Good walking shoes — Zoo, tea estate and Rock Garden all involve 1–3km walking on uneven paths.
📦 Extra bag for tea — you will buy more tea than you planned. A small folding bag saves you.
🔋 Power bank — cold weather drains phone batteries faster. At -5°C, a phone can die in 20 minutes outdoors.
💧 Hydration tablets — mild altitude dehydration is common on Day 1. Electrolyte sachets help significantly.
🩺 Ibuprofen — altitude headaches respond well to ibuprofen. Acetaminophen is less effective at altitude.
📷 Telephoto lens or zoom phone — Kanchenjunga from Tiger Hill is a long distance view. Maximum zoom makes the difference.
🌧️ Waterproof jacket — Darjeeling receives over 3,000mm of annual rainfall. Even in the dry season, afternoon showers occur.
One last thing: Darjeeling rewards patience. The mountain shows itself on its own schedule. Some visitors never see Kanchenjunga clearly. Others see it on every morning. Accept the uncertainty — and if the clouds part while you are standing at Tiger Hill and the world's third-highest mountain reveals itself in the dawn light, you will understand why people come back every year.
