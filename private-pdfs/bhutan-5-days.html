Region by Region
Paro
Day 1 + Day 5
Gateway to Bhutan. Paro Airport — one of the world's most challenging landings, only 8 certified pilots globally. Paro Dzong, National Museum, ancient cantilever bridge. Tiger's Nest monastery is 3 hours from Paro town on foot.
Thimphu
Days 2–3
Bhutan's capital — only capital in the world with no traffic lights. Buddha Dordenma (51m bronze statue), Folk Heritage Museum, Textile Museum, weekend market. A city that moves at a wonderfully unhurried pace.
Punakha
Day 4
Former capital of Bhutan. Punakha Dzong — the most beautiful fortress in the kingdom — sits at the confluence of two rivers. Suspension bridge (180m), Chimi Lhakhang fertility temple, and the dramatic Dochula Pass en route.
Tiger's Nest (Paro Taktsang)
Day 5
The most iconic image of Bhutan — a monastery clinging to a 3,120m cliff. Three-hour hike return. Built in 1692 at the site where Guru Rinpoche meditated in the 8th century. The most important day of your trip.

Essential: Bhutan SDF & Entry for Indians
🇩🇧 Sustainable Development Fee (SDF) — What Indians Actually Pay
SDF for Indians: $15/night (approx. ₹1,250/night) — NOT the $250/day charged to other nationalities
Visa: Indians do NOT need a visa — a valid Indian passport or voter ID is sufficient
Permit: Apply through your licensed Bhutanese tour operator — they handle the Bhutan permit/clearance entirely
Operator requirement: All Indians must book through a licensed Bhutanese operator (mandatory by law)
Budget package: ₹8,000–12,000/day all-inclusive (accommodation, meals, guide, transport, entry fees)
Mid-range package: ₹15,000–25,000/day all-inclusive through operator
Book ahead: 1–2 months in advance — Bhutan limits tourist numbers and operators have limited capacity
Flights: Only Drukair and Bhutan Airlines fly to Paro (PBH) — from Kolkata, Delhi, Mumbai, Kathmandu
01
Day One · Arrival
Paro — The Kingdom Begins
Land at Paro (PBH) · Rinpung Dzong · National Museum · Paro market dinner
Approx. spend: incl. in operator package · Museum entry ₹600
Arrival
Paro International Airport (PBH) — The World's Most Dramatic Landing
Only 8 pilots in the world are certified to land at Paro. The aircraft weaves between Himalayan peaks before suddenly the runway appears in a narrow valley. Book the window seat on the right side for breathtaking Himalaya views on approach from India. Your operator's representative meets you at the airport. Flights from Kolkata (1hr), Delhi (2.5hrs), Mumbai (3.5hrs), Kathmandu (45min).
Operator representative meets you at airport · All transport included in package
Afternoon
Rinpung Dzong + National Museum
Rinpung Dzong is a 17th-century fortress-monastery perched above the Paro river — a stunning introduction to Bhutanese dzong architecture. Free with your guide. Walk across the ancient covered cantilever bridge at its base. The National Museum sits above the dzong — 10 galleries of thangka paintings, weapons, costumes, and natural history. A compact but excellent introduction to Bhutanese history and culture.
Dzong: Free with guide · National Museum: ₹600
Evening
Paro Town Market + Welcome Dinner
Paro's main market street is relaxed and genuinely Bhutanese. Buy Bhutanese incense, thangka paintings, yak cheese, hand-woven textiles. For dinner: try ema datshi — Bhutan's national dish — a fiery, creamy curry of green chillies and yak cheese. Warn your operator if you cannot handle spice. Red rice is the Bhutanese staple, nutty and slightly pink. Chang (rice wine) available at most restaurants.
Dinner: included in package · Market shopping: budget ₹500–2,000
02
Day Two · Capital of Happiness
Drive to Thimphu + Buddha Dordenma
Paro → Thimphu (1.5hrs) · Buddha Dordenma · Folk Heritage Museum · Weekend Market
Museum entry approx. ₹400 · Chang: ₹100
Morning
Paro → Thimphu Drive (1.5 Hours)
The road follows the Paro Chhu river through pine forests and traditional Bhutanese farmhouses. At 2,300m, Thimphu is one of the world's highest capitals. The drive is beautiful — watch for painted phalluses on farmhouse walls (a traditional fertility blessing practice). Arrive Thimphu by mid-morning. Check in to accommodation — your operator selects based on package tier.
Morning
Buddha Dordenma — Best at 9am
A 51-metre bronze Buddha statue on a hill overlooking the entire Thimphu valley — the largest sitting Buddha in the world at its elevation. The golden statue gleams in morning light against pine forests and blue sky. Inside the statue's base: 125,000 smaller Buddha statues. Free entry. The viewpoint from the Buddha's platform offers one of the finest panoramas in Bhutan. Arrive by 9am for the best light and fewer visitors.
Entry: Free · Best time: 9am sharp for morning light
Afternoon
Folk Heritage Museum + Textile Museum
The Folk Heritage Museum is a traditional Bhutanese farmhouse preserved intact — three floors of authentic rural life, granary, weaving room, kitchen with open fire, sleeping quarters. Guides explain daily life customs that have changed little in centuries (₹400). The Textile Museum next is free and showcases Bhutan's extraordinary weaving traditions — kira (women's dress) and gho (men's robe) take weeks to make by hand. Weekend market (Fri–Sun) near Changlimithang: local produce, handicrafts, incense, dried yak meat, fresh ngalum cheese.
Folk Heritage Museum: ₹400 · Textile Museum: Free · Weekend market: Free entry
Evening
Thimphu Nightlife — Chang & Traditional Music
Thimphu has a small but genuine bar scene — a few establishments around the Clock Tower Square play live traditional Bhutanese music. Chang (rice wine, ₹100 a glass) and ara (distilled grain spirit) are the local drinks. It is modest by any global standard, but there is something deeply charming about hearing traditional instruments in a country that has barely discovered neon signs. End early — Day 3 is full.
Chang: ₹100 · Dinner at local restaurant: ₹300–800 (or included in package)

03
Day Three · Monasteries & Local Life
Thimphu — Dzongs & Deep Culture
Tashichho Dzong · National Memorial Chorten · Simply Bhutan · Hot Stone Bath
Simply Bhutan: ₹1,200 · Hot stone bath: ₹1,500–3,000
Morning
Tashichho Dzong — Seat of Government
The official seat of the Bhutanese government and the throne room of the king. A massive white-and-red dzong rebuilt in 1962 with traditional joinery (no nails), overlooking the Wang Chhu river. The exterior and monastery gardens are accessible; inner administrative quarters are sometimes restricted. Free to visit with your guide. Best photographed in early morning light when monks in red and saffron robes cross the courtyards.
Entry: Free · Best time: Morning for monks' activity
Mid-Morning
National Memorial Chorten — Daily Ritual
A beautiful white stupa standing in the middle of Thimphu, built in 1974 in memory of the third king. Every morning and evening, hundreds of Thimphu residents walk clockwise circles around it — elderly women with prayer wheels, monks, school children, businessmen. Sit on a bench and watch for 20 minutes. This is the authentic pulse of Bhutan, unhurried and profound. One of the most peaceful experiences on this trip.
Entry: Free · Budget 20–30 minutes here
Afternoon
Simply Bhutan Museum — Immersive Cultural Experience
Simply Bhutan is a living-culture museum and hands-on experience: try on traditional Bhutanese dress (kira and gho), shoot a traditional Bhutanese bow (archery is the national sport), taste ara (local grain spirit) and butter tea, see traditional crafts demonstrated live. Staff explain each tradition with warmth and detail. ₹1,200 entry — compulsory admission but genuinely worth every rupee. Allow 1.5 hours.
Entry: ₹1,200 · Duration: 1.5 hours · Archery and dress try-on included
Evening
Hot Stone Bath (Dotsho) — Traditional Bhutanese Wellness
The most authentically Bhutanese wellness ritual — a wooden tub filled with river water, medicinal herbs (artemisia, sichuan pepper bark), and river stones heated red-hot in a fire and rolled in through a channel. The mineral-rich water reaches 38–42°C. Profoundly relaxing after two days of walking and altitude. Many hotels and guesthouses offer this. ₹1,500–3,000 depending on accommodation tier. Book through your operator in advance.
Hot stone bath: ₹1,500–3,000 depending on accommodation · Book ahead
04
Day Four · The Beautiful Valley
Punakha — Bhutan's Most Stunning Dzong
Dochula Pass (3,100m) · Punakha Dzong · Suspension Bridge · Chimi Lhakhang
Punakha Dzong entry: ₹600 · Chimi Lhakhang: Free
Morning
Dochula Pass — 108 Chortens at 3,100m
The 2-hour drive from Thimphu to Punakha crosses the Dochula Pass at 3,100m — on a clear morning, the entire eastern Himalayan range is visible, including peaks over 7,000m. Druk Wangyal Chortens: 108 memorial stupas built in concentric rings, creating a dreamlike landscape. The pass has a small café and monastery. Dress warmly — at 3,100m the temperature is 10°C cooler than Thimphu. If clouds obscure the views, sunrise is the best window.
Dochula Pass: Free · Café tea: ₹80
Mid-Morning
Punakha Dzong — The Most Beautiful Fortress in Bhutan
Built in 1637 at the confluence of the Mo Chhu (Mother River) and Pho Chhu (Father River), Punakha Dzong is widely considered the most beautiful fortress-monastery in Bhutan — and possibly in the Himalayas. White walls, golden roofs, intricate woodwork. Inside: monks' quarters, ancient thangkas, and the preserved body of Zhabdrung (Bhutan's unifying leader). In spring (March), jacaranda trees in the courtyard burst purple. ₹600 entry. Allow 90 minutes.
Entry: ₹600 · Duration: 90 minutes · Best: Morning before tour groups arrive
Afternoon
Punakha Suspension Bridge + Chimi Lhakhang
Punakha Suspension Bridge is Bhutan's longest — 180 metres across the Po Chhu river, draped in prayer flags, a 10-minute walk from the dzong. Free and perfectly walkable. Then: Chimi Lhakhang — the Fertility Temple. A 45-minute walk through mustard fields and rice paddies to a hilltop temple of the "Divine Madman" — Drukpa Kunley, a tantric Buddhist saint celebrated for outrageous behaviour and fertility blessings. Pilgrims come from across Asia for blessings. The walk through rice fields alone is worth it.
Suspension Bridge: Free · Chimi Lhakhang: Free · Walk: 45 min each way
Evening
Return Thimphu or Overnight Punakha
Option A: Return 2 hours to Thimphu for your base accommodation. Option B: Stay overnight in a Punakha guesthouse (budget ₹2,500–5,000, often included in mid-range packages) and wake up to the dzong reflected in the river at dawn — the most magical photograph in Bhutan. Either way: depart Punakha by 7am next morning to return to Paro for Tiger's Nest.
Punakha guesthouse overnight: ₹2,500–5,000 · Or return to Thimphu included in package
05
Day Five · The Main Event
Tiger's Nest — The Hike of a Lifetime
Start by 8am · 3hr hike up · Monastery visit · Kichu Lhakhang · Farewell dinner
Trailhead parking: ₹300 (via operator) · Horse: ₹1,500 return · No entry fee
8:00 AM
Paro Taktsang (Tiger's Nest) — Begin the Hike
Start by 8am sharp — morning light hits the monastery perfectly and you beat the crowds. The trailhead is in a conifer forest. The hike is 2.5–3 hours up (900m elevation gain), 1.5–2 hours down. Moderate difficulty — fit adults with good shoes manage comfortably. Optional: hire a horse to the halfway point café (₹1,500 return) — saves 45 minutes but the walk through the forest is beautiful. The monastery perches impossibly on a 3,120m cliff face, built in 1692 exactly where Guru Rinpoche arrived on a flying tigress in the 8th century.
Horse to halfway: ₹1,500 return · Packed lunch from operator recommended
Halfway
Halfway Cafeteria — Lunch with a View
At the halfway point (approx. 1.5hrs up), there is a cafeteria with outdoor seating directly facing Tiger's Nest across the valley. The view is magnificent — the monastery gleaming white against sheer rock and pine forest. Food is average (basic Bhutanese thali, ₹400–600) but the view is priceless. Take your lunch break here on the ascent or descent. Your legs will thank you. Good for a 20-minute rest either way.
Thali lunch: ₹400–600 · Tea: ₹80
Summit
Tiger's Nest Monastery — The Sacred Space
Remove shoes before entering. No photography inside the monastery — respect this fully. The interior is intimate and intensely sacred: flickering butter lamps, ancient murals, low chanting monks, the smell of juniper incense. Four main temples, each containing significant statues and relics. The cave where Guru Rinpoche meditated is accessible. Standing inside a 330-year-old monastery on a cliff face at 3,120m with the Paro valley 900m below is one of those experiences that stays with you permanently.
Entry: No fee · No photos inside · Shoes off · Modest dress required
Afternoon
Kichu Lhakhang — 7th Century Temple (Optional)
Kichu Lhakhang is one of Bhutan's oldest and most sacred temples, built in the 7th century by Tibetan King Songtsen Gampo — the same king who built Jokhang temple in Lhasa. A peaceful, flat 10-minute walk in Paro valley. Two temples in a garden courtyard, ancient and beautiful, with resident monks. Free entry. A gentle counterpoint to Tiger's Nest's dramatic hike. Then: Paro town farewell dinner — ema datshi one last time. Fly out next morning.
Kichu Lhakhang: Free · Farewell dinner: included in package or ₹400–1,000

Insider Tips
📅
Book Your Operator Early
Bhutan limits tourist numbers and licensed operators have limited capacity. Book 1–2 months ahead for Mar–May and Sep–Nov peak seasons. Last-minute bookings often impossible for these windows.
🏔️
Tiger's Nest: Start Before 8am
Morning light hits the monastery perfectly from 8–10am. Starting early also means beating tour groups. The trail is much more atmospheric in the cool morning air through conifer forest mist.
🧥
Pack Layers — 15°C Swings
Even in summer, Bhutan's temperature swings 15°C between day and night. Paro and Punakha are warmer; Thimphu (2,300m) and Dochula Pass (3,100m) are significantly cooler. Light down jacket essential.
🛍️
No Plastic Bags in Bhutan
Bhutan banned plastic bags in 1999 — one of the first countries in the world to do so. Bring reusable bags for shopping. Also: no tobacco sales in Bhutan (bring your own if needed, duty applies).
📷
Photography Etiquette
Always ask before photographing inside dzongs and temples. No photography inside Tiger's Nest monastery — non-negotiable. Locals are generally happy to be photographed if asked with a smile.
🌶️
Ema Datshi Warning
Bhutan's national dish (chilli cheese curry) is genuinely fiery — made with large green or red Bhutanese chillies. Tell your operator your spice tolerance clearly. Most operators can arrange milder versions.
💧
Altitude — Stay Hydrated
Thimphu sits at 2,300m; Dochula Pass at 3,100m; Tiger's Nest at 3,120m. No acute altitude issues for most people but stay well hydrated, avoid alcohol on Day 1, and carry altitude headache tablets.
💵
Indian Rupees Accepted
Indian rupees (₹) are accepted as legal tender across Bhutan alongside the Bhutanese ngultrum (BTN, pegged 1:1 to INR). You do not need to exchange currency — just bring rupee notes. ₹2,000 notes not always accepted.
Packing List
Good trekking shoes — essential for Tiger's Nest (900m elevation gain)
Warm layers — light down jacket, fleece, thermal base layer
Modest clothing for monasteries — shoulders and knees covered
Rain jacket — showers possible in all seasons in the hills
Altitude headache tablets (Diamox or paracetamol)
Small daypack for Tiger's Nest hike (water, snacks, jacket)
Camera — no drones allowed anywhere in Bhutan
Sunscreen — high altitude UV is intense even on cloudy days
Indian rupees (₹100 and ₹500 notes preferred)
Reusable shopping bags — plastic bags banned in Bhutan
Valid Indian passport or voter ID (no visa needed)
Operator contact and Bhutan permit document (operator provides)
