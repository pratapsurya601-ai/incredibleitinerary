Day 1
🏖️ North Goa — Baga, Anjuna, Vagator
The energy hub of Goa. Baga and Calangute for beach action, Anjuna for the famous flea market (Wednesdays only), Vagator cliffs for the best sunset north of the Mandovi River. Stay in Anjuna or Baga for maximum access.
Day 2
🌊 South Goa — Palolem, Cola, Cabo
Quieter, cleaner, more beautiful. Palolem is Goa's most photogenic beach — a perfect crescent bay with calm water. Cola beach hides a freshwater lagoon separated from the sea by a sandbar. Cabo de Rama fort for history and views.
Day 3
💦 Dudhsagar + Old Goa
India's tallest waterfall accessible by jeep safari through the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary. Return via a spice plantation lunch and the UNESCO churches of Old Goa — Basilica of Bom Jesus houses the remains of St. Francis Xavier.
All Days
🛺 Getting Around
Rent a scooter (₹300–₹400/day) — it's the only way to properly explore Goa. Taxis are overpriced and scarce on app. Use Rapido Bike for short solo trips. Inter-zone travel (North to South) takes 1 hour by road — plan accordingly.

"Goa rewards slow travel and punishes rushing. Three days is enough to see both coasts and the hinterland — if you stop trying to do everything and commit to one zone each day."

Choose Your Goa Experience
💰 Budget Backpacker
₹3,000/day per person
Hostel dorm (₹600–₹900/night), beach shack meals (₹150–₹250/plate), rented scooter for the full trip. Anjuna flea market, Palolem by bus (₹60). Dudhsagar on shared jeep tour (₹600–₹800/person). Real Goa at minimum cost.
💑 Couple Getaway
₹6,000/day per couple
Boutique guesthouse or Airbnb in Anjuna (₹2,000–₹3,500/night), private scooter, candlelit dinner at Thalassa (₹2,000–₹3,000 for two), sunset cocktails at Vagator. Cola beach lagoon swim — one of India's most romantic spots.
🎉 Party Trip
₹8,000/day per person
Stay in Baga or Anjuna for maximum nightlife access. Tito's Lane, LPK Waterfront, Curlies at Anjuna beach. Day 2 at Palolem silent disco (headphone party on the beach, starts 10pm). Budget includes drinks, club entries, late-night food.
🌅 Relaxed Beach
₹12,000/day per person
Resort stay at Palolem or Morjim (₹5,000–₹10,000/night), beach yoga, Ayurvedic massage (₹1,500–₹2,500), private taxi all days, seafood at Fisherman's Wharf or Britto's. Zero rush. Maximum Goa peace.

Goa 3-Day Itinerary Day 1 — North Goa
01
Day One · North Goa
Arrive · Beaches · Anjuna · Vagator Sunset
Baga / Calangute · Anjuna Flea Market (Wed only) · Vagator cliff · Thalassa dinner
💰 Budget: ₹1,200–₹2,000 · Couple: ₹4,000–₹7,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Arrive at Goa Airport (GOI) · Check In · Rent Scooter
Goa International Airport (Dabolim/GOI) is in South Goa — if you're staying in North Goa (recommended for Day 1), pre-book a taxi (₹700–₹1,000) or use Rapido. Check in to your stay in Anjuna or Baga. First task after dropping bags: rent a scooter from any local shop near your guesthouse. Show your driving licence.
Airport taxi: ₹700–₹1,000 · Scooter rental: ₹300–₹400/day (full tank included)
11 AM
Baga & Calangute Beach — North Goa's Main Strip
Calangute is Goa's most popular beach — busy, commercial, great for people-watching. Baga (1km north) is livelier with more beach shacks, water sports and a younger crowd. Try parasailing (₹500–₹800), jet ski (₹800/15 min) or just settle into a shack with a kingfisher and some fish curry rice. Don't leave without trying Sol Kadi — a Goan cooling drink made from kokum and coconut milk.
Water sports: ₹500–₹800 · Fish curry rice at shack: ₹150–₹250
2 PM
Anjuna Flea Market (Wednesdays Only) or Anjuna Beach
If you're here on a Wednesday: the Anjuna Flea Market (8am–6pm) is one of Asia's best — Kashmiri handicrafts, Tibetan jewellery, Rajasthani textiles, spices, vintage clothing. Bargain hard — start at 40% of asking price. Budget ₹500–₹2,000 for shopping. Any other day: Anjuna beach itself is rockier but far less crowded than Baga, with a more bohemian vibe.
Market entry: Free · Shopping budget: ₹500–₹2,000
5:00 PM
Vagator Cliff — Best Sunset in North Goa
Drive north 10 minutes from Anjuna to Vagator. Park at the top, walk to the red laterite cliff overlooking Little Vagator beach. The cliff drops straight into the Arabian Sea. The view south across the entire North Goa coastline is extraordinary at golden hour. Arrive by 5pm for a good spot — it gets crowded at peak season.
Free · Sunset: 6:00–6:30pm (Oct–Feb)
8 PM
Dinner at Thalassa (Couple/Splurge) or Anjuna Beach Shacks (Budget)
Thalassa (Siolim, 15 min from Vagator): Goa's finest Greek-Mediterranean restaurant, open-air with sea views. Grilled seafood, wood-fired pizza, Greek mezze. Reservation essential (call ahead). Budget: ₹2,000–₹3,500 for two. Budget option: Stay at Anjuna beach shacks — prawn masala (₹250), grilled pomfret (₹350), cold beer (₹120). Sit with your feet in the sand.
Thalassa: ₹2,000–₹3,500 for two · Beach shack: ₹400–₹700 for two
Where to stay in North Goa: Anjuna > Vagator > Baga — in that order. Anjuna gives you access to the flea market, quieter beach and the best restaurants. Baga is louder but closer to the main beach strip. Avoid Calangute for staying — noisy, overpriced, worse value than Anjuna.
Party tip: Night life doesn't start until 11pm in Goa. LPK Waterfront and Curlies (Anjuna) are the go-to venues. Entry is usually free for women, ₹500–₹1,000 for men (includes drinks). Taxis back at 2am are hard — arrange return with your driver beforehand.

Goa 3-Day Itinerary Day 2 — South Goa
02
Day Two · South Goa
Palolem, Cola Lagoon & Cabo de Rama Fort
Colva · Benaulim · Cola hidden beach · Palolem crescent · Cabo de Rama at sunset
💰 Budget: ₹1,000–₹1,800 · Couple: ₹3,500–₹6,000 (excl. accommodation)
8:00 AM
Drive South — North to South Goa is 1 Hour
Leave by 8am on your scooter (or hire a taxi for the day, ₹2,500–₹3,500). The Zuari bridge crossing is the dividing line. South Goa feels immediately different — narrower roads, taller palms, far fewer tourists, longer emptier beaches. First stop: Colva or Benaulim for a quick break and breakfast at a local bakery (Goan pão bread with butter and chai, ₹30–₹50).
Scooter fuel for the day: ₹100–₹150 · Day taxi: ₹2,500–₹3,500
10 AM
Cola Beach — Goa's Best Kept Secret
This is the one most visitors miss. Cola (also written Khola) is a hidden cove 20km north of Palolem. The beach has a freshwater lagoon — a large lake separated from the Arabian Sea by a narrow sandbar. You can swim in fresh water, walk across the sandbar, and dive into the sea — all in one spot. Access via a rough track (10 min walk from the parking area). No crowds, no development, just nature.
Free · Bring your own water and snacks · No facilities on beach
1:00 PM
Palolem Beach — Goa's Most Beautiful
A perfect crescent-shaped bay with calm, clear water — the most photogenic beach in Goa. The southern end curves into a small island (Canacona Island) connected at low tide. The beach has the best-quality shack restaurants in Goa. Order the grilled kingfish thali (₹250), fresh prawn curry (₹350) or a lobster if budget allows (₹800–₹1,500 depending on size). The water is calm and safe for swimming — far better than North Goa's rough surf.
Shack lunch: ₹250–₹600 · Sun lounger: ₹100–₹200/day
4:00 PM
Cabo de Rama Fort — Colonial History on the Cliff
A 16th-century Portuguese fort 20km north of Palolem, perched on a 150m cliff above the Arabian Sea. Named after the Hindu epic Ramayana — legend says Ram and Sita sheltered here during exile. The fort is largely ruined but the setting is extraordinary: a 180-degree view of the South Goa coastline and sea. Arrive at 4:30pm — the golden light makes it even more dramatic. Free entry, almost no tourists.
Entry: Free · 30 min from Palolem by scooter
Evening
Seafood Dinner at Palolem Beach Shacks
Return to Palolem for dinner as the sun sets. The beach shacks light up with lanterns after dark. Magic of Palolem is best experienced at night — string lights, the sound of the sea, fresh Goan seafood. Try: Dropadi, German Bakery (quirky but excellent), or simply whichever shack you ate lunch at. The silent disco on the beach (headphone party, ₹300–₹500 entry) runs from around 10pm on weekends.
Dinner: ₹400–₹800/person · Silent disco entry: ₹300–₹500
Stay in Palolem tonight if you plan to do Dudhsagar on Day 3 — Dudhsagar jeep safaris depart from Kulem/Colem village, 1.5 hrs from Palolem. Book your jeep safari the evening before (ask your guesthouse), leave by 5:30am. This makes the logistics seamless.
⚠️ Benaulim warning: Benaulim and Colva are often recommended in older guides. They're now overdeveloped and overpriced — skip them for anything beyond a quick breakfast stop. Palolem and Cola are vastly superior alternatives right now.

Goa 3-Day Itinerary Day 3 — Hinterland
03
Day Three · Hinterland & Departure
Dudhsagar Waterfall, Spice Plantation & Old Goa
6am jeep start · Dudhsagar · Spice lunch · Basilica of Bom Jesus · Evening flight
💰 Budget: ₹1,500–₹2,500 · Couple: ₹3,500–₹5,000 (excl. accommodation)
5:30 AM
Depart for Dudhsagar Jeep Safari (Must Book Ahead)
Leave your accommodation by 5:30am — the jeep safaris depart from Kulem/Colem village between 6:00–7:00am. The jeep takes you through the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary over rough forest tracks (45 min each way) to the base of Dudhsagar. Jeeps carry 6–8 people. Book through your guesthouse or a Goa travel agent the evening before — do NOT just turn up, especially Oct–Feb when spots fill fast. The forest route itself is spectacular — dense Western Ghats jungle, rivers to cross, possible wildlife sightings.
Jeep safari: ₹2,800/jeep (shared among 6–8 people = ₹400–₹500/person) · Private jeep: ₹3,500–₹4,500
8:00 AM
Dudhsagar Waterfall — India's Fifth Tallest
At 310 metres, Dudhsagar ("Sea of Milk") is one of India's most dramatic waterfalls. The water cascades in multiple tiers down a black rock face into a turquoise pool. You can swim in the pool at the base — the water is cold and clear even in December. The view looking up is extraordinary: the Goa-Karnataka railway viaduct crosses above the waterfall and trains occasionally pass during your visit. Morning light (8–10am) is optimal — the falls are in shadow by midday.
Sanctuary entry fee: ₹300/Indian · ₹1,500/foreign national · Swimming: included
11 AM
Spice Plantation Lunch — Savoi or Tropical
On your way back from Dudhsagar, stop at one of Goa's spice plantations in Ponda (Savoi Plantation, Tropical Spice Plantation). A 45-minute guided walk shows you cardamom, nutmeg, vanilla, pepper, areca palms and cashew trees growing together — the original polyculture farming system. Lunch included: a Goan thali with rice, fish curry, sol kadi, kokum sherbet — as many servings as you want. One of the best meal experiences in Goa and often overlooked.
Entry + tour + lunch: ₹600–₹800/person · Well worth the cost
2:00 PM
Old Goa — Basilica of Bom Jesus (UNESCO)
Old Goa (Velha Goa) was the Portuguese capital — at its peak in the 1600s it was larger than Lisbon. The Basilica of Bom Jesus (1605) houses the relics of St. Francis Xavier — the mummified body of the Jesuit missionary who brought Christianity to Asia. The 17th-century baroque architecture is extraordinary; the gilded interior unlike anything else in India. Also visit Se Cathedral (the largest church in Asia) next door — free, 20 minutes from the basilica.
Entry: Free · Open 9am–6pm · 30 min from Panjim / 45 min from airport
Evening
Head to Goa Airport (GOI) for Evening Flight
Old Goa to airport is 30–40 minutes. Pre-book your taxi the night before (₹500–₹700 from Old Goa). Rapido works well from Old Goa too. Allow 2 hours before departure — Goa airport gets heavily congested on evenings in peak season (Dec–Jan). If your flight is early next morning, Panjim city (30 min from airport) has good affordable hotels and excellent seafood restaurants along the Mandovi river promenade for a last dinner.
Taxi to airport: ₹500–₹700 · Rapido: ₹200–₹350
Dudhsagar is seasonal: The waterfall runs best October through January — full-flow after monsoon, still powerful through December. By February–March the flow reduces significantly. The jeep trail is closed during monsoon season (June–September) entirely due to flooding. Book your trip between October and January for the best experience.

5 Pro Tips — What Most Goa Guides Won't Tell You
🛺
Rent a scooter — ₹300/day changes everything
Taxis in Goa are expensive (₹300–₹600 per short trip) and hard to find at night. A rented scooter (₹300–₹400/day, fuel included) gives you total freedom. Valid Indian driving licence required. Most guesthouses can arrange one in 15 minutes. Wear a helmet — checkpoints are common.
📱
Use Rapido instead of OLA/Uber
Ola and Uber are largely non-functional in Goa (local taxi lobby keeps them out). Rapido Bike works well for solo travellers for short hops (₹30–₹100 per trip). For longer journeys, negotiate with local taxis directly — ask your guesthouse for recommended drivers to avoid tourist pricing.
🦐
Best beach shacks — locals' list
North: Curlies (Anjuna), Britto's (Baga), A Reverie (Calangute). South: Dropadi (Palolem), Cafe Inn (Palolem), Home (Agonda). Avoid shacks with laminated menus with photos — always a red flag. The best ones have handwritten daily specials based on the morning's catch.
📅
Avoid Dec 20 – Jan 5 (peak madness)
Christmas-New Year week transforms Goa: prices triple, beaches get dangerously crowded, water quality drops, scams increase. October–November (post-monsoon, lush green Goa) or January 6–February is the sweet spot — same sun, fraction of the crowds, 50–60% lower accommodation costs.
💦
Book Dudhsagar the evening before
The forest department limits the number of jeeps entering the sanctuary per day. Spots fill by 8pm the evening before. Book through your guesthouse or call a local travel agent — not an online aggregator (they add ₹500–₹1,000 and don't actually guarantee your spot). Confirm the meeting point and time clearly.
🌊
North vs South — know before you go
North Goa = parties, flea market, more tourists, rougher sea, better nightlife. South Goa = quieter, cleaner water (safe swimming), fewer crowds, better beaches for couples and families. The two zones are genuinely different — this guide covers both. Pick your base based on your travel style, not just what's trending on Instagram.
