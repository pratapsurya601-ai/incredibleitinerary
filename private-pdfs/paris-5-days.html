Day 1
🗼 Eiffel Tower & Seine
The Iron Lady — constructed for the 1889 World Fair and now the world's most visited monument. Book tickets online (summit ₹3,000/person) to skip queues. The Seine river cruise at dusk with the tower glittering is unmatched. End on Champs-Élysées, the grand avenue.
Day 2
🖼️ Louvre & Notre Dame
The world's largest art museum with 35,000 works. Arrive at 9am on weekdays — Mona Lisa crowds peak after 11am. Book skip-the-line tickets online (₹2,200). Notre Dame Cathedral, under restoration after the 2019 fire, reopened in December 2024 — check access times before visiting.
Day 3
🎨 Montmartre & Orsay
The hilltop village where Picasso, Monet and Renoir lived and painted. Sacré-Cœur basilica at sunrise offers the best view over all Paris. Then descend to the Musée d'Orsay — the world's finest Impressionist collection including Van Gogh, Monet and Renoir originals.
Day 4
🏰 Palace of Versailles
Louis XIV's extraordinary palace — 700 rooms, the Hall of Mirrors (357 mirrors, 30,000 candles), and 800 hectares of formal gardens. Take RER C from Paris (45 min, ₹400 return). Book palace entry online (₹3,500) to skip the often 2-hour queue at the gate.

"To know Paris is to know a great deal." — Henry Miller. Come for the art, stay for the baguettes, leave with a lifetime of memories and an irrational desire to return immediately.

Visa for Indian Travellers: Schengen visa required — apply at the French Consulate (VFS Global) at least 3–4 weeks in advance. Fee: approximately ₹7,000 + VFS service charges. Required documents: bank statement (3 months), hotel bookings, travel insurance (minimum €30,000 cover), ITR, leave letter. Apply in person at the VFS centre in your city. Multiple-entry Schengen visas are sometimes granted — request one if you plan future European travel.
Flights from India: Delhi/Mumbai to Paris CDG — ₹45,000–₹80,000 return. Air France flies direct from Delhi (~8.5 hrs). Connecting options via Dubai/Abu Dhabi (Emirates, Etihad) or via Istanbul (Turkish Airlines) can be cheaper. Book 2–3 months ahead for best prices.

Paris 5-Day Itinerary Day 1–2 — Eiffel Tower, Louvre & Notre Dame
01
Day One · Arrival & Iconic Paris
The Eiffel Tower, Seine & Champs-Élysées
Arrive CDG · RER B to city · Eiffel Tower summit · Bateaux Mouches Seine cruise · Champs-Élysées evening walk
💰 Budget: ₹3,500–₹5,000 · Mid: ₹8,000–₹12,000 (excl. accommodation)
Arrival
Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) — Getting to the City
Take RER B train from CDG Terminal 2 directly to central Paris (Châtelet-Les Halles or Saint-Michel). Journey: 45 minutes, ₹400. A taxi costs approximately ₹3,000–₹4,500 — only worth it if you have heavy bags. The RER B is safe, fast and used by Parisians daily. Buy tickets at the station machines (select "Paris zone 1-5"). Check into your hotel and freshen up — most hotels allow early luggage storage if your room isn't ready.
RER B train: ₹400 · Taxi: ₹3,000–₹4,500 · Budget hotels: ₹4,000–₹7,000/night · Mid: ₹9,000–₹16,000
2:00 PM
Eiffel Tower — Book Online, Avoid the Queue
The symbol of France and one of the most recognised structures on earth. Book tickets at tour-eiffel.fr at least 2 weeks ahead — summit tickets (₹3,000/person) are the best experience. The 2nd floor (₹1,800) has the famous glass floor and is less crowded. Arrive exactly at your booked time — late arrivals are turned away. The tower is most beautiful at dusk when the city turns golden, and at night when the sparkle lights run for 5 minutes every hour from dusk to 1am.
Summit: ₹3,000/person · 2nd floor: ₹1,800 · Gardens (ground): free · Book online — sells out weeks ahead
6:00 PM
Bateaux Mouches Seine River Cruise — Paris from the Water
A 1-hour cruise along the Seine passing Notre Dame, the Louvre, Musée d'Orsay and the Eiffel Tower from the water. Bateaux Mouches departs from Pont de l'Alma (near the Eiffel Tower). The evening cruise with the city lit up is genuinely magical. Bring a jacket — it's cold on the water. Book online: ₹1,800/person. Alternatively, hop-on-hop-off Batobus day passes let you use the boat as transport (₹3,500 for full day).
Bateaux Mouches: ₹1,800/person · Evening cruise is more atmospheric · Book at bateaux-mouches.fr
Evening
Champs-Élysées — The Most Famous Avenue in the World
Walk the 1.9km boulevard from Place de la Concorde to the Arc de Triomphe. The arc itself is worth climbing (₹1,200 — fantastic 360° city views). The avenue is lined with flagship stores — Louis Vuitton, Cartier, Ladurée (the famous pink macaron shop — get a box, worth every rupee). Evening is best — the avenue is lit and Parisians are out. Dinner options on side streets: brasseries serving steak-frites, French onion soup, crêpes.
Arc de Triomphe summit: ₹1,200 · Laduree macarons: ₹400/box · Brasserie dinner: ₹2,500–₹5,000
02
Day Two · Art & History
The Louvre, Tuileries & Sainte-Chapelle
9am Louvre opening · Mona Lisa · Tuileries Garden stroll · Notre Dame exterior · Sainte-Chapelle stained glass
💰 Louvre: ₹2,200 · Sainte-Chapelle: ₹1,500 · Metro pass: ₹250/day
9:00 AM
Louvre Museum — Arrive at Opening, Head Straight to Mona Lisa
The world's most visited museum holds 35,000 works across 3 wings. Buy skip-the-line tickets online (louvre.fr) — ₹2,200. Arrive at 9am (Wednesdays and Fridays the museum stays open until 9:45pm — slightly less crowded). The Mona Lisa is in the Denon Wing, Salle 711. It's smaller than you expect (77cm × 53cm) but the room and the experience are extraordinary. Also: Venus de Milo, Winged Victory of Samothrace, Vermeer's The Lacemaker. Allow 3–4 hours minimum.
Entry: ₹2,200 (online, includes same-day re-entry) · Free for under-18 · Closed Tuesdays
1:00 PM
Tuileries Garden & Lunch — Paris in the Open Air
Exit the Louvre into the Tuileries Garden — the formal French garden between the Louvre and Place de la Concorde, free to walk through. Grab a crêpe or sandwich from one of the garden kiosks (₹400–₹700) and eat on a chair by the central pond. This is what Parisians do on lunch breaks. The garden connects directly to the Orangerie museum (Day 5) and offers views down to the Eiffel Tower on a clear day.
Garden: free · Crêpe/sandwich: ₹400–₹700 · Sit on the iconic green metal chairs
3:00 PM
Notre Dame Cathedral — Paris's Heart, Reopened 2024
Notre Dame Cathedral, devastated by fire in April 2019, completed a miraculous restoration and reopened in December 2024 — more beautiful than ever. Queue to enter (free, timed entry slots available online). The Gothic interior — the rose windows, flying buttresses, the nave — is astonishing. Even if interior access is limited, the exterior on the Île de la Cité island is one of the great architectural experiences in Europe. Walk to the back (apse) for the best view of the flying buttresses.
Entry: free · Timed entry booking recommended · Tower access: ₹1,200 when available
5:00 PM
Sainte-Chapelle — The Most Beautiful Gothic Chapel in the World
Five minutes' walk from Notre Dame on the Île de la Cité, Sainte-Chapelle is arguably the most beautiful interior in Paris — some say in Europe. Built in the 1240s for King Louis IX to house Christ's Crown of Thorns, its upper chapel is entirely walls of stained glass — 1,113 individual panes, 15 metres tall. On a sunny afternoon, the interior glows like a jewel box. Worth every rupee. Book online to skip the security queue.
Entry: ₹1,500 · Buy online at sainte-chapelle.fr · Combined ticket with Conciergerie: ₹2,000

Paris 5-Day Itinerary Day 3–5 — Montmartre, Versailles & Farewell
03
Day Three · Artistic Heart of Paris
Montmartre, Sacré-Cœur & Musée d'Orsay
Morning Montmartre village · Sacré-Cœur views · Artist squares · Musée d'Orsay Impressionists · Saint-Germain evening
💰 Orsay: ₹2,000 · Sacré-Cœur free · Metro: ₹250/day Navigo pass
9:00 AM
Montmartre — The Artist Village on the Hill
Take Metro line 2 to Abbesses station — the deepest station in Paris, with a beautiful Art Nouveau entrance. Montmartre is the hill where Renoir, Monet, Picasso, Toulouse-Lautrec and Van Gogh all lived and painted. The neighbourhood retains its village character — cobbled lanes, ivy-covered walls, tiny bakeries. Wander without a map. Le Consulat café (one of Paris's most photographed facades) is on Rue Norvins. The windmill Moulin de la Galette featured in Renoir's famous painting.
Metro: ₹250/day Navigo pass · Croissant breakfast at local boulangerie: ₹150
10:00 AM
Sacré-Cœur Basilica — Paris at Your Feet
The white Romano-Byzantine basilica at the top of Montmartre hill is free to enter. Climb the dome for the city panorama (₹700) or simply sit on the front steps — the view over all of Paris is extraordinary, especially in morning light. The interior has perpetual adoration (prayer in progress 24 hours a day). Place du Tertre next door is the famous artist square — portrait painters, caricaturists, oil paintings. Charming and touristy in equal measure.
Basilica: free · Dome: ₹700 · Place du Tertre portrait: ₹1,500–₹3,000
2:00 PM
Musée d'Orsay — The World's Greatest Impressionist Collection
Housed in a converted Beaux-Arts railway station on the Seine, the Orsay contains the finest collection of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art in the world — more Van Gogh than any museum outside the Netherlands, Monet's cathedrals and haystacks, Renoir's Bal du moulin de la Galette, Seurat's pointillism, Degas's dancers. Arrive before 2pm to avoid afternoon tour groups. Book online (₹2,000) and enter via the right-side pre-booked entrance. Allow 2–3 hours.
Entry: ₹2,000 · Book at musee-orsay.fr · Closed Mondays · Top floor café has Seine views
Evening
Saint-Germain-des-Prés — Left Bank Literary Paris
The Left Bank neighbourhood of Sartre, Hemingway, Simone de Beauvoir and James Baldwin. Café de Flore and Les Deux Magots are the legendary literary cafés — expensive (€8/coffee) but historic. Walk along Boulevard Saint-Germain and Rue de Buci market street. Browse Shakespeare and Company bookshop (on the Right Bank near Notre Dame — the most famous English-language bookshop in the world, free to browse). Dinner in Saint-Germain: French bistro with duck confit or bouillabaisse.
Café de Flore coffee: ₹700 · Bistro dinner: ₹3,000–₹6,000 · Shakespeare & Co: free entry
04
Day Four · Royal Excess
Palace of Versailles — The Sun King's Dream
RER C to Versailles · Hall of Mirrors · Grand Trianon · Marie Antoinette's Estate · Gardens
💰 Palace entry: ₹3,500 · RER C return: ₹400 · Gardens: free (fountain shows extra)
8:30 AM
RER C to Versailles — Leave Paris Early to Beat Crowds
Take RER C from any central Paris station to Versailles-Château-Rive Gauche (45 minutes, ₹400 return). BUY YOUR PALACE TICKET ONLINE BEFORE LEAVING PARIS — the ticket queue at the gate regularly reaches 2 hours in peak season. Without a pre-booked ticket, you may not get in. Book at chateauversailles.fr. Arrive at the palace by 9:30am when it opens. The palace gets very crowded after 11am. Wear comfortable shoes — the gardens alone cover 800 hectares.
Palace entry: ₹3,500 · RER C return ticket: ₹400 · Book palace tickets at chateauversailles.fr
10:00 AM
Hall of Mirrors & Royal Apartments — 700 Rooms, One King
The Hall of Mirrors is the centrepiece — 73 metres long, 357 mirrors reflecting 17 large windows overlooking the gardens. Built to intimidate visiting heads of state and reflect Louis XIV's absolute power. The King's Grand Apartments have painted ceilings, marble floors and furniture that was melted down to fund wars. The Queen's apartments where Marie Antoinette lived until the Revolution. The Versailles audio guide (₹500, available at palace) is excellent — use it.
Included in palace ticket · Audio guide: ₹500 · Photography allowed inside (no flash)
Afternoon
Gardens, Grand Trianon & Marie Antoinette's Estate
The formal French gardens (free to walk in) with perfectly geometric parterres, fountains and the Grand Canal are stunning. Rent a rowboat on the Grand Canal (₹1,500/hour) for a memorable experience. The Grand Trianon was Louis XIV's private escape from palace protocol — beautifully proportioned. The Petit Trianon was Marie Antoinette's private retreat, and her Hameau de la Reine (her fake peasant village where she played at rustic life) is adjacent. The fountain shows (weekends, ₹1,200 extra) are worth seeing if visiting on a Saturday or Sunday.
Gardens: free · Trianon domain: ₹1,500 · Rowboat: ₹1,500/hr · Fountain shows (Sat/Sun): ₹1,200
05
Day Five · Final Paris Moments
Orangerie, Le Marais & Farewell Dinner
Musée de l'Orangerie (Monet's Water Lilies) · Le Marais neighbourhood · Picasso Museum · Farewell dinner
💰 Orangerie: ₹1,600 · Le Marais shopping: budget as desired · Final dinner: ₹3,000–₹8,000
9:30 AM
Musée de l'Orangerie — Monet's Water Lilies
One of the most moving art experiences in the world. Claude Monet painted his monumental Water Lilies series in the last years of his life, nearly blind, and donated them to France on one condition — that they be displayed in oval rooms with natural light. The Orangerie in the Tuileries Garden was built specifically for these paintings. The two oval rooms contain eight enormous canvases — you sit in the centre and are completely surrounded. Arrive when it opens (9:30am) for a meditative experience before the tour groups arrive.
Entry: ₹1,600 · Buy online at musee-orangerie.fr · Closed Tuesdays · Combined ticket with Orsay available
Noon
Le Marais — Medieval Streets, Falafel & High Fashion
The historic Jewish quarter turned art gallery district turned trendy neighbourhood. The best shopping in Paris for independent designers, vintage clothing and concept stores. Place des Vosges — the oldest planned square in Paris, built in 1612, with red brick arcades — is the most beautiful square in the city. Stop at L'As du Fallafel on Rue des Rosiers for the best falafel sandwich in Paris (₹500, cash only, queue outside). The Picasso Museum (₹1,800) is here — worth a visit if you have time.
Picasso Museum: ₹1,800 · Place des Vosges: free · Best falafel: L'As du Fallafel ₹500
Evening
Farewell Dinner — Paris Deserves a Proper Last Meal
For a true Paris evening: book a table at a traditional Parisian bistro serving set menus (formule — starter, main, dessert) for ₹3,000–₹5,000. Order the house wine (carafe). Begin with French onion soup (soupe à l'oignon) or escargots if you're adventurous. Main: duck confit (confit de canard), steak-frites, or sole meunière. Dessert: crème brûlée or tarte tatin. If budget allows, a final evening Seine promenade to watch the Eiffel Tower sparkle at 10pm is the perfect Paris farewell.
Set menu bistro dinner: ₹3,000–₹5,000 · Wine (carafe): ₹800–₹1,500 · Seine promenade: free

Insider Tips — Read Before You Go
🎫
Book Everything Online — Paris Rewards the Prepared
Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Versailles, Sainte-Chapelle, Orsay — all sell out. Book 2–4 weeks ahead. On the 1st Sunday of each month, national museums (Louvre, Orsay, Orangerie) are FREE — still book a free time slot online.
🚇
Navigo Easy Pass — Your Paris Transport Card
Buy a Navigo Easy card at any Metro station (₹150 card deposit) and load t+ tickets (₹250 each) or a weekly Navigo pass (₹2,500, valid Mon–Sun). The Metro goes virtually everywhere in central Paris. Walk when you can — central Paris is compact and beautiful on foot.
🥐
Eat at a Boulangerie — Paris's Secret Weapon
The Parisian boulangerie (bakery) offers breakfast and lunch at a fraction of café prices: croissant ₹120, baguette sandwich ₹350, pain au chocolat ₹150. Look for "Boulangerie" signs — they're everywhere. This is how Parisians eat every day. The best croissant you will ever eat costs less than ₹200.
💳
Cards Everywhere — Carry Some Cash Too
Paris is largely cashless — restaurants, shops and museums accept Visa/Mastercard. But keep €50–100 cash for small cafés, street markets and tips. Use a zero-forex card (Niyo, ICICI Sapphiro) to avoid conversion fees. Inform your bank about international travel before departing India.
🌡️
Paris Weather — Layer Up
April–June: 12–22°C, occasional rain. July–August: 20–30°C, hot and crowded. September–October: 12–20°C, beautiful golden light, fewer tourists. Bring a light jacket at all times of year — Paris evenings are cool. A packable rain jacket is essential April–May.
🛡️
Pickpockets — Stay Alert in Tourist Areas
Paris has persistent pickpocket issues around Eiffel Tower, Louvre entrance, Metro Line 1, and Montmartre steps. Keep your bag in front of your body. Never leave a bag on the back of a chair in restaurants. The "petition" scam near Sacré-Cœur: decline firmly and walk away without engaging.
🗣️
Learn 5 French Phrases
Parisians respond dramatically better when you attempt French: Bonjour (good day — ALWAYS say this when entering a shop), Merci (thank you), S'il vous plaît (please), Excusez-moi (excuse me), Parlez-vous anglais? (do you speak English?). Starting with Bonjour before any request is non-negotiable etiquette.
🏥
Travel Insurance — Mandatory for Schengen
Schengen visa requires proof of travel insurance with minimum €30,000 medical cover. Buy from Indian providers (Tata AIG, Bajaj Allianz) for ₹1,500–₹2,500 for 5–7 days. Keep hard copy of the policy document with your passport throughout Europe.
Packing for Paris
👗 Smart casual clothing — Paris is stylish. Avoid overtly touristy outfits. A simple outfit + scarf = instant chic.
👟 Comfortable walking shoes — you will walk 12–18km daily on cobblestones. Break them in before you go.
🌂 Compact umbrella — Paris rain is light but frequent April–May. A fold-flat umbrella lives in your bag.
🔌 EU Type C adapter — French plugs are Type E/F round 2-pin. One universal adapter covers all Europe.
📱 Google Maps offline — download Paris offline before leaving India. WiFi is available in most cafés.
💊 Prescription medicines — carry extra supply. French pharmacies (green cross) are excellent but prescriptions are needed.
📋 Printed bookings — Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Versailles, and your hotel confirmations printed. Some attractions require print confirmation.
💶 €50–100 cash — for small cafés, markets, tips. Withdraw from airport ATM on arrival rather than exchanging in India.
🌐 International SIM or eSIM — Airtel/Jio international plan or Airalo eSIM for Europe. Data is essential for navigation.
One last thing: Paris is best experienced slowly. Don't try to see everything. Sit in a café for an hour. Get lost in an arrondissement you don't know. Buy a baguette and eat it while walking. The city rewards those who wander without agenda as much as those with a full itinerary.
