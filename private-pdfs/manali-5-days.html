"Manali rewards the patient traveller. Go slow, acclimatise properly, and the mountains will give you everything — snow in June, stars at altitude and silence you cannot find anywhere else in India."

Getting There: Fly Delhi → Bhuntar (Kullu Airport, 50km from Manali, ₹3,000–₹8,000). Or take the overnight Volvo bus from Delhi ISBT Kashmere Gate (14 hrs, ₹1,200–₹1,800). Buses are comfortable, arrive early morning — gives you a full first day.
Altitude Note: Manali sits at 2,050m. Take Day 1 easy — no vigorous activity. Rohtang (3,978m) and Kheerganga (2,950m) can cause AMS (headache, nausea) if you rush. Carry Diamox if prone to altitude sickness.

Manali 5-Day Itinerary Day 1–2 — Arrival & Solang Valley
01
Day One · Manali Arrival
Arrive, Acclimatise & Explore Old Manali
Mall Road · Hadimba Temple · Manu Temple · Old Manali Cafes
💰 Budget: ₹800–₹1,500 · Couple: ₹2,500–₹5,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Arrive & Check In — Rest First
If arriving by overnight bus, check in early or drop bags and get breakfast. Do not rush to any high-altitude spot on Day 1. Spend the first few hours just walking at town level — your body needs 12–24 hrs to start adjusting to 2,050m altitude.
Afternoon
Hadimba Temple & Manu Temple
Hadimba Devi Temple (circa 1553) — a four-storey pagoda-style wooden temple in the middle of a deodar cedar forest. Must visit. Remove shoes before entering. Free entry. Manu Temple: a 20-min walk uphill through Old Manali village — quieter, fewer tourists, excellent views of the Beas River valley below.
Free · Remove footwear at Hadimba · 30 min each
4:00 PM
Mall Road Walk & Market
The main promenade — lined with shops selling Kullu shawls, dry fruits, woolens, silver jewellery and trekking gear. Good place to pick up last-minute supplies (gloves, thermals, rain cover) before Rohtang. Evening light over the mountains from here is beautiful.
Shopping: ₹200–₹2,000 depending on what you need
Evening
Old Manali Cafes — Cafe 1947 / Dylan's
Old Manali (across the bridge) is where backpackers congregate. Cafe 1947 — wooden interiors, live acoustic music, excellent Israeli and Indian food, ₹200–₹400/dish. Dylan's Toasted & Roasted — best coffee in Manali, great sandwiches, ₹150–₹300. Both have roof seating with mountain views.
Budget dinner: ₹250–₹400 · Couple: ₹800–₹1,500
02
Day Two · Solang Valley
Solang Valley — Adventure & Snow
Zorbing · Paragliding · Cable Car · Book Rohtang Permit Tonight
💰 Budget: ₹1,200–₹2,000 · Couple: ₹4,000–₹7,000 (excl. accommodation)
9:00 AM
Solang Valley (15km from Manali)
Shared taxi from Mall Road ₹100/person or private cab ₹600–₹800 return. The valley opens up dramatically as you approach — snow-capped peaks on three sides. In winter (Nov–Mar) the entire valley is under snow and is a ski resort. In summer: green meadows, adventure sports, river.
Activities
Zorbing / Paragliding / Cable Car / River Crossing
Zorbing (rolling downhill in a transparent ball): ₹400. Paragliding tandem: ₹2,500 (15–20 min flight, stunning views). Cable car to Shitidhar: ₹500 return (top has snow even in May–June). River crossing / zip line: ₹300–₹500. Choose 2–3 activities — no need to rush.
Zorbing ₹400 · Paragliding ₹2,500 · Cable car ₹500 · Zip line ₹300
Evening
Book Rohtang Permit Online — Mandatory
Rohtang Pass requires an online permit from the Himachal Pradesh government portal (rohtangpermits.nic.in). Only 1,200 vehicles per day are allowed. Permit fee ₹500+ (varies by vehicle type). Book at least 1–2 days in advance — slots fill quickly in peak season (June, September–October).
Permit: ₹500+ · Book at rohtangpermits.nic.in · Carry printout
Bike Rental Option: Royal Enfield Bullet ₹500–₹800/day, smaller bikes ₹300–₹500/day. Riding Rohtang on a Bullet is iconic but requires a valid licence, mountain riding experience and a full fuel tank before you leave Manali (no petrol stations beyond town).

Manali 5-Day Itinerary Day 3–4 — Rohtang & Kasol
03
Day Three · Rohtang Pass
Rohtang Pass — 3,978m Above Sea Level
Leave by 6am · Rahala Falls · Snow Photography · Back by 2pm
💰 Budget: ₹1,200–₹2,000 · Couple: ₹3,000–₹5,000 (transport + activities)
6:00 AM
Depart Manali — Early Start Is Non-Negotiable
Traffic on the Manali–Rohtang road becomes gridlocked by 9am. Leave by 6am or you will spend 3 hours stuck in a queue of vehicles. Shared cab from Mall Road: ₹300–₹400/person return. Private SUV (mandatory beyond certain points): ₹2,500–₹3,500 return for 4 people. Carry your permit printout.
Shared cab ₹300–₹400/person · Private SUV ₹2,500–₹3,500
On the Way
Rahala Falls (2,501m)
A spectacular waterfall visible from the road about halfway to Rohtang. The water comes directly from glacial snowmelt — ice cold, crystal clear. Most drivers stop here for 15 minutes. The gorge around it is impressive even in summer. Don't skip this.
Free · Roadside stop · 15 minutes
9:00 AM
Rohtang Pass Summit — Snow in June
At 3,978m, snow persists on the pass even in peak summer (May–June). The view from the top: two completely different worlds — the Kullu Valley behind you (green, forested, warm) and the Lahaul & Spiti cold desert ahead (grey, barren, otherworldly). Most tourists only see the tourist zone — walk 10 minutes further for real solitude.
Snow gear rental at the pass: ₹200–₹400 (jacket, boots, gloves)
Note
Rohtang Closure: November to April
Rohtang Pass is completely closed due to heavy snowfall from November to April (sometimes until mid-May). The best windows are May–June and September–October. July–August is monsoon — the road is prone to landslides and is often blocked. Check current road status on the HPRTC website before visiting.
2:00 PM
Back in Manali — Rest, Recover, Lunch
Return by 2pm to avoid the worst of afternoon traffic. Altitude (3,978m) can cause mild headaches even in healthy people — drink lots of water, eat light. Afternoon: rest, cafes in Old Manali, or explore Naggar Castle (25km south, medieval stone-and-wood Himalayan architecture, ₹50 entry).
⚠️ AMS Warning: Rohtang is at 3,978m. If you feel severe headache, nausea, shortness of breath or confusion — descend immediately. Do not push through altitude sickness symptoms. Carry Diamox (acetazolamide) if advised by your doctor.
04
Day Four · Kasol or Kheerganga
Kasol Day Trip — Or Kheerganga Trek
Parvati Valley · Israeli Food Belt · Hot Spring at the Summit
💰 Budget: ₹800–₹1,500 · Adventure: ₹1,500–₹3,000 (Kheerganga camp + trek)
Option A
Kasol Day Trip (75km, ~3 hrs each way)
Kasol sits in the Parvati Valley along the Parvati River. Famous for its bohemian atmosphere, Israeli bakeries and Israeli food (hummus, shakshuka, fresh bread). Very laid-back. Walk along the river, try Moon Dance Cafe or Evergreen Cafe. Bus from Manali: ₹120–₹180. Taxi: ₹1,800–₹2,500 return.
Bus ₹120–₹180 each way · Taxi ₹1,800–₹2,500 return
Option B
Kheerganga Trek (from Kasol) — 12km, 5 hrs Up
Start from Barshaini village (30 min beyond Kasol by local bus). Trek through dense pine and birch forest, crossing wooden bridges over roaring streams. Camp at the top (₹500/person, basic tents provided). The reward: a natural geothermal hot spring at 2,950m — sitting in hot mineral water while surrounded by snow peaks is extraordinary. Descend next morning.
Camp ₹500 · Hot spring free · Trek guide optional ₹500
Option C
Naggar Castle & Roerich Art Gallery
Only 25km south of Manali. Naggar Castle (15th century, mountain-stone architecture, ₹50 entry) overlooks the Kullu Valley. Next to it: the Nicholas Roerich Art Gallery — Russian painter who fell in love with the Himalayas. Peaceful, uncrowded, genuinely interesting. A good Day 4 alternative if you're trekked-out.
Naggar Castle ₹50 · Roerich Gallery ₹50 · Taxi ₹600–₹800 return

Manali 5-Day Itinerary Day 5 — Final Day & Departure
05
Day Five · Final Day
Jogini Falls, Markets & Departure
Morning trek · Woolens & dry fruits · Evening bus or flight
💰 Budget: ₹500–₹1,000 · Couple: ₹1,500–₹3,000
7:00 AM
Jogini Waterfall Trek (3km, 2 hrs)
Start from Old Manali village — the trailhead is a 10-minute walk from the bridge. 3km trail through apple orchards and deodar forest, gaining about 200m elevation. The waterfall at the top cascades over multiple tiers — no one knows about this one, so it's quiet even in peak season. Free, no permit needed. Best done in the cool morning hours.
Free · No permit required · Easy–moderate trail
Alternate
Jana Waterfall (15km from Manali)
If Jogini feels too familiar, Jana Waterfall near the village of Jana is higher, more dramatic and almost completely tourist-free. 20-minute walk from the road. Local bus ₹30 or taxi ₹400–₹500 return. Few people know about it — you may well have it to yourself.
Bus ₹30 · Taxi ₹400–₹500 return · Free entry
10:00 AM
Mall Road Market — Last Chance Shopping
Kullu shawls (₹400–₹2,000 — hand-woven, warmer than any pashmina at this price). Dry fruits from Himachal: walnuts, apricots, almonds (₹300–₹800/kg, far cheaper than city prices). Silver jewellery in Old Manali: rings, pendants, earrings. Tibetan market behind Mall Road: singing bowls, thangka paintings, incense.
Kullu shawl ₹400–₹2,000 · Dry fruits ₹300–₹800/kg
Afternoon
Lunch, Pack & Depart
Last meal at Johnson's Cafe (upscale, trout and river fish, ₹400–₹700/dish) or at any Old Manali dhaba (₹100–₹200, home-cooked Himachali dal and rajma). Evening buses to Delhi depart from the HRTC bus stand at 4pm, 5pm, 6pm. Book in advance during peak season — Volvo ₹1,200–₹1,800. Flight from Bhuntar: pre-arrange taxi ₹900–₹1,200 (1.5 hrs).
Bus to Delhi: ₹1,200–₹1,800 · Taxi to Bhuntar airport: ₹900–₹1,200
Best Hostels in Manali: Zostel Old Manali (dorm ₹600–₹800, private ₹1,800–₹2,500 — excellent social atmosphere, right in Old Manali village). The Hosteller Old Manali (₹700–₹900 dorm — rooftop with Himalaya views, great breakfast included). Both book up fast in June and September — reserve at least a week in advance.
Don't Leave Without: A jar of local Himachali apple jam (₹80–₹150) or a bottle of Manali HPMC apple juice. Also: fill your bag with walnuts — Manali walnuts are among the finest in the country and cost a third of what you'd pay in Delhi or Mumbai.

Manali 5-Day Itinerary Packing List & Pro Tips
What to Pack

Manali Packing List

Clothing — Layers Are Everything

□Thermal base layer top & bottom (mandatory)
□Fleece or down mid-layer jacket
□Waterproof rain jacket / shell (Jul–Aug monsoon)
□3–4 t-shirts (cotton or merino wool)
□Trekking trousers (quick-dry, 2 pairs)
□Warm hat and gloves (nights are cold even in June)
□Sturdy trekking shoes (waterproof recommended)
□Sandals / flip-flops for town and temples
□Light scarf / modesty cover for temples

Health & Safety

□Diamox (acetazolamide) if prone to AMS — consult doctor
□Ibuprofen / paracetamol (altitude headaches)
□Lip balm with SPF (UV is intense at altitude)
□SPF 50 sunscreen (stronger UV above 2,000m)
□Blister plasters (new trekking shoes)
□Rehydration sachets (altitude dehydration)

Documents & Money

□ID (Aadhaar / Passport — required for permits)
□Rohtang permit printout (mandatory)
□This PDF guide (offline)
□Hotel and bus booking confirmations
□Cash in ₹ — ATMs are limited beyond Manali town
□UPI set up (most shops accept it now)
□Emergency contact numbers saved offline

Gadgets & Extras

□Power bank (long travel and trekking days)
□Offline Google Maps downloaded (Himachal)
□Headlamp / torch (camps, trekking)
□Dry bag or waterproof pack cover
□Reusable insulated water bottle
□Trekking poles (optional but useful for Kheerganga)
Pro Tips
⛽
Full tank before Rohtang
There are no petrol stations beyond Manali town on the Rohtang route. Fill up the night before — whether you're on a bike or in a car. Running out of fuel on a mountain pass is not a fun experience.
🌧️
Avoid July–August for Rohtang
Monsoon brings landslides and road closures on the Manali–Rohtang stretch. The road can be blocked for days. If you're visiting Jul–Aug, stick to Manali town, Solang Valley and the Beas Valley — Rohtang is a gamble.
💵
Cash is king beyond Manali
Solang Valley, Kasol, Kheerganga and small dhabas are largely cash-only. The single ATM in Kasol often runs out during peak season. Withdraw enough from Manali town before heading out — carry at least ₹3,000 in cash.
🏕️
Kheerganga: call ahead for camping
There is no reliable online booking for Kheerganga camps. Ask your hostel to call directly (they usually know the camp operators). Alternatively, just show up — camps always find space, but a prior booking guarantees a sleeping bag during cold nights.
