Visa
🛂 UAE Visa for Indians
Indians need a UAE Tourist Visa — not on arrival. Apply online via UAE ICA (ica.gov.ae) or through a reputed travel agent. Cost: ₹4,000–₹6,000 for 30 days. Processing takes 3–5 working days. Apply at least 1 week before travel. Keep a printed copy on you.
Currency
💵 Money in Dubai
UAE Dirham (AED). 1 AED ≈ ₹22.5 (verify before travel). Dubai is an expensive city — budget realistically. Always withdraw AED from airport ATM on arrival (better rates than exchange counters). UPI/cards work in malls; carry AED cash for souks and abra rides.
Getting Around
🚇 Dubai Metro — Your Best Friend
Dubai Metro (Red + Green line) covers all major tourist spots. Buy a Nol Card at the airport for ₹90 (AED 4). Metro rides cost ₹30–₹150 per trip. Clean, air-conditioned, on time. Avoid taxis — Dubai taxis are expensive (₹300–₹800 per ride). Use Careem (like Uber) if Metro isn't convenient.
Flights
✈️ Flying from India
IndiGo, Air India, and Emirates fly from Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kochi and more. Flight time: 3–4 hours. Book 6–8 weeks ahead for best fares. Terminal 3 (Emirates) is particularly stunning — worth arriving early. Dubai airport (DXB) is connected to the city by metro.

Dubai 4-Day Itinerary Day 1 — Downtown Dubai
01
Day One · Downtown Dubai
Burj Khalifa, The Fountain & Dubai Frame
World's tallest tower · Fountain show · DIFC arts district
💰 Budget: ₹3,500–₹5,000 · Couple: ₹8,000–₹12,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Burj Khalifa — At the Top (Book Weeks Ahead)
The world's tallest building at 828m. Go early to avoid queues — book online at burjkhalifa.ae well in advance (prices double at the gate and tickets often sell out). At the Top (124th floor, 452m): stunning 360° views of Dubai. Ideal for first-timers. Do not wait to book on the day — it will cost double and may not be available.
At the Top 124F: ₹3,000 · At the Top Sky 148F: ₹6,000 · Book online only
Afternoon
Dubai Mall — World's Largest Mall (Free)
1,200 shops, an indoor ice rink, a massive aquarium, and a waterfall inside the building. Free to enter. Dubai Aquarium & Underwater Zoo inside (optional, ₹2,200). Just walking through is an experience — the scale is genuinely surreal. Souk Al Bahar across the bridge offers the best angle to photograph the Burj Khalifa reflected in the fountain lake.
Mall entry: Free · Aquarium: ₹2,200 optional
6:00 PM
Dubai Fountain Show — Free, Every 30 Minutes
The world's largest choreographed fountain — jets shoot 150m into the air, dancing to Arabic music. Shows run every 30 minutes after 6pm. Watch from the Souk Al Bahar bridge for the best view. Completely free. The combination of the Burj Khalifa lit up, the fountain, and the Dubai skyline at dusk is one of the most spectacular sights on earth.
Free · Shows every 30 min after 6pm, every 20 min at 1pm & 1:30pm
5:00 PM
Dubai Frame — Where Old Meets New (Before Sunset)
A 150m picture frame structure bridging old and new Dubai. One side shows historic Deira and Bur Dubai; the other shows modern skyscraper Dubai. The glass-bottomed sky bridge walkway between the two towers is genuinely nerve-wracking (in a good way). Perfect golden-hour stop. Go before the fountain show.
₹1,200 · Zaabeel Park, Dubai · Metro to Al Jafiliya
Evening
Dubai Opera Walk + DIFC Art Galleries
Walk past the stunning Dubai Opera building (dhow boat-shaped architecture). DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre) art galleries are free and open evenings — world-class contemporary art. Dinner: Comptoir 102 (healthy, ₹2,500/person), Zuma DIFC (fine dining, ₹5,000+), or head back to Dubai Mall food court (₹500–₹800).
Critical booking tip: Book Burj Khalifa tickets the moment you finalize your travel dates — ideally 2–3 weeks ahead. Tickets sell out on weekends and during peak season. The online price is also 30–50% cheaper than at-the-gate. burjkhalifa.ae is the only official site.

Dubai 4-Day Itinerary Day 2 — Old Dubai
02
Day Two · Deira + Bur Dubai
Gold Souk, Creek & Al Fahidi Heritage District
Abra crossing · Gold Souk · Spice Souk · Al Seef · JBR Beach
💰 Budget: ₹500–₹1,500 · Couple: ₹3,000–₹8,000 (excl. accommodation + gold purchases)
Morning
Deira Gold Souk — World's Largest
Over 300 gold shops under one roof — the largest concentration of gold shops in the world. Overwhelming and dazzling in equal measure. Best to buy 22K gold here (hallmarked, genuine). Gold is sold by weight — the price per gram is fixed by the government daily, but making charges are negotiable. Bargain 10–20% on making charges. Mornings are less crowded. Go before 10am.
Free entry · Gold prices set daily by Dubai Gold & Jewellery Group
10:30 AM
Deira Spice Souk — Frankincense, Saffron & More
A labyrinth of sacks filled with saffron, frankincense, dried rose petals, turmeric and exotic spices. The air is thick and heady. Iranian saffron here is excellent — ₹200–₹500 for a good quantity. Frankincense (luban) makes a great take-home gift. Bargain gently. The souk has changed but remains atmospheric and genuine.
Free entry · Saffron ₹200–₹500 · Frankincense ₹100–₹300
11:30 AM
Abra Water Taxi Across Dubai Creek — ₹5
The most authentic Dubai experience: a wooden abra (water taxi) across the Dubai Creek from Deira to Bur Dubai. Costs AED 1 (₹22) per person. The 5-minute crossing on a packed wooden boat with the old city skyline on both sides is irreplaceable. This is how Dubai residents have crossed the creek for 200 years. Do not skip this.
AED 1 (≈ ₹22) per person · Runs constantly through the day
Noon
Al Fahidi Historical District + Dubai Museum
The oldest surviving part of Dubai — 150-year-old wind-tower buildings (barjeel), narrow lanes, and a glimpse of pre-oil Dubai. Completely free to walk. The Dubai Museum inside Al Fahidi Fort is excellent — shows how Dubai went from a fishing village to a megacity in 50 years. ₹90 entry, 45 minutes. Al Seef waterfront next to it is great for lunch.
District: Free · Dubai Museum: ₹90 · Al Seef restaurants: ₹400–₹1,200
Evening
La Mer Beach or JBR Beach — Free Sunset
Both are free public beaches. JBR (Jumeirah Beach Residence) Walk is livelier — outdoor restaurants, street performers, beachfront cafes, and a great atmosphere at sunset. La Mer is quieter and more curated. Metro to Jumeirah Lakes Towers then a short Careem to JBR. Walk the entire JBR strip (1.5km) for dinner options ranging from ₹500 to ₹3,000.
Beach entry: Free · Dinner at JBR: ₹500–₹2,000
Gold buying tips for Indians: Check the day's gold rate on the Dubai Gold & Jewellery Group website before entering the souk. The metal price is non-negotiable — only the making charges (craftsmanship fee) can be bargained. Stick to shops displaying the official certification logo. Carry your passport for larger purchases. Customs limit for gold jewellery brought into India: ₹50,000 (men) / ₹1,00,000 (women) duty-free.

Dubai 4-Day Itinerary Day 3 — Marina & Palm
03
Day Three · Marina + Palm Jumeirah
Ain Dubai, Atlantis & Sunset at the Marina
Dubai Marina Walk · Bluewaters Island · Palm Jumeirah · Pier 7
💰 Budget: ₹2,000–₹4,000 · Couple: ₹6,000–₹15,000 · Full Atlantis day: ₹8,000/person
Morning
Dubai Marina Walk — Most Photogenic in Dubai
A 7km waterfront promenade lined with restaurants, yachts, and some of Dubai's most striking residential towers. Walk the full loop early (8–10am) when it is cool and uncrowded for the best photography. Marina Mall is at one end if you need coffee. Take the Metro (Red Line) to DMCC station — it drops you right in the heart of the Marina.
Free · DMCC Metro station · Best: early morning or sunset
11:00 AM
Ain Dubai — World's Largest Ferris Wheel (Bluewaters Island)
250m tall — taller than the London Eye. Located on Bluewaters Island (10 min from JBR by free shuttle or short Careem ride). One cabin ride takes 38 minutes. Stunning 360° views of the Marina, Palm, and Arabian Sea. Book online. The island itself has great dining and a quirky Madame Tussauds if you have kids. Worth it for the views alone.
Standard cabin: AED 130 (≈ ₹3,000) · Book online at aindubai.com
1:00 PM
Palm Jumeirah — Monorail + The View at The Palm
Take the Palm Monorail from Atlantis to the Gateway Station and back (₹800 return). Walk the Palm trunk and crescent boardwalk for photos of the Marina skyline. The View at The Palm — a stunning observation deck on the 52nd floor of The Palm Tower — is highly recommended: ₹1,800, incredible panorama of the entire Palm and city. Or go full day at Atlantis Aquaventure Waterpark (₹8,000, all-day access to slides and private beach — best for families and waterpark lovers).
Monorail: ₹800 return · The View: ₹1,800 · Atlantis: ₹8,000 full day
Sunset
Pier 7 Rooftop — Seven Restaurants, One View
Dubai Marina's iconic circular tower with seven different restaurant floors — seafood, Japanese, Indian, Italian and more. Take the elevator to your restaurant of choice. The view of the Marina at sunset with the Ain Dubai in the background is spectacular. Book a table in advance for weekend evenings. Dress smart-casual. Budget ₹2,000–₹5,000 per person for dinner.
Dinner from ₹2,000 pp · Book on OpenTable or restaurant website
Atlantis Aquaventure tip: Book online (atlantis.com) at least 1 week ahead for the best price. Gate tickets cost 20–30% more. Arrive by 9am — the most popular slides have 45-min queues by noon. The private beach access and lazy river are included. Bring reef shoes (the deck gets hot). Best for families with children or if you love waterparks.

Dubai 4-Day Itinerary Day 4 — Desert Safari
04
Day Four · Desert + Departure
Desert Safari — Dunes, Camels & Bedouin Stars
4x4 dune bashing · Sandboarding · Camel ride · BBQ under stars · OR: IMG Worlds
💰 Desert Safari: ₹3,000–₹5,000/person · IMG Worlds: ₹7,000/person
Morning
Last Morning — Dubai Mall or Gold Souk Shopping
If departing late, morning is ideal for any last-minute shopping. Dubai Duty Free at the airport is excellent but expensive. Gold Souk in Deira opens by 9am (best for jewellery). Dubai Mall for chocolates, dates, perfumes, and branded goods. Dubai is a tax-free shopping destination — electronics, perfumes and branded bags are often cheaper than India (check customs limits before buying).
Dubai Duty Free: open 24/7 at DXB · Customs limit: USD 500 per person duty-free
3:00 PM
Desert Safari — The Unmissable Dubai Experience (3pm–10pm)
Book through a reputed tour operator (not hotel desk touts — they overcharge). Pickup from hotel by 3pm. The safari includes: 45 minutes of thrilling 4x4 dune bashing (sand roller-coaster — genuinely exhilarating), sandboarding on 100m dunes, camel ride, quad biking (optional extra), henna painting, traditional Emirati dress photos, Tanoura (whirling dervish) dance show, and a lavish BBQ buffet dinner in a Bedouin-style camp under a sky full of stars. Returns to hotel by 10pm.
Standard: ₹3,000/person · Premium with private camp: ₹5,000/person · Book online
Alternative
IMG Worlds of Adventure — Largest Indoor Theme Park
If you have children or prefer to skip the desert, IMG Worlds of Adventure is the world's largest temperature-controlled indoor theme park. Four zones: Marvel (Spider-Man, Thor, Iron Man rides), Cartoon Network (Ben 10, Adventure Time), IMG Boulevard, and Dinosaur Land. Full day experience. Arrive at 10am. Rides suitable for ages 6 and above.
₹7,000/person · imgworlds.com · Book online for 15% discount · City of Arabia Metro
Departure
Airport Transfer — Book in Advance
Dubai Metro Red Line connects the city to DXB Airport (Terminal 1 and 3). Take metro from Union or DMCC to Airport Terminal 1/3 — ₹110–₹150, 30–45 minutes. Pre-book a Careem for a stress-free transfer if you have luggage. Arrive at airport 3 hours before international departure. Emirates Terminal 3 is worth exploring even if you're flying another airline — not possible, but Terminal 3 departures lounge is something else.
Metro to airport: ₹110–₹150 · Careem: ₹600–₹1,000 depending on area
⚠️ Desert Safari booking warning: Do NOT book through hotel reception — they add a 40–60% markup. Book directly via Viator, GetYourGuide, or a reputed Dubai tour agency online. Also verify the operator is licensed by Dubai Tourism (DTCM). Read reviews on Tripadvisor before paying. Average group safari should cost no more than ₹3,500/person including dinner.

Visa + Cultural Tips
🛂
UAE Visa — Apply via Official Channel
Apply on the UAE ICA official website (ica.gov.ae) or Emirates airline visa portal. Avoid random agents on Instagram. Cost: AED 175–350 (₹4,000–₹6,000). 30-day tourist visa. Processing: 3–5 working days. Keep printed copy.
👗
Dress Code — Respectful, Not Restrictive
Dubai is modern and tolerant, but respect is important. Cover shoulders and knees in souks, mosques and heritage areas. Beachwear only on beaches and pool areas. Malls are now relaxed — sleeveless is generally fine — but a light cover-up is always a good idea.
🌙
Ramadan Etiquette
If visiting during Ramadan (dates shift each year), avoid eating, drinking or smoking in public during daylight hours. Restaurants operate with curtained dining. Nightlife is subdued. On the positive side: iftar buffets are spectacular and Ramadan is a beautiful time to be in the souks after sunset.
🚫
What Not to Do in Dubai
No alcohol in public spaces (only licensed venues). No public displays of affection. Do not photograph people without permission — especially Emirati locals and women. Swearing in public can result in a fine. VPNs are in a legal grey area — use with caution. Drugs carry severe penalties.
