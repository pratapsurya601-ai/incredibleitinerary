Day 1–2
🏙️ Ahmedabad
India's first UNESCO World Heritage City (2017). Sabarmati Ashram — Gandhi's home and the launch site of the Salt March. Adalaj Stepwell — 15th-century Indo-Islamic geometric architecture. Akshardham temple complex (free, extraordinary). Calico Museum of Textiles (appointment required, world's finest collection of Indian textiles). Manek Chowk night market for Gujarati thali and street food.
Day 3
🦁 Sasan Gir National Park
The last natural habitat of the Asiatic lion — found nowhere else in the wild. 500+ lions in the Gir forest. Jeep safaris (₹2,500–₹3,500/jeep, max 6 people) give excellent lion sightings — success rate is very high (80%+) with experienced guides. Also: leopards, hyenas, nilgai, sambar, marsh crocodiles. The Gir landscape — dry deciduous teak forest on Gujarat's Saurashtra coast — is itself beautiful.
Day 4–5
🕉️ Somnath & Dwaraka
Somnath — one of the 12 Jyotirlingas, rebuilt multiple times after repeated Mughal invasions, now standing defiantly on the Arabian Sea coast. Dwaraka — the mythological city of Lord Krishna, mentioned in the Mahabharata. Dwarkadhish Temple is one of the Char Dham. Bet Dwarka island offshore has additional temples and is reached by boat (₹30). A profound Vaishnava pilgrimage circuit.
Day 6–7
🏜️ Rann of Kutch
The Great Rann of Kutch is the largest salt desert in the world — 30,000 sq km of flat white salt crust stretching to the horizon. During Rann Utsav (November–February), a festival tent city is set up with cultural performances, local food, and camel rides under a full moon on the salt flats. Outside the festival: drive independently to the white Rann viewpoint (Dhordo village, free). The visual is unlike anywhere else on Earth.

"Gujarat is where Gandhi was born, where lions still roam wild, where a 1,000-year-old temple faces the Arabian Sea, and where the ground turns white and infinite at the world's edge."

Note on Alcohol: Gujarat is a dry state — alcohol is prohibited by law. No alcohol available anywhere in Gujarat except through a liquor permit for non-residents (obtainable at the airport on arrival — free, covers the duration of your stay). Fine dining restaurants sometimes serve to permit holders. Most travellers find this a non-issue for a 7-day trip focused on heritage, wildlife, and pilgrimage.

Days One & Two · Ahmedabad
Gandhi's City — Heritage, Stepwells & Night Markets
Sabarmati Ashram · Adalaj Stepwell · Akshardham · Calico Museum · Manek Chowk night market
💰 Ashram: free · Akshardham: free · Calico Museum: free (appointment) · Manek Chowk dinner: ₹150–₹300
Day 1 AM
Sabarmati Ashram — Gandhi's Home & the Salt March Origin
Sabarmati Ashram (free) was Gandhi's home from 1917–1930. From here he led the Dandi Salt March (240 miles, 1930) — the act that galvanised the independence movement. The ashram preserves his living quarters (Hriday Kunj), spinning wheel, sandals, and correspondence exactly as he left them. The exhibition hall has an excellent chronological history of Gandhi's life and the independence struggle. Deeply moving. One of the most important historical sites in modern India. Allow 2 hours.
Sabarmati Ashram: free · Open 8:30am–6:30pm · 2hrs recommended · No photography inside Hriday Kunj
Day 1 PM
Adalaj Stepwell — 15th-Century Geometric Masterpiece
Adalaj Vav (20km from Ahmedabad) is a five-storey octagonal stepwell built in 1498 CE. The entire structure below ground level is carved with extraordinary detail — decorative niches, intricate geometric and floral patterns, miniature pavilions, erotic sculpture (minor but present), and columns descending into the water below. The architecture blends Hindu and Islamic elements seamlessly. The structure was designed so that each landing received cooling shade at a different time of day. One of the finest examples of stepwell architecture in India. Free entry.
Adalaj Stepwell: free · 20km from Ahmedabad · 1hr visit · Best in morning light
Day 2 AM
Akshardham Temple & Calico Museum
Akshardham Ahmedabad (free, BAPS Swaminarayan) is even more spectacular than the Delhi version — the original 1992 complex with extraordinary pink sandstone and white marble carving, a miniature Nilkanth Varni statue at the centre, and extensive grounds. Allow 3 hours. Calico Museum of Textiles (free, appointment required — call ahead to Shahibaug: 079-2286-8172) is arguably the world's finest collection of Indian historic textiles — Mughal brocades, temple hangings, royal costumes. Morning session only (10am–12:30pm). Book the appointment a day ahead.
Akshardham: free · No photography allowed · Calico Museum: free but appointment essential · Call ahead
Day 2 Night
Manek Chowk — Night Market & Gujarati Street Food
Manek Chowk is a remarkable Ahmedabad institution — during the day, a jewellery market (one of India's largest wholesale gold and silver trading areas). At night (from 9pm), the same space transforms into a street food paradise. Dozens of stalls serve sev puri, dabeli (the Kutchi burger — spiced potato, pomegranate, peanuts in a bun), Gujarati thali, bhelpuri, corn, and the famous Ahmedabad pav bhaji. The Gujarati thali at any of the fixed restaurants nearby (Agashiye on Lal Darwaja, HBC on Relief Road) is also extraordinary — 15+ dishes, unlimited servings, ₹300–₹600.
Manek Chowk street food: ₹100–₹200 · Gujarati thali restaurant: ₹300–₹600 · Night market from 9pm
03
Day Three · Sasan Gir
The Only Wild Asiatic Lions on Earth
Ahmedabad → Sasan Gir (360km, 5hrs) · Dawn and afternoon jeep safaris · Lion sighting near certain
💰 Jeep safari: ₹2,500–₹3,500/jeep (6 people) · Forest entry: ₹100 each · Stay near park: ₹1,500–₹4,000
5:30 AM
Dawn Jeep Safari — Asiatic Lions at Dawn
Book your Gir National Park jeep safari in advance on the official Gujarat forest department portal (girlion.gujarat.gov.in) — slots fill quickly in peak season (November–February). The dawn safari (5:30–9am) gives the best light, most active animals, and coolest temperatures. Asiatic lions are the main attraction — smaller than African lions, with a distinctive belly fold and a single main mane. They are usually found in prides of 2–4. The success rate for lion sightings at Gir is among the highest of any wildlife safari in India — 80–90% with knowledgeable guides. Also watch for: spotted deer, sambar, nilgai, wild boar, langur monkeys, Indian pythons, marsh crocodiles.
Safari: ₹2,500–₹3,500/jeep (max 6) · Forest entry: ₹100 each · Book online at girlion.gujarat.gov.in
Afternoon
Second Safari + Devaniya Leopard Territory
The Devaniya zone of Gir (slightly separate from the main lion zone) is excellent for leopard sightings — which are rarer and more exciting than lions for most wildlife photographers. An afternoon safari (3–6:30pm) through the dry teak forest is very different from the dawn experience — the light is golden and dramatic, and nocturnal animals begin stirring. Book both zones to maximise the wildlife experience.
Afternoon safari: same rates · Devaniya zone: slightly different booking · Ask hotel to arrange
04
Day Four · Somnath
One of the 12 Jyotirlingas — On the Arabian Sea
Sasan Gir → Somnath (85km) · Jyotirlinga darshan · Veraval fishing harbour · Sound & Light Show evening
💰 Somnath Temple: free · Evening aarti: free · Sound & Light: ₹50 · Stay Somnath: ₹800–₹2,500
Morning
Somnath Temple — Rebuilt Seven Times, Still Standing
The Somnath Jyotirlinga temple has been destroyed and rebuilt at least 7 times in its history — by Mahmud of Ghazni (1026, famously looting its legendary wealth), by subsequent rulers, and most recently rebuilt in 1951 after Independence by Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel as a symbol of national resurrection. The current temple stands directly on the Prabhasa Patan coast with the Arabian Sea visible behind it. A unique pillar (Baan Stambh) points to Antarctica — the inscription states that no land exists between Somnath and the South Pole in this direction. Evening aarti (7pm) is extraordinary — waves, chanting, fire, the Arabian Sea.
Somnath Temple: free · No photography inside · Sound & Light Show: ₹50 at 8pm · Aarti: 7pm
Afternoon
Veraval Fishing Harbour — One of India's Largest
Veraval (5km from Somnath) is Gujarat's largest fishing harbour — thousands of boats, the smell of brine and catch, colourful painted boats in every direction, women mending nets on the quay, the fish auction at dawn. It's chaotic, vivid, and entirely un-touristy. The contrast between the sacred Somnath just down the road and the earthy, working life of Veraval harbour is very Gujarat. Watch the boats return in late afternoon with their catch.
Veraval: free · Best activity: observe the fish auction (4–6am) or afternoon boat return

Day Five · Krishna's City
Dwaraka — Lord Krishna's Legendary City
Somnath → Dwaraka (230km, 4hrs) · Dwarkadhish Temple · Bet Dwarka Island · Nageshwar Jyotirlinga
💰 Dwarkadhish Temple: free · Bet Dwarka boat: ₹30 · Nageshwar Jyotirlinga: free
Morning
Dwarkadhish Temple — One of the Char Dham
Dwaraka is one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites of all India (with Badrinath, Puri and Rameswaram). The Dwarkadhish Temple (dedicated to Lord Krishna as the King of Dwarka) dates to the 16th century but stands on much older foundations — submarine archaeological surveys have found submerged structures in the Gulf of Khambhat dating to 7,000+ years ago, associated with the ancient city of Dwarka mentioned in the Mahabharata. The five-storey temple has elaborate carving and a 78-metre spire. Morning darshan (6:30am–12:30pm) before the massive midday crowds.
Dwarkadhish Temple: free · No photography inside · Morning darshan less crowded · Modest dress required
Afternoon
Bet Dwarka Island — The Island Where Krishna Lived
Bet Dwarka (also called Shankhodhar) is a small island 30km from Dwaraka, accessible by boat (₹30, 15 minutes). According to tradition, this is where Krishna actually lived — while Dwarkadhish is where he ruled. The island has the Dwarkadhish temple with a black stone idol, several other smaller temples, and a peaceful, pilgrimage atmosphere. The boat crossing across the Gulf of Khambhat is itself pleasant. Walking the island takes 1.5 hours.
Bet Dwarka boat: ₹30 return · Island walk: 1.5hrs · Multiple small temples · Very peaceful
En Route
Nageshwar Jyotirlinga (16km from Dwaraka)
Nageshwar is one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines — if you're already doing the Gujarat circuit, visiting a third Jyotirlinga (Somnath and Nageshwar are both in Gujarat) is a remarkable devotional bonus. The Nageshwar temple has a massive (25-metre) statue of sitting Shiva visible from the road. Free entry. The temple complex is spacious and less crowded than Somnath.
Nageshwar Jyotirlinga: free · 16km from Dwaraka · Easy road detour
6–7
Days Six & Seven · Rann of Kutch
The White Salt Desert & Kutch Handicrafts
Dwaraka → Bhuj (260km, 5hrs) → White Rann → Rann Utsav (Nov–Feb) · Kutch handicrafts from Bhuj
💰 White Rann entry: ₹100 · Rann Utsav tent city: ₹15,000–₹30,000/night · Bhuj hotel: ₹800–₹3,000
Day 6
Dwaraka → Bhuj (260km, 5hrs) — Gateway to Kutch
The drive from Dwaraka to Bhuj (district headquarters of Kutch) crosses the flat, scrubby terrain of the Saurashtra peninsula. Bhuj is a fascinating town — heavily rebuilt after the devastating 2001 earthquake (26,000 killed) with the old city walls partially preserved. The Prag Mahal palace (₹50, Victorian-Italianate architecture built 1865), Aina Mahal (₹50, mirror palace), and the Kutch Museum (₹10, excellent regional culture) are all in Bhuj. More importantly, Bhuj is the gateway to the Rann and to the world-famous Kutch handicraft villages.
Bhuj Prag Mahal: ₹50 · Aina Mahal: ₹50 · Kutch Museum: ₹10 · Bhuj hotel: ₹800–₹3,000
Day 6 Evening
White Rann at Sunset — The Earth's Edge
The White Rann viewpoint at Dhordo village (80km from Bhuj) is the most accessible entry point to the Great Rann of Kutch. Entry: ₹100. At sunset and under a full moon, the white salt crust extends to every horizon — pure, blinding white against a darkening sky. The effect is genuinely disorienting — it feels like the end of the world, or the beginning. Walk onto the salt crust (firm in dry season, soft after monsoon). The silence and scale are extraordinary.
White Rann entry: ₹100 · Dhordo village: 80km from Bhuj · 1.5hrs drive · Sunset timing ideal
Day 7 AM
Rann Utsav — The Festival of the Rann (Nov–Feb)
The Rann Utsav festival (November to February) is Gujarat Tourism's extraordinary tent city on the edge of the White Rann. Air-conditioned Swiss-tent villages, cultural performances (garba, folk music, puppet shows), camel rides on the salt flat under a full moon, and local food. Package rates: ₹15,000–₹30,000/night per couple, all-inclusive. You can also visit the Rann Utsav grounds on a day ticket (₹800) without staying. Outside festival season: visit the White Rann independently through Dhordo village (₹100 entry) for the same extraordinary landscape without the festival crowd.
Rann Utsav package: ₹15,000–₹30,000/night couple · Day visit: ₹800 · Independent visit year-round: ₹100
Day 7 PM
Kutch Handicraft Villages — Ajrakh, Bandhani, Embroidery
Kutch produces some of the finest textile crafts in India. The craft villages near Bhuj are accessible by day trip: Ajrakhpur (Ajrakh block printing — indigo and madder natural dyes, geometric Islamic patterns), Dhamadka (second Ajrakh centre), Nirona (Rogan art — a rare metallic paint on fabric, practiced by one family in the world), Bhujodi (Kutchi weaving), and the villages of the Rabari tribe (extraordinary embroidery and mirror work). Buy directly from artisans at source — no middlemen, better prices, and you see the craft being made. A Kutchi embroidered blouse: ₹500–₹3,000. Ajrakh cotton: ₹400–₹2,000 per metre.
Craft villages: free to visit · Ajrakh fabric: ₹400–₹2,000/metre · Kutchi embroidery: ₹500–₹5,000

Gujarat 7-Day Itinerary Insider Tips, Food & Handicraft Guide
Insider Tips — Know Gujarat Before You Go

Vibrant Gujarat — What to Know

🦁
Gir Safari Booking — Must Book Online
Gir National Park safari slots MUST be booked online at girlion.gujarat.gov.in. The quota is limited and fills up weeks ahead in November–February peak season. You cannot just show up at the gate. Book at least 2–4 weeks ahead for peak season, or 1 week ahead for shoulder months. The online system requires Indian mobile number for OTP. If you can't book online, some travel agents in Sasan Gir town can arrange slots through quota — at a commission.
🍽️
Gujarati Food — The Thali Culture
Gujarati thali is one of India's greatest culinary experiences — 15+ small portions of vegetarian dishes served on a large plate, all unlimited refills, ₹200–₹600 depending on the restaurant. Standard components: dal (sweet Gujarati variety), kadhi (yoghurt curry), vegetables (dry and wet preparations), roti, rice, papad, pickles, dessert, buttermilk. Must-try: fafda-jalebi (fried chickpea flour strips with syrup, classic Gujarat breakfast), undhiyo (seasonal root vegetable curry), thepla (spiced flatbread), and Gujarati mithai (sweet shops everywhere).
🏺
Kutch Crafts — Buy Authentically
The best way to buy Kutch handicrafts is directly from the artisan villages (Ajrakhpur, Bhujodi, Nirona) — you see the craft being made, prices are fair, and the money goes directly to the craftspeople. Bhuj's main market has craft shops but with significant markup. The Dastkar Bazaar craft fair (usually November–January during Rann Utsav) brings all major artisans together. Kutch craft cooperatives: Shrujan in Bhuj (women's embroidery collective, highest quality, fair-trade priced).
🚗
Car Hire Is Essential for This Circuit
The Gujarat circuit (Ahmedabad–Gir–Somnath–Dwaraka–Rann–Ahmedabad = 1,200km) requires a private hired car. Public buses exist but are slow and don't connect the less-visited sites. A 7-day car hire with driver (standard Indica/Swift) costs ₹8,000–₹12,000 depending on vehicle. Negotiate for a fixed rate covering all kilometres. Hire from reputable Ahmedabad-based travel agencies. Your hotel can usually arrange this.
🌡️
Season — October to March Only
Gujarat in summer (April–June) reaches 45°C+ in Kutch and 42°C elsewhere — genuinely dangerous for outdoor activities. Monsoon (July–September): Gir is closed (animals dispersed), Rann is flooded, roads to Kutch problematic. Best period: October–March — Gir safaris excellent, Rann Utsav festival in full swing (November–February), pleasant temperatures (22–32°C daytime, 12–18°C nights in Kutch). Navratri festival (October) in Ahmedabad — the garba dancing is extraordinary.
🕉️
Temple Dress Code
All three temples on this circuit (Somnath, Dwarkadhish, Nageshwar) have strict dress codes. Cover shoulders and knees. No shorts. No sleeveless tops. No leather items inside the sanctum. Photography prohibited inside all three. Cloakrooms available for bags and footwear. Evening aartis at Somnath (7pm) and Dwarkadhish (early morning) are the most powerful times to visit. Arrive 20 minutes early for good viewing position.
🏛️
Ahmedabad — UNESCO World Heritage City
Ahmedabad is India's first (and so far only) UNESCO World Heritage City (2017). The old city (Walled City) area east of the river has extraordinary Indo-Islamic architecture — the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque (1573) with its famous intricate stone jali tree-of-life window, the Jama Masjid, the Swaminarayan Temple, and hundreds of havelis with wooden carved facades. The Heritage Walk organized by Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation (7:30am daily, free) covers these in 2 hours with knowledgeable guides.
💃
Navratri in Ahmedabad — World's Largest Garba
Navratri (nine nights of dance, October) in Ahmedabad has been called the world's largest dance festival — hundreds of thousands of people dance garba (circular folk dance in concentric circles) simultaneously across the city. The Gymkhana Ground event is the most spectacular. Wear traditional chaniya choli (women) or kediyum (men) to participate. The energy, colour, and music are overwhelming in the best possible way. If your Gujarat trip overlaps with Navratri — stay for it.
Packing for Gujarat
👗 Temple-appropriate clothing — cover shoulders and knees for all three Jyotirlinga temples.
☀️ Sunscreen SPF 50+ — Rann of Kutch salt flat reflection is intense. Burn risk high even in winter.
🕶️ Sunglasses — the white Rann is blinding in daylight. Polarised preferred.
💧 1.5L water bottle — Gujarat is hot even in winter. Kutch is particularly dry and arid.
🎒 Extra bag for shopping — Kutch crafts and Gujarati textiles are bulky. Pack light going in.
💊 Motion sickness tabs — Saurashtra roads to Gir and Dwaraka are winding in sections.
📱 Uber/Ola in Ahmedabad — works well. Outside Ahmedabad: rely on your hired car driver.
💰 Cash for craft villages — Kutch artisan villages are cash-only. Carry ₹5,000–₹8,000 in small notes.
🔭 Binoculars — essential for Gir safari. 8x42 minimum for bird and wildlife identification.
