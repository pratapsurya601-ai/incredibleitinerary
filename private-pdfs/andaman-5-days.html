Andaman Islands 5-Day Itinerary Day 1–2 — Port Blair & Havelock
01
Day One · Port Blair Arrival
Cellular Jail, Corbyn's Cove & Seafood
Arrive IXZ · Cellular Jail · Light & Sound Show · Corbyn's Cove · Aberdeen Bazaar dinner
💰 Budget: ₹400–₹800 · Mid-range: ₹1,500–₹2,500 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Land at Veer Savarkar Airport (IXZ) — Check In
Pre-book a hotel near Aberdeen Bazaar for easy access to everything. Taxis from airport: ₹300–₹500. The area around Aberdeen Bazaar is Port Blair's beating heart — markets, ferries, and food all within walking distance. Freshen up and get your bearings.
Budget guesthouses: ₹800–₹1,200/night · Mid-range: ₹2,500–₹4,000/night
Afternoon
Cellular Jail National Memorial — India's "Kala Pani"
This is why you stay a day in Port Blair. The colonial-era prison where India's independence fighters were exiled and tortured. Seven radiating wings, solitary confinement cells, the gallows. The guided tour is haunting and essential. The 3pm Light & Sound Show (in English) brings the history to life in a way nothing else can — book ahead, it fills up.
Entry: ₹30 · Light & Sound Show: ₹200 — book ahead at the counter
Evening
Corbyn's Cove Beach — Sunset & Seafood
A 20-minute auto ride from the city. Black sand, gentle waves, a relaxed atmosphere and one of Port Blair's best sunsets. Beach shacks serve cold beer and grilled fish. Then head to Aberdeen Bazaar for dinner at a local dhaba — fresh fish curry and rice is the move. Look for Icy Spicy or any packed local place.
Auto to beach: ₹80 · Fish curry + rice: ₹150–₹200 at local dhaba
02
Day Two · Ferry to Paradise
Havelock Island & Radhanagar Beach
6:30am ferry · Check in · Radhanagar Beach sunset · Market dinner
💰 Ferry: ₹500–₹1,200 · Beach huts: ₹800–₹1,500 · Mid-range: ₹3,000–₹6,000
6:30 AM
Ferry from Phoenix Bay Jetty to Havelock — 2.5 hours
Book Makruzz or Green Ocean ferry online at least 2 days ahead at makruzz.com or journey.in — they sell out. Government ferries are cheaper but slower and less comfortable. The crossing takes 2–2.5 hours; carry motion sickness tablets if you're sensitive. The government jetty on Havelock is at Havelock Village (Jetty No.1).
Makruzz/Green Ocean: ₹900–₹1,200 · Government ferry: ₹500 · Book 48hrs ahead
Noon
Check In — Havelock Accommodation
Havelock has the best accommodation range in the Andamans. Budget beach huts at Vijay Nagar beach are charming. For mid-range, Emerald Gecko and Symphony Palms offer good value. Barefoot Resort and Manta Ray Island Resort are the luxury options — beachfront, stunning, worth every rupee if your budget allows.
Beach huts: ₹800–₹1,500 · Mid-range: ₹3,000–₹6,000 · Barefoot/Manta Ray: ₹12,000+
4:30 PM
Radhanagar Beach (Beach No. 7) — Asia's Best Sunset
A 2km stretch of powder-white sand curving into impossibly clear turquoise water, backed by forest. Voted Asia's best beach by Time magazine. The walk from the main road is 10 minutes through jungle. Entry fee: ₹50. Arrive by 4:30pm — the beach fills up by 5pm and the sunset is the main event. This is the reason you came to the Andamans.
Entry: ₹50 · Auto from market: ₹150 · Best light: 5:30–6:30pm
Evening
Havelock Market Shacks — Tuna & Coconut Water
The market near the jetty has several open-air shacks serving fresh catch. Tuna steak grilled with spices is the must-order at ₹350. Cold kingfisher, coconut water ₹40, and the most relaxed atmosphere you've encountered in weeks. No rush, no noise — just the sound of waves and a generator.
Tuna steak: ₹350 · Coconut water: ₹40 · Full meal: ₹500–₹700

Andaman Islands 5-Day Itinerary Day 3–4 — Havelock Diving & Neil Island
03
Day Three · Underwater Havelock
Elephant Beach, Scuba Diving & Kalapathar
9am boat to Elephant Beach · Snorkelling · Optional scuba dive · Kalapathar sunset
💰 Boat + snorkelling: ₹300–₹500 · Scuba dive: ₹3,500–₹4,500
9:00 AM
Boat to Elephant Beach — Snorkelling in Coral Gardens
30-minute speedboat ride from the jetty to Elephant Beach — one of the most spectacular snorkelling spots in all of India. The coral reef starts just metres from shore: parrotfish, angelfish, sea turtles, stingrays and vast fields of staghorn coral. Snorkelling equipment is included in the boat fare. Best in morning when the water is calmer and visibility highest.
Boat + snorkel gear: ₹300–₹500/person · 30 min each way
Afternoon
Scuba Diving — World-Class Visibility at 20–30 Metres
The Andamans has visibility of 20–30 metres — among the best in Asia. If you have never dived before, a Discover Scuba session is the single best thing you can do here. Barefoot Scuba (at Barefoot Resort) and Dive India are the two most reputable operators. After a 45-minute briefing, you do one dive to 6–9m with an instructor. Absolutely no experience required. If you're certified, the sites around Havelock rival the Maldives.
Discover Scuba (1 dive): ₹3,500–₹4,500 · Certified: ₹2,500–₹3,500/dive
Evening
Kalapathar Beach — the Quiet Sunset Spot
While Radhanagar gets the crowds, Kalapathar (Black Rock Beach) is the photographer's choice. Black volcanic rocks contrast against white sand and turquoise water. Almost never crowded. A 20-minute walk from the market, or ₹100 auto ride. The sunset here is softer, more private. Also visit the ATM at Havelock market — there are only two on the entire island. Carry extra cash.
Auto: ₹100 · Free entry · ATM: near market (only 2 on island — carry extra cash)
04
Day Four · The Quiet Island
Neil Island — Bicycles, Beaches & Natural Bridge
Morning ferry · Bharatpur Beach · Natural Bridge · Laxmanpur sunset · Neil's Kitchen
💰 Ferry: ₹500–₹900 · Cycle hire: ₹100/day · Stay: ₹800–₹2,000
10:00 AM
Ferry to Neil Island — Havelock–Neil Route
Take the morning ferry from Havelock to Neil Island (check schedule the night before — timings change by season). Neil Island is smaller and calmer than Havelock — 5km long, no ATMs, one main road and a pace of life that feels like the 1990s. This is the Andaman that hasn't been discovered yet. Hire a bicycle at the jetty for ₹100/day — the entire island takes 2 hours by bike.
Ferry: ₹500–₹900 · Book the evening before · Cycle hire: ₹100/day at jetty
Morning
Bharatpur Beach + Natural Bridge
Bharatpur Beach (near the jetty) has excellent snorkelling with gear rentals at ₹200. The coral here is shallower and easier for beginners. From Bharatpur, cycle 2km to the Natural Bridge — a naturally occurring rock arch formation carved by the sea over millennia. The 20-minute walk through jungle to reach it is half the experience. Photogenic and unusual.
Snorkel gear: ₹200 · Natural Bridge: free · 20 min walk from road
Evening
Laxmanpur Beach — Best Sunset on Neil Island
Laxmanpur Beach on the western shore has the best sunset on Neil Island — the silhouettes of the rock formations against the orange sky are spectacular. A 3km cycle from the main village. Almost nobody here compared to Radhanagar. Sit on the sand, watch the fishing boats come in, and understand why people never want to leave the Andamans.
Free · Cycle 3km from village · Best light: 5:00–6:30pm
Dinner
Neil's Kitchen — Best Fish Curry on Neil Island
A small family-run restaurant near the main junction — no signage, just locals pointing you to it. The fish curry with steamed rice is widely considered the best on the island at ₹250. The prawns in coconut gravy are equally excellent. Cash only, limited seating. If it's full, wait — it's worth it.
Fish curry + rice: ₹250 · Cash only
Stay on Neil overnight if possible: The night sky on Neil Island, away from any light pollution, is extraordinary. Sleeping in a beachside hut at ₹800–₹2,000 and waking up to the sound of the ocean is the Andaman experience at its purest.

Andaman Islands 5-Day Itinerary Day 5 · Budget · Tips
05
Day Five · Farewell to Paradise
Ross Island, North Bay Sea Walk & Departure
North Bay water sports · Ross Island ruins · Aberdeen Bazaar shopping · Evening flight
💰 Sea Walk: ₹3,500 · Speed boat: ₹150 · Ross Island entry: ₹75 boat
8:30 AM
North Bay Island — Sea Walk (Walk on the Ocean Floor)
The sea walk at North Bay is unlike any experience you'll have elsewhere in India. A helmet is placed over your head, connected to a surface oxygen supply, and you walk on the actual ocean floor at 5–6 metres depth — fish swim around you, you can touch the coral. No swimming required, no certification needed. Also available: speed boat ride (₹150), glass-bottom boat, and basic snorkelling. Book the sea walk ahead at the jetty the previous evening.
Sea walk: ₹3,500 · Speed boat: ₹150 · Glass-bottom boat: ₹300
Late Morning
Ross Island — Jungle-Covered British Ruins
Now officially renamed Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Island. A short ₹75 boat ride from Aberdeen Jetty. The entire island is a haunting tableau of British colonial ruins — ballroom, barracks, church — all now completely overgrown with jungle and colonised by spotted deer that roam freely. Peacocks strut through the ruins. The deer come right up to you. 2–3 hours is ideal; last ferry back by 3pm.
Boat: ₹75 return · Entry free · 2–3 hrs recommended
Afternoon
Aberdeen Bazaar Shopping — Shells, Spices & Departure
Final stop: Aberdeen Bazaar for shells and shell jewellery, Andaman coconut oil (exceptional quality), spice boxes from the local market, and a glass of coconut water for the road. Flight check-in by 4pm for evening departures. Flights back to Chennai, Kolkata, and Delhi operate daily. The Andamans will follow you home in your dreams.
Spice boxes: ₹200–₹500 · Shell jewellery: ₹100–₹2,000 · Airport: 30 min from Aberdeen

Andaman Islands 5-Day Itinerary Insider Tips & Packing List
Insider Tips — Read Before You Go

Know Before You Go

⛴️
Book Ferries 48 Hours Ahead
Use makruzz.com or journey.in for Makruzz and Green Ocean ferries. Peak season (Dec–Feb) they sell out days ahead. Book as soon as your travel dates are fixed. Government ferry counters at Phoenix Bay open at 6am.
💵
Carry ₹5,000+ Cash from Port Blair
There are only 2 ATMs on Havelock and none on Neil Island. Carry sufficient cash when leaving Port Blair. Most small restaurants and guesthouses are cash-only. Cards work only at larger resorts.
🌊
Monsoon: Avoid Jun–Sep Entirely
June to September sees ferries cancelled for days at a time, many resorts close, and rough seas make swimming and snorkelling impossible. The Andamans are a Oct–May destination. Best conditions: November to March.
🤿
Best Snorkelling Spots
Elephant Beach (Havelock) for variety and ease. Bharatpur Beach (Neil) for shallow beginners. Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park (near Wandoor, Port Blair) for the most biodiverse reef in the Andamans — day trip worth adding.
🏊
Scuba at Havelock — Don't Miss It
The Andamans has world-class diving with 20–30m visibility. Barefoot Scuba and Dive India are both certified PADI operators. Even a single Discover Scuba dive will be among your life's best experiences. Book a day ahead — slots fill up.
🌅
Radhanagar Sunset: Arrive by 4:30pm
The beach fills completely by 5pm in peak season. Walk down by 4:30pm to find your spot on the sand. The forest walk from the parking area takes 10 minutes. No food stalls on the beach itself — eat before you go or bring snacks.
📶
SIM Cards — Which Networks Work
Airtel and BSNL work reasonably well across Port Blair and Havelock. Jio is patchy on outer islands including Neil. Download offline maps (Maps.me or Google Maps offline) before you leave Port Blair. WiFi is available at most mid-range resorts.
🌡️
Sunscreen is Your Most Important Packing Item
The Andaman sun is equatorial and relentless. Bring SPF 50+ and apply every 2 hours. You will burn badly in the first day if unprepared. Reef-safe sunscreen is preferred and appreciated — the coral ecosystem is fragile.
Packing List for Andaman Islands
☀️ SPF 50+ Sunscreen — bring enough for 5 days. Overpriced in Port Blair.
👙 Swimwear × 3 sets — you'll be in the water daily. Quick-dry fabric essential.
🥽 Snorkel mask — bring your own for hygiene. Rental masks can be poorly maintained.
👟 Water shoes — for coral walks, rocky beaches and sea walk. Essential.
💊 Motion sickness tablets — ferry crossing can be rough. Take 30 min before boarding.
💵 Sufficient cash — ₹5,000+ minimum when leaving Port Blair for outer islands.
🔦 Torch / headlamp — power cuts happen on outer islands, especially at night.
📵 Offline maps downloaded — no reliable data on Neil Island. Download before you leave.
🦟 Mosquito repellent — evenings near jungle/beach are particularly bad. DEET-based preferred.
🎒 Day backpack — for beach days. Waterproof or carry a dry bag inside.
🩴 Flip-flops + sandals — easy on/off for beaches and boat boarding.
🏥 Basic first aid kit — antiseptic for coral cuts (very common). Nearest hospital is in Port Blair.
Coral respect: Do not touch, stand on, or collect coral. The reef ecosystem takes decades to recover from damage. Andaman's coral bleaching from climate change is already a concern — be a responsible visitor. Use reef-safe sunscreen, don't feed fish, and take nothing but photographs.
