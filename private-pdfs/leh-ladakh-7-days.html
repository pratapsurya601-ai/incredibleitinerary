Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Read This First

⚠️ Read Before You Book Anything

Ladakh sits at 3,500m and above — higher than most Alps peaks. Every year, visitors who skip acclimatisation are evacuated by army helicopter at a cost of ₹1–3 lakh. This section is not optional reading. Altitude sickness (AMS) does not care how fit you are. A 25-year-old marathon runner and a 60-year-old with no fitness history are equally at risk.

🚨 AMS Symptoms — Know These
  • Headache that worsens on lying down — first warning sign
  • Nausea or vomiting — do not dismiss this
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness especially on standing
  • Fatigue disproportionate to activity level
  • Loss of appetite — common on Day 1–2
  • Shortness of breath at rest — serious sign
  • Confusion or loss of coordination — descend immediately
  • Persistent dry cough — early sign of HAPE (pulmonary oedema)
✅ Acclimatisation Protocol
  • Day 1 in Leh: REST COMPLETELY. No sightseeing, no alcohol, no climbing stairs unnecessarily
  • Drink 3–4 litres of water daily throughout the trip
  • Day 2: Gentle walks only — market, flat monastery. No high passes.
  • Do not fly from Leh and go directly to Khardung La. Two nights in Leh minimum before any high pass
  • Sleep low, explore high — always return to lower altitude to sleep
  • Avoid hot showers on Day 1 (can trigger headaches)
  • No alcohol for first 48 hours — accelerates dehydration
  • Start Diamox 250mg twice daily 24 hrs before arrival if prescribed by doctor
Medicines to Carry — Get a Prescription Before You Travel
Diamox (Acetazolamide)
250mg twice daily. AMS prevention and treatment. Consult doctor. Causes increased urination — drink more water.
Disprin / Aspirin
For altitude headaches. 650mg as needed. Do not take more than 3 tablets/day. Avoid on empty stomach.
Dexamethasone
Emergency use only for severe AMS. 8mg loading dose. Only use if descend is impossible. Prescription required.
When to Descend Immediately: If headache + vomiting does not resolve with rest and Diamox after 4 hours, descend to a lower altitude immediately — even at night. Losing 500m of altitude is often sufficient. Every hour of delay worsens the outcome. SNM District Hospital Leh: +91-1982-252014. Army Helpline: +91-1982-252126.
Pangong (4,350m) and Nubra (3,048m) note: Even after acclimatising in Leh (3,500m), Pangong is higher. Symptoms can re-emerge. Keep medicines accessible at all times. Ensure your travel insurance covers high-altitude evacuation — most standard policies do not.

Permits Required — Apply on Day 1 or 2 in Leh
Inner Line Permit (ILP) — Nubra Valley
Mandatory for all Indian nationals. Apply at DC Office, Leh (Fort Road). Bring 2 passport photos, Aadhaar/Passport photocopy. Processing time: same day, usually 1–2 hours. Open Mon–Sat, 10am–4pm.
₹400 per person · Valid 7 days
Inner Line Permit (ILP) — Pangong Lake
Separate permit needed for Pangong. Same DC Office. Apply for both Nubra and Pangong together in a single visit. Carry 4 photocopies of IDs total (2 each). Permits are checked at multiple army checkposts.
₹400 per person · Valid 7 days
Photography Permit
Required for professional photography/videography near border areas and some monasteries. Casual tourist photos on smartphones generally do not require this. Drone use is restricted near Leh city and army installations — prohibited without prior clearance from CIAL Leh.
₹25–₹100 depending on site
Foreign Nationals
Foreign tourists require Protected Area Permit (PAP) for restricted areas (Pangong, Nubra). Apply through a registered travel agency in Leh or at the DC Office. Passport required. Takes 1–2 days. Some areas remain restricted for foreign nationals entirely.
₹500–₹1,000 via agency

Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Day 1–2 — Leh Acclimatisation
01–02
Days One & Two · Leh City
Arrive Leh — Rest, Then Explore
Acclimatise · Leh Palace · Thiksey · Hemis Monastery
💰 Budget: ₹1,500–₹2,500/day · Couple: ₹4,000–₹7,000/day (excl. flights)
Day 1 Rule — NON-NEGOTIABLE: After landing in Leh, go directly to your hotel. Sleep, eat light, drink water. Do NOT visit any attraction today. Do NOT consume alcohol. Your body needs 24–36 hours to begin adapting to 3,500m altitude.
Day 1 — Arrive and Rest
Morning
Arrive at Kushok Bakula Rimpochhe Airport, Leh
Most Delhi and Mumbai flights land by 9–10am. The airport is 5km from Leh town — taxi ₹300–₹400. The moment you step off the plane you will feel the thin air. Walk slowly. Do not rush. Pre-book your hotel; many include airport pickup.
Airport taxi: ₹300–₹400 · Hotel pickup: often free
All Day
Hotel Room — Mandatory Rest
Drink 3 litres of water. Eat light — dal, rice, soup. Avoid heavy protein or meat on Day 1 as digestion is harder at altitude. Garlic soup is a local remedy said to aid acclimatisation (available in most Leh restaurants). Nap if tired — your body is working hard just to breathe.
Evening
Hall of Fame Museum (Optional — only if feeling well)
A 10-minute flat walk from most central hotels. The Indian Army's tribute to Kargil War heroes and Siachen soldiers. Deeply moving. Flat terrain — no exertion. If you have any headache, skip this too and rest. Entry ₹50.
Entry: ₹50 · Open 9am–7pm
Day 2 — Gentle Leh Exploration
Morning
Leh Palace & Namgyal Tsemo Gompa
The 17th-century nine-storey palace overlooks Leh. It was modelled on the Potala Palace in Lhasa. Take the path up slowly — it is a 20-minute uphill walk. The views over the Indus Valley are extraordinary. Namgyal Tsemo Gompa (monastery) sits above the palace — another 10 minutes up. Worth every step if you feel okay.
Palace entry: ₹15 (ASI monument) · Gompa: Free
Afternoon
Thiksey Monastery (19km from Leh)
One of the most visually spectacular monasteries in Ladakh — 12 storeys built into a hillside, resembling the Potala. Home to 500 monks. The maitreya (future Buddha) statue inside is 15m tall and takes your breath away in the best possible sense. Taxi from Leh: ₹600–₹800 return. Visit before 1pm to catch morning prayer sounds.
Taxi return: ₹600–₹800 · Entry: ₹50 · Best: 8–11am
Late Aftn
Hemis Monastery + Shanti Stupa Sunset
Hemis is Ladakh's largest and wealthiest monastery, run by the Drukpa Kagyu order. Famous for the Hemis Festival (Jun/Jul — coincide if possible). Return to Leh for Shanti Stupa — a white Buddhist stupa on a hilltop with 360° views. The sunset here, with mountains turning pink-orange, is one of the most beautiful sights in India. 269 steps up — take it slow.
Hemis entry: ₹50 · Shanti Stupa: Free · Combined taxi: ₹1,200–₹1,500
Evening
Leh Main Bazaar — Get Permits + Dinner
Visit the DC Office for Inner Line Permits (open until 4pm — go early). Then explore Leh bazaar: Tibetan handicrafts, pashmina shawls (buy here, not at tourist shops), thangka paintings. Try dinner at a local dhaba — thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup, ₹120) or momos (₹80–₹120). The best dhabas are the ones without English menus.
Permits: ₹400/person · Dinner: ₹150–₹400

Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Day 3 — Nubra Valley
03
Day Three · Khardung La → Nubra
Nubra Valley via Khardung La
World's Highest Motorable Road · Diskit · Hunder Dunes · Camel Ride
💰 Budget Biker: ₹1,800 · Couple/Taxi: ₹6,500–₹9,000 total (taxi + camp)
6:30 AM
Depart Leh — Early Start Essential
The 120km drive to Nubra takes 4–5 hours. Leave by 6:30–7am to reach Khardung La before 10am (afternoon clouds and wind can be severe). Pack a warm jacket, water and energy bars. Fill fuel in Leh — no reliable petrol station on this route. Have your Inner Line Permit ready for checkpost.
9:00 AM
Khardung La Pass — 5,359m World's Highest Motorable Road
At 5,359 metres, the air has half the oxygen of sea level. Limit your stop to 15–20 minutes maximum. The altitude here is intense — shortness of breath is normal. Take photos, absorb the lunar landscape and move on. The BRO (Border Roads Organisation) maintains a tea stall at the top — a cup of warm chai is recommended. Do not overexert yourself.
Tea at top: ₹30 · Keep stop to max 20 minutes
11:30 AM
Khardung Village — Descend & Breathe
After the pass, the descent to Khardung village (3,600m) brings relief. Stop for 20 minutes to let your body adjust. The valley below opens dramatically — steep canyon walls, sparse poplars, and the Shyok river appearing in the distance. The change in landscape from pass to valley is striking.
1:30 PM
Diskit Monastery & Giant Maitreya Buddha
Diskit is Nubra's main town and home to a 1,000-year-old monastery overlooking the valley. The 32-metre Maitreya Buddha statue (inaugurated by the Dalai Lama in 2010) faces Pakistan — a powerful image of peace. The monastery is an active institution with 90+ monks. Visit the main prayer hall and the top terrace for panoramic Nubra views.
Diskit Monastery entry: ₹50 · Good restaurant nearby for lunch ₹150–₹250
4:00 PM
Hunder Village — Sand Dunes & Bactrian Camels
Hunder's white sand dunes against a backdrop of snow-capped mountains are one of Ladakh's most surreal sights — the "cold desert". Double-humped Bactrian camels (an endangered species, original to Central Asia, stranded here by the old Silk Route) are available for rides on the dunes. ₹500 for a 20-minute ride. Best photographed at golden hour — the camels, dunes and mountains together are extraordinary.
Camel ride: ₹500 per camel (fits 2 people) · Dunes: free entry
Evening
Stay at Hunder Camp — Night Sky
Hunder has excellent tent camps ranging from basic (₹1,500/night) to luxury Swiss tents (₹5,000–₹8,000/night). Dinner is included at most camps. At night, the Nubra sky is extraordinary — virtually zero light pollution. The Milky Way is visible with the naked eye. Bring a fleece even in July — temperatures drop to 5–8°C at night.
Budget tents: ₹1,500–₹2,500 · Premium camps: ₹4,000–₹8,000 (incl. meals)
Silk Route History: Hunder was a key stop on the ancient Silk Route connecting Ladakh with Yarkand (now in China). The Bactrian camels were used as cargo animals. The route was used until Indian independence in 1947. Some families in Nubra are descended from Silk Route traders.

Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Day 4 — Pangong Lake
04
Day Four · Nubra → Shyok → Pangong
Pangong Lake — The Colour-Changing Sky
5-Hour Drive · Shyok Valley · Lakeside Camp · Unforgettable Sunset
💰 Taxi Nubra→Pangong: ₹4,500–₹6,000 · Camp: ₹2,000–₹8,000 (meals incl.)

"Pangong is not a lake you photograph and leave. It is a lake you sit beside in silence for two hours and understand why people cry when they first see it."

7:00 AM
Depart Hunder — Nubra to Pangong via Shyok Valley
The Nubra–Pangong route (via Shyok Valley) opened to civilian traffic relatively recently and is now the most scenic road in Ladakh. 160km, 5 hours. The Shyok River road hugs the riverbank through narrow gorges, past ancient villages and herds of kiang (wild Tibetan asses). This drive itself is worth the entire trip. Keep your ILP ready — multiple checkposts.
Shared jeep Nubra→Pangong: ₹1,500–₹2,000 · Private taxi: ₹4,500–₹6,000
Noon
Lunch at Durbuk Village
Durbuk, roughly halfway, has a few small dhabas serving dal, rice and Maggi. The food is basic but hot. Altitude is around 3,900m here — you may notice mild breathlessness again if you have been feeling better at Nubra's lower altitude. Eat light, drink water.
Lunch: ₹100–₹200 per person
2:00 PM
First Glimpse of Pangong 4,350m
As you crest a small rise, the lake appears below you — an impossible blue-green shimmer stretching 134km into Tibet. Two-thirds of the lake is in China. The blue changes shade every hour: turquoise in morning light, royal blue at noon, electric blue-violet at sunset, mirror-silver at night. The surrounding mountains are completely barren — not a tree in sight. This is a UNESCO tentative World Heritage site.
Afternoon
3 Idiots Shooting Point & Merak Village
The famous scene from 3 Idiots was filmed at the eastern end of Pangong. A marker now stands there. More interesting is Merak village — a small settlement of Changpa nomads who live by the lake year-round, surviving -40°C winters. Their black yak-hair tents and pashmina-producing Changthangi goats make for extraordinary photographs if you engage respectfully.
5:30 PM
Sunset at Pangong — Do Not Miss This
Sit at the lakeside as the light falls. The mountains turn from brown to orange to purple. The lake surface turns from blue to violet to near-black. The silence is complete. No boat traffic, no village sounds, just wind. This is the moment everything in this 7-day trip builds toward.
Night
Stay at Lakeside Camp
Over 30 tent camps now line the Pangong shore. Budget camps (₹2,000–₹3,000) have basic beds and shared bathrooms but are right on the lake. Premium Swiss-tent camps (₹5,000–₹8,000) offer attached bathrooms and better bedding. All include dinner and breakfast. The sound of the lake at night — silent except for soft waves — is deeply calming.
Budget camp: ₹2,000–₹3,000 · Premium camp: ₹5,000–₹8,000 (meals incl.)
Important: There are no ATMs at Pangong Lake. Carry sufficient cash (minimum ₹5,000) before leaving Leh. Mobile data is also unreliable — download offline maps (Maps.me or Google Maps offline) before departing.

Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Day 5–6 — Return to Leh
05–06
Days Five & Six · Chang La → Leh
Return via Chang La — River, Monastery & Market
Chang La Pass · Magnetic Hill · Zanskar Rafting · Leh Shopping
💰 Taxi Pangong→Leh: ₹4,000–₹5,000 · Activities: ₹600–₹1,200 extra
Day 5 — Pangong to Leh via Chang La
Dawn
Sunrise at Pangong — Wake Up Before 5:30am
The sunrise at Pangong is even more remarkable than the sunset. The sky turns pink, then orange, then the lake ignites in colour. You will have it nearly to yourself — most visitors are still asleep. Do not waste this. Set an alarm.
8:00 AM
Depart Pangong — Chang La Pass (5,360m)
The return route to Leh goes via Chang La — the third highest motorable pass in the world at 5,360m. Slightly higher than Khardung La but less congested. Road conditions are good. The BRO has built a café and army post at the top. Same altitude rules — limit stop to 15–20 minutes, do not run around. The descent to Durbuk and then Sakti is beautiful.
No toll · Chang La–Leh journey: ~4 hours total
Afternoon
Thiksey Monastery (if skipped on Day 2)
En route from Chang La back to Leh, Thiksey is an ideal stop. By now your body is better acclimatised and you will appreciate the climb up the monastery steps far more than on Day 2. Catch the butter lamp offering ceremony if you arrive between 12:30–1:30pm.
Entry: ₹50
Evening
Back in Leh — Hot Shower, Rest, Good Dinner
After days of rough roads and camping, a good hotel shower in Leh feels remarkable. Have dinner at one of Leh's better restaurants — The Tibetan Kitchen (Leh Main Bazaar) for authentic Tibetan food, or Bon Appetit for a mix of Indian and continental. Budget ₹300–₹600 per person.
Dinner at better restaurant: ₹300–₹600/person
Day 6 — Leh Day Trips & Shopping
Morning
Magnetic Hill + Gurudwara Pathar Sahib
Magnetic Hill (30km from Leh on Srinagar highway) is an optical illusion where vehicles appear to roll uphill against gravity. Scientifically it is a visual trick of perspective, but deeply entertaining. 5 minutes away is Gurudwara Pathar Sahib — one of the most beautiful gurdwaras in the country, built in 1517 to commemorate Guru Nanak's visit to Ladakh. Managed by the Indian Army. Langar (free community meal) is served all day.
Both sites: Free · Taxi from Leh: ₹800–₹1,200 return
Afternoon
Sangam — Confluence of Indus & Zanskar Rivers
Just past Gurudwara Pathar Sahib, the turquoise Zanskar meets the murky brown Indus in a dramatic visible line — two rivers flowing side by side without mixing for several hundred metres, the colour boundary clearly visible. One of Ladakh's most beautiful and overlooked spots. Free to visit. Ideal photography between 10am–2pm when colours are most distinct.
2:30 PM
Zanskar River Rafting — Grade II/III
From the Sangam point, rafting operators offer a 90-minute Zanskar rapids experience ending near Leh. Grade II–III (suitable for beginners). The canyon walls tower 300m above. Rafts are run by certified operators. ₹600–₹800 per person. Book on site or through any hotel in Leh. Best done in the afternoon when river flow is typically calmer.
Rafting: ₹600–₹800 per person · Life jackets included
Evening
Leh Market — Final Shopping
Main Bazaar and Changspa Road for: Pashmina shawls (genuine, ₹3,000–₹15,000 — avoid anything under ₹2,000 that claims to be "pure pashmina"), Ladakhi thangka paintings, prayer wheels, turquoise and silver jewellery, Himalayan sea buckthorn juice, dried apricots from Nubra (sweeter than store-bought, ₹200–₹300/kg), and yak cheese. Bargain politely — most vendors will go 20–30% below initial ask.
Budget ₹2,000–₹10,000 depending on shopping preferences

Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Day 7 — Departure
07
Day Seven · Departure Day
Fly Out of Leh — Early Morning Departure
Early Alarm · Airport Tips · Flight Booking Advice
💰 Airport taxi: ₹300–₹400 · Fly Delhi/Mumbai from ₹4,000–₹12,000 (IndiGo / Air India)
Critical Flight Booking Warning: Leh airport frequently cancels flights due to poor visibility and mountain weather — particularly in the morning fog window. Book a flight before noon (ideally 6–8am) as weather deteriorates by afternoon. NEVER book a connecting flight out of Delhi on the same day as your Leh departure. Leave a buffer of minimum 4 hours in Delhi. Always buy comprehensive travel insurance covering flight cancellation.
Morning — Departure Day Protocol
4:30 AM
Wake Up — Most Leh Flights Depart 6:00–8:00am
IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet and Go First (where operational) all fly Leh to Delhi in approximately 1hr 15min. GoAir previously also operated. Check current airlines — schedules change seasonally. Leh airport is tiny — check-in opens 2 hours before departure. Lines move fast but arrive no later than 90 minutes before. Set two alarms.
5:00 AM
Hotel Checkout & Taxi to Airport
Pre-arrange your taxi the night before (₹300–₹400). Airport is 5km from most central hotels — 15 minutes. The early morning Leh streets in dim light with mountains visible are unexpectedly beautiful. Many travellers say this quiet pre-dawn Leh is their strongest memory of the trip.
Pre-booked taxi: ₹300–₹400 · Book the night before
At Airport
Leh Airport — What to Expect
Kushok Bakula Rimpochhe Airport (IXL) is a military-shared airport with strict security. No photography inside the airport complex. Bags are X-rayed at the entrance gate before even entering the terminal. The security queue can take 30–45 minutes on peak days. The departure lounge is small — arrive early to get a seat. The mountain views from the runway on takeoff are spectacular.
Flight Booking Advice for Leh
📅
Book 60–90 Days in Advance
Leh has limited seats (small airport, limited runway capacity). Jun–Aug tickets sell out fast. Prices double-triple closer to travel dates. Book as soon as your dates are confirmed.
✈️
Best Airlines for Leh
IndiGo has the highest frequency (Delhi–Leh, Mumbai–Leh). Air India also operates. Always check direct routes — connecting through Jammu or Chandigarh adds 4+ hours with no benefit.
⛅
Weather Cancellation Buffer
Mountain airports cancel without warning. Build 1 extra day on return if possible. If cancelled, Air India has historic priority on rescheduling Leh passengers. Keep airport hotel numbers saved.
🎒
Baggage & Weight Tips
Leh routes often have 15kg check-in + 7kg cabin weight limits. Repacked sleeping bags and camping gear gets heavy fast. Ship excess baggage from Leh post office (₹200–₹400) to your Delhi address — cheaper than excess baggage fees.

Leh Ladakh 7-Day Itinerary Packing List
High-Altitude Packing List

Pack for the Mountains, Not the Brochure

Even in July, temperatures in Ladakh can drop to 5°C at night and at high passes. In Leh town it can be 30°C by afternoon. You need both extremes covered.

🧥 Clothing & Layering

☐Thermal base layer top & bottom (merino wool preferred — lightweight, odour-resistant, warm even when wet)
☐Mid-layer fleece jacket — 200gsm minimum
☐Down jacket / puffer jacket — even in June-July, essential for Khardung La, Chang La and night camps
☐Windproof outer shell jacket (rain jacket doubles up)
☐3–4 pairs quick-dry trekking trousers/pants
☐Thermal leggings for night camps
☐Warm gloves — light liner gloves minimum
☐Warm beanie / woollen hat (hat is mandatory at 5,000m passes)
☐Neck gaiter / buff (protects against cold wind and dust on bike)
☐Good quality trekking socks × 4 pairs (wool or synthetic)
☐Sturdy trekking shoes or ankle-support boots
☐Sandals or flip-flops for camp evenings

🏥 Health & Safety

☐Diamox (Acetazolamide) 250mg — consult doctor before trip for prescription
☐Disprin / Aspirin for altitude headaches
☐Dexamethasone — emergency use only, prescription required
☐Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) — for dehydration
☐Water purification tablets (areas beyond Pangong have no clean water access)
☐Sunscreen SPF 50+ (UV radiation is 50% stronger at altitude — reapply every 2 hours)
☐Lip balm with SPF — lips crack badly in dry high-altitude air
☐Quality UV-400 sunglasses — snow blindness is a real risk
☐Moisturiser — skin becomes extremely dry; basic Vaseline works
☐Basic first aid: bandages, antiseptic cream, blister plasters, pain balm
☐Pulse oximeter (₹800–₹1,500 on Amazon) — monitor blood oxygen at altitude
☐Personal prescription medicines — carry double the required quantity

🏍️ Bike Gear (Bikers Only)

☐Full-face helmet — renting from agencies is possible but bring your own if possible
☐Riding jacket with CE armour (shoulder, elbow, back)
☐Riding gloves — waterproof, full-finger
☐Knee guards / riding pants with armour
☐2-litre spare fuel can
☐Basic toolkit + puncture repair kit (agencies often provide)
☐Bike insurance documents + RC copy

Emergency Contacts — Save These Before You Leave Home
🚑 SNM District Hospital, Leh +91-1982-252014 / +91-1982-252364 · 24-hour emergency. The only proper hospital in Ladakh. For severe AMS, get here immediately — they have oxygen and basic stabilisation.
🪖 Army Helpline (Leh) +91-1982-252126 · For emergencies in restricted areas, road blockages, or if a vehicle breaks down on high passes. Army patrols Khardung La and Chang La regularly.

Fuel Stations — Critical Information
Fuel availability is the #1 logistical challenge for bikers in Ladakh: The only reliable petrol pumps are in Leh town (Indian Oil + Hindustan Petroleum, Main Bazaar area). After Leh: Diskit in Nubra Valley has one pump (sometimes limited supply). Pangong Lake — NO FUEL AT ALL. Chang La route has no fuel between Leh and Sakti. Always fill your tank to the brim in Leh before departing. Carry minimum 2 litres extra in a fuel can. A Royal Enfield 350 gets 25–30kmpl — plan accordingly for the 120km Leh–Khardung La–Nubra leg and the 160km Nubra–Pangong leg.
