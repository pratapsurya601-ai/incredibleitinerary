Day 1 Morning
🏯 Shaniwar Wada
The seat of the Peshwa (Prime Ministers of the Maratha Empire) from 1732. The original seven-storey palace burned down in 1828 — only the massive fortification walls and towers remain. But the scale is extraordinary — the Dilli Darwaza (main gate) is fitted with iron spikes to prevent elephant attacks. Evening Sound and Light Show (₹50) dramatically narrates Peshwa history.
Day 1 Evening
🍪 Koregaon Park & Kayani Bakery
Koregaon Park is Pune's hippest neighbourhood — tree-lined streets, boutique restaurants, expat cafes, and the Osho International Meditation Resort (formerly Rajneesh Ashram). Kayani Bakery (established 1955) is an Irani cafe on MG Road — the queue for their shrewsbury biscuits (buttery, crumbly, legendary) begins before the shop opens. Arrive 15 minutes before opening time.
Day 2 Dawn
🌅 Sinhagad Fort
Sinhagad (Lion's Fort, 25km from Pune) — the site of the Battle of Sinhagad (1670) where Tanaji Malusare scaled the cliff face at night to capture the fort from the Mughal garrison (sacrificing his life in the process). One of the most important battles in Maratha history. The sunrise trek (5am jeep + 30-min walk) is magnificent. Free entry. Famous for the bhakri, pithla, and zunka local food served at the fort.
Day 2 Afternoon
🕊️ Aga Khan Palace
Built by Sultan Muhammad Shah Aga Khan III in 1892. Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi, and Sarojini Naidu were imprisoned here by the British from 1942–44 during the Quit India Movement. Kasturba Gandhi died here. Her samadhi (memorial) and Mahadev Desai's (Gandhi's secretary) are in the garden. The palace is one of the most emotionally significant freedom struggle sites in Maharashtra. ₹300 entry.

"Pune has been the intellectual capital of Maharashtra for 300 years. The Peshwas built it. The British educated it. The students own it. The food is extraordinary."

How to Reach: From Mumbai — Deccan Queen / Shatabdi Express (2hrs, ₹150–₹600 depending on class, runs daily) or Pune Expressway by road (2–2.5hrs). Pune Airport (PNQ) has direct flights from Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Kolkata. The city is very well connected. Pune has its own Metro but it's still being expanded — Ola/Uber or autorickshaws for most sights.

Day One · Peshwa History & City Life
Shaniwar Wada, Dagdusheth & Koregaon Park

9:00 AM
Shaniwar Wada — The Peshwa Palace
Shaniwar Wada (₹25 entry) is the physical embodiment of Maratha power. The massive fortified walls (built 1736 CE) survive in remarkable condition — the Dilli Darwaza (main gate, iron-spiked for elephant-resistance), Mastani Darwaza (named after Peshwa Bajirao I's legendary lover Mastani — their tragic love story is well-known), and the Khidki Darwaza (window gate). Inside the walls, only foundations remain of the original palace, but the space is enormous — 625,000 sq ft. A guide (₹100–₹150) helps bring the history to life.
Entry: ₹25 · Guide: ₹100–₹150 · Sound & Light Show (evening): ₹50 · Open 8am–6:30pm
11:00 AM
Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple
One of the most famous Ganesh temples in India — the idol is believed to be over 125 years old. Dagdusheth was a famous halwai (sweet maker) who built this temple after losing his child to plague. The temple now runs extensive charitable activities. The Ganesh idol is adorned with gold and silver ornaments worth crores. During Ganesh Chaturthi (August–September), this temple draws millions. Year-round, it's a living, breathing devotional space in the heart of old Pune.
Entry: free · Prasad: ₹20–₹50 · Morning and evening aartis are most atmospheric
Noon
Misal Pav — The Ultimate Pune Breakfast/Lunch
Misal pav is Pune's signature dish — a spicy sprouted moth bean curry with crispy farsan (crunchy toppings), served with pav (dinner rolls). Every Puneri has their favourite misal spot. The most famous: Bedekar Misal (Narayan Peth — they've been making it since 1923, very spicy), Ramnath (Gokhale Nagar — milder, family friendly). The level of spice is genuinely fierce — ask for "medium" if you're heat-sensitive. Cost: ₹80–₹120 for a full meal with chai.
Misal pav: ₹80–₹120 · Full meal with chai · Bedekar (Narayan Peth) or Ramnath (Gokhale Nagar)
2:00 PM
Laxmi Road — Traditional Pune Shopping
Laxmi Road (near Shaniwar Wada) is Pune's traditional shopping district — Paithani sarees (silk with zari borders, ₹3,000–₹50,000), Kolhapuri chappals (hand-stitched leather sandals, ₹500–₹2,000, unique to Maharashtra — best bought in Pune), traditional jewellery, bangles, and Punekar speciality foods. The street becomes particularly vibrant in the evening with hawkers and streetfood stalls. Also try Chitale Bandhu's kharvas (a traditional milk sweet, ₹40/piece) — an institution since 1950.
Paithani sarees: ₹3,000–₹50,000 · Kolhapuri chappals: ₹500–₹2,000 · Kharvas: ₹40
4:00 PM
Kayani Bakery — Shrewsbury Biscuits & Mawa Cakes
Kayani Bakery (MG Road, Est. 1955) is a Pune institution. The famous shrewsbury biscuits (large, buttery, crumbly cookies with a slight vanilla-lemon flavour) have a queue that starts before the shop opens. Limit of 1–2 boxes per person during busy periods. Also: mawa cakes (small sponge cakes with dried milk base — rich and moist), raspberry cream rolls, and Irani chai (brewed in the back). This is one of India's genuinely legendary bakeries.
Shrewsbury biscuits: ₹400–₹600/box · Mawa cakes: ₹50 each · Queue before 4pm or 9am opening
Evening
Koregaon Park — Cafe Culture & Nightlife
Koregaon Park is Pune's most cosmopolitan neighbourhood — wide tree-lined streets (a rarity in Indian cities), excellent restaurants (Malaka Spice, Little Italy, BBC — Barista Brewhouse Cafe), wine bars, live music venues, and the Osho International Meditation Resort campus (visit during the day — meditation centre, bookshop, and pleasant grounds). This is where Pune's enormous student and young professional population spends its evenings. Pune nightlife is genuine and vibrant — this is the starting point.
Dinner in Koregaon Park: ₹400–₹1,200 · Drinks: ₹200–₹500 · Osho campus tour: ₹50 (daytime)

Day Two · Sunrise Fort & Freedom History
Sinhagad Sunrise, Aga Khan Palace & Departure
5am sunrise Sinhagad trek · Maratha history · Aga Khan Palace (Gandhi imprisonment) · Departure or day trips
💰 Sinhagad: free · Aga Khan Palace: ₹300 · Fort food: ₹100–₹200
5:00 AM
Sinhagad Fort Sunrise Trek (25km from Pune)
Book a shared jeep or Ola to Sinhagad Fort the evening before — jeeps depart from Sinhagad Phata (base) for the last 5km when road opens (usually 5:30am). Walk or jeep the final climb. Sinhagad (Lion's Fort, 1,312m) rises dramatically from the Deccan plateau — the sunrise from the ramparts, with the valley below bathed in golden light and Pune in the distance, is one of the finest views in Maharashtra. The fort's history is extraordinary: in 1670, Tanaji Malusare's night raid using a trained lizard (ghoral) to scale the cliff — one of the great stories of Maratha military history.
Entry: free · Jeep from Sinhagad Phata: ₹100–₹150 return · Trek from base: 30min · Allow 3hrs total
8:00 AM
Fort Food — Pithla Bhakri & Tak (Buttermilk)
Sinhagad Fort is famous for its simple local food stalls at the summit — bhakri (thick jowar/bajra flatbread), pithla (chickpea flour curry), zunka (dry chickpea curry), and fresh tak (spiced buttermilk). This is the most authentic Maharashtrian village food available at any tourist site in India. Eat with your hands, sitting on the fort wall. ₹80–₹120 for a full meal. The view while eating is complimentary. Many Pune residents make this trek specifically for the sunrise + food combination.
Bhakri + pithla: ₹80–₹120 · Tak: ₹20 · Best consumed with fort views as backdrop
11:00 AM
Aga Khan Palace — Where Gandhi Was Imprisoned
Aga Khan Palace (₹300) was built by Sultan Muhammad Shah Aga Khan III in 1892 as relief work for a famine. In 1942, the British government imprisoned Mahatma Gandhi, Kasturba Gandhi (his wife), Sarojini Naidu, and Gandhi's secretary Mahadev Desai here during the Quit India Movement. Kasturba Gandhi died here in February 1944. Her samadhi is in the garden — a simple, moving memorial. Gandhi's spinning wheel (charkha) and personal belongings are displayed inside. A deeply significant freedom struggle site, largely undervisited relative to its importance.
Aga Khan Palace: ₹300 · Open 9am–5:30pm · Photography permitted outside · 1.5hrs recommended
Afternoon
Day Trips from Pune — Lonavala or Alibag
If time permits: Lonavala (65km, 1.5hrs) — Bhushi Dam (seasonal waterfall), Tiger's Leap viewpoint, Karla and Bhaja Buddhist rock-cut caves (1st century BCE, extraordinary). The chikki (peanut brittle) from Lonavala is famous across Maharashtra. OR: Alibag (2hrs by road, or 1hr ferry from Mumbai's Gateway of India) — coastal fort, beach, simpler pace than Mumbai. Kolaba Fort accessible on foot at low tide from Alibag beach.
Lonavala by road: ₹1,500–₹2,000 cab return · Karla Caves entry: ₹25 · Alibag ferry from Mumbai: ₹120

Pune 2-Day Itinerary Tips, Food Guide & Day Trips
Insider Tips — Know Pune Before You Go

Puneri Pride — Tips from Locals

🍪
Kayani Bakery — Arrive Before Opening
Kayani Bakery (MG Road) opens at 9am and 4pm. Arrive 15 minutes before either opening time — the queue starts before the shutters go up. Once they sell out of shrewsbury biscuits (usually within 2 hours of opening), they're gone for the day. The limit is often 2 boxes per person when demand is high. Mawa cakes are also extraordinary — don't miss them. The biscuits travel well — they last 2–3 weeks at room temperature.
🌅
Sinhagad — Sunrise Is Non-Negotiable
Sinhagad at noon is just a hot fort with tourists. Sinhagad at sunrise — with mist in the valley below, the Purandar Fort visible in the distance, golden light hitting the ancient walls, and the smell of bhakri cooking at the summit stalls — is one of the most atmospheric experiences in Maharashtra. Wake at 4:30am. It's worth every minute of lost sleep.
🌡️
When to Visit Pune
October to February: ideal (22–30°C daytime, 12–18°C nights). Pleasantly cool relative to Mumbai. August–September: Ganesh Chaturthi festival — 11 days of extraordinary celebrations centred on Dagdusheth Ganpati and Kesariwada. The city becomes one enormous celebration — if you can handle the crowds, unforgettable. March–May: hot but manageable (32–38°C). Monsoon (June–July): dramatic Western Ghat waterfalls, all forts look incredible in mist.
🎓
The Student City Vibe
Pune has 4 major universities and hundreds of colleges — over 500,000 students. This creates a permanently young, educated, cosmopolitan energy unlike most Indian cities. The restaurant scene, cafes, live music, and nightlife reflect this. FC Road (Fergusson College Road) and Koregaon Park are the best areas for the student city vibe — excellent cafes, bookshops, street food, and people-watching.
🍽️
Pune Food — Essential Eating List
Must-eat: Misal pav (Bedekar, Ramnath), shrewsbury biscuits (Kayani), vada pav (street stalls everywhere), kothimbir vadi (coriander fritters — uniquely Maharashtrian, best at Chitale), Poha (flattened rice with vegetables, standard Pune breakfast), Mastani (Pune's signature thick milkshake with ice cream — Sujata Mastani, Swargate). Pune's cafe culture (European-style cafes) is excellent — Good Luck Cafe (MG Road, 1935) for bun maska and Irani chai.
🏯
Maratha History Context
Pune was the de facto capital of the Maratha Empire during the Peshwa period (1713–1818). At their peak, the Marathas controlled most of the Indian subcontinent. The Peshwas (Bajirao I in particular) were among the most brilliant military commanders in Indian history. The Shaniwar Wada, Sinhagad, Raigad (near Mumbai), and the fort circuit of Maharashtra all tell this story. Understanding Maratha history makes every Pune sight significantly more meaningful.
🚗
🚗
Getting Around Pune
Pune traffic is bad but manageable with Ola/Uber. Autorickshaws are available but not always willing to go long distances. For Sinhagad Fort (25km), book an Ola or hire a private auto from your hotel (₹800–₹1,200 return with waiting time). The Pune Metro is expanding but doesn't yet cover most tourist sites effectively. Car rentals available for Lonavala day trips.
🌿
Osho Ashram — What to Expect
The Osho International Meditation Resort in Koregaon Park is Rajneesh/Osho's legacy institution — still actively functioning. The grounds are beautiful (17 acres, marble paths, gardens). Visitors can take day passes (₹200–₹400) for the meditation centre and gardens. The Osho Bookshop is exceptional. The resort is genuinely open to curious visitors. Dress code: maroon robes (available at entry for rent). An unusual, peaceful, and thought-provoking place regardless of your views on Osho.
Day Trips from Pune — Worth Knowing
🗻 Lonavala (65km) — Bhushi Dam, Tiger's Leap, Karla Caves. 1.5hrs drive. Budget ₹2,500 for a full day.
🏖️ Alibag (110km) — Beach, Kolaba Fort, slower pace. Or: ferry from Mumbai Gateway to Alibag (1hr, ₹120).
🏯 Raigad Fort (110km) — Chhatrapati Shivaji's capital fort. Ropeway ₹180. Maratha Empire heart.
🍷 Nashik Vineyards (180km) — India's wine country. Sula, Grover, Soma vineyards do tours and tastings.
🕌 Pandharpur (230km) — Most sacred Vithoba temple. Major Maharashtra pilgrimage. Wari festival spectacular.
🌊 Mahabaleshwar (110km) — Hill station, strawberry farms, Arthur's Seat viewpoint. 2.5hrs drive.
🛖 Kolhapuri Chappals — Buy directly in Pune at Laxmi Road (better prices than tourist shops). Genuine handstitched leather.
🧁 Chitale Bandhu — Multiple Pune locations. Bakarwadi (spicy flaky rolls), chivda, kharvas. Best food gift from Pune.
📚 FC Road Bookshops — Fergusson College Road has India's best density of secondhand and student bookshops. Excellent browsing.
