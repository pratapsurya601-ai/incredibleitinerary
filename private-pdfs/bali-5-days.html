Before You Go

Essentials for Indian Travellers

🛂
Visa — Free on Arrival (No Fee Since 2024)
Indian passport holders get a 30-day Visa on Arrival at Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) — completely free since February 2024. No pre-application needed. Just show your return ticket, hotel booking, and a bank statement. Can be extended once for another 30 days (small admin fee applies).
💱
Currency — Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
1 IDR = ₹0.0052  |  ₹1 = ~193 IDR. So ₹1,000 = ~1,93,000 IDR. Change cash at money changers in Kuta/Seminyak (better rates than airport). Avoid airport exchange. Bring USD or withdraw IDR from local ATMs — BNI and BCA have the best rates. Tip: withdraw larger amounts to avoid per-transaction fees.
✈️
Flights from India
IndiGo and AirAsia fly direct from Mumbai (BOM) and Delhi (DEL) to Ngurah Rai, Bali (DPS). Flight time is 5–6 hours. Book 2–3 months ahead for best fares. Typical return fare: ₹18,000–₹35,000. Check prices on Skyscanner or Google Flights. Fly out Delhi/Mumbai — no transit required.
📱
SIM Card & Getting Around
Buy a local SIM at the airport — Telkomsel or XL Axiata, 30-day unlimited data for ~₹800. Grab app works across all of Bali (book cabs, food delivery). Avoid unmetered taxis — always use Grab. Scooter rental is ₹300–400/day and the easiest way to get around. International driving licence required legally.
🌤
Best Time to Visit
April to October (dry season) is ideal — sunny, low humidity, perfect for beaches and outdoor activities. July–August is peak season (book hotels early). November–March is wet season — heavy afternoon downpours but cheaper prices. Avoid December 20–January 5 (overcrowded, expensive). April and May are the sweet spot.
🍛
Food for Indians
Bali food is not spicy by Indian standards. Nasi goreng (fried rice), mie goreng (fried noodles), gado-gado, and satay are safe bets. Vegetarians will find plenty of options — Ubud has excellent vegetarian restaurants. Indian food is available in Seminyak and Kuta if you need a fix. Coconut water is ₹40–60 IDR equivalent.

Bali 5-Day Itinerary Day 1 — Ubud
01
Day One · Cultural Heart of Bali
Ubud — Rice Terraces, Temples & Kecak Fire Dance
Arrive · Tegallalang · Monkey Forest · Ubud Palace · Kecak dance
💰 Budget: ₹2,000–₹3,000 · Couple: ₹6,000–₹10,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Tegallalang Rice Terraces
Bali's most iconic landscape — cascading emerald rice paddies carved into steep hillsides 15km north of Ubud. Arrive before 9am to beat the crowds and get the best light for photos. The famous Instagram swing is here too. Walk the full terrace trail (45 min). Watch your belongings near the parking area.
Entry: ₹500 (~10,000 IDR) · Best light: 7–9am
Afternoon
Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary
Home to over 700 Balinese long-tailed macaques in a lush jungle sanctuary in the heart of Ubud. Three ancient temples inside the forest. The monkeys will steal anything — secure your glasses, earrings, phone, and bags. Don't eat in there. Visit Pura Dalem Agung Padangtegal temple. Genuinely extraordinary atmosphere.
Entry: ₹500 (~10,000 IDR) · Allow 60–90 min
3:00 PM
Ubud Palace & Ubud Art Market
Puri Saren Agung (Ubud Palace) is the former royal residence at the heart of town — free to enter, still a working royal family home. Just opposite is the Ubud Art Market selling sarongs, wood carvings, silver jewellery, batik fabric. Bargain hard — start at 30% of the first asking price, walk away if needed.
Palace: Free · Market: Bargain from 30% of asking price
6:00 PM
Kecak Fire Dance — Uluwatu or Ubud
The most spectacular performance in Bali — 50+ men chanting in trance-like rhythm (no instruments, only voices) as a Ramayana story unfolds in firelight. The Uluwatu temple setting at cliff-edge with the ocean behind is more dramatic. Ubud version is more accessible. Book ahead — sells out in peak season.
₹600 (~120,000 IDR) · Book at your hotel or online
Dinner
Locavore (Luxury) · Warung Babi Guling (Budget)
Budget/Culture: Warung Babi Guling Ibu Oka — Ubud's most famous suckling pig warung, serving Bali's signature dish since 1950s. Full plate ₹400 (~80,000 IDR). The crispy skin is extraordinary. Arrive at noon or 6pm before it sells out. Luxury: Locavore, one of Asia's best restaurants — Balinese tasting menu, ₹5,000–₹8,000/person, book weeks ahead.
Where to stay in Ubud: Book a rice field view villa — waking up to terraced green fields and mist is the definitive Ubud experience. Options: Komaneka at Bisma (luxury, ₹25,000+/night), Bisma Eight (couple, ₹8,000–₹15,000/night), Ketut's Place (budget, ₹2,000–₹4,000/night with rice field view).

Bali 5-Day Itinerary Day 2 — Ubud & Spiritual
02
Day Two · Temples, Treks & Traditions
Ubud — Holy Springs, Ancient Carvings & Volcano Sunrise
Tirta Empul · Gunung Kawi · Batur volcano trek · Cooking class
💰 Budget: ₹1,500–₹2,500 · Couple: ₹5,000–₹10,000 (excl. accommodation)
3:00 AM
Batur Volcano Sunrise Trek (Optional but Unmissable)
Mount Batur (1,717m) is Bali's active volcano — the sunrise trek from the crater rim is one of Southeast Asia's great experiences. Start at 3am with a guide, reach the summit by 5:30am as the sky turns orange over the caldera lake. Active fumaroles, views of Agung and Lombok on clear days. Book through your hotel — never go alone.
₹3,000–₹4,000 (~600,000–800,000 IDR) incl. guide · Return by noon
Morning
Tirta Empul Holy Spring Temple
Bali's most sacred water temple — pilgrims have come to purify themselves in these natural springs since 962 AD. You can participate in the purification ritual in the spring pools (sarong provided, ₹300 entry). Deeply moving experience. Come early (8am) to see genuine devotion before tourist crowds arrive. Respectful dress essential — cover shoulders and legs.
Entry: ₹300 (~60,000 IDR) · Sarong provided · Allow 60 min
10:00 AM
Gunung Kawi Ancient Rock Carvings
Eleven 7-metre royal tombs carved directly into a cliff face in 1080 AD — among the most impressive ancient monuments in Bali and still actively used for prayer. Descend 315 steep steps through rice paddies to reach the site. Return to Ubud via local roads — stop at small family temple ceremonies if you see one (always welcome, dress modestly).
Entry: ₹250 (~50,000 IDR) · Hire scooter ₹300/day
OR: 5:30 AM
Campuhan Ridge Walk (Free Sunrise Alternative)
If you skip the volcano trek, this is Ubud's best free morning activity. A 9km ridge walk through jungle and rice paddies starting from the centre of Ubud. Start at sunrise (5:30am) for golden light and cool air. Completely free. Bring water. Walk to Karsa Kafe for breakfast at the end — set among rice fields, excellent coffee.
Completely free · 9km · Allow 2–3 hours
Afternoon
Balinese Cooking Class
One of Bali's best experiences — visit a local market at 8am to buy ingredients, then learn to cook 5–6 Balinese dishes (nasi goreng, satay lilit, lawar, black rice pudding) in a family compound kitchen. You eat what you cook. Take the recipes home. Offered by dozens of local families around Ubud — Paon Bali and Lobong Culinary are consistently excellent.
₹2,500 (~500,000 IDR) per person incl. market visit and full meal
Volcano trek weather tip: Check weather the night before — cloudy nights mean zero visibility at summit. Your guide will know. Reschedule if the forecast is bad (most operators allow this). Bring a jacket — it's cold at 3am and at the summit even in dry season.

Bali 5-Day Itinerary Day 3 — Seminyak & Canggu
03
Day Three · Beach & Beach Clubs
Seminyak & Canggu — Sunsets, Surf & Beach Clubs
Move to coast · Seminyak sunset · Canggu surf · Tanah Lot optional
💰 Budget: ₹2,500–₹4,000 · Couple: ₹8,000–₹15,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Ubud to Seminyak (1.5 hrs by Grab)
Check out of Ubud and head to Seminyak on the southwest coast — Bali's most stylish beach strip. The drive takes 1.5–2 hours depending on traffic (Bali traffic is notoriously bad). Book Grab in advance from Ubud — metered taxis will quote 3x the Grab price. Check in, then head straight to the beach for lunch.
Grab: ~₹600–₹800 (~130,000 IDR)
Afternoon
Seminyak Beach & Ku De Ta
Seminyak Beach is Bali's most fashionable stretch — wide, clean, excellent surf but strong undertow (swim between flags). Ku De Ta beach club sits right on the sand — world-famous for its sunset views, cocktails and international crowd. Day pass not required — order drinks and you can stay. One cocktail is ~₹900 (180,000 IDR). Worth it for the setting.
3:00 PM
Potato Head Beach Club
Bali's most architecturally impressive beach club — a curving wall of antique wooden window frames forms the entrance. Day pass includes a drinks credit. Infinity pool overlooking the ocean, DJ, great food menu. The iconic Bali beach club experience. Book ahead for weekend visits or peak season.
Day pass: ₹3,000 (~600,000 IDR) incl. drinks credit
4:30 PM
Canggu — Surf Lesson at Echo Beach
Canggu is 20 min north of Seminyak — hipper, younger, more casual. Echo Beach is beginner-friendly with consistent waves and many surf schools on the sand. A 2-hour lesson with board and instructor included. Berawa Beach nearby is more relaxed. Finish the day at La Brisa or Deus Ex Machina Cafe — both iconic Canggu hangouts.
Surf lesson: ₹2,000 (~400,000 IDR) · 2 hrs incl. board
OPTION
Tanah Lot Temple at Sunset (30km from Seminyak)
Bali's most photographed temple — sitting on a sea stack rock surrounded by crashing waves. The sunset silhouette of the temple is iconic. Best visited in the final hour of daylight (5:30–6:30pm). Get there early and walk the full coastal path. Can be done as a detour en route from Ubud to Seminyak or as an afternoon trip.
Entry: ₹600 (~120,000 IDR) · 30km from Seminyak
Beach safety: Bali's west-coast beaches have powerful rip currents and strong undertow. Always swim between the red-and-yellow flags where lifeguards are present. Seminyak, Canggu and Kuta have regular drowning incidents among tourists who ignore the flags — please don't.

Bali 5-Day Itinerary Day 4 — Uluwatu
04
Day Four · The Bukit Peninsula
Uluwatu — Clifftop Temple, Hidden Beaches & Jimbaran Seafood
Move south · Uluwatu temple · Padang Padang · Jimbaran sunset dinner
💰 Budget: ₹2,000–₹3,000 · Couple: ₹7,000–₹15,000 (excl. accommodation)
Morning
Seminyak to Uluwatu (30 min)
Move south to the Bukit Peninsula — Bali's southernmost tip, dramatically different from the north. Clifftop limestone headlands with turquoise ocean below. Check in to your Uluwatu accommodation — the cliff-edge villas here have the best views in Bali. Then head immediately to Uluwatu Temple before the tourist rush.
10:00 AM
Pura Luhur Uluwatu Temple
One of Bali's six key temples, perched 70m above the Indian Ocean on a sheer limestone cliff. The views are spectacular. The resident macaques are aggressive — secure your glasses, earrings, phones and bags (they've learned to steal them and bargain for food). Sarong is provided at entry. Walk the full clifftop path (30 min). Come back at 6pm for the sunset Kecak performance.
Entry: ₹300 (~60,000 IDR) · Sarong included
12:00 PM
Padang Padang Beach
Walk down 100 steps cut into the limestone cliff to reach this stunning small cove — turquoise water, white sand, surrounded by rocky outcrops. Famous from the film Eat Pray Love. Excellent snorkelling on the right side of the cove. Gets busy by noon — arrive before 11am for fewer people. Strong current outside the cove — stay inside.
Entry: ₹150 (~30,000 IDR) · 100 steps down to beach
2:00 PM
Bingin Beach & Single Fin (Sundays Only)
Bingin is Uluwatu's most atmospheric beach — accessed via steep stairs, surrounded by budget warung cafes on the cliff. Great for snorkelling and watching surfers on one of Bali's finest reef breaks. Single Fin is the legendary clifftop bar overlooking the break — their Sunday Sessions (from noon) are the best weekly party in Bali: DJ, good food, incredible views.
No entry fee · Drinks from ₹400 (~80,000 IDR)
Dinner
Jimbaran Seafood on the Beach
Drive 20 min north to Jimbaran Bay — a long crescent beach where dozens of restaurants set tables right on the sand. Order fresh lobster, tiger prawns, red snapper, crab — all grilled over coconut husks with sambal and lime. Candles at sunset, waves lapping at your feet. ₹2,000–₹3,000 for two people with fresh lobster and prawns. Negotiate — prices are often posted but adjust for quantity.
₹2,000–₹3,000 for two · Fresh seafood grilled to order
⚠️ Monkey warning at Uluwatu: The monkeys at Uluwatu temple are notorious thieves. They specifically target glasses, sunglasses, jewellery, phones, hats and bags. If a monkey grabs something, don't pull it back — they can bite. Guards will help negotiate the return (usually with food). Put everything away before you enter.

Bali 5-Day Itinerary Day 5 — Nusa Penida or Departure
05
Day Five · Island Day Trip or Relaxation
Nusa Penida Day Trip — or — Spa & Departure
Fast boat from Sanur · Kelingking Beach · Angel's Billabong · OR Spa day
💰 Nusa Penida day trip: ₹2,500–₹3,500 all-in · Spa day: ₹500–₹1,500
OPTION A
Nusa Penida Full Day Trip (Highly Recommended)
Take the fast boat from Sanur Harbour (45 min, ₹1,200 return). Nusa Penida is a rawer, wilder island with jaw-dropping clifftop scenery. Full day trip packages including boat + private driver + all sites: ₹2,500–₹3,500 per person. Book through your hotel or reputable operator the night before. Early boat (7am) is essential to beat crowds at Kelingking.
Full day trip: ₹2,500–₹3,500 incl. boat + driver · 7am departure
9:00 AM
Kelingking Beach — Bali's Most Iconic View
The T-Rex cliff — a limestone peninsula shaped like a Tyrannosaurus Rex head jutting into turquoise water — is the most photographed view in Indonesia. The viewpoint is at the top. You can hike down to the beach (40 min down, harder back up, slippery — wear good shoes) but the view from the top is the money shot. Go early before it fills with day-trippers.
No entry fee · Strong shoes required if hiking down
11:30 AM
Angel's Billabong & Broken Beach
Angel's Billabong is a natural infinity pool cut into coastal rock — at low tide it fills with clear turquoise seawater and you can swim in it. 200m away is Broken Beach — a dramatic coastal arch with crashing waves inside. One of the most geologically spectacular spots in Bali. Check tide times — Angel's Billabong is accessible only at low tide.
Entry: Small local fee (~₹50) · Check tide times
2:00 PM
Crystal Bay — Manta Ray Snorkelling
Crystal Bay is one of Southeast Asia's best snorkel sites — Mola-Mola (ocean sunfish) spotted July–October, manta rays year-round in the deeper water. Snorkel gear hire: ₹250. The bay itself is sheltered and beautiful even without the marine life. Return fast boat from Nusa Penida at 4:30–5pm, back in Sanur by 5:30pm.
Snorkel gear: ₹250 · Return boat 4:30–5pm
OPTION B
Spa Day & Final Shopping (If Departing)
Bali is famous for its spas — a 2-hour traditional Balinese massage (full body + head + reflexology) costs ₹500–₹700 at a local spa, ₹1,500 at a hotel spa. Jari Menari and Taksu Spa in Ubud are excellent. Then: final shopping at Seminyak's boutiques (local batik, Balinese silver, essential oils), last coconut on the beach, and Grab to Ngurah Rai airport (DPS).
2hr Balinese massage: ₹500–₹1,500 · Airport Grab: ₹400–₹600
Nusa Penida safety: The roads on Nusa Penida are steep, narrow and poorly maintained — do not hire a scooter unless you're an experienced rider. Always book a private driver for the day (included in most packages). The roads to Kelingking in particular are extremely challenging.
