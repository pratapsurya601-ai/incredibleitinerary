Day 1
⛪ Sagrada Família
Antoni Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece — under construction since 1882 and still not complete. The most visited monument in Spain. Towers soaring 172m high, facades covered in organic stone carvings, an interior that looks like a stone forest. Book tickets months ahead — they regularly sell out. Add the tower access for extraordinary city views.
Day 2
🌿 Park Güell & El Born
Gaudí's otherworldly public park — mosaic benches, gingerbread gatehouses, colonnaded hall and terrace with panoramic city views. Timed entry required. Afternoon in El Born — Barcelona's most fashionable neighbourhood, with Picasso Museum, boutiques and the magnificent 19th-century Boqueria-style iron market El Mercat del Born.
Day 3
🏘️ Gothic Quarter & Beach
The Barri Gòtic — 2,000 years of Roman walls, medieval lanes, Barcelona Cathedral and the Jewish quarter — is one of Europe's most atmospheric old cities. Las Ramblas (watch for pickpockets), the legendary Boqueria market, and Barceloneta beach — the Mediterranean at its most beautiful in the afternoon light.
Day 4–5
⚽ Camp Nou & Day Trips
Day 4: Camp Nou stadium tour (FC Barcelona), Montjuïc hill (castle, Olympic stadium, MNAC museum). Day 5: Sitges day trip (40min by train — charming coastal town with beautiful beach) or Montserrat (1hr — serrated mountain monastery with extraordinary views, cable car up).

"Barcelona is a city that has everything: sea, architecture, food, nightlife, culture. It is perhaps the most complete city in Europe." — Anonymous traveller, and entirely correct.

Visa & Flights: Schengen visa required — apply at Spanish Consulate/VFS Global (₹7,000 approx, 3–4 weeks). Flights: Delhi/Mumbai to Barcelona (BCN) — ₹40,000–₹72,000 return via Middle Eastern hubs. Aerobus from airport to Plaça Catalunya: ₹500, 35 minutes. The T-Casual Metro card (10 trips, ₹1,200) covers all Metro and bus travel in central Barcelona.

Day One · Gaudí's Masterpiece
Sagrada Família & Gaudí Houses
Morning Sagrada Família · Towers · Gaudí houses walking route · Passeig de Gràcia · Tapas evening
💰 Sagrada Família: ₹2,800 · Towers: ₹3,500 (book months ahead) · Metro: ₹1,200 10-trip card
9:00 AM
Sagrada Família — Book Months Ahead, Arrive at Opening
The Basílica de la Sagrada Família is the most visited site in Spain — over 4.5 million visitors per year. Antoni Gaudí devoted the last 43 years of his life to this project (he is buried in the crypt). Book tickets at sagradafamilia.org — they regularly sell out weeks ahead in peak season. The Nativity facade (Gaudí's original) faces northeast, the Passion facade faces southwest. The interior — designed to replicate a forest of stone columns branching into a canopy — is unlike any church interior in the world. The stained glass on the Nativity side creates warm golden light in the morning. Tower access (extra ₹700) gives the finest city view.
Basic entry: ₹2,800 · With towers: ₹3,500 · Book at sagradafamilia.org · Allow 2–3 hours
Noon
Gaudí Houses Walking Route — Passeig de Gràcia
The Block of Discord (Manzana de la Discordia) on Passeig de Gràcia has three competing modernista buildings on the same block: Casa Batlló (Gaudí — dragon scale roof, ₹3,500 entry), Casa Amatller (Puig i Cadafalch), and Casa Lleó Morera (Domènech i Montaner). Casa Milà (La Pedrera) is 2 blocks away — Gaudí's undulating stone apartment building with rooftop warrior chimneys (₹2,800). Passeig de Gràcia is Barcelona's most elegant boulevard — the luxury shopping equivalent of Paris's Champs-Élysées.
Casa Batlló: ₹3,500 · Casa Milà: ₹2,800 · Outside photo: free · Tapas lunch: ₹800–₹1,500

Day Two · Park & Neighbourhood
Park Güell, Gràcia & Palau de la Música
Morning Park Güell (timed entry) · Gràcia neighbourhood lunch · Palau de la Música Catalana · El Born evening
💰 Park Güell: ₹1,200 · Palau de la Música: ₹2,000 · El Born dinner: ₹1,500–₹3,000
9:30 AM
Park Güell — Gaudí's Mosaic Fantasy in the Hills
Originally commissioned as a residential park that never sold, Park Güell is now one of Barcelona's most popular sites. The monumental zone (ticketed area — ₹1,200, buy online at parkguell.barcelona) includes the Dragon Staircase, the Hall of a Hundred Columns (actually 86), and the Great Terrace with its famous dragon mosaic bench and panoramic views over the city and Mediterranean. The free park surrounding the monumental zone is lovely for walking. Go early — the terrace gets very crowded by 11am. The park is in the Gràcia neighbourhood — explore its charming plazas after.
Monumental zone: ₹1,200 · Book at parkguell.barcelona · Free areas: always open · Go at 9:30am
2:00 PM
Palau de la Música Catalana — The Most Beautiful Concert Hall in Europe
Designed by Lluís Domènech i Montaner (Gaudí's great rival), the Palau de la Música Catalana (1908) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and arguably the most beautiful concert hall in the world. The stained glass ceiling — an inverted dome of blue, green and amber glass — floods the auditorium with natural light during daytime concerts. Guided tours available (₹2,000, highly recommended). Evening concerts (check palaumusica.cat) — attending an actual concert here is an extraordinary experience (₹2,500–₹5,000 for tickets).
Guided tour: ₹2,000 · Concert tickets: ₹2,500–₹5,000 · Book at palaumusica.cat

Day Three · Old City & Sea
Gothic Quarter, Boqueria Market & Barceloneta
Gothic Quarter walking · Barcelona Cathedral · Boqueria lunch · Las Ramblas · Barceloneta beach afternoon
💰 Cathedral: free (entrance fee at certain hours) · Boqueria lunch: ₹600–₹1,200 · Beach: free
9:00 AM
Gothic Quarter — 2,000 Years of Barcelona
The Barri Gòtic (Gothic Quarter) is built on and around a 1st century BC Roman settlement — you can still see sections of the original Roman wall. Carrer del Bisbe with its neo-Gothic bridge, Plaça de Sant Jaume (city government square), Plaça del Rei (the medieval royal palace — one of the finest Gothic secular spaces in Europe), and the Jewish quarter (El Call) with its dark, narrow lanes and medieval synagogue. Barcelona Cathedral — free entry, with 13 white geese living in the cloister (city tradition, 600 years old). The best way: wander without a map and let the quarter reveal itself.
Gothic Quarter: free to wander · Barcelona Cathedral: free (cloister €3 / ₹280) · 2 hours minimum
11:30 AM
Mercat de la Boqueria — But Watch the Pickpockets on Las Ramblas
La Boqueria is Barcelona's most famous covered market — extraordinary produce stalls, seafood, jamón ibérico, fresh juice (₹200), chocolate, dried fruits. It's beautiful but extremely crowded and tourist-priced — eat at the market bars at the back (counter seating, locals eat here). Las Ramblas is the famous tree-lined pedestrian boulevard connecting the city to the sea — it's lovely but has the highest concentration of pickpockets in Barcelona. Keep your bag in front. Do not accept free flowers (they charge you). Do not use the casas de cambio (exchange bureaux) on the Ramblas — rates are terrible.
Boqueria entry: free · Fresh juice: ₹200 · Lunch at market bar: ₹600–₹1,000 · Watch your belongings!
3:00 PM
Barceloneta Beach — The Mediterranean at Its Most Glorious
Barcelona's city beach — 1.1km of sand directly accessible from the Gothic Quarter by a 15-minute walk. The Mediterranean here is cleaner than you might expect — clear blue-green water, gentle waves, excellent for swimming May–October. Rent a lounger and umbrella (₹500) or bring your own towel. The Barceloneta neighbourhood behind the beach has excellent seafood restaurants on its grid of narrow streets. Paella (fideuà — made with noodles instead of rice is the local version) is best eaten at lunch, not dinner — restaurants cook it fresh at midday. El Chiringuito (beach bars) serve cold beer and snacks.
Beach: free · Lounger & umbrella: ₹500 · Barceloneta fideuà lunch: ₹1,200–₹2,000/person

9:30 AM
Camp Nou — The Cathedral of Football
FC Barcelona's stadium (capacity: 99,354 — Europe's largest) underwent a major renovation in 2023–2026. The Barça Experience museum tour (₹2,500) takes you through the dressing rooms, the dugout, the press box, and the pitch-side tunnels. The club's history — Messi, Cruyff, Ronaldinho — is told through extraordinary memorabilia. Football lovers should try to book an actual match ticket (FCB vs. any La Liga opponent) — the atmosphere is electrifying. Check fcbarcelona.com for fixtures and tickets 6–8 weeks ahead.
Museum tour: ₹2,500 · Match tickets: ₹4,000–₹18,000 · Book at fcbarcelona.com
Afternoon
Montjuïc — Castle, Cable Car & MNAC Museum
Take the Montjuïc cable car (Teleféric de Montjuïc — ₹1,800 return) to the hilltop. The Castell de Montjuïc (17th-century fortress) has panoramic views over the port and city. The MNAC (Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya) in the magnificent Palau Nacional has the world's finest collection of Romanesque art — 11th-13th century frescoes transferred from Pyrenean churches in the early 20th century, uniquely spectacular. The view from the MNAC's esplanade down the Avinguda de la Reina Maria Cristina to the sea is another of Barcelona's great vistas.
Cable car: ₹1,800 return · Castell: ₹800 · MNAC: ₹1,200 · Free first Sunday each month

Barcelona Food Culture — Essential Knowledge
Pan con tomate (bread rubbed with tomato and olive oil) comes with everything — and should. Patatas bravas (fried potatoes with spicy sauce) are the definitive tapas. Jamón ibérico bellota (acorn-fed ham) is extraordinary. Eat paella at LUNCH not dinner — it's cooked fresh midday only. Vermouth (vermut) before lunch is the Catalan aperitivo tradition — try it at a traditional bar.
🎫
Sagrada Família — Book Months, Not Weeks Ahead
In July and August, Sagrada Família tickets sell out 6–8 weeks ahead. In May, June, September and October, book 3–4 weeks ahead. The website is sagradafamilia.org. Pre-booked tickets always include skip-the-queue entry — without a pre-booked ticket you join a long standby queue and may not get in at all on busy days.
🌡️
Best Months — Avoid July–August
July and August: 30–35°C, extremely crowded, beaches are packed. May–June and September–October: 22–27°C, manageable crowds, beautiful light. October is the secret best month — warm enough for the beach, empty museums, lower prices. March–April: 15–20°C, excellent for sightseeing.
🦺
Pickpockets — A Real Issue in Barcelona
Barcelona has one of Europe's highest pickpocket rates, concentrated on Las Ramblas, La Boqueria, Metro Line 3, and Barceloneta beach. Use a cross-body bag worn in front. Keep phones in front pockets. Avoid sitting near doors on the Metro. Never put down a bag on a restaurant chair. Distraction scams: someone spills something on you while an accomplice picks your pocket.
