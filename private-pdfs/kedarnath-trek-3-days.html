Trek Route
🥾 Gaurikund to Kedarnath
Gaurikund (1,982m) → Jungle Chatti (4km, 2,700m) → Bheembali (7km, 3,100m) → Linchauri (10km, 3,250m) → Kedarnath (16km, 3,583m). Total ascent: 1,601m. Trail is paved in lower sections, rocky above Bheembali. Allow 5–7 hours for ascent.
Non-Trek Options
🚁 Helicopter & Pony
Helicopter from Phata, Sirsi or Guptkashi helipads: ₹5,000–₹8,000 one way. Book on official Uttarakhand helicopter portal — fills up weeks ahead in season. Pony: ₹2,500–₹3,500 one way from Gaurikund. Doli (palanquin, 4 bearers): ₹5,000–₹6,000 one way. These options serve those with mobility constraints or health conditions.
At the Temple
🕉️ The Jyotirlinga
One of 12 Jyotirlinga shrines — among the most sacred places in Hinduism. The stone temple dates to the 8th century (rebuilt multiple times). A large naturally occurring rock forms the Shiva lingam. The 2013 floods destroyed everything around the temple but the structure stood — the massive boulder behind it deflected the glacier outburst. The spiritual atmosphere is profound.
Beyond the Temple
🏔️ Bhairon Temple & Scenery
Bhairon Nath Temple sits 500m above Kedarnath temple — a 30-minute uphill walk. The views from here of the entire Kedarnath valley, the temple below, and the ring of Himalayan peaks (Kedarnath, Kedar Dome, Bharatekuntha) are extraordinary. Many pilgrims miss this. Do not miss this.

"At 3,583 metres, with snow on the ground in June, looking at a stone temple that has stood for 1,200 years — you understand why people walk 16km to stand here."

Altitude Sickness Warning: Kedarnath at 3,583m is genuinely high altitude. Do NOT arrive from Rishikesh and trek the same day. Spend at least one night at Sonprayag or Gaurikund (below 2,000m) to acclimatise. Symptoms: headache, nausea, dizziness — descend immediately if severe. Deaths occur every season. Cardiac patients, those with lung conditions, or poorly acclimatised trekkers are at serious risk.

Kedarnath Trek — 3-Day Guide Day-by-Day Trek Plan
01
Day One · Acclimatisation
Reach Sonprayag — The Essential Rest Day
Rishikesh → Sonprayag/Gaurikund (220km, 6–7hrs) · Register at checkpost · Rest and prepare
💰 Shared taxi from Rishikesh: ₹400–₹600 · Private car: ₹3,500–₹5,000 · Stay: ₹500–₹2,000
5:00 AM
Rishikesh/Haridwar → Sonprayag (220km, 6–7hrs)
Depart early from Rishikesh — shared taxis and SUVs leave from the main bus stand from 5am. The route climbs through Devprayag (beautiful Ganga confluence viewpoint, stop for 20 minutes), Srinagar Garhwal (fuel stop), Rudraprayag (another major Himalayan confluence), then up to Sonprayag. The last section beyond Rudraprayag is mountain road with views of the Mandakini river gorge. Sonprayag sits at 1,800m. Some travellers push on to Gaurikund (1,982m) for a slightly better acclimatisation altitude and to be at the trek start.
Shared taxi Rishikesh → Sonprayag: ₹400–₹600 · Private car: ₹3,500–₹5,000
Afternoon
Registration Checkpost + Gaurikund Hot Spring
At Sonprayag, your Char Dham registration is verified at the official checkpost — have your registration printout/SMS and photo ID ready. Shared jeeps then transport you the final 5km to Gaurikund (₹30–50). Gaurikund has the Gauri hot spring — Goddess Parvati is said to have meditated here. Bathing in the warm sulphur spring is traditional before beginning the trek. The water is genuinely warm and therapeutic. Organise your pony or porter for tomorrow if needed.
Checkpost: registration verification · Gaurikund jeep: ₹30–₹50 · Hot spring: free
Evening
Preparation Evening — Critical for Success
This evening matters for tomorrow's success. Eat a proper carbohydrate-rich dinner. Hydrate heavily. Pack your day bag: 1.5L water, glucose biscuits, energy bars, warm layer (it will be cold at 3,583m even in June), rain poncho, headlamp, medications. Carry no unnecessary weight — every extra kilo costs you on the final climb. Cash only beyond Gaurikund. Boots should be broken in — blisters at altitude are miserable. Sleep by 9pm.
Accommodation Gaurikund: ₹500–₹1,500 · GMVN dormitory: ₹300 · Day bag: 5–7kg max
02
Day Two · The Sacred Trek
Gaurikund to Kedarnath — 16km, 1,600m Climb
5am start · Gaurikund → Jungle Chatti → Bheembali → Linchauri → Kedarnath · Evening darshan
💰 Trek: free · Pony: ₹2,500–₹3,500 · Lunch en route: ₹100–₹200 · Camp at Kedarnath: ₹500–₹1,000
5:00 AM
Trek Begins — Gaurikund to Jungle Chatti (4km)
Start at dawn — the trail gets extremely crowded midday. The first 4km to Jungle Chatti (2,700m) is a gradual climb through rhododendron and oak forest. This section is the easiest and most beautiful. The sound of the Mandakini river, mist in the trees, other pilgrims with their morning energy — this initial section is joyful. Jungle Chatti has tea stalls and rest points. Take a break, drink fluids, don't rush.
4km · 1.5hrs at moderate pace · Jungle Chatti tea: ₹20
7:00 AM
Jungle Chatti to Bheembali (3km more, 7km total)
The gradient increases steadily. Bheembali (3,100m) is the midway meal point — a cluster of tea stalls and basic food stalls serve Maggi noodles, tea, poori-sabzi, and energy drinks. Stop here for a proper meal and a 20-minute rest. Your body needs fuel for the final climb. The views of the Kedarnath valley begin opening up from here. If you've hired a pony, the pony route overlaps the trekking route — walk to one side to avoid the animals.
3km · 1.5hrs · Bheembali meal: ₹60–₹150 · Rest 20 min mandatory
9:00 AM
Bheembali to Linchauri to Kedarnath (9km more)
The final 9km is the most demanding — altitude is taking effect, the trail is rockier, the air is thinner. Linchauri (10km, 3,250m) has a checkpoint and rest area. Above Linchauri, the forest gives way to alpine meadow. You begin seeing snowfields. The Kedarnath temple appears as you round the final ridge — a small stone structure dwarfed by enormous snow-covered peaks on three sides. The moment of first sighting is genuinely moving for most trekkers regardless of religious affiliation.
9km · 3–4hrs · Altitude: 3,583m · Take it slow above Linchauri
1:00 PM
Rest, Acclimatise, Then Darshan
Arrive at Kedarnath, check into your tent/GMVN accommodation (₹500–₹1,000/night, book ahead), eat and rest for 2 hours. Your body needs recovery time after a 1,600m climb. Then join the darshan queue — afternoon queues are manageable (1–2 hours). The temple interior is cool and dark; the Shiva lingam is touched by thousands of hands daily. The evening aarti (around 7pm) performed by the priests with the glacial landscape as backdrop is extraordinary.
GMVN tent: ₹500–₹800 · Private tent: ₹800–₹1,200 · Darshan: free · Abhishek pooja: ₹500–₹1,500
Dusk
Bhairon Nath Temple — 500m Above, Worth Every Step
Above the main temple complex, a 30-minute steep climb leads to the Bhairon Nath Temple. Bhairon (a form of Shiva) is considered the guardian deity of Kedarnath. The temple is small but the views from up here — the entire bowl of the Kedarnath valley, the temple at your feet, glacial peaks in a semicircle — are among the finest mountain views in India. Many pilgrims skip this. Go.
500m above main temple · 30min climb · Free · Best at dusk before it gets dark
03
Day Three · Dawn Darshan & Return
5am Abhishek, Trek Down, Depart
4:30am wake · 5am Abhishek darshan · 7am begin descent · 16km back down · Depart valley
💰 Abhishek darshan: ₹1,500 · Descent: free · Return taxi from Sonprayag: ₹400–₹600
4:30 AM
Early Morning — The Temple at Dawn
Wake in the dark at Kedarnath. Step outside — stars, silence, the outline of glacial peaks against the pre-dawn sky, and the temple lit by flickering lamps. This is what 3,583m feels like before the world wakes. If you've booked the Abhishek darshan (₹1,500, extremely limited seats — book through the Kedarnath Trust at the temple the previous evening), you will be inside the temple at 5am watching priests perform the personal ritual bath of the Shiva lingam with milk, honey, flowers, and water. An intensely private and spiritual experience unavailable during regular darshan hours.
Abhishek darshan: ₹1,500 · Limited to ~50 people · Book evening before at trust office
7:00 AM
The Descent — Easier but Watch Your Knees
The return trek to Gaurikund (16km) takes 3–5 hours descending. The descent is harder on knees than the ascent — trekking poles are invaluable here. Pace yourself on the rocky sections. Stop at Bheembali for lunch. The forest sections on the lower half are beautiful in the morning light. Arrive Gaurikund by noon-1pm. Take the jeep back to Sonprayag, then a taxi back to Rishikesh. You will sleep well tonight.
Descent: 3–5hrs · Trekking poles essential · Gaurikund-Sonprayag jeep: ₹30–₹50

Kedarnath Trek — 3-Day Guide Altitude Safety, Tips & Packing
Altitude Safety — Read This Before You Go

Know Before You Climb

Altitude Sickness Protocol: Acclimatise at least one night below 2,000m (Rishikesh or Rudraprayag) before ascending to Kedarnath. Do NOT rush the ascent. Symptoms of Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS): persistent headache, nausea, vomiting, extreme fatigue, loss of coordination, confusion. If symptoms appear above Linchauri — descend immediately. Do not sleep it off at altitude. Diamox (acetazolamide 250mg twice daily) helps prevent AMS — start 24 hours before ascent, consult your doctor. Stay hydrated (3L+ water per day). Avoid alcohol. Cardiac patients should consult a physician before undertaking this trek.
📋
Registration is Mandatory — Do It Before Arriving
Register on devasthanam.uk.gov.in. Free, takes 10 minutes with Aadhaar details. Carry printout + original ID. Checkpost at Sonprayag verifies this before allowing you to proceed. Without registration you will be turned back regardless of how far you've travelled. Also: no entry for those with uncontrolled diabetes, hypertension or cardiac conditions per government guidelines.
🌦️
Weather Changes in Minutes at Altitude
A clear morning at Gaurikund can become a snowstorm at Kedarnath by afternoon. This is not an exaggeration — it has happened in June. Always carry a rain poncho and warm layer regardless of morning weather. Check local weather via the official Kedarnath weather service. If warned of bad weather, do not attempt the trek — the consequences can be fatal.
💊
Medical Facilities on the Route
Basic first aid posts exist at Jungle Chatti, Bheembali, Linchauri, and at Kedarnath itself. A government medical camp at Kedarnath handles altitude-related emergencies. However, evacuation from Kedarnath in an emergency requires helicopter (weather permitting) or a long carry down the mountain. Prevention is everything — do not ignore warning signs.
🐴
Pony and Doli — Official Rates Only
Pony and doli rates are government-fixed and posted at the official booking counters at Gaurikund. Book only at official counters — do not book through agents in Rishikesh who charge commissions. Pony operators are not permitted to overcharge. If quoted above official rates, report to the checkpost. Ponies are mule-type animals (not horses) and are extremely sure-footed on mountain trails.
💸
Cash Only Beyond Gaurikund
No ATMs exist at Kedarnath. The nearest working ATM is in Sonprayag. Carry at least ₹3,000–₹5,000 cash for food, accommodation, porter tips, and pooja at the temple. Card machines exist at GMVN counter at Kedarnath but connectivity is unreliable. Don't count on it.
📵
No Network Above Sonprayag
BSNL has some coverage in patches but most private networks (Jio, Airtel, Vi) have no signal above Sonprayag. Download offline maps of the route on Google Maps or Maps.me before departing Rishikesh. Inform your family/friends of your plan and check in when you return to Sonprayag. WhatsApp a location before you lose signal.
🕉️
Temple Etiquette
Remove footwear before the temple complex. No leather items permitted inside. No photography inside the sanctum — strictly enforced. Dress modestly. The queue can be 1–3 hours long at peak times (10am–2pm). For a calmer experience, join the Abhishek morning darshan (5am, limited tickets) or arrive for last darshan before the temple closes (9pm).
❄️
Snow in June — Yes, Really
Kedarnath opens in late April/early May and the surrounding terrain is often snow-covered until mid-June. Snow on the trail makes it slippery — microspikes or careful footwork required. After the season's first heavy snow, the trail can be closed for 1–2 days. Check conditions on the official Uttarakhand tourism portal before departing. September–October is often the most stable weather window.
What to Carry — Kedarnath Trek
🧥 Warm jacket — down or heavy fleece. Temperatures at 3,583m: 2–10°C even in June.
🌧️ Rain poncho — sudden rain/snow above Linchauri. ₹200 from any hill town.
👟 Trekking shoes — waterproof, ankle support. Non-negotiable on rocky terrain.
🏑 Trekking poles — essential for the descent. Saves knees on 16km downhill.
💧 2L water bottle — fill at Bheembali. Stay hydrated — 3L+ per day at altitude.
🍫 Energy food — glucose biscuits, dry fruits, energy bars, ORS sachets.
💊 Medical kit — paracetamol, ORS, Diamox (doctor-prescribed), antacid, bandages.
🔦 Headlamp — 5am start in the dark. Spare batteries essential.
💰 Cash ₹3,000–₹5,000 — no ATMs at Kedarnath. Carry in waterproof bag.
