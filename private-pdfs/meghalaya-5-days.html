Day 1
🏙️ Shillong
The "Scotland of the East" — India's only rock and roll city. Colonial-era architecture, Ward's Lake, the vibrant Laitumkhrah cafe district, Don Bosco Museum (exceptional tribal culture collection), Police Bazaar. Laid-back, cosmopolitan for a hill station, excellent food and music scene.
Day 2
🌊 Cherrapunji (Sohra)
Cherrapunji receives 12,000mm of rainfall annually — one of the wettest places on Earth. Nohkalikai Falls (340m — India's highest plunge waterfall), Seven Sisters Falls, limestone caves, and valleys of perpetual mist and waterfalls. Even in the dry season the landscape here feels like another world.
Day 3
🌿 Mawlynnong & Dawki
Mawlynnong — declared Asia's cleanest village, living root bridge walk, bamboo sky-walk with views into Bangladesh. Dawki — the Umngot river is so transparent the boats appear to float on air. Bangladesh border visible from the bridge. One of the most photogenic river scenes in India.
Day 4
🌉 Living Root Bridges
The Khasi tribe has trained the roots of rubber fig trees across streams for 500+ years, creating living bridges that grow stronger over time. The double-decker root bridge at Nongriat (3,500 steps each way) is the most famous. A single-deck bridge at Mawlynnong is accessible in 10 minutes. The Laitlum Canyons (Meghalaya's Grand Canyon) at sunset are free and extraordinary.

"Meghalaya is what happens when a cloud decides to stay — 5,000 years of rain, moss, and roots growing into living architecture across rushing streams."

How to Reach: Fly to Guwahati (GUW) — direct flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata (₹3,000–₹8,000 one way). From Guwahati airport, take a shared Sumo/taxi to Shillong (3hrs, 100km). Shared Sumo: ₹400–₹500 from Paltan Bazaar stand. Private taxi: ₹2,000–₹2,500. No ILP required for Indian citizens in Meghalaya.
Cultural Note: Meghalaya has three main tribes — Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo. The Khasi and Jaintia are matrilineal — property, surname and inheritance pass through the mother. Women hold significant social and economic power here. This makes Meghalaya notably safe for solo female travel. Respect local customs; dress modestly in villages away from tourist areas.

Meghalaya 5-Day Itinerary Days 1–3 — Shillong, Cherrapunji & Mawlynnong
01
Day One · Guwahati to Shillong
The Scotland of the East
Fly to Guwahati → 3hr drive to Shillong · Ward's Lake · Don Bosco Museum · Laitumkhrah cafe district
💰 Guwahati–Shillong Sumo: ₹400–₹500 · Don Bosco Museum: ₹100 · Food: ₹300–₹600
Morning
Guwahati → Shillong (100km, 3hrs)
From Guwahati airport or railway station, shared Sumos (large shared taxis) leave for Shillong from Paltan Bazaar and ISBT bus stand. ₹400–₹500 per seat. The route climbs through the Meghalaya hills — a dramatic transition from the Brahmaputra plains to the cloud-draped highlands. Views begin opening up after Jorabat. Arrive Shillong (1,500m) by noon if departing early.
Shared Sumo: ₹400–₹500 · Private taxi: ₹2,000–₹2,500 · 3hrs
Afternoon
Don Bosco Museum — The Best Tribal Culture Museum in Northeast India
Seven floors of meticulously curated exhibits on the cultures, traditions, textiles, weapons, music, religious practices and history of the tribes of Northeast India. The rooftop Sky Walk has panoramic views of Shillong. This is genuinely one of the finest ethnographic museums in Asia — give it 2–3 hours. Entry ₹100. Photography permitted. The gift shop has authentic northeast tribal crafts.
Entry: ₹100 · Hours: 9am–5:30pm · 2–3hrs recommended
Evening
Ward's Lake + Laitumkhrah Cafe District
Ward's Lake is a colonial-era artificial lake in the centre of Shillong — pleasant walking paths, pedal boats, willow trees. More interesting is the Laitumkhrah area — Shillong's hip neighbourhood with independent cafes, live music venues, bookshops, and incredible food. Try jadoh (Khasi red rice with pork), dohneiiong (pork with black sesame), and tungtap fish chutney at any local eatery. Shillong is exceptional for food.
Ward's Lake: ₹10 entry · Cafe dinner: ₹200–₹500 · Live music venues: ₹0–₹200 cover
02
Day Two · Cherrapunji (Sohra)
Waterfalls, Caves & the Wettest Place on Earth
Shillong → Cherrapunji (54km) · Nohkalikai Falls · Mawsmai Cave · Eco Park · Seven Sisters Falls
💰 Mawsmai Cave: ₹50 · Eco Park: ₹20 · Nohkalikai viewpoint: ₹50 · Taxi day: ₹2,000–₹2,500
9:00 AM
Shillong → Cherrapunji (54km, 1.5hrs)
Hire a local taxi for the day from Shillong to cover Cherrapunji and return (₹2,000–₹2,500). The road to Cherrapunji is extraordinarily scenic — the plateau edge with views 2,000m down into Bangladesh, mist rolling up from below, sudden patches of dense forest. Cherrapunji (now officially Sohra) sits at 1,430m. The landscape feels perpetually damp and deeply lush even in the dry season.
Day taxi Shillong–Cherrapunji circuit: ₹2,000–₹2,500 · Distance 54km
10:30 AM
Nohkalikai Falls — India's Highest Plunge Waterfall (340m)
The most dramatic waterfall in India. Nohkalikai drops 340 metres off the plateau edge into a brilliant green pool below. In monsoon it becomes a roaring white curtain visible for kilometres. Even in dry season the volume is significant. The name comes from a tragic local legend (Ka Likai was a woman — the falls mark her fate). The viewpoint (₹50) gives you a dramatic edge-of-the-world perspective. Do not skip this.
Viewpoint entry: ₹50 · Best in monsoon (off-season for tourism) · 20-min walk from road
12:00 PM
Mawsmai Cave — Limestone Cavern Walk
Mawsmai Cave (₹50) is a 150-metre-long limestone cave with stalactites, stalagmites, and formations illuminated by path lighting. It's narrow in sections — not suitable for claustrophobics — but well-maintained and genuinely beautiful. The cave is one of hundreds in the Cherrapunji area; the heavy rainfall over millennia has created extraordinary cave systems throughout this limestone plateau.
Entry: ₹50 · 30min walk-through · Narrow sections — wear comfortable clothes
Afternoon
Eco Park + Thangkharang Park — Bangladesh Views
Eco Park (₹20) has viewpoints over the Bangladesh plains 1,500m below — on clear days you can see for 100km into the flat green expanse of Bangladesh. Seven Sisters Falls is visible from here when water flow is good (monsoon season is best). Thangkharang Park has similar Bangladesh panoramic views. Both are quiet and undervisited — take your time here.
Eco Park: ₹20 · Thangkharang: ₹30 · Seven Sisters Falls: best in monsoon
03
Day Three · Mawlynnong & Dawki
Asia's Cleanest Village & the Invisible River
Shillong → Mawlynnong (90km) · Root bridge · Sky Walk → Dawki river boat ride · Bangladesh border
💰 Mawlynnong entry: ₹50 · Dawki boat: ₹600–₹800 · Full day taxi: ₹3,000–₹3,500
8:00 AM
Mawlynnong Village — Asia's Cleanest Village
Mawlynnong, 90km from Shillong, was awarded "Asia's Cleanest Village" by Discover India magazine. The village is genuinely immaculate — bamboo dustbins, swept paths, flowers everywhere, no litter in sight. The Khasi tribe here practices a 100% literacy rate and a strong community cleanliness ethic. Walking through the village is a meditative experience. The single-deck living root bridge is a 10-minute walk from the village — ancient, mossy, beautiful and walkable.
Mawlynnong entry: ₹50 · Root bridge walk: free · Village bamboo sky-walk: ₹30
11:00 AM
Mawlynnong Bamboo Sky-Walk — Bangladesh Views
A bamboo viewing platform rises above the jungle canopy adjacent to Mawlynnong. On clear days you can see the flat plains of Bangladesh stretching to the horizon. The platform sways slightly in the wind — not for the faint-hearted, but entirely stable. The surrounding jungle from above, with clouds drifting through, is memorable.
Sky-walk: ₹30 · 10min climb · View extends into Bangladesh on clear days
1:00 PM
Dawki — The River That Isn't There
Dawki, 16km from Mawlynnong, sits on the Umngot river — arguably the clearest river in India and one of the clearest in Asia. The water is so transparent that boats appear to float on air, their shadows visible on the riverbed below. Boat rides (₹600–₹800, 30–45 minutes) let you float over multicoloured pebbles in 4 metres of perfectly clear water. The suspension bridge over the Umngot is the India–Bangladesh border crossing. A surreal and beautiful place.
Boat ride: ₹600–₹800/boat (4–6 people) · 30–45min · Best Oct–Nov and Feb–Mar

04
Day Four · The Living Root Bridge Trek
Nongriat Double-Decker Root Bridge
3,500 stone steps down into a jungle valley · Double-decker root bridge · Swim in natural pools · Laitlum Canyon sunset
💰 Nongriat trek: free · Guide: ₹500–₹800 · Laitlum Canyons: free
7:00 AM
Tyrna Village → Nongriat Root Bridge Trek (3,500 Steps)
The trek to Nongriat's double-decker root bridge is one of the most unique and rewarding walks in India. From Tyrna village (12km from Cherrapunji — day taxi needed, overnight in Cherrapunji makes sense), 3,500 stone steps descend steeply into a jungle valley — waterfalls, dense ferns, the sound of rushing streams, living root bridges across every water crossing. The double-decker bridge at the bottom has two layers of living root architecture trained over 200+ years. Swim in the Rainbow Falls natural pool nearby. Return is 3,500 steps back up. Allow 5–6 hours for the complete round trip. Hire a guide from Tyrna (₹500–₹800) — they know the path well and can point out biodiversity.
Trek: free · Guide: ₹500–₹800 · Round trip: 5–6hrs · Bring lunch and 2L water
Afternoon
Easier Alternative: Mawlynnong Single-Deck Root Bridge
For those who prefer not to tackle 3,500 steps, the single-deck living root bridge at Mawlynnong is a 10-minute walk from the village — fully accessible, equally ancient, still growing. The bridge carries a person's weight easily — walk across, feel the roots and bark beneath your feet, watch the clear stream below. The smaller scale makes it no less miraculous.
Sunset
Laitlum Canyons — Meghalaya's Grand Canyon at Dusk
Laitlum Canyons (40km from Shillong, free entry) is the most underrated place in Meghalaya. The Khasi Hills drop dramatically into deep gorges here — a sweeping canyon landscape draped in cloud and jungle. At sunset, the mist rises from below, turning golden in the slanted light. Almost no tourists visit this relative to its extraordinary visual drama. Drive here for the last 2 hours of daylight.
Laitlum Canyons: free · 40km from Shillong · Sunset timing essential · Drive access
05
Day Five · Return or Extend
Shillong Morning, Then Guwahati
Morning Shillong — Police Bazaar market · Drive back to Guwahati (3hrs) · Or extend to Kaziranga rhino safari
💰 Police Bazaar shopping · Return Sumo: ₹400–₹500 · Kaziranga extension: see NE India guide
Morning
Shillong Police Bazaar — Last Shopping and Food
Police Bazaar is Shillong's main market — excellent for Khasi handicrafts (bamboo products, handwoven textiles, beaded jewellery), Meghalaya honey, dried shiitake mushrooms, and smoked meats. Try a last bowl of jadoh (red rice with pork) at any local eatery. The Bara Bazaar area has wholesale northeast handicrafts at very reasonable prices. Buy cinnamon and black pepper from the Northeast spice stalls — some of the best in India.
Police Bazaar shopping: budget as desired · Northeast handicrafts from ₹200 · Jadoh meal: ₹80–₹150
Noon
Drive Back to Guwahati or Extend to Kaziranga
Return shared Sumo to Guwahati (₹400–₹500, 3hrs) for your flight. OR — highly recommended extension — continue 4 hours from Shillong to Kaziranga National Park (Assam) for 2 nights of one-horned rhinoceros and elephant safaris. Kaziranga is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with the highest density of one-horned rhinos anywhere on Earth. See the full Northeast India 10-day guide for details on combining Meghalaya and Kaziranga.
Sumo back to Guwahati: ₹400–₹500 · Kaziranga extension: 2 extra days

Meghalaya 5-Day Itinerary Insider Tips & Packing
Insider Tips — Read Before You Go

Know Before You Go

🌧️
Season Matters Enormously
Oct–Nov: post-monsoon, everything lush and green, waterfalls still full, roads clear, excellent weather. Mar–May: before monsoon, pleasant temperatures, Dawki river at its clearest. June–Sep: monsoon season — Cherrapunji receives 12m of rain, roads become treacherous, some roads close. Nohkalikai Falls is most dramatic in monsoon but travel is difficult. Avoid June–September unless you're specifically chasing waterfalls and accept the access challenges.
💰
Cash is Essential — ATMs Are Rare
Shillong has good ATMs. Beyond Shillong — at Cherrapunji, Mawlynnong, Dawki — there are very few working ATMs. Most guesthouses, taxis and activity operators outside Shillong are cash-only. Carry at least ₹5,000–₹8,000 cash before leaving Shillong for the day trips. Withdraw from multiple ATMs in Shillong to be safe.
🎵
Shillong's Music Scene
Shillong is India's rock and roll capital — genuinely. Live music venues in Laitumkhrah play original rock, blues and jazz most evenings. Lou Majaw (India's Bob Dylan equivalent) is from Shillong. Check what's on at local venues when you arrive — a live music evening in a Shillong cafe is one of those unexpectedly brilliant travel moments.
🍖
Food — Pork is King Here
Northeast India is predominantly pork-eating territory. Jadoh (red rice with pork), dohneiiong (pork with black sesame), and tungrymbai (fermented soybean) are Khasi staples. Excellent for non-vegetarians — one of India's most unique regional cuisines. Vegetarians will find options but must ask specifically. The smoked meats and dried fish available in markets are excellent gifts to bring back.
🛡️
Safety for Solo Female Travellers
Meghalaya is widely considered among the safest states in India for solo female travel — a direct result of the matrilineal culture. Women hold social authority, incidents of harassment are significantly rarer than in North India, and locals are generally respectful and helpful. This is not to say normal precautions don't apply — but solo women consistently report feeling more at ease here than almost anywhere else in India.
📋
No ILP Required for Indians in Meghalaya
Inner Line Permit (ILP) is not required for Indian citizens visiting Meghalaya, Assam, Tripura or Mizoram. ILP IS required for Indians visiting Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, and Manipur — apply online through those states' official portals. Foreign nationals require permits for most Northeast states — check current regulations before travel.
🚌
Shared Sumos — How They Work
The primary transport between towns in Northeast India is the shared Sumo (Tata Sumo SUV) with 8–10 passengers. They depart when full from specific stands. Morning departures are most frequent. For more flexibility, book the entire vehicle for 1.5–2x the shared price. Sumos are faster than buses on mountain roads. On busy tourist routes (Guwahati–Shillong), they depart very frequently throughout the day.
🥾
Nongriat Trek — What to Know
The 3,500-step descent to the double-decker root bridge is steep — worn stone steps, often wet and slippery. Wear closed-toe shoes with grip (not sandals). The descent takes 1.5hrs, the climb back up 2hrs. Carrying a walking stick helps (available to borrow at Tyrna). Start early (7am) to avoid the midday heat and have the bridge to yourself before tour groups arrive. Bring a waterproof bag — it is usually misting or raining.
Packing for Meghalaya
🌧️ Rain jacket — not optional. It can rain any time, any season in Meghalaya.
👟 Grippy trekking shoes — wet stone steps are very slippery. No sandals for root bridge.
💧 Reusable water bottle — fill at guesthouses. Stay hydrated on trek days.
💰 Cash ₹5,000–₹8,000 — ATMs scarce outside Shillong. Essential.
📵 Offline maps — download Maps.me offline for Meghalaya before arriving.
🦟 Insect repellent — jungle areas have mosquitoes. DEET-based recommended.
🧴 Waterproof bag/cover — for camera and phone at Dawki boat and root bridge.
🎽 Quick-dry clothing — you will get wet. Avoid cotton, which stays wet and cold.
🔋 Power bank 10,000mAh — power cuts common in hill stations. Essential.
