Thailand 10-Day Itinerary Pre-Trip Essentials · Days 1–3 Bangkok
Before You Fly

Pre-Trip Essentials

🛂 Visa — Free, No Pre-Application
Indians get visa on arrival — 30 days, free since 2024. No pre-application needed. Queue at immigration with passport photo + onward ticket. Fast lane available.
✈️ Flight — Direct from India
Direct Bangkok (BKK/DMK) from Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai. 4–5 hrs. ₹8,000–18,000 return. Fly into Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK), out of Phuket (HKT).
💱 Currency — Thai Baht
₹1 = ~0.42 THB (so ₹2,400 = 1,000 THB). Superrich exchange booths in Bangkok give the best rates — avoid airport counters. Carry some THB cash for beaches.
📱 SIM + Apps
DTAC or TrueMove H at airport. 15GB for ~₹350 (30 days). Install: Grab (rides + food), Klook (activities, often 20-30% cheaper than direct), Google Maps.
Days 1–3 · Bangkok

Bangkok: Grand Palace, Markets & Ayutthaya

01
Day One · Bangkok
Grand Palace, Wat Pho & Wat Arun
Grand Palace · Emerald Buddha · Reclining Buddha massage · Wat Arun sunset
💰 Activities: ~₹2,400 · Mid budget hotel: ₹2,000–6,000/night
Arrive
Suvarnabhumi Airport → Hotel via BTS or Grab
BTS Skytrain from Suvarnabhumi to central Bangkok: ₹450 (Airport Rail Link + Skytrain). Grab costs ~₹800 but faster with luggage. Hotel: Sukhumvit or Silom area ₹2,000–6,000. Budget: near Khao San Road ₹600–1,500/night.
BTS + Skytrain: ₹450 · Grab: ~₹800 · Check-in, freshen up
Morning
Grand Palace + Wat Phra Kaew (Emerald Buddha)
The most ornate complex in Thailand — 218,000 sqm of gilded spires, mosaic-encrusted walls, mythological murals and the sacred Emerald Buddha (actually jade, 66cm tall). Dress code strictly enforced: cover knees and shoulders — free sarong loan available at entrance. Arrive at opening (8:30am) to beat crowds. Allow 2–2.5 hours.
Entry: ₹1,250 · Open 8:30am–3:30pm · Dress code: cover knees & shoulders
Afternoon
Wat Pho — Reclining Buddha + Traditional Massage
5-min walk from Grand Palace. The 46m-long gold Reclining Buddha is one of Thailand's most impressive sights. Wat Pho is also the birthplace of traditional Thai massage — the school here is the most respected in the country. Book a 1-hour massage (₹900) in the pavilions within the temple complex.
Entry: ₹500 · 1hr traditional massage: ₹900
Sunset
Chao Phraya River Ferry → Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)
Take the Chao Phraya Express Boat (₹30) across the river to Wat Arun — the most beautiful temple in Bangkok at sunset. The 70m prang (tower) is encrusted with colourful porcelain chips that catch the evening light magnificently. Climb the steep steps to the middle terrace for river views. Back by ferry for dinner.
Ferry: ₹30 each way · Entry: ₹150 · Best: 5:00–6:30pm
Dinner
Thip Samai Pad Thai + Optional Jay Fai
Thip Samai on Mahachai Road — the most famous pad thai in Bangkok since 1966. Queue ~30 min, order the "Special" wrapped in egg crepe. ₹280. Or splurge on Jay Fai's legendary crab omelette (₹5,000+, Michelin-starred street food — book weeks ahead online). Both are bucket-list worthy.
Thip Samai pad thai: ₹280 · Jay Fai crab omelette: ₹5,000+ (book ahead)

Thailand 10-Day Itinerary Days 2–5 · Bangkok cont. · Chiang Mai
02
Day Two · Bangkok
Floating Market + Chatuchak + Rooftop Bar
Damnoen Saduak · Chatuchak Weekend Market (Sat–Sun) · Rooftop bar sunset
💰 Floating Market tour: ₹2,500 · Chatuchak shopping: ₹2,000–5,000
8:00 AM
Damnoen Saduak Floating Market
2 hours from Bangkok — most photogenic floating market in Thailand. Vendors in straw hats selling tropical fruits, noodles and spices from wooden boats on the canals. Join a group tour from Bangkok (₹2,500, includes transport + boat ride). Alternatively: Amphawa Floating Market (closer, Fri–Sun, more local and authentic).
Group tour: ₹2,500 including transport · Boat ride included
Afternoon
Chatuchak Weekend Market (Saturday & Sunday Only)
One of the world's largest weekend markets: 15,000 stalls across 35 acres. Everything imaginable — antiques, plants, vintage clothes, ceramics, street food, live animals, handcrafts. Budget ₹2,000–5,000 for shopping. Open Sat–Sun 9am–6pm only. Nearest BTS: Mo Chit. On weekdays: MBK Center or Terminal 21 for shopping instead.
Free entry · Saturday & Sunday 9am–6pm · BTS Mo Chit
Sunset
Rooftop Bar — Bangkok Skyline
Lebua Sky Bar (The Hangover bar) — 64th floor, ₹2,500/drink, smart dress, no shorts. Free entry. OR Vertigo at Banyan Tree (₹3,500). Budget option: Octave Rooftop at Marriott Sukhumvit (₹1,200) — great view, relaxed dress code, no minimum spend. All are spectacular at golden hour.
Octave: ₹1,200 · Lebua: ₹2,500 · Vertigo: ₹3,500
03
Day Three · Bangkok Day Trip
Ayutthaya — Ancient Capital of Siam
Train from Hua Lamphong · Bicycle or tuk-tuk · UNESCO ruins · Evening return
💰 Train: ₹50 · Bicycle: ₹200 · Day total: ~₹2,000
7:00 AM
Hua Lamphong Station → Ayutthaya (1.5hrs)
Train from Hua Lamphong Station ₹50 — slow, scenic and quintessentially Thai. Alternatively Grab to Ayutthaya (~₹2,000) for groups. At Ayutthaya: rent bicycle (₹200/day) for maximum flexibility, or share a tuk-tuk for the day (₹1,500). The ruins spread over a large island — cycling is by far the best way.
Train: ₹50 return · Bicycle: ₹200/day · Tuk-tuk: ₹1,500/day shared
All Day
Ayutthaya Historical Park — UNESCO World Heritage
Former capital of the Kingdom of Siam (1350–1767). Wat Phra Si Sanphet — three great chedis, the most iconic image of Ayutthaya. Wat Mahathat — famous Buddha head entwined in banyan tree roots (do not take selfies at same height as Buddha). Wat Phra Ram, Wat Chaiwatthanaram — magnificent riverside ruins. Entry ₹250 per major site.
₹250 entry per major temple · Full day · Return Bangkok by 7pm
04–05
Days Four–Five · Northern Thailand
Chiang Mai — Elephants, Temples & Night Bazaar
Doi Suthep · Elephant Sanctuary · Thai cooking class · Night Bazaar · Nimman area
💰 Flight BKK→CNX: ₹1,500–3,000 · Elephant Sanctuary: ₹4,000–6,000
Day 4 AM
Fly Bangkok → Chiang Mai (1.5hrs) + Doi Suthep
Fly from Suvarnabhumi or Don Mueang (₹1,500–3,000) or overnight train from Bangkok (11hrs, sleeper ₹800 — scenic and romantic). Hotel: Old City inside the moat is the best base (₹1,200–4,000). Afternoon: hire tuk-tuk to Doi Suthep temple (₹400, 30km drive up mountain). Gold-spired Wat Phra That Doi Suthep has stunning views over the city. Entry ₹200.
Flight: ₹1,500–3,000 · Tuk-tuk Doi Suthep: ₹400 · Entry: ₹200
Day 4 Eve
Nimman Area Cafes + Sunday Walking Street
Nimman (Nimmanhaemin Road) is Chiang Mai's hipster district — excellent coffee shops, boutique galleries and Thai fusion restaurants. If it's Sunday: Sunday Walking Street along Wualai Road (6pm–midnight) is extraordinary — local artisans, street food, live traditional music. The best street market in Thailand.
Sunday Walking Street: Free entry · Street food from ₹80/dish
Day 5 AM
Elephant Sanctuary — Ethical, No Riding
ONLY book ethical sanctuaries where elephants are not ridden, not chained, not used for performance. Elephant Nature Park (most reputable, founded by Lek Chailert) or Elephant Jungle Sanctuary. Half-day: feed, bathe and walk with elephants in natural surroundings. Transport from Chiang Mai included. Book 2 days ahead.
Half-day including transport: ₹4,000–6,000 · Book: elephantnaturepark.org
Day 5 PM
Thai Cooking Class + Night Bazaar
3-hour cooking class (₹1,500–2,500): visit local market, learn 4–5 dishes (pad thai, green curry, mango sticky rice), take home recipe cards. Afternoon finish. Evening: Chiang Mai Night Bazaar along Chang Khlan Road — silk, silverware, hill tribe crafts, street food. More authentic than Bangkok's Chatuchak.
Cooking class: ₹1,500–2,500 · Night Bazaar: free entry

06–07
Days Six–Seven · Phuket
Phuket — Big Buddha, Phi Phi Islands & Beaches
Fly Chiang Mai → Phuket · Big Buddha · Kata Beach · Phi Phi day trip · Bangla Road
💰 Flight CNX→HKT: ₹2,000–4,000 · Phi Phi tour: ₹2,500–3,500
Day 6 AM
Fly Chiang Mai → Phuket + Big Buddha
Fly Chiang Mai → Phuket (1.5hrs, ₹2,000–4,000). Hotel: Patong Beach area — budget guesthouses ₹1,500, mid-range resorts ₹4,000–8,000, overlooking Kata Beach is quieter and prettier. Afternoon: Big Buddha on Nakkerd Hills — 45m white marble statue visible from most of Phuket. Free, 20 minutes from Patong. Spectacular 360-degree views.
Flight: ₹2,000–4,000 · Big Buddha: Free · Tuk-tuk: ₹300–400
Day 6 PM
Kata Beach + Promthep Cape Sunset
Kata Beach is significantly cleaner and less crowded than Patong — great snorkelling off the south end. Afternoon: drive to Promthep Cape (southern tip of Phuket) for the most famous sunset viewpoint in southern Thailand. Crowds gather but the panorama over the Andaman Sea is worth it.
Kata Beach: Free · Promthep Cape: Free
Day 7
Phi Phi Islands Day Trip — Maya Bay & Snorkelling
The Phi Phi Islands are among the most beautiful in the world — steep limestone cliffs, turquoise water, white sand. Day trip from Phuket (₹2,500–3,500) includes Maya Bay (made famous by The Beach with Leonardo DiCaprio), snorkelling at multiple reefs, Monkey Beach, and Pileh Lagoon. Book via Klook — often ₹500–800 cheaper than local agents. Departs 8am.
Phi Phi day trip via Klook: ₹2,500–3,500 · Includes lunch & snorkel gear
Day 7 Night
Bangla Road — Patong's Famous Party Street
If nightlife is your scene: Bangla Road in Patong is one of Asia's most infamous streets — 400m of neon, live music bars, cabaret, street food. Even if you don't drink, walking through at 10pm is an experience. Budget ₹800–2,000 for the evening. Tiger Kingdom (if you must — controversial, tigers sedated, ₹3,000) is nearby.
Bangla Road: Free entry · Budget drinks from ₹300
08–09
Days Eight–Nine · Krabi
Krabi & Railay Beach — Limestone & Emerald Water
Speedboat Phuket → Krabi · Railay by longtail · 4 Islands Tour · Rock climbing
💰 Ferry: ₹800 · Longtail to Railay: ₹300 · 4 Islands: ₹1,800
Day 8 AM
Phuket → Krabi + Railay Beach by Longtail
Speedboat or ferry Phuket → Krabi (2hrs, ₹800). Or fly (35 min, ₹1,500). From Ao Nang Beach, longtail boats go to Railay Beach every 20 minutes (₹300, 15 min). Railay is completely cut off from the mainland by limestone cliffs — no roads, no cars. Magical. Hotel: Railay Bay Resort or budget bungalows ₹1,500–5,000.
Ferry Phuket→Krabi: ₹800 · Longtail Ao Nang→Railay: ₹300
Day 8 PM
Railay West Beach + Rock Climbing
Railay West Beach — the classic postcard view. Dramatic limestone cliffs rising from crystal-clear water. Railay is world-famous for rock climbing — vertical karst walls with routes for all levels. Equipment rental ₹1,500, guided half-day ₹3,000. Even beginners can do the easier routes. Sunset from Railay Viewpoint (20-min scramble up jungle trail, free) is spectacular.
Beach: Free · Rock climbing equipment: ₹1,500 · Guided: ₹3,000
Day 9
4 Islands Boat Tour — Snorkelling & Emerald Cave
Full-day longtail boat tour to four islands around Krabi: Ko Mook, Ko Kradan, Ko Libong and Ko Waen. Highlight: Emerald Cave (Tham Morakot) on Ko Mook — swim 80m through a pitch-black tunnel to emerge in a hidden beach surrounded by sheer cliffs. Lunch included. Book through your guesthouse or Klook. Departs 8:30am.
4 Islands tour including lunch: ₹1,800 · Departs 8:30am
10
Day Ten · Departure
Final Day — Islands or Fly Home from Phuket
Optional Ko Lanta day trip · Ferry Krabi → Phuket · Fly home from HKT
💰 Ferry Krabi→Phuket: ₹800 · Ko Lanta ferry: ₹500
Morning
Optional Extension: Ko Lanta or Ko Phi Phi Don Overnight
If your flight is afternoon/evening: day trip to Ko Lanta (ferry ₹500, 2hrs from Krabi) — more relaxed than Phi Phi, white sand beaches, excellent diving. Or overnight Ko Phi Phi Don (budget bungalow ₹1,500) if you didn't go on the day trip. Or simply relax on Railay for a final morning.
Ko Lanta ferry: ₹500 return · Ko Phi Phi bungalow: ₹1,500
Afternoon
Ferry Krabi → Phuket → Tan Son Nhat Airport (HKT)
Ferry Krabi → Phuket (2hrs, ₹800) then Grab to Phuket International Airport (HKT). Allow 4 hours total for this journey. Most evening flights to India depart 7–11pm. Phuket airport has good dining options — have one final mango sticky rice before boarding.
Ferry + Grab: ₹1,200 · Arrive airport 2.5hrs before flight
💡 Krabi vs Phuket: Phuket is bigger, better infrastructure, more nightlife. Krabi is more beautiful, more adventurous, better rock climbing and kayaking. Railay Beach has no equivalent in Phuket. If you can only do one — do Krabi. If you can do both — do both.

Thailand 10-Day Itinerary Essential Tips & Packing List
Know Before You Go

Essential Thailand Tips

🛂
Visa on Arrival — Join the Queue Confidently
Indians get 30-day visa on arrival, free since 2024. No pre-application. At immigration: join the VOA queue (not e-Visa queue). Have passport photo (4x6cm), onward ticket, and cash for emergency proof of funds. Queue is usually 20–40 minutes.
🏯
Temple Etiquette — Cover Up Always
All temples require covered knees and shoulders — no exceptions. Free sarong loans available at Grand Palace entrance. Remove shoes before entering temple buildings. Women should never touch or hand anything directly to a monk. Speak quietly inside temple compounds.
🛺
Tuk-Tuks — Agree Price Before Getting In
Tuk-tuks are fun but negotiate the price firmly before you get in. Typical city ride: ₹150–400. If a tuk-tuk offers to take you sightseeing "for free" they will take you to gem shops or tailor shops where they earn commission — politely decline.
💱
Superrich Exchange — Best Rates in Asia
Bangkok's Superrich exchange booths (bright orange stores) consistently offer the best THB rates for INR/USD/EUR. Look for them on Ratchadamri Road, Asok, and near Chatuchak. Rates are 5–8% better than airport counters — meaningful on larger amounts.
🐘
Elephant Sanctuaries — Never Ride
Riding elephants causes spinal damage. Hooks (ankus) cause physical harm. Only visit sanctuaries where elephants roam freely, no riding, no performance. Elephant Nature Park (Park Artitaya) is the gold standard. Avoid "elephant shows" and camps using hooks. No hooks = ethical.
🍜
Street Food — Follow the Queue
Thai street food is extraordinarily safe and delicious. Pad thai, som tam, mango sticky rice, satay, roti — all amazing. The safety rule: find the stall with the longest queue of locals. High turnover = fresh ingredients. Jay Fai's street stall is Michelin-starred — ₹5,000 a dish but legendary.
🌊
Phi Phi & Krabi via Klook
Book Phi Phi Islands and 4 Islands tours through Klook — typically 20–30% cheaper than hotel tour desks. Instant confirmation. Klook also has elephant sanctuary bookings, Grand Palace skip-the-queue tickets, and cooking class reservations at discounts.
🎉
Songkran (April) — Book 3 Months Ahead
Songkran (Thai New Year, April 13–15) is the world's biggest water fight — streets become a water war. Absolutely magical experience. But hotels book out 3 months ahead and prices triple. Plan specifically for Songkran or plan around it — don't get caught unprepared.
What to Pack

Thailand Packing List

Clothing & Beach Gear

□Rash guard — sun protection for snorkelling and islands
□Temple cover-up: lightweight scarf (shoulders + knees)
□Flip flops — you will remove shoes constantly at temples
□Comfortable walking shoes for city days
□Swimwear (2 sets — one always drying)
□Compact umbrella (random tropical showers year-round)
□Light breathable clothes — hot and humid everywhere

Health & Safety

□DEET insect repellent (dengue mosquitoes in cities too)
□Stomach medication (Norflox + ORS + antacid)
□SPF 50 sunscreen — tropical UV is intense even on cloudy days
□Travel insurance — mandatory for water sports + motorbike

Money & Tech

□Niyo or Wise card — best THB exchange rates at ATMs
□Cash THB ₹5,000 equivalent for islands (ATMs limited)
□Power bank — long beach and island days
□Waterproof phone case for islands and boat trips
□Grab app installed and registered before landing
□Klook app — book activities at discounted prices
□Dry bag for longtail boat rides to Railay (spray gets heavy)

Documents

□Passport (minimum 6 months validity from travel date)
□Passport-size photos (2) for visa on arrival queue
□Onward/return flight printout for immigration
□Travel insurance documents (required at some sanctuaries)
