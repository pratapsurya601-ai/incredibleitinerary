Day 1
🗽 Staten Island Ferry & Brooklyn
The Staten Island Ferry is FREE and gives the closest views of the Statue of Liberty and Lower Manhattan skyline from the water — the best free activity in NYC. Brooklyn Bridge walk, DUMBO neighbourhood with cobblestones and bridge framing the Manhattan skyline for the perfect photograph.
Day 2
🏙️ Central Park & Met Museum
Central Park — 341 hectares in the middle of Manhattan — is one of the world's great urban parks. The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met) is the largest art museum in the Western Hemisphere with 1.5 million objects — Egyptian Temple of Dendur, European masters, Arms and Armour. Times Square at night for the full sensory assault.
Day 3
🏛️ Lower Manhattan & Grand Central
9/11 Memorial reflecting pools (free) and One World Trade Observatory. Wall Street, the New York Public Library (read room free), Grand Central Terminal (free to walk — the celestial ceiling is extraordinary). This is the original Manhattan that built the world.
Day 4–5
🎨 Brooklyn & MoMA
Brooklyn: Smorgasburg food market (weekends), Prospect Park or Brooklyn Botanic Garden, Williamsburg for indie culture and street art. Day 5: MoMA (Warhol, Picasso, Frida Kahlo), the High Line elevated park (free), Chelsea Market, Governors Island ferry.

"New York is not a city — it's the world." — Vin Diesel. Eight million people, 800 languages, every cuisine, culture and ambition on earth, compressed into 783 square kilometres.

US B1/B2 Visa for Indians: Apply at usembassy.gov — visa fee ₹12,000 (non-refundable). In-person interview required. Processing: 3–6 months for first-time applicants — apply early. Required documents: bank statements (6 months), employment letter, property proof, ITR, strong ties to India (job, family, property). The US visa is the most difficult and expensive tourist visa for Indian passport holders — apply at least 6 months ahead for peak travel seasons.
Flights: Delhi/Mumbai to JFK (New York) — ₹60,000–₹1,10,000 return. Air India direct from Delhi (~15hrs). Emirates via Dubai, Etihad via Abu Dhabi, Qatar via Doha are all popular. Newark (EWR) is sometimes cheaper than JFK. AirTrain + NYC Subway from JFK: ₹1,600 (50 min). Buy a 7-day unlimited Metro card (₹2,200) on arrival.

New York 5-Day Itinerary Day 1–3 — Manhattan, Brooklyn & Skyline
01
Day One · Iconic New York
Staten Island Ferry, Brooklyn Bridge & DUMBO
Free Staten Island Ferry (Statue of Liberty views) · Brooklyn Bridge walk · DUMBO photo spot · Brooklyn Heights Promenade
💰 Staten Island Ferry: FREE · Bridge walk: FREE · Budget well for NYC food (₹800–₹2,000/meal)
Arrival
JFK to Manhattan — AirTrain + Subway
From JFK: take the AirTrain to Jamaica station, then the A or E subway to Midtown (50 minutes total, ₹1,600). From Newark (EWR): NJ Transit train to Penn Station (30 minutes, ₹1,800). Taxis from JFK to Manhattan are fixed rate at $70 (₹5,800) — only worthwhile with heavy luggage and multiple people. Uber/Lyft are often cheaper. Buy a 7-day unlimited subway MetroCard (₹2,200) or use contactless bank card (works on all NYC subway and buses). Do NOT exchange currency at airport booths — terrible rates. Use a zero-forex card or ATM in the city.
AirTrain + subway: ₹1,600 · 7-day MetroCard: ₹2,200 · Hotel in Midtown: ₹9,000–₹20,000/night
3:00 PM
Staten Island Ferry — Free Statue of Liberty Views
The Staten Island Ferry is one of New York's great free experiences — a 25-minute ferry ride from Whitehall Terminal (lower Manhattan) to St. George Terminal, Staten Island, passing the Statue of Liberty at close range. Completely free, runs 24 hours, 7 days a week. Take the right side of the ferry going out (downtown-facing) for the best Statue of Liberty views. No need to get off at Staten Island — just turn around and take the next ferry back. Pair this with views of the Manhattan skyline from the water — you'll see why this is on every best-free-NYC-activities list.
Ferry: COMPLETELY FREE · Nearest subway: Whitehall St (R/W) or South Ferry (1) · Best in late afternoon light
5:00 PM
Brooklyn Bridge Walk & DUMBO
Walk the Brooklyn Bridge from Manhattan (start at the City Hall Park entrance) to Brooklyn — 1.1km, 20 minutes. The pedestrian walkway is elevated above the car traffic — you walk through the gothic stone towers with Manhattan behind you and Brooklyn ahead. DUMBO (Down Under the Manhattan Bridge Overpass) is Brooklyn's most photogenic neighbourhood — the view of Manhattan Bridge framing the Manhattan skyline from Washington Street is the most photographed spot in Brooklyn. The Jane's Carousel on the waterfront is beautiful. Have pizza at Juliana's (cash only, best pizza in NYC by many accounts) or Grimaldi's next door.
Bridge walk: free · DUMBO: free · Juliana's pizza: ₹1,200–₹1,800 (cash only)
02
Day Two · Park & Art
Central Park, The Met & Times Square
Central Park morning walk · The Metropolitan Museum · Upper East Side · Times Square evening — the full Manhattan sensory experience
💰 Central Park: FREE · The Met: ₹2,500 (suggested donation) · Times Square: FREE
8:00 AM
Central Park — 341 Hectares of Urban Sanctuary
Enter at 59th Street and Fifth Avenue. Central Park was designed by Frederick Law Olmsted (1858) and is one of the world's greatest landscape architecture achievements — entirely artificial, built on what was rocky scrubland. Highlights: the Bethesda Fountain and Terrace, Strawberry Fields (John Lennon memorial opposite the Dakota building where he lived), the Reservoir, Bow Bridge, and Belvedere Castle. In autumn (October–November), the deciduous trees turn red and gold — among the most beautiful urban landscapes in the world. Rent a bike (₹700/hour) to cover more ground.
Central Park: FREE · Bike rental: ₹700/hour · Strawberry Fields: free · Bow Bridge: free
10:30 AM
The Metropolitan Museum of Art — The Greatest Art Museum in America
The Met is on the eastern edge of Central Park (Fifth Avenue) — the world's largest art museum by gallery space. Highlights: The Temple of Dendur (an actual Egyptian temple, 15 BC, inside a glass-enclosed wing), European Paintings (Vermeer, Raphael, Rembrandt, Van Eyck), Arms and Armour, ancient Greek and Roman sculpture, Costume Institute. The recommended donation is ₹2,500 — you can technically pay less but it funds the museum. The rooftop sculpture garden (open May–October) has extraordinary Central Park and skyline views. Allow 3–4 hours minimum.
Suggested donation: ₹2,500 · Rooftop: included · Open Fri–Sat until 9pm · Closed Wednesdays
Evening
Times Square — Overwhelming, Unmissable, Entirely New York
Times Square is not subtle. 40 million visitors per year, 1,500+ LED displays, 24-hour activity, characters in costume for paid photos. Go in the evening (9pm+) for the full light show effect. It's crowded and commercial — but there's genuinely nothing else like it on earth. Walk through rather than lingering. The adjacent Theater District has Broadway shows (tkts.co.uk for same-day half-price tickets). Two Boots Pizza near Times Square: New York pizza slice ₹250 — the city's classic cheap meal.
Times Square: free · Broadway show: ₹4,000–₹12,000 · Half-price at TKTS booth: ₹2,000–₹6,000
03
Day Three · History & Architecture
9/11 Memorial, Wall Street & Grand Central
9/11 Memorial reflecting pools · One World Observatory · Wall Street · New York Public Library · Grand Central Terminal
💰 9/11 Memorial: free · One World Observatory: ₹3,500 · Grand Central: free
9:00 AM
9/11 Memorial — Where the Twin Towers Stood
The 9/11 Memorial consists of two enormous reflecting pools set in the exact footprints of the World Trade Center towers that collapsed on September 11, 2001. The names of all 2,977 victims are inscribed on the bronze parapets surrounding the pools. Water falls 9 metres down the sides into a second basin below — a profoundly simple and moving design. Entry to the memorial plaza is free. The 9/11 Museum (₹2,500) below ground contains artefacts, survivor testimonies, and the preserved slurry wall. The museum is extraordinary but emotionally intense — allow 2–3 hours and prepare yourself.
Memorial: FREE · Museum: ₹2,500 · Book museum at 911memorial.org · Allow 2–3 hours minimum
11:30 AM
One World Trade — Highest Observatory in the Western Hemisphere
One World Trade Center (Freedom Tower) at 541 metres is the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere. The One World Observatory on floors 100–102 (₹3,500) has a spectacular sky-rise elevator with a time-lapse of Manhattan's growth, and panoramic 360° views from the top. On a clear day you can see 80km in every direction — New Jersey, Connecticut, Long Island. Buy tickets online (oneworldobservatory.com) to skip the queue. The building's design — a 1,776-foot tower (the year of US independence) — makes it both beautiful and symbolic.
Observatory: ₹3,500 · Book at oneworldobservatory.com · Allow 1.5 hours · Best on clear days
Afternoon
Grand Central Terminal — The World's Most Beautiful Train Station
Grand Central Terminal (not "station" — locals will correct you) is a working commuter rail terminal and one of the world's great public spaces. The Main Concourse ceiling — a painted mural of the night sky (2,500 stars, in reverse, based on a medieval manuscript — the "mistake" in the stars' positions was intentional) — is extraordinary. Free guided tours on Fridays at 12:30pm. The Whispering Gallery: stand in opposite corners of the Vanderbilt Hall arched corridor and whisper — the curved ceiling carries your voice across the room. The Grand Central Market has exceptional food vendors (New York Public Library is nearby — the Rose Main Reading Room is free and spectacular).
Grand Central: FREE to enter · Tours: FREE Fridays 12:30pm · Whispering Gallery: free · NYPL: free

Day Four · Brooklyn Culture
Smorgasburg, Brooklyn Botanic Garden & Williamsburg
Smorgasburg food market (weekends) · Brooklyn Botanic Garden · Prospect Park · Williamsburg indie culture & street art
💰 Smorgasburg: free entry · Botanic Garden: ₹1,200 · Williamsburg: free to walk
Weekends
Smorgasburg — New York's Greatest Food Market
Smorgasburg runs every Saturday at Prospect Park and Sunday at the Brooklyn Waterfront (Williamsburg) April–November. Over 100 food vendors from NYC's most creative food entrepreneurs — ramen burgers, lobster rolls, Korean BBQ, birria tacos, Japanese karaage, matcha desserts, Filipino lechon. Entry is free. Budget ₹1,500–₹3,000 for a glorious food crawl through everything. The Sunday waterfront location has extraordinary Manhattan skyline views while you eat. Arrive at 11am when it opens — runs until 6pm.
Entry: free · Budget ₹1,500–₹3,000 for food · Sat: Prospect Park · Sun: Williamsburg waterfront
Afternoon
Williamsburg — Brooklyn's Creative Neighbourhood
Williamsburg is Brooklyn's most vibrant creative neighbourhood — independent record stores, vintage clothing, street murals by major artists (the Bushwick Collective murals in adjacent Bushwick are worth a visit), artisanal coffee shops, excellent ramen and pizza. The Bedford Avenue strip is the main drag. The neighbourhood that spawned the modern indie music scene, artisanal food culture, and the "Brooklyn aesthetic" that has now spread globally. Walk north toward Greenpoint for Polish food and a more neighbourhood feel. Domino Park on the waterfront has lawn space and excellent Manhattan skyline views.
Williamsburg: free · Domino Park: free · Coffee at artisanal café: ₹400 · Vintage shopping: budget as desired

NYC budget tips: dollar pizza slice (₹250) at any pizza-by-the-slice shop — genuine New York meal. Dollar dumplings in Chinatown (Mott Street — ₹250 for 5 dumplings). Bagel with cream cheese from any deli (₹300). Pretzels from street carts (₹150). NYC is expensive for hotels but has excellent cheap food if you know where to look.

🚇
The Subway — New York's Greatest Equaliser
The NYC subway runs 24 hours every day — the only major metro system in the world that does. The 7-day unlimited MetroCard (₹2,200) is the best value for 5+ days. Use maps.me or the MTA app for real-time maps. Avoid the A express train at night (the local C or 2/3 trains are safer). Sit in the middle cars of trains — more people present.
🍕
The NYC Food Rules
New York bagel: must be from H&H Bagels or Ess-a-Bagel — thick, chewy, with cream cheese (₹300). NYC pizza: thin crust, fold it in half lengthwise, eat while walking (₹250/slice). Chinatown dumplings: Mott Street, ₹250 for 5. Hot dog from street cart: ₹200. Halal cart chicken over rice: ₹400 — beloved by New Yorkers at 53rd & 6th Ave ("Halal Guys").
🏙️
Best NYC Viewpoints — Free vs Paid
Free views: Brooklyn Bridge Park, Top of the Rock viewing deck from Staten Island Ferry, Roosevelt Island tramway (₹250 — same as subway fare). Paid: One World Observatory (₹3,500), Top of the Rock at Rockefeller Center (₹3,000 — includes Empire State Building in the view), Edge at Hudson Yards (₹3,500). The Edge has an outdoor glass floor section — terrifying and spectacular.
📅
Best Time to Visit NYC
September–November: perfect — warm days, cool evenings, autumn foliage in Central Park (mid-October is peak). April–June: spring flowers, pleasant temperatures, less crowded than summer. July–August: hot and very crowded (35°C+, 80% humidity) but peak energy. December–February: cold (-5 to 5°C) but NYC in snow is magical — and hotel prices drop 30–40%.
