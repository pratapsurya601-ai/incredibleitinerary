Day 1 — Must Do
🌅 Taj Mahal at Sunrise
Gates open 30 minutes before sunrise (check online for exact time by date). The pale pink pre-dawn light on white marble is unlike any other hour at the Taj. By 8am, tour groups arrive and the atmosphere changes. Book tickets online at asi.payumoney.com to skip the queue entirely — a crucial time-saver on Day 1.
Day 1 — Afternoon
🏯 Agra Fort
The massive red sandstone fort built by Akbar in 1565, expanded by Jahangir and Shah Jahan. From the Musamman Burj tower, Shah Jahan — imprisoned here by his own son Aurangzeb for his final 8 years — could see the Taj Mahal, his wife's tomb, across the river. This heartbreaking context transforms the view.
Day 2 — Morning
🏙️ Fatehpur Sikri
Akbar's masterwork: a complete Mughal capital city built in 1571, abandoned just 14 years later (possibly due to water shortage). Remarkably preserved. The Buland Darwaza (Victory Gate, 1601) at 54 metres is the tallest gateway in the world. The Panch Mahal, Diwan-i-Khas and Jodha Bai's palace are extraordinary examples of Mughal spatial planning.
Day 2 — Afternoon
💎 Itmad-ud-Daulah & Kinari Bazaar
The "Baby Taj" — built in 1628 by Nur Jahan for her father. The first Mughal monument built entirely of marble (the Taj came 15 years later), and the first to use pietra dura (inlaid gemstone work). Then Kinari Bazaar for Agra's famous petha (crystallised pumpkin sweet, ₹150/kg) and leather goods before evening departure.

"Shah Jahan spent the last 8 years of his life imprisoned in Agra Fort, looking across the Yamuna at the monument he built for his wife. The Taj Mahal is the most expensive love letter ever written."

Best Time to Visit: October to March. The Taj in winter fog (November–January mornings) can be spectacular — or the monument can disappear entirely into white mist. Full moon nights: the Taj is open for 5 nights around each full moon (₹750 extra, evening ticket, book separately at asi.payumoney.com). If visiting in summer, arrive at Taj gates at exactly 6am — by 9am it is too hot to walk comfortably.

Day One · The Wonder of the World
Taj Mahal at Sunrise, Agra Fort & Mehtab Bagh
5am wake · Taj sunrise gates · Agra Fort afternoon · Mehtab Bagh for sunset view of Taj from across Yamuna
💰 Taj: ₹1,100 · Agra Fort: ₹650 · Mehtab Bagh: ₹300 · Total entries: ₹2,050
5:30 AM
Taj Mahal — Sunrise Entry (Gates Open 30 Min Before Sunrise)
Book tickets in advance at asi.payumoney.com — the online ticket line at the East or South Gate is completely separate from the cash queue and moves in minutes. Bring your booking confirmation (phone screenshot is fine) and government ID. Ticket includes booties for your shoes (mandatory on white marble plinth). Enter from the East Gate for the classic first view down the reflecting pool. The pale dawn light on white marble for the first 45 minutes is the reward for the early wake. By 7:30am, guided groups begin arriving; by 9am, the crowds are substantial. Spend 2–2.5 hours. No tripods, no food, no tobacco inside.
Entry: ₹1,100 Indians (incl. marble complex + main mausoleum) · Book online to avoid 45-min cash queue · Open Sat–Thu (closed Friday)
Inside Taj
The Main Mausoleum — What Most Visitors Miss
The exterior draws all eyes, but the interior of the main mausoleum is extraordinary: the octagonal inner chamber with its carved marble screen (jali) surrounding the cenotaphs, the walls inlaid with 28 types of semiprecious stone in floral patterns. The light inside is dim and cool. The cenotaphs are decorative — the actual graves of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal are in a plain crypt directly below, at garden level. The four corner minarets lean slightly outward — intentionally, so that in an earthquake they would fall away from the main structure.
Main mausoleum entry included in ₹1,100 ticket · Queue at the south entrance of the main dome · Remove shoes
1:00 PM
Agra Fort — Where Shah Jahan Spent His Last Years
Built by Akbar in 1565 using local red sandstone, the fort was progressively marbled by Shah Jahan, who added the Khas Mahal and Musamman Burj. The Musamman Burj — the octagonal tower from which Shah Jahan is said to have gazed at the Taj — has a view directly across the Yamuna to the distant white dome. This sight, with the knowledge of his eight years of imprisonment here, is one of the most moving experiences in India. The Diwan-i-Khas marble hall has a couplet from the 14th century Sufi poet Amir Khusro: "If there be paradise on earth, it is this, it is this, it is this."
Entry: ₹650 · Auto from Taj: ₹60 · Allow 2 hours · Guides available at gate: ₹400–₹600
Sunset
Mehtab Bagh — Sunset View of Taj from the North Bank
Mehtab Bagh (Moonlight Garden) is on the opposite bank of the Yamuna from the Taj — the location where Shah Jahan reputedly planned to build a "Black Taj" as his own tomb (historians debate this). The view of the Taj from here at sunset, with the dome reflected in the Yamuna during monsoon season, is considered by many photographers to be superior to any angle inside the complex itself. Fewer tourists, better light. Auto from Agra Fort: ₹80.
Entry: ₹300 · Auto from Agra Fort: ₹80 · Best light: 45 minutes before sunset · Bring telephoto lens or zoom

Fatehpur Sikri, Baby Taj & Kinari Bazaar

💰 Auto/taxi to Fatehpur Sikri: ₹600–₹1,200 return · Entries: ₹610 + ₹310 · Total day: ₹1,500–₹2,500
8:00 AM
Fatehpur Sikri — Akbar's Abandoned Capital
Hire a taxi or auto for the day (₹1,200 including Fatehpur Sikri and return). The drive is 40km on a single road, 1 hour. Fatehpur Sikri was built by Emperor Akbar between 1571–1585 as his new capital — an attempt to blend Hindu, Jain and Islamic architectural traditions. It was abandoned possibly due to water scarcity, leaving a remarkably intact ghost city. The Buland Darwaza (Victory Gate, 54 metres tall), built to celebrate Akbar's conquest of Gujarat, is the tallest gateway in Asia. Dargah of Salim Chishti inside — still an active Sufi shrine where people tie threads and make wishes.
Entry: ₹610 · Taxi for the day: ₹1,200 · Allow 2.5–3 hours · Guides at gate: ₹300–₹500
12:30 PM
Itmad-ud-Daulah — The Baby Taj, First in Marble
Often overlooked by visitors rushing to the Taj, Itmad-ud-Daulah is historically more significant than it appears. Built in 1628 by Empress Nur Jahan for her father Mirza Ghiyas Beg, it was the first Mughal monument built entirely of white marble (the Taj came 15 years later), and the first to use pietra dura — the technique of inlaying semiprecious stones into marble that would define the Taj. The smaller scale makes the intricate inlay work more visible and accessible. The garden setting is beautifully maintained. Far fewer tourists than the Taj.
Entry: ₹310 · Auto from Fatehpur Sikri or city: ₹80 · 1 hour is sufficient · Closed Friday
2:30 PM
Kinari Bazaar — Petha, Leather & Marble Inlay Souvenirs
Agra's old bazaar near Jama Masjid. Agra petha is the city's most famous export — a crystallised pumpkin sweet in dozens of varieties (plain, kesar, angoori) sold in 250g to 1kg boxes (₹80–₹200). Try before buying — Panchhi Petha near Taj is the most reliable brand. Agra leather: shoes, bags, belts from the city's famous tanneries — negotiate firmly (start at 50% of asking price). Marble inlay work (pietra dura) souvenirs range from ₹200 coasters to ₹5,000 table tops — verify the marble is genuine (cold to touch, slightly heavy).
Petha: ₹80–₹200 per box · Leather goods: ₹300–₹3,000 · Marble souvenirs: ₹200–₹5,000

Insider Tips — Read Before You Go
🎟️
Book Taj Tickets Online — Non-Negotiable
Book at asi.payumoney.com. The walk-up cash queue can be 45–90 minutes during peak season (Oct–Feb). Online booking takes 5 minutes and you collect from a separate, fast counter. You need your booking reference and government-issued ID. The ticket includes entry to the main mausoleum — a separate inner gate requires showing the ticket again. Keep both the email confirmation and government ID accessible throughout your visit.
🌅
Sunrise vs Sunset — The Real Debate
Sunrise wins for photography: the light is softer and the crowds are minimal for the first 90 minutes. The pre-dawn pink on white marble cannot be replicated at any other time. Sunset has the golden hour warm light but is crowded. Full moon nights (₹750 surcharge) offer a completely different experience — the marble glows eerily silver, crowds are limited to 400 people per slot. If you can arrange only one visit: sunrise. If you can arrange two: sunrise plus a full moon night.
📸
Photography Rules Inside Taj
Phone photography is permitted everywhere in the complex. Tripods are not permitted. No photography inside the main mausoleum (some guards enforce this, some don't — respect the rule). The best photographs: (1) Classic pool reflection from entrance gate; (2) Frame the Taj between cypress trees on the side path; (3) From Mehtab Bagh across the river; (4) Close-up pietra dura inlay work on the tomb plinth. Drone photography is strictly prohibited and results in confiscation and fine.
🚫
Avoid Touts — Specific Agra Scams
Auto drivers who offer "free city tour" — they earn commission from carpet and marble shops. The "government marble emporium" near the Taj that is actually private and overpriced. "Marble inlay" souvenirs made of resin painted white (test: it will be warm to touch and very light). Guides outside the Taj who claim to be "official ASI guides" without showing an ID card issued by the Archaeological Survey of India. The genuine ASI guide service is at the main ticket counter.
👟
Footwear Inside the Taj Complex
You cannot take any vehicle past the outer gate — it's a 10-minute walk from the ticket counter to the inner gate. On the white marble plinth, booties (overshoes) are provided and mandatory to protect the marble. Slip-on shoes are much easier to manage than laced shoes since you remove shoes at multiple temples throughout the day. Inside Itmad-ud-Daulah and Agra Fort, you also remove footwear at several points.
🚂
Getting to Agra — Best Options
From Delhi: Gatimaan Express (Delhi Hazrat Nizamuddin to Agra Cantt, departs 8:10am, arrives 9:50am, ₹755 Executive Chair Car or ₹505 AC Chair Car — the fastest and most comfortable option). Shatabdi Express also available. Bus from Delhi: 3–4 hours, ₹200–₹400. Avoid night trains unless in sleeper class — arrive fresh for the early Taj visit. From Jaipur: 4 hours by train or taxi — natural Golden Triangle stop.
Packing for Agra
👟 Slip-on shoes — you remove footwear at multiple points. Slip-on sandals save enormous time and frustration.
🧴 SPF 50 sunscreen — the Taj complex has minimal shade. The white marble reflects UV intensely.
💧 2L water bottle — no water inside the Taj complex except a small cafe. Carry your own, especially in summer.
🕌 Modest clothing — for Jama Masjid and the Fatehpur Sikri dargah. Shoulders and knees covered.
🎟️Downloaded booking confirmation — save the Taj booking email as a screenshot for offline access.
💵 Cash for autos — auto drivers in Agra may not accept apps. Keep ₹1,000 in small notes for day transport.
📱 Offline maps — Agra's auto drivers are notorious for taking "scenic routes." Google Maps prevents this.
🧊 Small cooler bag — for day 2's petha purchases, especially in summer. Petha softens in heat.
🌂 Small umbrella/hat — Mehtab Bagh and Fatehpur Sikri have no shade in afternoon. Essential in summer.
Honest advice: The Taj Mahal will exceed your expectations. Every photograph you have seen fails to prepare you for the actual scale and perfection of the proportions. Go at sunrise. Stand in front of it in silence before reaching for your phone. Give it 5 minutes of undistracted attention. This is arguably the finest building human beings have ever constructed.
