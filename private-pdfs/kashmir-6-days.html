Kashmir 6-Day Itinerary Day 1–2 — Srinagar & Gulmarg
01
Day One · Srinagar Arrival
Dal Lake, Mughal Gardens & the Old City
Arrive Srinagar · Shikara sunset · Mughal Gardens · Old City · Ahdoos dinner
💰 Budget: ₹1,500–₹2,500 · Couple: ₹4,000–₹7,000 (excl. accommodation)
Arrive
Srinagar Airport (SXR) — Check into Houseboat
Pre-book your houseboat directly on Dal Lake. Take an auto or prepaid taxi from the airport (₹300–₹500). Your houseboat is moored on the lake — a shikara will ferry you to it. Settle in, have Kashmiri Kahwa tea, and let the silence of the lake sink in.
Houseboat: ₹2,000–₹8,000/night · Deluxe: ₹5,000–₹12,000
4:30 PM
Shikara Ride — Dal Lake at Sunset
Hire a shikara from the main ghat or directly through your houseboat. The 1-hour ride takes you past floating vegetable gardens, lotus beds, fishermen's nets and other houseboats. The mountains reflected in still water at golden hour are unforgettable. Negotiate the rate before boarding.
₹500/hour (negotiate) · Best: 5:00–6:30pm
Morning D1
Mughal Gardens — Shalimar, Nishat & Chashme Shahi
Three terraced gardens built by Mughal emperors on the shores of Dal Lake. Shalimar Bagh (built 1619 by Jahangir) — most romantic, best flowerbeds. Nishat Bagh — largest, best lake views from the upper terraces. Chashme Shahi — smallest but has a natural spring with the purest drinking water. Visit all three in one auto-rickshaw loop (negotiate ₹300 round trip).
Entry ₹24/person each · Auto: ₹300 round trip
Afternoon
Old City Walk — Jama Masjid & Shah Hamdan Mosque
The old city's wooden architecture is unlike anywhere else in India — influenced by Central Asian and Persian styles. Jama Masjid (1400 AD) has 378 wooden pillars. Shah Hamdan Mosque is covered in papier-mâché artwork. Walk the lanes of Nowhatta — see the wazwan cooks, copper craftsmen, and the city's real life.
Free entry · Modest dress required
Dinner
Ahdoos Restaurant — The Definitive Wazwan
Residency Road's most famous address since 1918. Order the full wazwan: Rogan Josh (the original), Gushtaba (minced meat in yoghurt gravy) and Yakhni. One thali serves two comfortably. The Seekh Kebab starter is exceptional. Cash preferred. Budget ₹600–₹900 for two.
₹300–₹450 per person · Residency Road
Stay on the lake, not in a hotel: Many visitors make the mistake of booking a hotel in the city. The houseboat on Dal Lake IS the Kashmir experience — the morning mist, the shikara delivering fresh bread, the silence. Don't skip it to save ₹1,000.

Kashmir 6-Day Itinerary Day 2 — Gulmarg
02
Day Two · Gulmarg Day Trip
Gulmarg — Asia's Highest Gondola at 4,200m
Cab from Srinagar · Gondola Phase 1 & 2 · Apharwat Peak · Snow · Back to Srinagar
💰 Budget: ₹2,500–₹3,500 · Couple: ₹5,000–₹7,000 (incl. gondola, excl. cab)
7:00 AM
Srinagar to Gulmarg — 56km, ~1.5 hrs
Hire a cab from your houseboat or from Dal Gate. The road winds through apple orchards and pine forests. Leave early — gondola queues build rapidly after 9am. Shared cabs from Dalgate bus stand (₹300/person) if you're on a tight budget.
Private cab: ₹1,200 return · Shared: ₹300/person
9:00 AM
Gondola Phase 1 — Gulmarg to Kongdori (2,600m → 3,080m)
The first phase of Asia's highest and longest cable car. 8-minute ride over dense pine forests, emerging above the treeline to a wide alpine meadow at Kongdori. Mountain views begin here — you can see Nanga Parbat (8,126m) on a clear day. Buy Phase 1 and Phase 2 tickets together at the base to avoid re-queuing.
Phase 1 ticket: ₹900/person · Buy both phases together
9:30 AM
Gondola Phase 2 — Kongdori to Apharwat Peak (3,080m → 4,200m)
The second phase is the one you cannot miss. The gondola rises steeply above all vegetation into a world of snow, rock and sky. Apharwat Peak has permanent snow year-round — even in July. At the top: 360-degree Himalayan panorama, bone-dry thin air, and a temperature 15–20°C colder than Srinagar. Bring a warm jacket. Altitude sickness can occur — ascend slowly, hydrate.
Phase 2: ₹900/person extra · Snow guaranteed year-round
Winter Only
Skiing at Gulmarg (Dec–Mar)
India's premier ski resort — over 1,000m of vertical, 13km of runs. Equipment rental (skis + boots + poles) ₹1,000–₹1,500/day. Professional instructors ₹2,000–₹3,000/day for group lessons. The powder snow quality rivals European resorts at a fraction of the cost. Snow conditions are best January–February.
Equipment rental: ₹1,000–₹1,500/day · Instructor: ₹2,000–₹3,000
Afternoon
Explore Gulmarg Meadow + Lunch + Return to Srinagar
At Kongdori level, walk the meadow (stunning in summer with wildflowers). Lunch at the Highlands Park Hotel restaurant (₹400–₹600 for a meal) or packed meals from Srinagar. Leave Gulmarg by 3pm to avoid evening traffic on the mountain road. Back in Srinagar by 5pm — sunset shikara if time permits.
⚠️ Gondola queue reality: On weekends and peak season, gondola queues at Phase 1 can be 2–3 hours long. Arrive by 8:30am. Weekday visits are significantly easier. Buy tickets online via the JKTDC app if available, or at the booking counter — not from touts who charge ₹200–₹500 "commission."

Kashmir 6-Day Itinerary Day 3–4 — Pahalgam
03
Days Three & Four · Valley of Shepherds
Pahalgam — Valleys, Glaciers & the Lidder River
Drive via Awantipora · Betaab Valley · Aru · Baisaran · Chandanwari day trip
💰 Budget: ₹1,500–₹2,500/day · Couple: ₹4,000–₹6,500/day (excl. accommodation)
Drive D3
Srinagar → Pahalgam (96km, ~2.5 hrs) via Awantipora
En route, stop at Awantipora ruins — a 9th-century Pratihara temple complex, dramatically weathered. In October, the saffron fields between Pampore and Pahalgam turn purple — a sight worth slowing down for. Saffron from this region is the world's most expensive (₹3–₹5 lakh/kg). Buy genuine saffron only from certified government outlets, not roadside stalls.
Cab Srinagar–Pahalgam: ₹1,800–₹2,500 (private)
Afternoon D3
Betaab Valley (₹50) + Aru Valley (₹50)
Betaab Valley — named after the 1983 Bollywood film shot here — is a wide green valley surrounded by snow peaks. The river runs straight through. ₹50 entry. Aru Valley is 12km from Pahalgam: quieter, more pristine, end-of-road feeling. Meadows grazed by Gujjar shepherds. Book a local cab for the circuit (₹800–₹1,200).
Entry ₹50 each · Local cab ₹800–₹1,200 for both
Morning D3
Baisaran — "Mini Switzerland" (Pony or Walk)
A high meadow 5km from Pahalgam town, accessible only by pony or foot. Surrounded by dense fir forests with snow-capped peaks visible on all sides. The Swiss comparison is no exaggeration — rolling green meadows, wildflowers, silence. Pony ride up and back is the comfortable option. The walk takes 1.5 hours each way on a steep trail.
Pony: ₹400–₹600 return · On foot: free
Evening D3
Lidder River Walk
The Lidder River runs through Pahalgam town — glacier-cold, crystal-clear. Walk the riverside path at dusk. Trout fishing is possible (licence from Forest Dept, ₹200). The river banks are lined with willows and pines. Listen for the sound — it genuinely sounds different from other rivers.
Day 4
Chandanwari Day Trip — Amarnath Yatra Basecamp
16km from Pahalgam, Chandanwari (2,895m) is the starting point of the famous Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage. Even outside the yatra season, the scenery is spectacular — a natural amphitheatre of mountains with a river bridge. ATV rides available on the snow (₹300–₹500). A snow point at the top is accessible by pony (₹600 return). Back in Pahalgam by evening.
Cab to Chandanwari: ₹700–₹1,000 return · ATV: ₹300–₹500
Pahalgam accommodation: Stay in Pahalgam town itself, not a hotel on the highway. The riverside hotels (Heevan Resort, Hotel Pine View) have mountain views from the rooms and you can hear the Lidder River from bed. Prices: ₹1,500–₹6,000/night depending on season.

Kashmir 6-Day Itinerary Day 5–6 — Sonamarg & Srinagar Exit
05
Days Five & Six · Golden Meadow + Farewell
Sonamarg & Srinagar — Glaciers, Markets & Goodbye
Thajiwas Glacier · Zero Point · Jama Masjid · Pashmina shopping · Evening flight
💰 Budget: ₹2,000–₹3,000 · Couple: ₹5,000–₹8,000 (Day 5 all-in excl. accommodation)
7:00 AM D5
Pahalgam → Srinagar → Sonamarg (87km from Srinagar)
Return to Srinagar from Pahalgam, drop bags at your houseboat, and head to Sonamarg same day — or stay a night in Sonamarg (basic hotels, ₹1,000–₹3,000). The road to Sonamarg follows the Sindh River through willow-lined valleys. Sonamarg (2,740m) means "Golden Meadow" — named for the saffron-coloured grasses in autumn. The approach through the meadow with the Thajiwas glacier visible is one of India's great natural vistas.
Cab Srinagar–Sonamarg return: ₹2,500–₹3,500
Morning D5
Thajiwas Glacier Walk
A 3km walk from the main Sonamarg meadow leads to the base of the Thajiwas Glacier. The walk is easy — well-marked, gentle gradient — through meadows with wildflowers (May–July). At the glacier base you can touch glacial ice and cross a meltwater stream. Pony ride to the glacier is available for those who prefer not to walk.
Walk: free · Pony to glacier: ₹600–₹800 return
If Road Open
Zero Point — Zojila Pass Approach (4,108m)
Zero Point is 25km beyond Sonamarg — only accessible when the Zojila Pass road is open (typically Jun–Oct, check locally). The road climbs steeply to permanent snow fields. Hire a local taxi at Sonamarg — private vehicles are not permitted beyond a checkpoint. The final snowfields are vast — bring sunglasses (snow glare). This is the highest point most tourists reach in Kashmir.
Shared taxi from Sonamarg: ₹3,500 return (4-seater)
Morning D6
Day 6 — Srinagar: Old City, Markets & Shopping
Last morning in Srinagar. Revisit Jama Masjid and Shah Hamdan Mosque in the morning light. Head to the main bazaar for shopping. Genuine Pashmina shawls: buy from Government Emporium (fixed price, certified) to avoid fakes. Test: real Pashmina passes through a ring and is warm even when thin. Dry fruits (walnuts, almonds, apricots) — buy from Lal Chowk market, not airport. Kashmiri Kahwa tea (loose leaf, ₹200–₹500/250g).
Govt. Emporium Pashmina: ₹2,500–₹25,000 · Market: haggle hard
Evening D6
Srinagar Airport (SXR) — Depart
Allow 3 hours before departure — security at Srinagar Airport has additional checks (military presence, document verification, luggage scans). Pre-book your airport cab. If your flight is delayed, use the time for a last Kahwa at the airport cafeteria. Take a mental photograph of the Zabarwan hills from the runway — Kashmir has a way of staying with you.
Airport cab: ₹500–₹800 · Arrive 3 hrs early
⚠️ Genuine Pashmina vs Fake: 90% of "Pashmina" sold in tourist markets is acrylic or wool blend. Real Pashmina (from Changthangi goats) passes through a ring, feels extraordinarily soft, and is warm despite being thin. Price under ₹1,500 = almost certainly fake. Buy from J&K Govt. Handicrafts Emporium (GI-certified) or reputable weavers in old city.

Kashmir 6-Day Itinerary Packing List & Essential Tips
What to Pack

Kashmir Packing List

Clothing — Layer Up

□Thermal base layers (essential even in June at Gulmarg)
□Fleece mid-layer or warm sweater
□Windproof outer jacket (Sonamarg/Apharwat wind is brutal)
□Light cotton clothes for Srinagar city
□Comfortable walking shoes (for Old City)
□Waterproof trekking shoes (for glacier walks)
□Gloves and woollen hat (glacier areas)
□Modest clothing for mosques (cover arms and legs)
□Slip-on footwear (remove at mosques often)

Health & Practical

□SPF 50 sunscreen (high-altitude UV is extremely strong)
□Polarised sunglasses (snow glare at Apharwat/Zero Point)
□Lip balm — dry air above 3,000m cracks lips fast
□Altitude sickness tablets (Diamox — consult doctor first)
□Personal medications (pharmacies limited in remote areas)
□Small first-aid kit (bandages, antiseptic)

Money & Connectivity

□Carry substantial cash — ATMs unreliable in Gulmarg, Sonamarg, Pahalgam
□UPI works in Srinagar but patchy in valleys
□BSNL or Airtel prepaid SIM (Jio may not work in Kashmir on tourist SIM)
□Note: Mobile internet can be restricted during curfews — not frequent but possible
□Download Google Maps offline before leaving Srinagar
□Power bank — cold weather drains batteries faster
□Camera spare batteries (cold kills battery charge)

Documents

□Aadhar / Passport (ID checked at multiple checkpoints)
□Foreign nationals: PAP permit (arrange in advance)
□Hotel booking printouts (sometimes requested at checkpoints)
□Travel insurance covering altitude activities
🏔️
Altitude & Weather
Gulmarg (2,650m) and Apharwat (4,200m) are significantly colder than Srinagar even in summer. Temperatures at Apharwat can be -5°C in July. The gondola is wind-exposed — dress for it. Hydrate heavily above 3,000m.
📡
Mobile & Internet
Post-paid SIMs from BSNL or Airtel work best. Prepaid tourist SIMs can be restricted. Internet speeds vary. Download offline maps, WhatsApp caches and hotel confirmations before you head to remote valleys.
🍖
Best Wazwan Restaurants
Ahdoos (Residency Rd), Mughal Darbar (Residency Rd), and Shamyana (Lal Chowk) are Srinagar's most trusted wazwan spots. For authentic home-style, ask your houseboat host to arrange a traditional wazwan dinner — unforgettable.
⚠️
Safety & Awareness
Kashmir is safe for tourists — millions visit annually. Avoid political demonstrations, check JKTDC and local news for any advisories. Military checkpoints are routine (carry ID). Follow your houseboat host's guidance on current local conditions.
