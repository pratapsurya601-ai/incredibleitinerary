# Meghalaya in 5 Days — Living Root Bridges, Waterfalls & the Wettest Place on Earth

**Trip Type:** Nature + Adventure + Off-Beat
**Duration:** 5 Days / 4 Nights
**Budget:** ₹12,000–₹32,000 per person
**Best Time:** October–May (avoid June–August monsoon peak for trekking)
**Difficulty:** Moderate (involves significant trekking)

---

## Why Meghalaya Blew My Mind

I expected rain and jungle. I got that, plus: living bridges made of tree roots that take 25 years to grow, a lake so clear it looks photoshopped, Asia's cleanest village, the second-wettest place on earth, and more waterfalls than I could count.

Meghalaya is the kind of destination that makes you wonder why you've been spending all your travel time at famous landmarks.

---

## Day 1 — Arrive Shillong: Scotland of the East

**Getting to Shillong:** Fly to Guwahati (all major Indian cities connected) then taxi/shared cab to Shillong (100km, 2–3 hours, ₹600–₹1,000 shared cab). Alternatively, direct flights to Shillong airport (small, limited airlines).

**Shillong:** The capital of Meghalaya, known as the "Scotland of the East" for its colonial-era architecture, pine forests, and misty hills. Has a strong rock music culture — more live music per capita than almost any Indian city.

**Wards Lake (₹20):** A small lake in the centre of the city. Peaceful, photogenic, surrounded by flowering gardens. Best in morning.

**Laitumkhrah Market:** Shillong's main market for local produce — smoked pork, bamboo shoots, betel nut, dried fish, hand-loomed Khasi textiles. The street food here — jadoh (rice and pork dish), doh-khleh (pork salad), black sesame pork — is extraordinary.

**Police Bazaar:** The tourist zone. Several good cafés — Dylan's Café (named after Bob Dylan — Meghalaya's rock obsession is real) and Café Shillong are reliable.

**Where I stayed:** Tripura Castle, Shillong — ₹3,500/night (heritage property). Budget: Hotel Barapani — ₹1,400/night.

## Day 2 — Cherrapunji: Waterfalls at Every Corner

**Cherrapunji (Sohra)** — 54km from Shillong, hire a taxi for the day (₹2,000 full day with driver-guide).

Cherrapunji is officially the world's second wettest place (Mawsynram nearby is #1). Even in dry season (October–April), the valley is dramatically green and the waterfalls still flow.

**Nohkalikai Falls:** India's tallest waterfall — a 340m plunge into a turquoise pool. The viewing platform is free. The colour of the pool at the base is an otherworldly green-blue created by dissolved minerals.

**Seven Sisters Falls:** Seven streams falling side by side from the plateau edge. Best viewed from the Eco Park viewpoint (₹30 entry). In monsoon, the falls merge into a single massive cascade.

**Mawsmai Cave (₹40):** A limestone cave 150m deep. Stalagmites, stalactites, and passages narrow enough to require sideways movement. Well-lit, accessible, 30 minutes.

**Thangkharang Park (₹30):** Viewpoint over the Bangladesh plains. On clear days you can see 100km into the flat lands below Meghalaya's plateau edge. The contrast between the forested cliff and the flat green plains below is striking.

**Lunch at a local dhaba in Cherrapunji town:** Jadoh and local pork. ₹150 for a full meal.

## Day 3 — Nongriat: The Double Decker Living Root Bridge

This is the centrepiece of any Meghalaya trip. Plan for a full day.

**Nongriat village** is accessible only on foot from Tyrna village (30km from Shillong, taxi ₹1,800).

**The trek:** 3,500 steps down and back up. Each way takes 60–75 minutes. The path is stone-stepped, through dense tropical rainforest. You'll cross multiple single-decker living root bridges on the way.

**The Double Decker Root Bridge:** Two tiers of bridges grown from the aerial roots of Ficus elastica (Indian rubber fig) trees. The Khasi people train the roots over decades — the oldest bridges are estimated at over 500 years. The bridge is genuinely alive and growing, strengthened by living wood rather than static structure. It gets more robust every year.

**Rainbow Falls:** Another 45-minute trek from the Double Decker bridge (additional 1,500 steps down). A 70m waterfall into a natural pool. On sunny days, a rainbow forms in the mist. Swimming here is extraordinary.

**Total trek:** 7–8 hours if you go to Rainbow Falls. Start by 8am. Carry 3 litres of water, snacks, and good grip shoes.

**₹50 entry at Tyrna. ₹50 at the bridge. ₹40 at Rainbow Falls.**

## Day 4 — Dawki: The Glass River

**Dawki** (90km from Shillong, on the Bangladesh border). Hire a taxi from Shillong (₹2,800 round trip or stay overnight in Dawki).

**Umngot River:** The most extraordinary thing I've seen in Northeast India. The river is so clear that boats appear to float in mid-air — the bottom is perfectly visible 6–8m down. Local fishing boats, fishermen pulling nets, children swimming — all in water so transparent it looks photoshopped.

**Boat ride (₹800/hour, shared among 4):** Float over the clear water in silence. The effect is the same every time — everyone in the boat goes quiet and just watches the river floor pass beneath them.

**Shnongpdeng village:** 10km upstream from Dawki. Quieter than the main tourist zone. Camping by the river available (₹500/tent). Kayaking and cliff jumping (₹500 activity package). The river here is even clearer than Dawki.

---

## Day 5 — Mawlynnong & Return to Shillong

**Mawlynnong village (90km from Shillong):** Named Asia's Cleanest Village in 2003. The honour has been maintained. Every household participates in daily cleaning; there's not a scrap of rubbish visible. The bamboo dustbins, the swept paths, the flower gardens — it's both inspiring and slightly surreal.

**Living Root Bridge** (2km walk from the village): A single-decker example, much less crowded than Nongriat. Good for those who didn't make the full Nongriat trek.

**Sky Walk:** A bamboo platform 85 feet above the ground with views over the Bangladesh plain. ₹30.

**Drive back to Shillong** (2 hours) for evening departure or overnight.

## What Most People Get Wrong

- **Going in monsoon for waterfalls:** Yes, the falls are biggest. But the Nongriat trek becomes extremely dangerous (slippery steps, rivers flood the path). October–May is better for everything.
- **Only doing Cherrapunji:** Dawki and Mawlynnong are as extraordinary and far less visited.
- **Not carrying cash:** ATMs exist in Shillong but nowhere else. Carry ₹5,000–₹8,000 in cash.
