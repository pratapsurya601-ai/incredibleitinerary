# Andaman Islands in 5 Days — White Sand, Turquoise Water & Zero Crowds

**Trip Type:** Beach + Islands + Snorkelling
**Duration:** 5 Days / 4 Nights
**Budget:** ₹18,000–₹55,000 per person (including flights from mainland)
**Best Time:** October–May
**Difficulty:** Easy

---

## The Andamans: India's Best-Kept Secret (That Isn't So Secret Anymore)

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands sit in the Bay of Bengal, 1,400km from the Indian mainland. The water here is among the cleanest in India — clear to 20m depth in places. The beaches make Goa look like a parking lot.

This 5-day route covers the best of North and South Andaman, including the car-free Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep) — home to Radhanagar Beach, consistently ranked one of Asia's finest.

---

## Day 1 — Port Blair: History Before the Beaches

Every Andaman trip starts here (the only airport). Port Blair's history is brutal and important.

**Cellular Jail National Memorial (₹30):** Built by the British in 1906 to imprison Indian freedom fighters. The "Black Waters" (Kala Pani) — crossing the ocean was considered a spiritual death. The jail housed Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Batukeshwar Dutt, and hundreds of others. The Sound & Light Show (evening, ₹250) is one of the most moving experiences in India — the voices of freedom fighters projected through the echoing corridors.

**Anthropological Museum:** A sensitive account of the indigenous Andamanese tribes — some, like the Sentinelese, still choose total isolation. Understanding their history and the ethics of non-contact makes for a more thoughtful visit.

**Corbyn's Cove Beach:** The nearest beach to Port Blair. Not the Andamans' best, but good for a swim before dinner.

**Dinner:** New Lighthouse Restaurant — excellent prawn curry, crab, lobster. Seafood in Port Blair is extraordinary and far cheaper than on the mainland. ₹400–₹700 for a full meal.

**Where I stayed:** Sinclairs Bayview Port Blair — ₹4,500/night (good location, bay views). Budget: Hotel Shompen — ₹1,800/night.

## Day 2 — North Andaman: Ross Island & North Bay

**Government ferry to Ross Island (₹125):** The former British administrative capital, now a beautiful ruin overtaken by jungle. Spotted deer wander through the old church, the ballroom, the officers' quarters. The British officers' tombstones are still there. ₹50 entry, 30-minute boat from Phoenix Bay jetty.

**North Bay Island:** A short boat ride away. Glass-bottom boat ride (₹800) over coral reefs — no swimming required to see the fish and coral. The snorkelling is good if you rent equipment (₹300).

**Alternatively — Jolly Buoy Island (seasonal, October–May):** One of Andaman's most beautiful islands. Accessible by government ferry (₹2,000 for day package). Pristine coral, multiple snorkelling spots, white sand. No food stalls on the island — carry lunch.

---

## Day 3 — Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep): The Best Beach Day

**Government ferry Port Blair to Havelock:** 1.5–2 hours on Makruzz or Green Ocean ferry (₹1,100–₹1,800 for AC cabin, book 2 weeks ahead in season).

**Radhanagar Beach (Beach 7):** Time magazine once called this Asia's best beach. I won't argue. A 2km arc of soft white sand, protected by jungle on both sides, with waves that are strong enough to be fun but not dangerous. Almost no vendors or development — you just swim, read, exist. Best 2–4pm when the light is dramatic.

**Elephant Beach:** 25 minutes by boat from Havelock jetty (₹500/person shared boat). The snorkelling here is world-class — the coral garden starts in waist-deep water. Rent equipment at the jetty (₹400 for full set). The best coral in the Andamans is here.

**Where to stay on Havelock:**
- **Budget:** Pristine Beach Resort — ₹5,000–₹9,000/night (bamboo cottages, 5-minute walk from Radhanagar)
- **Mid-range:** Barefoot at Havelock — ₹6,000–₹12,000/night (eco-resort, excellent restaurant on the beach)
- **Splurge:** Taj Exotica Resort & Spa — ₹22,000–₹35,000/night

## Day 4 — Havelock Diving & Neil Island

**Scuba Diving:** Havelock is one of India's premier dive destinations. Visibility: 20–25m. Water temperature: 28°C. Major dive sites: Barracuda City, Mac Point, The Wall.
- **Discover Scuba (no certification needed):** ₹4,000–₹5,500 (2 dives with instructor)
- **Open Water certification course:** ₹18,000–₹22,000 (3 days)
- **Recommended: Aqua India Dive Centre, Dive India Havelock**

**Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep):** Ferry from Havelock, 45 minutes (₹450). Smaller, quieter, no resorts. **Natural Bridge** is a rock formation at Bharatpur Beach that looks like a doorway into the sea. Laxmanpur Beach at sunset is one of the Andamans' hidden gems. If you prefer a completely quiet beach day, Neil is better than Havelock.

---

## Day 5 — Return to Port Blair & Departure

Morning ferry back to Port Blair (departs 6am or 8am from Havelock, book ahead).

**Samudrika Marine Museum (₹50):** Port Blair's small but excellent natural history museum with a live aquarium showing Andaman marine species.

**Aberdeen Bazaar:** Last chance for souvenir shopping — Andamani shells, pearl jewellery, coconut shell crafts. The government emporium has the best shell products (ethically harvested).

**Airport for departure.**

## Essential Tips

- **Book Makruzz/Green Ocean ferries early.** They fill 2–3 weeks ahead in November–February peak season.
- **Carry cash.** ATMs on Havelock and Neil frequently run out. Port Blair is fine.
- **No plastic bags** are allowed on the islands. Bring your own bag.
- **Monsoon (June–September):** Most ferries cancel, many resorts close. The islands are spectacular in post-monsoon October — green, clean, empty.
- **Free snorkelling tip:** Kalapathar Beach (3km from Havelock jetty) has shallow coral you can explore with just a mask. Zero cost, zero crowds.
