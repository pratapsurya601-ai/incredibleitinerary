## Version A (Leadership/Strategy Angle)

I've been to Rajasthan 4 times. The first time I tried to cover everything in 7 days.

The last time I covered the right things. Huge difference.

What changed: I stopped trying to optimise for volume and started optimising for depth.

The lesson applied to work too — but that's for another post.

The Rajasthan circuit that actually works in 7 days:

**Jaipur (2 days):**
Not 3. Day 3 in Jaipur is diminishing returns. Two days for Amber Fort, Jantar Mantar, and an evening in the bazaars is precisely right.

**Jodhpur (1.5 days):**
The blue city is profound. Mehrangarh Fort took me 4 hours the first time and 3.5 hours the fourth time — I kept finding things I'd missed. The view of the city from the ramparts, with 3,000 blue houses spreading below you, is genuinely different from any other view in India.

**Jaisalmer (3 days):**
Most people give it 1–1.5 days. That's the mistake. You need:
- Day 1: The golden fort (living fort — 4,000 residents, temples, havelis, the whole thing)
- Day 2: Patwon Ki Haveli in the morning, departure for Sam Dunes afternoon
- Night: Overnight at Sam. Not a day trip. Overnight.

The night in the desert at Sam is why people love Rajasthan. You can't get it on a day trip.

## Version B (Practical/Travel)

Rajasthan is India's most spectacular state. Most people underdo it.

Here's what I see repeatedly:
- Too much time in Jaipur (3+ days)
- Too little time in Jaisalmer (1 day)
- Jodhpur rushed (half day)
- No overnight at Sam Dunes

The fix:

**7 days, train-based circuit from Delhi:**

Delhi → Jaipur (overnight train, ₹350 sleeper) → 2 days Jaipur → Jodhpur (5-hour train, ₹200 sleeper) → 1.5 days Jodhpur → Jaisalmer (4.5-hour train, ₹250 sleeper) → 3 days Jaisalmer → Jaisalmer Airport → Delhi (flight, ~₹4,500)

**Total transport cost: ₹5,300 per person** for 700km of the most spectacular terrain in India.

**The moment that makes it worth every rupee:**

2am at Sam Sand Dunes. The bonfire has gone out. The music stopped an hour ago. You walked 300m from the camp and now you're lying on a dune looking at 50 million stars over a landscape that looks like the moon.

No traffic. No city glow. Complete silence.

That's the one.

## Version D (Philosophical — High Engagement)

My grandfather built his life savings travelling to Rajasthan in 1972 — a 14-day train journey that took three months of planning.

Last week I planned the same trip in 20 minutes on my phone.

Same forts. Same desert. Same blue city. Same stars over Sam Dunes.

But in 1972, my grandfather had no competing notifications. No Instagram comparison. No "10 things you MUST do in Rajasthan" articles to pre-load expectations.

He just arrived. Looked. Was astonished.

---
