## Version A (Career + Travel Narrative)

I took my first proper vacation in 3 years last October.

Six days in Kashmir.

I'd been telling myself I was "too busy." The projects, the deadlines, the always-something. You know the feeling.

What I found in Kashmir:

A houseboat on Dal Lake where the Wi-Fi didn't work and nobody cared.

A Gondola to 3,979m above sea level where the silence was so complete that the loudest thing was my own breathing.

A Gujjar shepherd who offered me tea near Aru Valley without speaking a word of Hindi. We sat together for 20 minutes. It was one of the best conversations I've had this year.

Here's what 6 days in Kashmir taught me that 3 years of busyness didn't:

✅ Most "urgent" things can wait 6 days without consequence
✅ Beauty that you can't photograph still deserves your full attention
✅ Hospitality from people who have less than you is humbling in ways that are hard to articulate
✅ The return flight always comes too soon

## Version B (Professional/Business Travel)

The best productivity hack I found last year had nothing to do with apps or routines.

It was 6 days in Kashmir without email.

I know. Bear with me.

I run a small team. Taking 6 days off felt irresponsible. What happened while I was away:

→ The team made 3 decisions I would have made anyway
→ One decision I would have made differently (they made the better call)
→ Nothing caught fire

What happened to me:
→ I slept 9 hours a night for 6 nights
→ I read 2 books
→ I came back with a clarity of priorities I hadn't had in 18 months

The specific experience that did it: a shikara boat at 5:45am on Dal Lake. The boatman rowing in silence. The lake completely still. Snow peaks reflected perfectly in the water.

I hadn't felt that still in 3 years.
