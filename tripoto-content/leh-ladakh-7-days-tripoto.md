# Leh Ladakh in 7 Days — The Road Trip That Changes You

**Trip Type:** Adventure + Mountain + Road Trip
**Duration:** 7 Days / 6 Nights
**Budget:** ₹25,000–₹65,000 per person (excluding flights)
**Best Time:** June–September
**Difficulty:** Moderate to Challenging (altitude)

---

## Before You Go: The Altitude Reality

Leh sits at 3,524m. Khardung La Pass reaches 5,359m. These are serious altitudes. Altitude sickness is real and it doesn't care how fit you are.

**Day 1 and 2 in Leh must be rest days.** No hikes, no drinking, no heavy exertion. Drink 4–5 litres of water daily. Take Diamox if your doctor recommends it. I've seen fit marathon runners get evacuated. Don't be that person.

Once you've acclimatised? This place is the most extraordinary landscape in India. Maybe in the world.

## Day 1 — Arrive Leh: Do Nothing (Seriously)

Fly from Delhi (1 hour) or Mumbai (2 hours). The moment you step off the plane you're at 3,500m. Your body has no idea what just happened.

**Today's plan:** Check in. Drink water. Eat light (dal, rice — no heavy protein or alcohol). Sleep. Walk slowly.

If you feel a headache: that's normal. Drink more water and rest. If you feel breathless at rest or develop nausea: see a doctor immediately.

**Leh market in the evening:** Walk slowly through the main bazaar. Pashmina, turquoise jewellery, Ladakhi apricot jam (extraordinary — buy several jars), dried apricots. The market is calm, photogenic, full of monks and military personnel and trekkers.

**Where I stayed:** The Grand Dragon Ladakh — ₹6,500/night. Budget option: Old Ladakh Guest House — ₹1,500/night (basic, run by a Ladakhi family, beautiful rooftop).

---

## Day 2 — Leh: Gentle Exploration

Still no heavy activity. But gentle sightseeing is fine.

**Shanti Stupa:** A white Buddhist stupa on a hilltop 5km from Leh. 500 steps up. Take it slowly. The view from the top — snow peaks in every direction, the Indus valley below, Leh town spread out — is one of the defining images of Ladakh.

**Leh Palace:** A 9-storey former royal palace built in the 17th century, modelled on the Potala Palace in Lhasa. Partially ruined but magnificent. ₹100 entry. The rooftop view over the city and towards Stok Kangri is worth the climb.

**Monastery walk:** Sankar Monastery is 2km from Leh market. Small, active, free entry. Monks study here; it feels very different from the touristy major monasteries.

**Magnetic Hill (28km from Leh):** An optical illusion where the road appears to slope downhill but vehicles seem to roll uphill. Stop for the photo, move on — it's not worth more than 15 minutes.

---

## Day 3 — Nubra Valley: Over the World's Highest Pass

**Khardung La Pass (5,359m):** Commonly (if inaccurately) claimed to be the world's highest motorable road. Whatever the ranking, this is genuinely extraordinary. Snow walls 4m high on either side of the road in June. The air is thin enough that walking to the tea shop feels like an effort. Allow 3 hours from Leh; stop only briefly (altitude affects you fast if you're not acclimatised to it).

**Descent into Nubra:** From the barren high-altitude moonscape, the road descends into a green valley with sand dunes. The Shyok and Nubra rivers meet here. It's disorienting in the best way — you just crossed a 5,000m pass and now you're looking at camel rides in a desert.

**Hunder Village:** The famous Bactrian (double-humped) camel safari on the cold desert dunes. ₹400 for a 45-minute ride. The backdrop of snow peaks + sand dunes is genuinely surreal.

**Diskit Monastery:** Perched on a cliff above Nubra. 32m statue of Maitreya Buddha faces north, toward the Pakistan border. ₹50 entry. The views are staggering.

**Stay in Nubra:** Tents or guesthouses in Hunder or Sumur. ₹1,200–₹3,500/night including dinner.

---

## Day 4 — Nubra to Pangong Tso: The Epic Drive

This drive (160km, 5–6 hours) is one of the greatest road trips in the world.

The route crosses the Shyok River, climbs over the Wari La pass, and deposits you at the western shore of Pangong Lake — a 134km long alpine lake, 60% of which is in Tibet.

**The lake:** Appears from the valley like a strip of impossibly blue water between brown mountains. As you descend, it expands. Up close, the colour shifts from turquoise to azure to deep cobalt depending on the time of day and angle of sunlight. The shore is thin gravel. The water is ice-cold and salty.

**3 Idiots point:** Yes, the famous final scene of the film was shot here. There's a small hut with props. Every Indian tourist takes the same photo. Join them; it's fun.

**Stay at Pangong:** Luxury Swiss camps (₹8,000–₹15,000/person including all meals) or simple guesthouses in Spangmik village (₹1,500–₹2,500). The camps are overpriced but the experience of sleeping next to the lake is worth it if you can afford it.

---

## Day 5 — Pangong Sunrise: The Best Morning of Your Life

Set your alarm for 5am. Walk to the lake shore before sunrise. The mountains reflect on the still water. As the sun rises, the lake changes colour every 10 minutes — violet to orange to that deep impossible blue.

There are no words. Go.

Spend the morning slowly. Lunch at your camp. Departure after noon.

---

## Day 6 — Pangong to Leh: Via Chang La & Monasteries

**Chang La Pass (5,360m):** Second high-altitude pass of the trip. Similar experience to Khardung La — snow, thin air, a ramshackle tea stall where everything tastes better because of the altitude.

**Hemis Monastery** (on the route back): The largest and wealthiest monastery in Ladakh. Built in 1672. Remarkable thangka collection, ancient manuscripts, and during Hemis Festival (June–July) — the most spectacular masked dance festival in the Himalayas. ₹100 entry.

**Thiksey Monastery:** Often compared to Potala Palace for its tiered whitewashed walls on a hill. A 15m Maitreya statue inside. ₹50 entry.

**Return to Leh by evening.**

---

## Day 7 — Leh: Final Morning

**Spituk Monastery:** Just 8km from Leh Airport, a working monastery with morning prayers at dawn. Monks in maroon robes chanting in a candlelit hall. Free entry.

**Airport, departure.**

The journey back to Delhi feels like re-entering a different planet. You'll spend the flight looking out the window wondering when you can come back.

## Critical Permits Required

- **Inner Line Permit (ILP):** Required for Nubra Valley, Pangong, Tso Moriri, and Dha-Hanu. Online via [lahdclehpermit.in](https://lahdclehpermit.in). ₹400–₹600 per area. Get before you leave Leh — some checkposts won't let you through without it.
- **Protected Area Permit (PAP):** Required for foreign nationals. Apply at Leh DC Office.
