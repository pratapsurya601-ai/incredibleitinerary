# Sikkim in 6 Days — Monasteries, Mountain Views & the Forgotten Himalayan Kingdom

**Trip Type:** Mountains + Culture + Nature
**Duration:** 6 Days / 5 Nights
**Budget:** ₹18,000–₹45,000 per person
**Best Time:** March–May (rhododendrons), October–December (Kanchenjunga views)
**Difficulty:** Moderate

---

## The India Most Indians Haven't Seen

Sikkim is a former kingdom that became India's newest state in 1975. It's also India's most organic state — chemical pesticides are banned. It has more monasteries per capita than anywhere else in India. Kanchenjunga (8,586m), the world's third-highest mountain, dominates the horizon.

Most Indians have heard of it. Very few have been. Their loss.

---

## Day 1 — Arrive Gangtok: The Capital in the Clouds

**Getting there:** Fly to Bagdogra Airport (Siliguri), then shared cab to Gangtok (125km, 4 hours, ₹300–₹400 per seat). Or overnight bus from Siliguri (₹250).

**Gangtok** sits at 1,650m in the Himalayas. On a clear day, Kanchenjunga is visible from the main MG Marg promenade — an impossible white pyramid floating above the clouds.

**MG Marg (pedestrian zone):** Gangtok's main street. Clean, traffic-free, lined with cafés and shops. The best Darjeeling tea you'll ever drink costs ₹60 at any shop here. Buy 200g of first-flush tea to take home (₹400–₹800 for genuine first-flush).

**Enchey Monastery (free):** A 200-year-old Nyingma monastery perched on a hilltop above Gangtok. The prayer hall interior — paintings of wrathful deities, butter lamps, monks chanting at dawn — is one of the most atmospheric spaces in the Himalayas.

**Flower Exhibition Centre (March–April, ₹30):** Sikkim has 600+ species of orchid and the official flower is Dendrobium nobile. In spring, the exhibition is spectacular.

**Where I stayed:** Chumbi Mountain Retreat, Gangtok — ₹5,500/night (stunning Kanchenjunga view). Budget: Hotel Sonam Delek — ₹1,500/night.

---

## Day 2 — Rumtek Monastery & Tsomgo Lake

**Rumtek Monastery (24km from Gangtok, ₹50):** One of the most important monasteries outside Tibet. The seat of the Karma Kagyu sect of Tibetan Buddhism. The main stupa holds the sacred relics of the 16th Karmapa. The monastery complex has a heavy security presence (due to a succession dispute) but the prayer hall, monks' quarters, and courtyard are open.

**Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake, 38km from Gangtok, ₹200):** A glacial lake at 3,753m. Turquoise, surrounded by snow peaks, accessible by taxi (₹1,600 for full day including Baba Mandir and Nathu La border).

**Nathu La Pass (4,310m, at the India-China border):** Open to Indian nationals only. Requires a permit from Gangtok's tourism office (₹200, takes 30 minutes). The actual border — Chinese soldiers on one side, Indian on the other — is surreal. Even more surreal: the Indian side has a gift shop selling "I visited the India-China border" mugs.

**Baba Mandir:** A temple dedicated to an Indian soldier whose spirit is said to protect the high-altitude frontier. Deeply revered by the army. The story (true or legendary) is moving.

## Day 3 — Pelling: Kanchenjunga Up Close

**Drive to Pelling** (130km from Gangtok, 4 hours, ₹2,800 by taxi). The drive through Sikkim's western valleys — cardamom farms, waterfalls, hanging bridges — is extraordinary.

**Pelling** sits at 2,150m with direct views of Kanchenjunga's south face — the mountain appears enormous from here, just 40km away as the crow flies.

**Pemayangtse Monastery (₹50):** The most important and oldest monastery in West Sikkim. The 3-storey wooden model of the mythical Zangdok Palri (Guru Rinpoche's heavenly palace) on the top floor is an extraordinary work of folk art.

**Khecheopalri Lake (13km from Pelling, free):** A sacred lake — birds supposedly remove any leaf that falls on the surface. The forest walk to the lake is peaceful. Monks pray at the lakeshore at dawn.

**Rabdentse Ruins (₹50):** The ruined capital of the former Kingdom of Sikkim. Stone walls, prayer flags, views over three valleys simultaneously. Almost no visitors.

**Where I stayed in Pelling:** Norbu Ghang Resort — ₹4,500/night (Kanchenjunga view from bed). Budget: Hotel Sonamchen — ₹1,200/night.

---

## Day 4 — Yuksom: Where Sikkim Began

**Yuksom** (40km from Pelling). The first capital of Sikkim — where the first Chogyal (king) was crowned in 1641.

**Dubdi Monastery (30-minute uphill walk from Yuksom):** Sikkim's oldest monastery, built in 1701. Completely off the tourist circuit. The monk who opened the door for me appeared surprised to have visitors. The views from the monastery courtyard over the valley are enormous.

**Khangchendzonga National Park entry:** Yuksom is the base for the Kanchenjunga Base Camp trek (11 days). Even for non-trekkers, the walk to **Kabhur** viewpoint (4km round trip from Yuksom) gives views of Kanchenjunga Base Camp peaks.

**Tashiding Monastery (25km from Yuksom, ₹50):** Set on a conical hill above the confluence of two rivers. Prayer wheels line the entire path up. On Bhumchu festival (February), the water level in a sacred vessel is said to predict Sikkim's future for the year.

---

## Day 5 — Ravangla & Return to Gangtok

**Ravangla (55km from Yuksom):**

**Buddha Park (₹50):** A 130-foot golden statue of the Shakyamuni Buddha, inaugurated by the Dalai Lama in 2013. The surrounding garden has prayer flags, meditation spaces, and Kanchenjunga visible behind the statue on clear days. One of the best Buddha images in India.

**Ralang Monastery (free):** A massive modern monastery complex, very active. The prayer hall interior — 3 storeys of paintings, 1,000 butter lamps, monks chanting — is extraordinary. Photography inside with permission.

**Drive back to Gangtok** (75km, 2.5 hours).

---

## Day 6 — Last Morning in Gangtok

**Ganesh Tok viewpoint:** 5km from Gangtok centre. The best morning view of Kanchenjunga from within Gangtok — if the sky is clear at sunrise. Free.

**Namgyal Institute of Tibetology (₹10):** Extraordinary museum of Tibetan Buddhist art, manuscripts, and ritual objects. One of the most important Tibetan cultural preservation centres outside Tibet itself. Scholars come from around the world. Open until 4pm.

**Departure:** Taxi back to Bagdogra for flight, or overnight bus to Kolkata.

## Permits Required

- **Inner Line Permit (ILP):** Required for areas near the China border (Nathu La, North Sikkim). Get from Sikkim Tourism office in Gangtok. Free, instant.
- **RAP (Restricted Area Permit):** Required for some North Sikkim areas. Takes 1 day to process.
