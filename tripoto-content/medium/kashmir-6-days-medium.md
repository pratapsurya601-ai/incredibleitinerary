I put off Kashmir for three years. The news cycle made it sound complicated. The logistics sounded expensive. The mountains looked too far away from my desk in Mumbai.

---

## Why Kashmir's Reputation Doesn't Match the Reality

The fear most Indian tourists have about Kashmir is understandable but largely outdated. The tourist areas — Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam — have been safe and functional for travellers for years. The army presence is visible, but so are the crowds of happy tourists from Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, and Karnataka.

The thing nobody tells you: Kashmiris are among the most welcoming hosts in India. The hospitality is intense and genuine.

## The Itinerary That Works

**Day 1 — Srinagar:** The houseboat, the shikara, the evening in Nishat Bagh watching the sun drop behind the Zabarwan mountains.

**Day 2 — Gulmarg:** The Gondola to Apharwat Peak (3,979m). Snow in October. The silence at altitude.

**Day 3 — Pahalgam:** The most beautiful road in India — 3 hours through apple orchards, past the ruins at Avantipora, along the Lidder River.

**Day 4 — Pahalgam valleys:** Betaab Valley at 8am before the crowds. Aru Valley where a Gujjar shepherd offered me tea and we communicated without a common language.

**Day 5 — Srinagar:** Shankaracharya Temple, Old City lanes, a Wazwan dinner.

**Day 6 — Slow morning, departure:** Houseboat breakfast, the morning light on Dal Lake, home.

## The 5 Things I Wish I'd Known

**1. Book houseboats directly.**
Call the houseboat owner directly (search "Dal Lake houseboat direct" on Google). Booking through platforms adds 25–40%. My houseboat — carved ceilings, private sit-out, 3 meals included — was ₹3,200/night direct vs ₹4,800 on a booking platform for the same property.

**2. The Gondola fills by 9am.**
Phase 2 (the higher cable car to Apharwat Peak) has limited capacity. If you arrive at 10am, you're queueing for 2 hours. Be there at 8:30am.

**3. Real pashmina smells like lanolin and has visible natural fibres.**
Fake pashmina (wool-acrylic) is sold everywhere for ₹500–₹800. Real pashmina starts at ₹4,000 and goes to ₹25,000+. Government emporiums (J&K Handicrafts, Srinagar) sell certified pashmina with certificates of authenticity.

**4. Wazwan is not optional.**
Order it at a proper restaurant, not a tourist hotel. The 7-course version (rogan josh, yakhni, gushtaba, kebabs) runs ₹800–₹1,200/person. Every rupee is justified.

**5. The lesser-known stops on the Pahalgam road are the best part.**
Avantipora ruins (9th century, no entry fee, virtually no tourists), Saffron fields of Pampore (October peak), the apple belt of Pulwama — your driver will know all of them if you ask.

## The Booking Strategy

**Flights:** Book 3–5 weeks ahead for reasonable prices. SpiceJet and Air India have consistent Delhi–Srinagar routes. Prices spike in July–August (peak season) and October (Durga Puja).

**Accommodation:** Don't book all in advance. Book the houseboat in advance (they fill), leave Pahalgam flexible. Small hotels in Pahalgam are available without bookings most of the year.

**Local transport:** Hire a cab from the airport with your houseboat-wala's recommendation — they know reliable drivers. Negotiate a per-day rate for the whole trip (₹2,500–₹3,500/day for a full SUV, all destinations).

## The One Moment That Stayed With Me

I was on the boat at 5:45am. The boatman hadn't spoken for 20 minutes. The lake was completely still, the reflection perfect, the mountains around Dal Lake pink in the first light.

A family of ducks swam through the reflection and broke it into ripples.

That was it. That was the moment.

No photo I took came close to capturing it. I stopped trying and just watched.

Kashmir will do that to you.
