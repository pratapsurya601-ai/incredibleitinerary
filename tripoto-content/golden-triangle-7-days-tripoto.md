# Golden Triangle in 7 Days — Delhi, Agra & Jaipur Done Right

**Trip Type:** Heritage + Culture + Architecture

**Best Time:** October–March

---

## The Classic Route, Stripped of Clichés

Delhi → Agra → Jaipur → Delhi. About 700km in a rough triangle shape. India's most travelled tourist route. The one everyone either does first or dismisses as "too touristy."

I've done it four times: once on a shoestring budget, once on a research trip, once with family, once showing a foreign friend India for the first time. It works every time. Because the Taj Mahal is still the Taj Mahal. Jaipur's Amber Fort is still extraordinary. And Old Delhi's chaos is still unlike anywhere on earth.

The trick is knowing where the clichés end and the real India begins.

## Day 1 — Old Delhi: Controlled Chaos at Maximum Volume

**Jama Masjid (free):** India's largest mosque. Built by Shah Jahan in 1656. The courtyard holds 25,000 people. Climb the south minaret for a view over Old Delhi's tangle of rooftops — ₹100 for the climb (cash only).

**Chandni Chowk by rickshaw:** Hire a cycle rickshaw for the 45-minute tour (₹200 negotiated). Your driver will narrate, dodge, weave through the market — it's magnificent madness. Stop at Paranthe Wali Gali (lane of stuffed flatbreads) — one of Chandni Chowk's oldest lanes, where families have been making paranthe since 1875. ₹80–₹120 per stuffed paratha.

**Khari Baoli (Spice Market):** Asia's largest spice market. Burlap sacks of turmeric, cardamom, red chilli, black pepper tower above you. The colours and smells are overwhelming. This is wholesale trade — you're not expected to buy small quantities, but vendors are used to tourists watching. Photograph freely, buy if you want.

**Red Fort (₹600 for foreigners, ₹50 for Indians):** Mughal emperor's palace-fort from 1648. The Lahori Gate entrance is magnificent. Allow 2 hours.

**Where I stayed:** The Lalit, Delhi — ₹8,500/night (centrally located heritage hotel). Budget: Moustache Hostel, Paharganj — ₹700/night dorm.

---

## Day 2 — New Delhi: The Mughals' Legacy & the Millennials' Village

**Humayun's Tomb (₹550):** The architectural prototype for the Taj Mahal, built 1565. Surrounded by a Persian garden (charbagh), recently restored. Stunning red sandstone and white marble. Almost nobody goes here compared to the Taj — it's peaceful, gorgeous, world-class.

**Lodhi Gardens (free):** A Delhi park containing genuine 15th-century Mughal tombs scattered among joggers, picnicking families, and mynah birds. Delhi's best free attraction.

**Hauz Khas Village:** A medieval reservoir + madrasa complex surrounded by a trendy café and boutique neighbourhood. Have lunch at a rooftop restaurant with views over the ancient ruins. The juxtaposition of Mughal architecture and millennial brunch spots is very 21st-century Delhi.

**Qutub Minar (₹600):** A 73m minaret built in 1193 — the tallest brick minaret in the world. The complex includes India's oldest mosque. Allow 90 minutes.

## Day 3 — The Taj Mahal: Worth All the Hype

**Gatimaan Express** (Delhi Hazrat Nizamuddin to Agra Cantt, 6:10am, 1hr 40min, ₹755 Chair Car). The fastest and most comfortable way. Book on IRCTC at least 1–2 weeks ahead.

**Arrive Agra by 8am.** Take a pre-paid auto to the Taj's South Gate. Entry: ₹1,300 + ₹200 shoe cover.

**The Taj Mahal:** You know what it looks like. What photos don't convey is the scale, the perfection of the marble inlay work, and the change in light across the day. At sunrise it's pink-gold. By mid-morning it's blinding white. At sunset (come back if you can) it's amber.

The famous first view — walking through the main gateway and seeing the Taj framed in the archway — is still breathtaking despite the crowds. The inlay work inside (pietra dura — semi-precious stones set into marble) is extraordinary under a torch.

**Agra Fort (₹650):** 2.5km from the Taj, an hour's walk. The fort built by Akbar where Shah Jahan was imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb. From Shah Jahan's imprisonment tower, you can see the Taj across the river — which is what historians believe he spent his last years looking at.

**Where to stay in Agra:** Hotel Saniya Palace (₹1,500/night, Taj view from rooftop). Splurge option: Trident Agra (₹8,000/night, Taj Garden view restaurant).

---

## Day 4 — Agra to Jaipur via Fatehpur Sikri

**Fatehpur Sikri (40km from Agra, ₹550):** Built by Akbar in 1571, abandoned 14 years later when the water supply failed. A complete Mughal capital — the palace, mosque, harem, audience hall — perfectly preserved in red sandstone. Almost no crowds. Take a local guide (₹400) to understand what each building was used for.

**Drive to Jaipur** (235km, 4 hours from Fatehpur Sikri). Hire a cab (₹3,500) or use the AC Volvo bus from Agra (₹350, book at RSRTC).

---

## Day 5 — Jaipur: Amber Fort & The Sunset View

**Amber Fort (₹550 + ₹100 jeep up):** See Day 2 of the Rajasthan guide. Don't miss the Sheesh Mahal inside.

**Panna Meena Ka Kund:** A step well (baoli) from the 16th century, 2km from Amber Fort. Blue and ochre geometric steps descending to water. Completely photogenic, almost always uncrowded. Free.

**Nahargarh Fort sunset:** See previous Rajasthan entry. Best at 6pm.

---

## Day 6 — Jaipur: The Pink City in Depth

**Jantar Mantar (₹200):** Astronomical instruments at 1:1 scale. The Samrat Yantra sundial is accurate to 2 seconds. Built 1734.

**Hawa Mahal (₹50):** The Pink City's icon. 953 windows, built for royal ladies to observe street processions.

**Bazaars:** Johari Bazaar for jewellery (Jaipur is India's gem cutting capital — precious and semi-precious stones significantly cheaper here than elsewhere). Bapu Bazaar for blue pottery, Sanganeri block-print fabric, Jaipuri quilts (razai). A Jaipuri quilt for ₹800–₹1,200 is one of the best-value souvenirs in India.

**Thali dinner at LMB (Laxmi Mishtan Bhandar):** A Jaipur institution since 1954. Rajasthani vegetarian thali — unlimited refills of dal baati churma, gatte ki sabzi, bajra roti. ₹450. Phenomenal.

---

## Day 7 — Jaipur to Delhi: Options

**Shatabdi Express** (Jaipur to Delhi, 5.5 hours, ₹895 Chair Car) — departs multiple times daily.

**Road trip option:** Hire a car from Jaipur and drive back via Sariska Tiger Reserve (tiger sighting possible — ₹2,500 for a jeep safari) and Neemrana Fort (lunch at this stunning step-palace converted to a hotel — ₹1,200 for walk-in visitors).
