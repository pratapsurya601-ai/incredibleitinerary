# Coorg in 3 Days — Coffee, Waterfalls & the Scotland of India

**Trip Type:** Nature + Relaxation + Offbeat
**Duration:** 3 Days / 2 Nights
**Budget:** ₹6,000–₹25,000 per person
**Best Time:** October–May
**Difficulty:** Easy

---

## Why Coorg is Karnataka's Best-Kept Secret

Coorg (Kodagu district) is one of the most rewarding short trips from Bangalore or Mysore. Three days is enough to see the main attractions without feeling rushed — and completely enough to understand why coffee plantation owners, bird watchers, and nature lovers keep coming back year after year.

The "Scotland of India" tag comes from the mist, the rolling hills, and the cold evenings. It's accurate enough to stick.

---

## Day 1 — Arrive Madikeri: Coffee Country Check-In

**Getting there from Bangalore:** KSRTC overnight bus (6 hours, ₹450–₹700) or drive (250km, 5 hours).

**Madikeri town:** The district headquarters. Small, walkable, at 1,150m elevation.

**Raja's Seat (free):** A garden and viewpoint said to be where the Coorg kings watched sunsets. The view over the coffee and spice-clad valleys is outstanding in evening light. Come at 5:30pm.

**Madikeri Fort (₹10):** A 17th-century fort converted to a government complex. Small but photogenic — the gates and the church inside are worth a walk.

**Abbey Falls (6km from Madikeri, ₹50):** A 70ft waterfall through a private coffee estate. The walk through the coffee plantation to reach the falls (10 minutes) passes workers and trees laden with coffee berries. The falls themselves are modest but the setting in the forest is beautiful.

**Coffee estate dinner:** Most Coorg homestays include dinner. A typical home-cooked Coorgi meal — pandi curry (pork in black vinegar), kadambuttu (steamed rice dumplings), bamboo shoot curry — is deeply satisfying. ₹200–₹400 at local restaurants if not staying at a homestay.

**Where I stayed:** Honey Valley Estate Homestay — ₹4,500/night (working coffee estate, stunning views, excellent Coorgi meals included). Budget: Hotel Capitol Village — ₹1,500/night.

## Day 2 — Dubare & Namdroling: Elephants and Tibetans

**Dubare Elephant Camp (₹600 for elephant interaction):** On the Kaveri River, 30km from Madikeri. Kerala-trained forest elephants are bathed and fed here in the morning. The program includes feeding, washing, and a brief interaction. Book in advance (Jungle Lodges & Resorts, official operator). The camp is responsible compared to most "elephant tourism" in India.

**Nagarhole National Park (border with Kerala):** Kerala's Wayanad and Karnataka's Nagarhole share a continuous forest. Jeep safaris (₹2,500 for 6-person jeep, 3-hour morning or evening safari) give chances to see gaur, sambar, spotted deer, wild elephant, and occasionally leopard and tiger. Book through the forest department office.

**Golden Temple, Bylakuppe (20km from Dubare):** The largest Tibetan settlement in India outside Tibet. The Namdroling Monastery here contains a 60ft golden Buddha flanked by two other equally large Buddhas — the hall is vast, glowing with gold, incense and butter lamps. Free entry. The surrounding settlement has Tibetan restaurants, markets, and prayer wheel paths. Extraordinary to find in the middle of Karnataka coffee country.

**Return to Madikeri for evening.** Dinner at a Tibetan restaurant in Madikeri (several good ones) — momos, thukpa, Tibetan butter tea. ₹200–₹350.

## Day 3 — Iruppu Falls & Coffee Shopping

**Iruppu Falls (Lakshmana Tirtha, 83km from Madikeri):** A temple waterfall on the border of Nagarhole forest. The waterfall cascades over a rocky ledge in several stages. The forest walk from the parking area (1.5km) passes the river — good birdwatching (hornbills, kingfishers, Malabar whistling thrush).

**Coffee Plantation Walk:** Back toward Madikeri, stop at any estate that offers a guided coffee walk. The Kelagur Estate (10km from Madikeri) and Abbi Estate both allow walk-ins. Coffee, cardamom, black pepper, and vanilla all grow here. Understanding the process — from cherry to bean to cup — is more interesting than it sounds. Free or ₹100 for guided walk.

**Coffee Shopping:** Coorg produces some of India's finest coffee — predominantly Arabica and Robusta, shade-grown under forest canopy. Estate-fresh coffee (not roasted by corporate brands) is available everywhere in Madikeri:
- Green beans (roast yourself): ₹200–₹300/kg
- Freshly roasted ground: ₹300–₹500/kg
- Branded "Coorg coffee" at airport: 2x the price, identical quality

**Buy from:** Coorg Consolidated Commodities (CCC), Madikeri — reliable, consistent quality.

**Spice shopping:** Coorg cardamom is India's finest — large, green, intensely aromatic. ₹300–₹500/100g at farm shops (vs. ₹800+ in Mumbai/Delhi grocery stores). Honey is also extraordinary.

**Departure:** Evening bus back to Bangalore (₹450) or drive.

## Secret Spots Most Guides Miss

- **Mandalpatti viewpoint:** 18km from Madikeri, requires a shared jeep from Abbi Falls (₹100 per seat). The viewpoint sits above the cloud layer — looking down at white cloud filling the valleys below the green peaks is extraordinary.
- **Brahmagiri Peak (3km trek from Iruppu):** A rewarding short trek with views into Kerala's Wayanad district.
- **Kaveri Nisargadhama (₹50, 1km from Dubare):** A river island with bamboo cottages and elephant sightings from the suspension bridge — quieter than Dubare camp.
