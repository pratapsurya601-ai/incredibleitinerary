# Rishikesh & Haridwar in 3 Days — Rafting, Temples & the Ganga Aarti

**Trip Type:** Spiritual + Adventure + Nature
**Duration:** 3 Days / 2 Nights
**Budget:** ₹4,500–₹18,000 per person (excluding travel from Delhi)
**Best Time:** September–June (avoid monsoon July–August for rafting)
**Difficulty:** Easy

---

## Two Cities, One River, Completely Different Souls

Haridwar and Rishikesh are 24km apart on the Ganges. They're both sacred. Both on a river that descends from the Himalayas. Both attract millions of pilgrims and travellers every year.

But they feel entirely different.

**Haridwar** is devotion — massive, noisy, overwhelmingly Hindu, with the Ganga Aarti as its centrepiece.

**Rishikesh** is yoga, adventure, and café culture — the kind of place where a dreadlocked Israeli backpacker and a 70-year-old Gujarati pilgrim sit at adjacent tables without either finding it odd.

Both are extraordinary. Three days covers both comfortably.

## Day 1 — Haridwar: Har Ki Pauri & the Evening Aarti

**Getting there:** Trains from Delhi to Haridwar are regular and cheap — Jan Shatabdi Express (5.5 hours, ₹535 CC) or Nanda Devi Express. Book on IRCTC.

**Har Ki Pauri Ghat:** The most sacred ghat on the Ganges outside Varanasi. The footprint of Lord Vishnu is said to be imprinted on a stone here. Pilgrims come to bathe and release their sins into the river. The ghat is always busy — dawn to midnight.

**Morning dip:** Even if you're not Hindu, standing in the Ganges at dawn in Haridwar is something. The water is cold (flows straight from glaciers) and the current is strong. Hold the chain. Don't let go.

**Mansa Devi Temple (cable car ₹250, or 30-minute walk):** Perched on Bilwa Parvat hill above Haridwar. One of the Shakti Peethas (sacred Goddess shrines). The views of Haridwar, the Ganges, and the first Himalayan ridges beyond are extraordinary. Go early to avoid queue — 2+ hour wait at peak times.

**Chandi Devi Temple (cable car ₹250):** The second hilltop temple. Connected to Mansa Devi by a 5km forest trail (2 hours, beautiful walk). Or take both cable cars.

**Haridwar Market:** Buy pooja items (marigold garlands, earthen lamps, camphor) from the bazaar near Har Ki Pauri. ₹50 for a full pooja set.

**Evening Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri (6:30–7pm, time varies by season):** The most famous aarti outside Varanasi. Priests wave enormous fire lamps over the Ganges as thousands of oil lamps (diyas) float downstream on leaf boats. The scale is extraordinary. Come 30 minutes early for a spot near the water. Photography allowed and spectacular.

**Where I stayed in Haridwar:** Hotel Ganga Kinare — ₹2,800/night (Ganges-view balcony). Budget: Hotel Aloha — ₹900/night.

## Day 2 — Rishikesh: Rafting, Ashrams & Lakshman Jhula

**Morning drive from Haridwar to Rishikesh** (24km, auto ₹200, taxi ₹400, or shared tempo ₹30).

**White Water Rafting:** Rishikesh is the rafting capital of India. The Ganges provides excellent Grade 2–4 rapids between Shivpuri and Rishikesh.
- **16km stretch (Shivpuri to NIM Beach):** ₹600–₹900, 2.5 hours, includes Grade 3–4 rapids (Golf Course, Three Blind Mice, Roller Coaster). Best for beginners wanting a thrill.
- **26km stretch (Marine Drive):** ₹1,200–₹1,500, 5+ hours. For those who want the full experience.
- **Season:** September–June. Most operators close July–August (monsoon floods).

**Lunch at Little Buddha Café (Lakshman Jhula side):** River-facing restaurant, excellent Israeli food (yes — Rishikesh has a large Israeli backpacker population), momos, pasta. ₹250.

**Lakshman Jhula:** The famous suspension bridge over the Ganges. Built in 1939. The 450m walk across, looking down at the turquoise Ganges below, is lovely. The road beside the bridge has cafés, yoga studios, and Ayurveda shops stretching several kilometres.

**Ram Jhula:** The older bridge, 1km upstream. Less touristy, similar views.

**Tera Manzil Temple (Trayambakeshwar):** A 13-storey tower painted in bright colours, each floor dedicated to a different deity. Climb to the top for views. Free entry.

**Beatles Ashram (Chaurasi Kutia, ₹150 for foreigners, ₹50 for Indians):** The Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's ashram where the Beatles spent 1968 writing the White Album. Abandoned since the 1980s, now partly maintained by the forest department. Graffiti-covered meditation caves, jungle-overgrown buildings, wild peacocks. Atmospheric and unique.

**Evening:** Rishikesh's café culture shines at night. Little Buddha, Café de Goa, and Pyramid Café on the Lakshman Jhula road stay open till 10pm. Live acoustic music at several venues.

**Where I stayed in Rishikesh:** Zostel Rishikesh — ₹700/night (dorm), great social atmosphere, 5 minutes from Lakshman Jhula. Mid-range: Hotel Dewa Retreat — ₹3,200/night.

## Day 3 — Yoga, Bungee & the Neelkanth Temple

**Morning yoga:** Rishikesh is the "World Capital of Yoga." Hundreds of ashrams offer classes.
- **Free:** Parmarth Niketan Ashram — open yoga sessions 6–8am on the river banks.
- **Drop-in:** Sivananda Yoga Vedanta Centre — ₹400/class.
- **Serious:** Yoga Niketan, Phool Chatti Ashram — residential courses from ₹2,000/day including accommodation.

**Bungee Jumping (Jumpin Heights, Mohan Chatti, 28km from Rishikesh):**
India's highest fixed-platform bungee — 83m over the Ganges gorge. Operated by experienced team with international safety standards. ₹3,550 for bungee. Giant Swing (₹3,550) and Flying Fox (₹1,550) also available. Book ahead — limited slots.

**Flying Fox, Giant Swing, and Zip Line:** The same complex at Mohan Chatti operates all three. ₹1,550 each or combo packages.

**Neelkanth Mahadev Temple (32km from Rishikesh, ₹0 entry):** One of Uttarakhand's most important Shiva temples, set in forest at 1,330m. The drive up through rhododendron forest is beautiful. The temple interior is always busy with pilgrims but the surrounding forest is peaceful. Best visited early morning.

## 4 Tips Nobody Puts on Travel Blogs

- **Haridwar Aarti timing changes monthly.** It follows sunset — can be 5:30pm in December or 7:15pm in June. Check the current timing on the day.
- **Best aarti spot in Haridwar:** Upper Ghat (Brahmakund) rather than main Har Ki Pauri ghat — fewer people, closer to the priests.
- **Rishikesh meals on a budget:** The Chotiwala Restaurant near Ram Jhula is touristy but reasonably priced (₹150 thali). For cheaper local food, walk 500m from the ghats into the interior streets.
- **Camping on Ganges banks:** Several campsites between Shivpuri and Rishikesh (₹700–₹1,500/person including tent, meals, campfire). Best experience for budget travellers.
