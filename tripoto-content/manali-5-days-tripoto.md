# Manali in 5 Days — Snow, Cafes & the Spiti Valley Gateway

**Trip Type:** Mountains + Adventure + Road Trip
**Duration:** 5 Days / 4 Nights
**Budget:** ₹8,000–₹28,000 per person (excluding travel from Delhi)
**Best Time:** October–June (winter snow); July–September (green valleys, Spiti access)
**Difficulty:** Easy to Moderate

---

## Why Manali Still Surprises You

Manali is India's most visited hill station. You've seen 10,000 photos of it. And yet — it still surprises you. The way Old Manali's café culture feels like somewhere in Nepal. The way Solang Valley's snow lasts until June. The way Rohtang Pass makes you feel like the world just ended and this is what's on the other side.

This 5-day guide goes beyond the tourist circuit — yes, Solang and Rohtang are in here, but so are the things most people miss.

---

## Day 1 — Arrive Manali: Old Town & Hadimba

**Getting here from Delhi:** Volvo overnight bus (₹800–₹1,400, 14 hours) is the classic choice. Direct flights (Delhi–Bhuntar Airport, 40km from Manali) are faster but pricier. Most travellers take the bus — it's an experience in itself.

**Hadimba Devi Temple:** A 16th-century wooden pagoda temple set in a cedar forest. The craftsmanship is extraordinary — layers of carved wood panels depicting gods, animals, and mythological scenes. Built in 1553. The forest around it is quiet and beautiful. ₹0 entry.

**Old Manali:** Cross the bridge over the Beas River and you're in a different world. Narrow lanes lined with guesthouses, Israeli restaurants, Tibetan jewellery shops, and cafés run by people who came here for a month and stayed for ten years. This is where the traveller culture of Manali lives.

**Best cafés:** Café 1947 (rooftop, mountain views, excellent momo), Drifters' Inn (the classic Old Manali hangout), Lazy Dog Lounge (river-facing, excellent chai).

**Where I stayed:** Apple Country Resort, Old Manali — ₹1,800/night (apple orchard setting, ridiculously comfortable)

---

## Day 2 — Solang Valley & Snow in June

**Solang Valley (14km from Manali):** In winter (December–March), this is a ski resort. In spring (March–June), the lower slopes are green while the upper slopes still have snow — you can be standing in wildflowers and throwing snowballs simultaneously.

**Activities at Solang:**
- Zorbing: ₹300/ride
- Paragliding: ₹2,500 (includes short briefing, 5-minute flight)
- Ropeway: ₹200 up/down
- Snowmobile (March–May): ₹500/ride

**Beas Kund Trek (10km, 5–6 hours):** Starts from Solang Valley, ends at a glacial lake at 3,700m. The trek crosses meadows of wild roses and columbines. You'll have it mostly to yourself. Take a guide from Solang (₹1,000–₹1,500).

**Evening in Manali:** Mall Road for dinner. Chopsticks Restaurant for momos and thukpa — ₹250 for a massive bowl of noodle soup on a cold evening.

---

## Day 3 — Rohtang Pass: The Classic Manali Day

**Rohtang Pass (3,978m):** Connects Manali with Lahaul-Spiti. Snow for 10 months of the year. The road opens June–November; in peak months, the queue of tourist vehicles stretches 10km.

**Permit required:** Online at rohtangpermits.nic.in (₹500 for private cars + ₹100 environmental fee). Get it 2 days before. Limited vehicles allowed daily — book early.

**What you'll find:** Snow fields, a tea stall selling Maggi at ₹80 (double the normal price, triple the satisfaction), views toward Lahaul Valley, and a queue of people in rented snow suits taking the same photos. It's touristy, yes. It's also legitimately spectacular.

**Better option than Rohtang:** If you have your own vehicle (or hire a jeep), continue 20km beyond Rohtang to **Sissu Village** in Lahaul. It's astonishingly beautiful — a turquoise river, waterfall, snow peaks, and almost no one else.

**Atal Tunnel (opened 2020):** The world's longest high-altitude road tunnel (9.02km) bypasses Rohtang and connects Manali to Lahaul year-round. If the Rohtang road is blocked, take the tunnel to Sissu — it's free and takes 15 minutes.

## Day 4 — Naggar Castle & Artist Villages

This is the day most Manali tourists miss. And it's one of the best days.

**Naggar** (22km from Manali, on the left/east bank of the Beas):

**Naggar Castle (₹50 entry):** A 500-year-old castle-hotel built by the Kullu royal family. Part of it is still a guesthouse (₹2,000–₹4,000/night — book if you want a unique stay). The stone and wood architecture, the courtyard, and the views down the valley are magnificent.

**Roerich Art Gallery (₹50):** The estate of Russian artist Nicholas Roerich, who settled here in 1928. His massive Himalayan landscape paintings — the Himalayas in all seasons and light conditions — are extraordinary. The gardens are immaculate.

**Naggar village lanes:** Tiny, authentic, nothing like the tourist Manali. Local women carry firewood, children play cricket on sloped fields, and the views are identical to the ones Roerich painted 90 years ago.

**Lunch at Ragini Hotel restaurant** (inside the castle): Himachali dhaam (festive meal) — madra (chickpeas in yogurt), rajma, sepu vadi, rice. ₹350.

---

## Day 5 — Kullu Rafting & Departure

**Kullu White Water Rafting:** The Beas River between Pirdi and Jhiri (14km, Grade 2–4) is one of the best rafting stretches in India outside Rishikesh.
- Season: July–October (high water) / May–June (lower but still good)
- Cost: ₹600–₹900/person for the full stretch
- Safety: Life jackets and helmets provided. No experience needed for Grade 2–3.

**Manikaran Gurudwara (75km from Manali, in Parvati Valley):** If you have time before departure — one of the most sacred Sikh sites, set in a geothermal hot spring valley. The langar (free community meal) feeds thousands daily. The hot springs are real — locals cook rice in the spring water.

**Bus or taxi back to Delhi (night):** Volvo sleeper buses leave Manali at 5–7pm and arrive Delhi 6am.

## Local Tips Nobody Writes About

- **Kasol detour (90km from Manali):** The Parvati Valley is the backpacker version of Old Manali. Better for longer stays; a day trip from Manali is rushed.
- **Apple season (August–October):** The entire valley is apple orchards. Farmers sell directly from the roadside. ₹40/kg for apples so fresh they taste different from anything you've had before.
- **Don't hire from tourist shops on Mall Road.** Snow suits, trekking boots — rent from equipment shops in Old Manali, they're 40% cheaper.
- **River Café, Old Manali:** Best sunset spot in town. ₹120 chai with unobstructed Beas River views.
