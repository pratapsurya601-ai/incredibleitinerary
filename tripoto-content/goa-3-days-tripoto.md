## The Truth About Goa

Everyone's been to Goa. Or thinks they have. Most people go to Calangute, Baga, or Anjuna — the loud, touristy northern belt — and come back thinking they've done Goa.

They haven't.

This 3-day guide splits your time between North and South Goa the right way: the famous parts, the quiet parts, and the places that'll make you want to come back next year.

## Day 1 — North Goa: The Party Coast Done Right

**Morning:** Rented a scooter from near the airport (₹350–₹500/day, petrol separate). Non-negotiable. You cannot do Goa without two wheels.

First stop: **Anjuna Flea Market** (Wednesdays only). If you're not there on a Wednesday, go to **Arpora Saturday Night Market** instead — better atmosphere, better food, better music. Bought silver jewellery, a hand-stitched bag, and ate the best prawn curry of my life at a stall run by a Goan aunty. ₹280 for a meal that would cost ₹800 at a beach shack.

**Afternoon:** **Vagator Beach** — skip the crowded main beach, walk 10 minutes north to **Ozran (Little Vagator)**. Dramatic red cliffs, smaller crowds, clear water. Scramble up the cliff at the northern end for views straight to Chapora Fort.

**Chapora Fort** (free entry): You know it as the "Dil Chahta Hai fort." Walls are crumbling but the views over the Chapora River and the Arabian Sea are worth the 15-minute climb.

**Evening:** Sunset drinks at **Antares** (Rick's Café) on Vagator — yes it's touristy, but the cliff-side location at sunset is genuinely beautiful. One cocktail, ₹500. The view: free.

**Night:** If you want the full Goa experience — the trance, the lasers, the beach parties — Curlies on Anjuna Beach starts late (post-midnight). If that's not your scene, the shacks on Baga play more relaxed music till midnight.

**Where I stayed:** Hostel Zostel Goa, Anjuna — ₹700/night (dorm)
Private option: Mama's Guesthouse, Vagator — ₹1,800/night

## Day 2 — Old Goa & Panjim: The Side Everyone Skips

This is Goa's secret. Most beach-seekers never come here. Their loss.

**Old Goa** (30 minutes from Anjuna): A UNESCO World Heritage site. The Portuguese built a cathedral city here in the 16th century; it was once bigger than Lisbon. Today: majestic ruins and working churches set among mango trees.

- **Basilica of Bom Jesus**: Holds the body of St Francis Xavier, preserved since 1552. The silver casket is opened once every 10 years. The Baroque interior is stunning regardless of your faith.
- **Se Cathedral**: The largest church in Asia when it was built. The Bell of Gold — cast from looted metal — rings every Sunday.
- Entry: Free. Morning light is magical.

**Panjim (Panaji)**: Goa's capital is walkable, charming, and underrated.

- **Fontainhas Latin Quarter**: Narrow lanes lined with Portuguese-era houses painted in ochre, terracotta, and cream. The most photogenic 30 minutes in Goa.
- **Mahalaxmi Restaurant**: Been serving Goan-Catholic food since 1938. Order the sorpotel (pork offal curry) and bebinca (layered coconut dessert). ₹400 for a proper Goan lunch.
- **Goa State Museum**: Small but surprisingly good. Free entry. Rare collection of Portuguese-era furniture, Hindu bronzes, and Goan Christian icons.

**Evening:** Walk the Mandovi riverside promenade. Grab a feni (Goa's cashew liquor, ₹60 a shot at local bars) and watch the casino boats light up at dusk.

## Day 3 — South Goa: Where the Real Beach Is

South Goa is everything North Goa isn't: quiet, clean, and genuinely beautiful.

**Palolem Beach**: The postcard beach. A crescent of calm, shallow water (great for swimming unlike the rough northern beaches), lined with wooden shack-cafes. Best in early morning before the day-trippers arrive from resorts.

**Butterfly Beach (boat from Palolem, ₹200/person)**: Only accessible by boat or a 45-minute forest trek. Tiny, cove-shaped, almost always peaceful. Go early, it fills by noon.

**Agonda Beach**: 15km north of Palolem. Wilder, longer, calmer. The sea here is crystal clear and the beach is never crowded. Olive Ridley turtles nest here November–February — if you're lucky, you'll see them.

**Cabo de Rama Fort** (free): A Portuguese fort on a 200m sea-cliff. Completely unknown to mass tourism. Views of the coastline from here are among the best in all of Goa.

**Last meal:** Shack lunch at Palolem — prawn balchão (spicy pickled prawns), local fish curry rice, sol kadhi (pink coconut cooler). ₹350. Perfect way to end.

## 5 Things That'll Ruin Your Goa Trip (Avoid These)

1. **Going to Calangute in December.** It's a parking lot. There are 15+ quieter beaches within 20km.
2. **Using Ola/Uber instead of renting a scooter.** Surge pricing is brutal. A scooter gives you freedom and costs less in 3 days than one Ola ride to the airport.
3. **Eating at beach shacks on the main strip.** Walk 200m off the main road and prices drop by 40%.
4. **Missing Old Goa entirely.** Most people spend 3 days on beaches and leave without seeing Goa's actual history.
5. **Booking rooms in November for peak New Year week.** NYE in Goa requires booking 3–4 months ahead. Prices double.
