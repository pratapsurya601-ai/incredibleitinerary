# Bali in 5 Days — Rice Terraces, Temples & the Real Island Beyond the Resorts

**Trip Type:** Beach + Culture + Nature + Wellness
**Duration:** 5 Days / 4 Nights
**Budget:** $30–$120/day per person (₹2,500–₹10,000)
**Best Time:** April–October (dry season)
**Difficulty:** Easy

---

## The Bali That Still Surprises You

Everyone says Bali is overcrowded and overhyped. They're right about parts of it. Kuta and Seminyak are essentially international beach resorts that could be anywhere. But Ubud's rice terraces at 6am with the mist coming in, the fire dance at Uluwatu as the sun drops into the Indian Ocean, the offering ceremonies on every temple step every single morning — that Bali still delivers.

This 5-day route covers the right parts and skips the tourist traps.

---

## Day 1 — Ubud: The Heart of Bali

**Fly into Denpasar (Bali's main airport).** Get a Grab (ride-hailing app) to Ubud — 1.5 hours, ~$12. Don't take airport taxis — they charge 3x.

**Tegallalang Rice Terraces:** 9km north of Ubud. The iconic cascading rice terraces, flooded with water, with coconut palms and morning mist. Go before 7:30am to be there before the tour buses. The terraces are free to walk through — ignore the "donation required" signs at private platforms; the public rice terrace paths are free.

**Ubud Monkey Forest (₹250,000 IDR / ~$15):** 700 Balinese long-tailed macaques live freely in a temple forest in the centre of Ubud. They will steal sunglasses, phones, food, and hats. They are also extraordinarily photogenic. Don't bring anything loose.

**Ubud Market (free):** Across the street from the main palace. Morning market (6–10am) is for locals — vegetables, flowers, offerings. After 10am it shifts to tourist goods — batik, silver jewellery, carved wood. Good place to buy Balinese handicrafts at lower prices than beach resort shops.

**Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren, free):** Active palace in the centre of Ubud. The Kecak Fire Dance is performed here on some evenings — check the schedule. ₹150,000 IDR (~$9) for a front-row seat.

**Dinner:** Locavore (Ubud's famous farm-to-table restaurant) for a special occasion. Mid-range: Warung Ibu Oka (suckling pig — babi guling — roasted whole, Ubud's most famous dish. Queue for it, it's worth it). ₹80,000–₹180,000 IDR for a main.

**Where I stayed:** Alaya Resort Ubud — $85/night (infinity pool overlooking the valley). Budget: Pondok Pitaya — $18/night (clean, fan-cooled, family-run).

## Day 2 — Temples & East Bali

Rent a scooter (₹80,000 IDR/day, ~$5) or hire a driver-guide ($50–$70/full day). Driver-guide is better value if doing multiple temples.

**Pura Tirta Empul (₹50,000 IDR):** A holy water temple where Balinese Hindus perform purification rituals in the sacred springs. You can participate (sarong required, provided at entrance). Watching the ceremony is profoundly moving; participating is an experience few tourists take the time for.

**Kintamani & Mount Batur:** The active volcano (last erupted 1994) sits in a massive caldera with Lake Batur below. Viewpoint from Kintamani restaurant strip (every restaurant has the same view — they don't charge for it, just order something). Sunrise trekking on Mount Batur (~3 hours, $35 guided) is one of Bali's most popular adventures.

**Pura Besakih (₹60,000 IDR):** Bali's mother temple — the most sacred Hindu site on the island — built on the slopes of Mount Agung (Bali's highest volcano). Politically complicated temple for tourists (guides are pushy), but the setting and scale are extraordinary.

**Tirtagangga Water Palace (₹30,000 IDR):** A royal water palace with ornamental pools, fountains, and stepping stones. Less visited than it deserves to be. The surrounding rice terraces are some of Bali's most beautiful.

## Day 3 — South Bali: Seminyak & Uluwatu

**Seminyak:** The upmarket beach strip. La Lucciola, Ku De Ta (now Atea), and Motel Mexicola are the classic institutions. This is where Bali's glossy beach club culture lives. One afternoon here — pool + cocktail + sunset — is worth doing even if it's not your scene. Spectacular at golden hour.

**Uluwatu Temple (₹50,000 IDR):** Perched on a 70m sea cliff on Bali's southwestern tip. The temple itself is accessible to worshippers only, but the cliff walk around the temple is open. One of the most dramatic settings in Southeast Asia — the Indian Ocean stretching to the horizon below you.

**Kecak Fire Dance at Uluwatu (₹150,000 IDR):** Performed at sunset on an open-air stage cut into the cliff. 150 men chanting "cak-cak-cak" in a rhythmic chorus while scenes from the Ramayana play out with fire. The backdrop of the ocean turning orange behind them is extraordinary. Buy tickets from the official temple booth only.

**Dinner at Jimbaran Bay:** 20 minutes from Uluwatu. A bay lined with seafood restaurants on the sand — you pick fresh fish, shellfish, and prawns from the display case, they grill it on the beach. Eat at sunset. Cost: ₹120,000–₹300,000 IDR depending on what you order.

## Day 4 — Nusa Penida: The Wild Island

**Nusa Penida** (boat from Sanur Port, 30 minutes, ₹250,000 IDR round trip): Bali's wild, rugged neighbour island. The roads are bad, the landscape is dramatic, the views are extraordinary.

**Kelingking Beach:** The famous T-Rex cliff viewpoint. One of the most-photographed spots in Southeast Asia. The cliff drops 200m to a white-sand beach shaped like a tyrannosaurus from above. The beach itself requires a 45-minute descent (very steep, ropes provided) — worth doing but start early before the heat.

**Angel's Billabong & Broken Beach:** Natural infinity pools and sea arch formations on Nusa Penida's west coast. The rock formations are extraordinary; the natural pool is swimmable at low tide.

**Crystal Bay:** Nusa Penida's best snorkelling. Mola mola (ocean sunfish — up to 3m) appear here August–October. Mantas are year-round. Rent mask+fins from beach vendors (₹50,000 IDR).

**Full-day scooter rental on Nusa Penida:** ₹80,000 IDR. The roads are rough (some sections are barely paved) but the freedom to explore independently is worth it if you're comfortable on uneven terrain. Driver-guide alternative: ₹350,000 IDR for full day.

---

## Day 5 — Canggu & Departure

**Canggu:** Bali's creative, hipster neighbourhood. Instagram-perfect cafés, surf breaks, coworking spaces, avocado toast at $4. Extremely expat-heavy. Either love it or mildly resent it. Good surf for beginners (Batu Bolong Beach, lessons ₹300,000 IDR/1.5 hours).

**Tanah Lot (₹60,000 IDR):** A sea temple on a rock formation off the coast, only accessible during low tide. The most visited temple in Bali for a reason — the silhouette against the sunset is iconic. Come at 5:30pm.

**Airport departure:** Grab from Canggu (₹180,000 IDR, 45 minutes).

## Bali Tips That Actually Help

- **Grab works everywhere.** Don't use Uber. Don't use metered taxis if you can avoid it (overpriced). Grab shows you the price before you get in.
- **Sarong required at every temple.** Most temples rent them (₹20,000 IDR). Carry your own if you're visiting multiple temples in a day.
- **Bring cash USD.** You can exchange at money changers (get a good rate) or withdraw from ATMs. Avoid airport exchange — terrible rates.
- **Bali Belly is real.** Don't drink tap water anywhere. Use sealed bottles.
- **The best price on crafts:** Ubud market >> Kuta souvenir shops. Same items, 60% lower price in Ubud.
