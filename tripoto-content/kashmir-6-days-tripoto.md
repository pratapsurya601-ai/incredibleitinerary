## Why I Went to Kashmir (And Why You Should Too)

I'd seen the photos on Instagram a hundred times — the shikaras gliding across Dal Lake, the mustard fields of Yusmarg, the snow-capped Himalayas behind every frame. I always assumed it was either too expensive or too complicated. I was wrong on both counts.

Six days in Kashmir changed the way I think about India. This isn't just a destination — it's a feeling. The kind that stays with you for years.

This is my honest account of 6 days in Kashmir — what I did, what I spent, what surprised me, and what I wish someone had told me before I went.

## Day 1 — Srinagar: Dal Lake & The Gardens That Stole My Heart

**Morning:** Landed at Srinagar Airport (SGR). The air hits you first — cool, clean, nothing like Delhi. Pre-booked an airport taxi (₹700 fixed rate, always fix the price before you get in).

Checked into a houseboat on Dal Lake. Yes, it sounds expensive. It isn't. A decent houseboat with meals included runs ₹2,500–₹4,000/night per person. Mine had carved wooden ceilings, a private sit-out facing the lake, and a Kashmiri wazwan breakfast I still dream about.

**Afternoon:** Hired a shikara for 2 hours (₹600 for the full boat, not per person — bargain hard). We floated through the floating vegetable markets, past the lotus garden, past families cooking on their houseboats. The boatman told me this lake feeds 300,000 people. The floating gardens produce tomatoes, cucumbers, and lotus stems sold across Srinagar every morning.

**Evening:** Walked to Nishat Bagh (₹24 entry). The Mughal garden terraces descend toward the lake with the Zabarwan mountains behind — it's one of those views where you just stand and look for a long time.

**Where I stayed:** Swiss-int Houseboat, Dal Lake — ₹3,200/night including breakfast + dinner
**What I spent today:** ₹5,800 (taxi + shikara + entry + dinner)

---

## Day 2 — Gulmarg: The Highest Cable Car in Asia

The drive from Srinagar to Gulmarg takes 2 hours. The road winds through apple orchards and pine forests. In spring, the meadows are bright yellow with mustard flowers.

**The Gondola:** Two phases. Phase 1 (Gulmarg to Kongdoori, 2,650m) costs ₹840. Phase 2 (Kongdoori to Apharwat Peak, 3,979m) costs ₹940. Do both if Phase 2 is open — the views from the top are planetary. Snow stays here until June.

I rented ski boots and walked through the snow like a toddler for 90 minutes. Zero skill, maximum joy.

**Lunch:** A small dhaba near the gondola base served Kashmiri chai (noon chai) and roti sabzi. ₹180 for a filling meal.

**Pro tip:** Go to the gondola before 9am or you'll queue for 2+ hours. Mid-morning crowds are brutal on weekends.

**What I spent today:** ₹3,400 (taxi share + gondola both phases + snow gear rental + meals)

---

## Day 3 — The Drive to Pahalgam: The Most Beautiful Road in India

I hired a cab for the day (₹3,500 Srinagar–Pahalgam with stops). The driver, Tariq, stopped unprompted at Avantipora ruins, a 9th-century Hindu temple now reduced to beautiful carved columns. No entry fee. No crowds. Just you and 1,200 years of history.

We drove through the apple belt of Pulwama (yes, that Pulwama — now peaceful, orchards everywhere), through Anantnag, and arrived in Pahalgam by evening.

**Pahalgam town:** Smaller than Srinagar, calmer, colder. The Lidder River runs straight through the main market. I sat on a boulder over the river for 40 minutes eating a kulhad of kehwa. It cost ₹30. The experience? Priceless.

**Where I stayed:** Hotel Moonlight, Pahalgam — ₹1,800/night (basic but clean, river view)

---

## Day 4 — Pahalgam Valleys: Betaab & Aru

**Betaab Valley (₹50 entry):** Named after the 1983 Sunny Deol film shot here. Lush green meadows, snow-fed streams, horse rides through the pines. Get here by 8am before the day-trippers arrive.

**Aru Valley (15km from Pahalgam):** Quieter, wilder, more beautiful. Requires a local vehicle (₹1,200 round trip from Pahalgam). The valley opens into a wide bowl of alpine meadows with the Kolhoi Glacier visible at the far end. A Gujjar shepherd offered me tea. We sat together without sharing a language and it was one of the best conversations I've ever had.

**Chandanwari (for Amarnath base trek):** If you have time, this is the starting point of the Amarnath Yatra trail. Even without attempting the full trek, the road up to the snowfields is spectacular.

**What I spent today:** ₹2,600 (local taxi + entries + horse ride + meals)

---

## Day 5 — Back to Srinagar: Temples, Crafts & Wazwan

Morning drive back to Srinagar (2 hours). Stopped at Shankaracharya Temple — perched on a hill 300m above the city, 243 steps up. The view of Dal Lake, the city, and the surrounding mountains makes every step worth it.

**Afternoon:** Old City / Nowhatta market. Kashmiri handicrafts are famous for a reason. Pashmina shawls (real ones start at ₹4,000 — if someone's selling for ₹500, it's wool+acrylic blend), paper maché boxes, walnut wood carvings, saffron (buy from government emporiums, not street stalls).

**Evening:** Wazwan dinner at a local restaurant. Wazwan is a 36-course feast — in its simplified restaurant version you'll get rogan josh, yakhni, gushtaba (meatball in yogurt gravy), and seekh kebabs. Budget ₹800–₹1,200/head. Worth every rupee.

---

## Day 6 — Slow Morning, Houseboat Breakfast, Departure

Slept in. Had the houseboat cook make me Kashmiri pink chai (noon chai), girda bread, and an egg dish with local greens. Sat on the deck watching the shikara vendors start their morning rounds.

Checked out, taxi to airport (₹700), home by afternoon carrying saffron, a walnut carving, and about 300 photos.

## 5 Things I Wish I'd Known Before Going

1. **The "AFSPA fear" is mostly outdated.** Srinagar, Gulmarg, Pahalgam are safe for tourists. Locals are among the most hospitable people I've met in India.
2. **Book houseboats directly, not through aggregators.** You'll pay 30% less and get better service.
3. **Carry warm layers even in summer.** Gulmarg and Pahalgam nights can drop to 5°C in June.
4. **Real saffron smells intensely floral.** Fake saffron has no scent or colours your hand orange immediately. Buy from J&K government emporiums.
5. **The gondola does break down.** Have a Plan B (horse ride to the top) and don't book your return flight too tight.

---

## 3 Mistakes Most Tourists Make

- **Going only to Srinagar.** The magic is in Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and the drives between.
- **Booking with big online travel agents.** Local operators (find them via Google Maps reviews) are cheaper, more flexible, and more knowledgeable.
- **Visiting in July-August peak.** Crowds triple, prices double. April-June and September-October are perfect.
